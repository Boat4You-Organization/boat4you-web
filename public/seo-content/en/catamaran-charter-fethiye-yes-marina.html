Top Sailing Routes from Fethiye

The waters around Fethiye offer extraordinary diversity. The classic one-week itinerary typically covers Ölüdeniz bay, where turquoise waters shelter beneath towering cliffs and the world's largest paragliding site. Anchor in the sandy bay, swim among colorful fish, and enjoy sunset drinks on deck as paragliders descend against a golden sky.

Butterfly Valley, located south of Ölüdeniz, is equally iconic. The anchorage lies in a dramatic amphitheater of limestone cliffs, accessible only by boat or challenging footpath. The valley hosts thousands of endemic Lycian Swallows butterflies during migration season, and a rustic beach restaurant serves grilled fish and Turkish mezze. Overnight anchoring here is an unforgettable experience on any Boat4You charter.

The 12 Islands present a collection of smaller islands ideal for day sailing and exploration. Each island offers unique anchorages, underwater archaeology sites, and opportunities for swimming and snorkeling. Some islands feature Lycian tombs carved into cliffsides—a window into Turkey's ancient heritage visible from the water.

For longer voyages, sailing east toward Göcek (35 kilometers) and Marmaris (65 kilometers) extends the adventure. The coastline features numerous hidden bays, small fishing villages, and uninhabited islands perfect for solitude and natural beauty.

Best Seasons for Catamaran Sailing

May through September represents the prime sailing season in Fethiye, when the meltemi wind provides consistent, predictable breezes typically ranging from 12 to 20 knots. These northerly winds create ideal sailing conditions and contribute to the region's clear skies and warm temperatures.

April and October are shoulder-season options offering pleasant weather, lighter crowds, and mild winds. Water temperatures are comfortable for swimming (between 18°C and 22°C). June through August peak summer sees the warmest water (up to 28°C) but also the most tourists and competitive pricing.

Winter sailing is possible but less common. The meltemi wind diminishes, but southern gales can occur. Water temperatures drop to 15-16°C, requiring thicker wetsuits for extended swimming.

Turkish Cuisine and Provisioning

One of sailing's greatest pleasures is dining well aboard. Turkish coastal cuisine is Mediterranean at its finest. Fethiye's markets overflow with fresh fish—sea bream, grouper, and sea bass caught daily by local fishermen. Turkish tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers, and eggplants are at peak ripeness during summer months. Feta cheese, yogurt, olives, and locally pressed olive oil are pantry staples.

Every anchorage along your route hosts small waterfront restaurants serving grilled fish, meze platters of olives, cheeses, and dips, and traditional kebabs. Many welcome yachtsmen to dine ashore or will deliver takeaway meals to the catamaran. Turkish coffee, strong and cardamom-spiced, is a morning ritual worth adopting during your charter.

Family-Friendly Sailing in Fethiye

Catamarans from Yes Marina are exceptionally suitable for family sailing. The shallow draft allows anchoring in protected bays where water is warm, calm, and ideal for young swimmers. Multiple cabins provide privacy and comfort, while the spacious cockpit and trampolines offer safe play areas. Many families bring children as young as four years old on week-long charters without difficulty.

Paragliding is available ashore at Ölüdeniz for adventurous adults. Cultural visits to Lycian archaeological sites or the charming village of Kayaköy add educational value to the sailing holiday.

Practical Information and Getting Started

Fethiye is approximately 42 kilometers from Dalaman International Airport, with shuttle services and rental cars available. The drive through pine forests takes roughly one hour. Immigration is straightforward for EU citizens; other nationalities should verify visa requirements for Turkey.

Before departure, all crew should hold valid passports. A sailing license or RYA/ASA certification is typically required to charter independently; Turkish Customs requires notification of departure and arrival at each port.

Insurance is mandatory and arranins comprehensive coverage. Security deposits are standard practice. Peak-season pricing runs June through August; shoulder seasons offer savings of 15-30%. Discounts often apply for week-long charters compared to shorter durations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a sailing license to charter a catamaran at Fethiye Yes Marina?

International sailing licenses (RYA Day Skipper or ASA 104) are typically required.

What is the best time to visit Fethiye for catamaran sailing?

May through September offers the most reliable meltemi winds and warmest weather. June-August are warmest but busiest. April-May and September-October provide excellent conditions with fewer tourists and competitive pricing.

How far is Dalaman Airport from Fethiye Yes Marina?

Approximately 90 kilometers, with a scenic drive of about one hour through Turkish countryside. Shuttle services and car rentals are readily available for airport transfers.

Can I bring children on a catamaran charter With us?

Absolutely. Catamarans are exceptionally family-friendly, with stable platforms, spacious accommodations, and access to calm, protected anchorages perfect for young swimmers.

Are provisions and provisioning services included in the charter?

Provisions are not included, but Yes Marina staff can assist with shopping lists and recommend suppliers. Many charterers pre-arrange provisioning delivery to the boat before departure.
