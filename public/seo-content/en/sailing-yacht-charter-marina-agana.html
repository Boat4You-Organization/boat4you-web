Sailing Routes from Marina Agana

The Drvenik islands—Drvenik Veli and Drvenik Mali—offer the quickest offshore sailing objective. Pine-backed coves, sandy holding, and minimal commercial traffic make these islands perfect for first-night anchorages. Wind-ward approaches from the north provide excellent sailing angles, particularly during spring and autumn when the mistral blows steady at 15–22 knots.

Šolta island, famous for olive and citrus groves, lies slightly west. Moreška village and Grohote cove provide sheltered harbors. Passage from Agana to Šolta—roughly 6 nm—often yields consistent sailing in moderate wind, rewarding crews with picturesque village provisioning stops.

Longer passages to Brač (15 nm) and Hvar (22 nm) remain comfortably within daily sailing distance, particularly if you depart Marina Agana by dawn. The channel between islands provides funneled wind and moderate swell, ideal conditions for testing your sailing yacht's performance. Summer thermals in July–August challenge patience; spring and autumn (April–May, September–October) deliver more consistent wind.

Seasonal Wind and Weather Patterns

The mistral (northwesterly) dominates spring and autumn, blowing 12–25 knots and providing the purest sailing. Summer brings lighter thermals and occasional lulls mid-day, though evening offshore breezes often reward patient crews. The Jugo (southeasterly) arrives sporadically, bringing warm air and larger swell; it's manageable for island shelter within hours of departure.

Spring (April–May) offers ideal conditions: consistent wind, water temperatures 17–19°C, and fewer boats. Summer (June–August) brings calm mornings and strong afternoon thermals—a mixed blessing for pure sailors. Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (21–23°C) with reliable wind patterns reminiscent of spring, making it many sailors' favorite season.
