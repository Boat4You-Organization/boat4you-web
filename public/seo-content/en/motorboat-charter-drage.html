Day-Trip Motorboat Adventures

Pašman island lies just 15 minutes away by motorboat from Marina Drage. The village of Pašman Town welcomes motorboat visitors with charming mooring and a harborside taverna serving fresh fish. A typical half-day excursion includes a morning departure, leisurely anchorage, lunch ashore, afternoon swimming, and early evening return—ideal for families or those seeking casual exploration without overnight commitment.

Ugljan island, stretching 23 kilometres northwest, accommodates motorboat day trips with ease. The village of Preko (45 minutes by motorboat) offers restaurants, shops, and beaches. Many families choose 24-hour motorboat stays (overnight mooring at Preko, full-day exploration, next-day return), blending day-trip simplicity with overnight flexibility.

Pakoštane (10 km north) and Biograd (15 km south) offer alternative motorboat destinations along the coast, featuring beaches, waterfront dining, and superstores for provisioning. These shorter journeys suit those with limited time or preference for easier logistics.

Multi-Day Motorboat Charters: Weekly Itineraries

A week-long motorboat charter from Marina Drage opens the northern Dalmatian coast. Days 1–2 explore Pašman and Ugljan islands, anchoring in multiple bays, visiting fishing villages, and enjoying leisurely swimming. Days 3–4 venture toward the Kornati National Park, arriving by midday and spending 24–48 hours exploring its 140 limestone islands, dramatic rocky formations, and anchorages of remarkable clarity.

Days 5–6 typically return northward via Telašćica or Sakarun Bay (both spectacular natural harbours worth overnight stays), or detour toward quieter coastal options. Day 7 completes the return to Marina Drage, with time for final provisioning and departure preparation.

Larger motorboats (35–40 feet) deliver economical cruising at 12–15 knots, burning 10–15 litres per hour—ideal for those prioritizing comfort and fuel conservation. Speedier vessels (25–30+ knots) reach destinations quickly, trading fuel efficiency for time savings and adventure variety.

Seasons & Motorboat Conditions

Spring (April–May) offers calm conditions, fewer crowds, and mild weather—ideal for families and cautious operators. Water temperatures reach 17–19°C; motorboats cruise predictably without wind variables. Summer (June–August) brings warm water (22–25°C), lively boat traffic, and busier anchorages, though Marina Drage remains quieter than larger bases.

Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (22–23°C) with manageable crowds and consistent weather. Winter operations are possible but demand respect for occasional storms and shorter daylight. Most motorboat charters operate April through October; winter availability is limited.

Food, Provisioning & Local Culture

Marina Drage's waterfront restaurants serve daily catches—gregada (traditional fish stew), grilled sea bass, octopus salads, and local pasta prepared from generations-old recipes. Owners often fish themselves or source from immediate neighbours; menus reflect the morning's catch rather than predetermined standards. Wine selections favour Croatian reds and whites from nearby regions.

Provisioning combines village markets (at Drage and nearby towns) with motorboat flexibility. Fresh bread, cheese, and local produce are sourced daily. Many motorboat charters feature a mix of dinners ashore and meals prepared aboard, combining provisioning economy with restaurant variety.
