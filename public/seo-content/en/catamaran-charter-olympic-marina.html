Wind, Weather, and Seasonal Sailing

The Aegean's summer Etesian winds (northerly, Force 3–4 typically) blow steadily June–September, creating the Mediterranean's most reliable sailing conditions. Spring and autumn bring lighter, more variable winds but equally rewarding sailing. Water temperatures range from 16 degrees Celsius in spring to a refreshing 25–26 degrees Celsius in late summer.

Olympic Marina's all-weather infrastructure handles transition seasons excellently. Autumn gales occasionally sweep the Aegean, but the marina's protective design ensures your catamaran remains secure.

Catamaran Advantages for Aegean Exploration

Catamarans' twin hulls provide superior stability—families and less-experienced sailors feel immediately confident. Shallow draft (typically 1.2–1.5 meters) allows exploration of protected anchorages monohulls cannot access. Spacious cabins and generous deck space suit larger groups perfectly. Modern catamarans perform well to windward, dispelling myths about multihull upwind ability.

Provisioning and Island Dining from Olympic Marina

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.
