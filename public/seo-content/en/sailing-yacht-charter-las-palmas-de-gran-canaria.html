ARC Rally Heritage: November Preparation Atmosphere

The Atlantic Rally for Cruisers annually transforms Las Palmas into a vibrant sailing festival. Each November, 200-250 oceangoing yachts gather to depart for the Caribbean in a coordinated fleet, promoting safety and camaraderie across a 3,000-nautical-mile Atlantic passage.

The rally's departure coincides with optimal Atlantic weather windows—the end of hurricane season, stable pressure systems, and consistent trade winds. ARC yachts spend August-November in Las Palmas completing final preparations, provisioning, crew training, and safety briefings.

This atmosphere permeates the harbor year-round. Marina bars buzz with passage planning discussions, weather routing seminars, and crew coordination meetings. Chartering during ARC season (August-November) immerses you in this preparation culture. You'll encounter experienced Atlantic sailors, learn from their passage experience, and absorb the maritime knowledge that transforms casual sailors into capable oceangoing cruisers.

Boat4You schedules extended charters to align with ARC season, allowing sailors to experience Las Palmas' unique cultural and maritime atmosphere while undertaking their own bluewater sailing adventures.

Sailing Routes and Passage Planning

Las Palmas' central location enables diverse passage planning. The main sailing corridor heads southward to Maspalomas (45 nm, 6-10 hours, typical conditions), offering sheltered overnight anchorage and beach resort access at Playa del Inglés or Playa de las Américas.

Westward passages traverse open Atlantic waters toward Tenerife's north coast (90 nm, 12-18 hours). This route showcases the Canaries' volcanic drama, passing beneath Mount Teide (3,718 meters, visible from 50 nm offshore on clear days) and historic harbor towns like Garachico.

Extended westward passages reach remote La Palma (80 nm) and El Hierro (120 nm), featuring pristine anchorages, minimal tourist infrastructure, and traditional Canarian character. These longer passages suit experienced sailors comfortable with multi-day open-ocean sailing. Trade winds remain favorable; typical passages require 14-24 hours depending on sea state and wind conditions.

For bluewater sailors, Las Palmas represents the departure point for transatlantic passages toward the Caribbean (3,000+ nm, 3-4 weeks), Azores (300+ nm), or African coast passages. arranges extended charters for these ambitious sailing dreams, providing crew coordination, provisioning logistics, and emergency communication support.

Weather Routing and Passage Strategy

Las Palmas' professional weather forecasting services provide daily routing briefings essential for Atlantic sailing. Wind patterns, swell forecasts, pressure system predictions, and optimal passage timing information help skippers plan safe, efficient routes.

The trade wind systems remain remarkably stable. Northeast winds blow consistently from October through May at 12-20 knots, with stronger velocities (15-25 knots) during winter months. Summer months experience lighter, more variable winds (8-15 knots) and occasional doldrums, extending passage durations but reducing heavy-weather risk.

Professional routing services can be engaged through the marina's weather office, providing detailed passage recommendations, current analysis, and contingency planning. includes routing briefings in comprehensive pre-departure packages.
