Sailing Routes & Island Destinations

From Lavrion, the northern Saronic Gulf is your playground. Hydra—a traffic-free island with whitewashed harbourfront—sits 2–3 hours upwind. The sheltered Spetses anchorage, with its pine-fringed bays, is equally rewarding. Eastbound routes take you to the dramatic cliffs and deep blue waters of Dokos, or round into the Argolic Gulf to explore Nafplio's Venetian harbour and the Temple of Poseidon at Cape Sounio.

Multi-day cruises venture further: loop Aegina and Poros for a classic three-island week, or sail the quieter Peloponnesian coast around Ermioni and Tolo.

Conditions & Sailing Characteristics

The Saronic Gulf is among Europe's most consistent and user-friendly sailing grounds. Summer Etesian winds blow steady from the north, typically 10–18 knots, with warm, stable air and minimal fog. Afternoon sea breezes develop as heating intensifies, giving excellent sailing by noon and slack evening conditions perfect for dinner ashore.

Wave heights average 0.5–1.5 metres in summer; occasional stronger southerlies bring slightly choppier seas but remain well within the comfort zone for cruising monohulls. Tidal range is negligible in the Saronic, simplifying navigation and anchorage planning.

Port Facilities & Provisioning

Lavrion Main Port offers everything a charter crew needs: fuel quays, water points, waste disposal, and ice-makers near the marina office. The port is well-supplied with tavernas, especially around the ferry terminal and waterfront. The town market (Thursdays and Fridays) stocks fresh fruit, vegetables, local cheese, and regional wines.

Supermarkets, bakeries, and fish shops are within 500 metres of the quays. Most charterers provision in the morning and weigh anchor by noon, enjoying a first night's sail to a nearby anchorage.
