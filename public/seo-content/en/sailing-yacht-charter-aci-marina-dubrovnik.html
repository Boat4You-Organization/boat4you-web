Sailing Yacht Routes from Dubrovnik

The Dalmatian coast presents excellent sailing conditions and diverse itinerary possibilities.

Elafiti Islands Sailing Circuit

The three primary Elafiti Islands—Koločep, Lopud, and Šipan—lie 3–5 nautical miles northwest of ACI Marina Dubrovnik. Prevailing northwest Adriatic breezes (typically 12–18 knots spring–autumn) provide reliable sailing conditions to these islands. Traditional anchorages between islands offer protected overnight mooring with excellent swimming and village exploration opportunities.

Mljet and Korčula Sailing Adventure

Approximately 35 nautical miles south, Mljet National Park offers forested landscapes, saltwater lakes, and historical sites. Nearby Korčula—traditionally associated with Marco Polo—provides medieval architecture, wine tastings, and excellent dining. The 70-nautical-mile itinerary suits 7–10 day charters combining sailing passages with cultural exploration. Spring and autumn prevailing winds favor the southern route.

Pelješac Peninsula Coastal Cruise

A shorter 2–4 day itinerary explores the Pelješac Peninsula's east coast, including Ston's UNESCO-listed fortifications and oyster farming operations. Consistent Adriatic breezes—particularly reliable April–May and September–October—facilitate pleasant sailing passages interspersed with anchorage relaxation.
