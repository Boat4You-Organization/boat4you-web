Sailing Yacht Charter from Bocca di Magra

Bocca di Magra's river-mouth harbour is one of the Mediterranean's most distinctive sailing bases. Rather than a formal marina, this natural anchorage at the confluence of the Magra River and Ligurian Sea offers protected mooring, minimal bureaucracy, and instant access to some of Europe's most spectacular sailing waters: Cinque Terre to the north and the limestone cliffs of Portovenere to the southeast. Bocca di Magra Harbour: Natural Mooring and Protection

Unlike most Mediterranean harbours, Bocca di Magra has no piers or fixed berths. Instead, vessels moor to buoys managed by the local harbor master (Capitaneria di Porto) or anchor freely in the river mouth's protected basin. The buoy field accommodates approximately 15–20 vessels year-round; visitor buoys are available on a first-come, first-served basis, though Boat4You reserves space in advance for charter yachts. Anchoring off the river mouth (in 4–6 metres of sand and mud) is an alternative if buoys are full; the holding is excellent, though you'll need stronger wind to achieve confidence in your anchor set.

The river mouth itself—roughly 80 metres wide at the entrance—opens to a sheltered basin approximately 300 metres across. This basin is deep enough (4–8 metres) for all Boat4You yachts and protected from prevailing northwest winds by the surrounding headlands. The only real wind that troubles the anchorage is a southeasterly Scirocco (rare, but when it arrives, it brings warm African air and potential grey skies). Tidal range is minimal (0.4–0.6 metres typically; up to 1.0 metre during spring tides), simplifying overnight watches.

Draft and Vessel Suitability

The river mouth's depth is the primary consideration. The entrance channel and basin maintain approximately 2.5–3.0 metres at low tide, with occasional silt bars reducing this to 2.2 metres after heavy river flooding. Boat4You charter yachts from Bocca di Magra are exclusively monohulls drawing 1.5–1.8 metres—comfortable safety margins. Vessels drawing 2.0 metres or deeper should depart or return only at or near high tide; your broker will provide tide tables and optimal timing.

Most yachts are 10–13 metres in overall length—the sweet spot for the river mouth's scale and for access to narrow anchorages (like Vernazzo in Cinque Terre, where tight spaces demand nimble boats). Catamarans are rarely offered from Bocca di Magra because their wider beam increases piloting complexity in the river mouth and makes anchoring in the tighter coves impossible.

Buoy Mooring Systems and Procedures

Bocca di Magra's buoy mooring system uses standard Mediterranean-style mooring buoys with spliced chain leads to seabed anchors. Your charter includes one complimentary night's mooring; when you arrive, contact the harbor master's office (positioned on the starboard bank of the river mouth) to confirm your assigned buoy. Procedure is simple: motor to your buoy, rig a bow line to the floating pickup, secure it to your fairlead, and moor stern-to the buoy's secondary swivel. Total setup time: 5–10 minutes.

Daily mooring fees for additional nights cost approximately €12–€18, payable when you depart. The system is honour-based; you simply inform the harbor master of your departure, and billing is settled via credit card or cash at a kiosk near the office. Boat4You arranges pre-payment for many multi-night stays, eliminating this detail from your charter week.

Tide Management and River Navigation

The Magra River is navigable for approximately 2–3 kilometres upstream from the mouth at high tide, with draft constraints between 1.2–1.6 metres depending on seasonal flow. Many sailors motor slowly upstream in the early morning, anchor in the peaceful river valley surrounded by Mediterranean scrub and wild boar habitat, and return to the sea basin in early afternoon. This unique experience—few Mediterranean sailors ever venture into actual rivers—is available only from Bocca di Magra.

Current speeds in the river mouth are modest (typically under 0.5 knots) except during spring tides, when flows can exceed 1.0 knot. Your broker will brief you before departure on optimal river-access windows and cautions about silting during high-water months (autumn rains can shift the river-mouth bar).

Check-In, Briefing, and Orientation

Bocca di Magra's check-in is streamlined. You'll arrive at the harbor master's office with your charter documents and vessel keys. A brief orientation (typically 30 minutes) covers local mooring etiquette, tide tables, river-mouth navigation, and mandatory reporting via VHF radio when departing or returning. Most charters include 2–3 hours of on-water practice with a professional broker or skipper before you sail solo.

If you've booked a crewed charter, your professional skipper will already be aboard at check-in, having completed all systems checks and weather briefings. You'll review your proposed itinerary (Cinque Terre day sail, Portovenere overnight, Lerici extension) over coffee, then motor out for lunch ashore before sunset returns you to the buoy mooring.

Provisioning and Waterfront Services

Bocca di Magra is minimally developed—a handful of waterfront restaurants and a small village shop constitute the local facilities. For provisioning, your options are: (1) use the village shop for essentials and fresh bread, (2) request that pre-stock your galley with supplies purchased in larger nearby towns (La Spezia, 15 kilometres), or (3) motor to Portovenere or Lerici for more comprehensive shopping and superior restaurants.

The two notable waterfront restaurants—Ivo Camere and Da Ivo—serve exceptional Ligurian seafood pasta and grilled fish. Both accept reservations and can provision a tender with packed lunches for day sails. The white-wine pairings (Vermentino, Pigato) from Ligurian vineyards are outstanding and reasonably priced.

Bocca di Magra's primary advantage is proximity to Cinque Terre, just 8 kilometres north. Arriving by sea offers a perspective unavailable to tourists arriving by train or hiking: the five villages stacked impossibly on their cliffs appear suddenly around the northern headland, a vision that justifies the entire sailing trip. Seasonal Variations and Weather Challenges

Spring (April–May) brings moderate conditions but occasional westerly storms; autumn (September–October) is more stable. Summer can see strong afternoon Maestrale winds funnelling through the Ligurian coast. Rare southeasterly Sciroccos—warm winds from Africa—can make the river-mouth anchorage uncomfortable; your broker will advise relocating if significant weather develops. Winter (November–February) is typically unsuitable for casual charters, though experienced sailors occasionally undertake winter passages. 's weather briefings include detailed forecasting and contingency planning; you'll rarely be surprised by deteriorating conditions.

Access to La Spezia and Regional Services

La Spezia, 15 kilometres north by motor, is a substantial working port with comprehensive facilities: shipyards, engine-repair specialists, full chandleries, provisioning markets, and larger hotels and restaurants. Many sailors use La Spezia as an alternative base for longer stays or multi-week charters requiring periodic maintenance. The town is also a transportation hub: train connections to Florence, Milan, and beyond; bus networks reaching inland villages; and rental-car agencies. can arrange temporary relocation to La Spezia if extended repairs become necessary during your charter.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the correct way to enter Bocca di Magra in a sailing yacht?

Motor slowly (under 3 knots) into the river mouth, keeping starboard (right) of the channel's green buoys and port (left) of the red buoys. The harbor master's office is on your starboard hand; signal your arrival via VHF radio (Channel 16, then switch to the assigned working frequency, typically Channel 74). Once inside the basin, reduce to idle speed and proceed to your assigned buoy. Rarely drop anchor inside the buoy field; typically use a mooring buoy to prevent collision with other vessels.

Is Bocca di Magra appropriate for inexperienced sailors?

Bocca di Magra's river-mouth entry, tidal management, and piloting demands are moderate—not extreme, but beyond complete beginners. recommends at least 5–7 days of recent sailing experience or a crewed charter with a professional skipper aboard. If you're confident with basic sail-handling and engine operation, a professional briefing and buoy mooring practice will prepare you adequately.

Can I stay in Bocca di Magra for multiple weeks?

Yes. Monthly mooring rates are available at reduced daily rates (typically €8–€12 per night for extended periods). can arrange multi-week charters with periodic provisioning runs to La Spezia or Portovenere. However, if you're planning a month-long stay, consider whether a temporary-resident mooring (arranged with the harbor master) or a mud-berth in La Spezia marina (more infrastructure) might better suit your needs.

What happens during stormy weather?

The river-mouth basin provides good protection from northerly and westerly gales but offers limited shelter from rare southeasterly Sciroccos. During forecasted strong winds, recommends either (a) motoring upstream into the river valley for maximum protection, (b) relocating to the nearby protected harbour at Portovenere (10 kilometres, 30 minutes motor), or (c) curtailing your charter and mooring ashore at a land-based facility. Your charter includes comprehensive weather briefing and contingency planning; rarely hesitate to contact our 24-hour support line if conditions deteriorate unexpectedly.

What are the boat-length and beam restrictions for Bocca di Magra?

No official maximum length exists; we've moored 16-metre yachts successfully. However, practical limits are around 13–14 metres overall length and 4 metres beam. The river mouth's width (80 metres at entrance) allows two-way traffic comfortably, but tight turns and proximity to anchored vessels demand careful helmsmanship. pairs larger yachts with professional skippers for Bocca di Magra charters to ensure safe, confident passages.

What other nearby harbours should I know about in case Bocca di Magra's buoys are full?

La Spezia (15 kilometres north) has excellent modern marinas and is rarely full. Portovenere (10 kilometres south) has limited visitor berths but offers sheltered anchorages off the harbour. Lerici (12 kilometres southeast) has a protected port and is another reliable fallback. pre-coordinates with all three ports to ensure you typically have a confirmed berth, eliminating the stress of searching for available mooring.

How do I know if my sailing experience is adequate for Bocca di Magra?

A good rule: if you've completed at least 5 days of recent coastal sailing (within the past 6 months), feel confident with engine operation, can identify basic navigation hazards, and are comfortable checking weather forecasts and tidal information, you're likely ready for Bocca di Magra.
