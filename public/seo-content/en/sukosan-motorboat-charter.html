The Appeal of Motorboats in Northern Dalmatia

Motorboats excel where speed and flexibility matter. The Maestral wind—which energizes sailors and catamaran crews—is irrelevant to motorboat operators. If conditions look perfect on Tuesday morning but your plan specified Wednesday, a motorboat allows spontaneous itinerary adjustment. The Kornati archipelago, 15–20 nautical miles from Sukošan, becomes a quick 1–2 hour dash rather than a half-day sail. A morning departure from D-Marin Dalmacija can place you anchored in Levrnaka Cove or Rava by late morning, maximizing swimming and exploration time.

RIBs—rigid-inflatable boats typically 6–8 metres—combine shallow draft with speed (30–35 knots). They access extremely shallow anchorages where larger vessels cannot venture and navigate the Kornati's narrow channels with ease. Their inflatable sponsons absorb chop, and open-air cockpits provide unobstructed views. Larger powerboats (10–14 metres) offer cabin shelter, galley facilities, and anchoring comfort suitable for families or groups seeking multi-day cruises with fewer stops.

Motorboat Types and Capabilities

RIBs (6–8 metres, 50–150 hp): Fast, nimble, perfect for day trips. Speeds of 30–35 knots enable Sukošan-to-Kornati passages in 45 minutes to 1.5 hours. Open cockpits and shallow draft allow exploration of coves inaccessible to larger vessels. Limited cabin space makes RIBs best for day charters or 1–2 night trips by couples or small groups. Fuel consumption is moderate relative to their speed; a typical 50-litre tank allows 4–6 hours of cruising.

Powerboats 10–14 metres (100–250 hp): Offer cabin shelter, galley, head, and sleeping quarters. Speed ranges from 20–30 knots depending on hull design and weight. Better suited for 3–5 day family or friend-group charters. Anchoring comfort exceeds RIBs. Fuel consumption is higher; planning fuel stops may be necessary on longer journeys.

No-Licence Boats (Under 10 m, Limited Horsepower): Several charter companies in D-Marin Dalmacija offer boats under local regulations not requiring boating certification. These typically max out at 30–45 hp, achieving 10–15 knot speeds—adequate for relaxed coastal exploration and anchorages reachable within 2–3 hours. Ideal for tourists seeking freedom without formal licensing.

Who Licenses Are and Aren't Required

Most countries, including Croatia, regulate motorboat operation by engine power and vessel length. Boats under 10 metres with engines under 50 hp (often called "license-free" boats) can be hired without proof of boating competency. Boat4You partners verify local regulations and assist clients in selecting appropriately licensed options based on your credentials and preferences. If you hold a boat operating license (RYA Power Boat, IYT Powerboat, or equivalent), you can charter larger, faster vessels independently. Bareboat motorboat charters typically require basic competency proof; hiring a skipper eliminates licensing requirements entirely.

Practical Advantages of Motorboats

Speed is the obvious advantage. A motorboat reaches Kornati in 1–2 hours versus 4–5 for a sailing yacht. But flexibility matters equally: if winds become too strong or too light, or if weather threatens, a motorboat maintains course and schedule. For families with fixed vacation schedules or groups with competing interests, this reliability is invaluable. Predictability allows children to anticipate arrival times, reducing motion sickness risk and frustration.

No sailing experience is required. Operating a motorboat—even one requiring licensing—involves basic compass navigation, engine management, and mooring skills learnable in a brief briefing. Skippers provided by charter companies handle all technical aspects for those preferring a fully guided experience. Fuel stops are manageable; D-Marin Dalmacija, Zadar harbours, and Rogoznica (south of Sukošan) all offer fuel docks.

Best Times and Conditions for Motorboating

Year-round operation is feasible, but May–October offers warm water (18–27°C), reliable weather, and accessible fuel/provisioning. July–August provide warm conditions and busy Kornati anchorages; May–June and September–October offer fewer crowds and lower prices. Winter operation (November–April) is possible but brings occasional Bura squalls, shorter daylight, and reduced provisioning access.

Motorboats are less affected by wind than sailing vessels. Maestral winds (12–18 knots) are negligible; stronger Bura squalls (15–25 knots) may create chop and reduced visibility but don't prevent operation. Wave state is the primary concern: 1–1.5 metre sea states are comfortable; 2+ metre conditions in RIBs become uncomfortable for passengers and less safe for inexperienced operators.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a boat licence to rent a motorboat from Sukošan?

It depends on the boat's size and engine power. License-free boats (typically under 10 m, under 50 hp) require no formal qualification. Larger boats usually need proof of competency (RYA Power Boat or equivalent). Hiring a professional skipper eliminates this requirement entirely. Boat4You advises you on options matching your credentials.

How fast are motorboats compared to sailing yachts?

RIBs cruise at 25–35 knots, reaching Kornati in 1–1.5 hours versus 4–5 hours for sailing yachts. Larger powerboats cruise slower (15–25 knots) but still outpace catamarans and monohulls significantly, especially on windless days.

What's fuel consumption like?

RIBs consume 40–70 litres/hour at cruising speed. A 50–75 litre tank provides 4–6 hours of range (200+ nautical miles). Larger powerboats consume 30–50 litres/hour, with 150–200+ litre tanks offering similar ranges. Fuel costs typically €40–€100/day for active day trips. Boat4You charter partners factor fuel into daily rates for transparency.

Are motorboats suitable for weak swimmers or non-swimmers?

Yes, with precautions. Life jackets and flotation aids are mandatory. Anchored motorboats in shallow bays provide safe swimming platforms. Skippers or experienced crew supervise water activities. Families with non-swimmers or young children should communicate this during booking; charter companies match you with appropriately crewed options.

Can motorboats explore the same anchorages as sailing yachts?

Yes, and often with better access. Motorboats' shallow drafts (often 0.8–1.2 metres for RIBs, 1.2–1.8 for larger boats) penetrate coves unreachable by deep-keeled monohulls. Anchoring is faster (engine control, no sail management). The trade-off is noise, fuel cost, and the absence of the silence sailors prize.
