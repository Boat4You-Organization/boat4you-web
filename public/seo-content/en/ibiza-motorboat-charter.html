Why a Motorboat Suits Ibiza

Motorboats excel in Ibiza's island-hopping culture. Fast cruising (15–25 knots) means covering large distances efficiently; Formentera is reachable in 45 minutes from central Ibiza, leaving hours for beach time. No sailing expertise required—point and go. Shallow draft (0.8–1.2 metres) accesses tight anchorages unavailable to larger yachts. Fuel efficiency is comparable to larger vessels when cruising economically. For groups prioritising exploration, swimming, and beach socialising over nautical tradition, motorboats deliver unmatched flexibility and value. Ibiza's protected bays and short inter-island passages suit day-chartering perfectly.

Top Routes and Anchorages

Depart Marina Ibiza or Santa Eulalia. Cala Salada is a 20-minute run; pristine white sand and shallow turquoise water ideal for swimming. Cala Bassa (30 minutes) offers sunset culture and beachside clubs. San Antonio (40 minutes) delivers Café del Mar fame and western nightlife. Talamanca (15 minutes) provides serene family-friendly swimming. Half-day trips to Formentera (45 minutes) unlock Ses Illetes' Caribbean-like beaches and Es Pujols village exploration. Ses Salines (southeast, 1 hour) showcases southern cliffs and posidonia meadows (UNESCO-protected). Es Vedrà circling (west, 30 minutes from Marina Ibiza) offers iconic photography at sunset.

Technical Considerations

Motorboats range 20–40 feet, displacing 4–15 tonnes. Single or twin outboard motors (50–150 HP per engine) or inboard diesels (100–250 HP) deliver reliable propulsion. Cruising speed 12–18 knots; maximum speed 25–35+ knots (fuel-dependent). Draft 0.8–1.2 metres is ideal for Ibiza's shallow bays. Modern motorboats feature GPS, depth sounders, and simple autopilot. Fuel consumption 8–15 litres/hour at cruise; daily fuel costs €15–€30 at Ibiza's €1.20/litre diesel prices. Most charter motorboats lack sleeping cabins; day-charter is standard. Weekend or multi-day options typically use small cabin space (berths for 1–2).

Pricing and Charter Terms

Motorboat day-charters (8 hours) range €400–€800; weekend charters (Friday–Sunday) €1,000–€2,000. Weekly bareboat motorboats (rare; typically house boats) cost €1,500–€3,000. Prices include fuel, insurance, and basic provisioning (ice, water). Skipper hire (€80–€120 daily) is optional; most guests operate independently after brief instruction. Deposits (20–30%) are non-refundable beyond 45 days. Boat4You recommends booking peak dates (July–August) 8+ weeks ahead; shoulder season (May–June, September–October) offers last-minute availability.

Best Time to Charter and Popular Bases

May, June, September, and October offer ideal conditions: warm water (22–24°C), consistent northerly winds, and manageable crowd levels. July–August delivers peak sunshine and warmest seas, alongside crowded beaches and occasional afternoon gusts, but is perfect for party-focused groups. Marina Ibiza (central, vibrant) suits guests wanting Ibiza Town access between beach runs. Santa Eulalia (northeast, quieter) appeals to families and relaxation-seekers. San Antonio (west) specialises in sunset experiences and nightlife access.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a boating license to rent a motorboat?

Spain requires a boating license for vessels over 5.5 metres or 40 HP (RYA Day Skipper or equivalent). Boat4You can arrange hire of a licensed skipper or confirm your credentials during booking.

Can we visit Formentera in a day from Ibiza?

Yes. A motorboat reaches Ses Illetes in 45 minutes. Budget 2–3 hours beach time, then return (1.5 hours). Full-day Formentera exploration is feasible; overnight moorings require prior coordination With us.

What's included in day-charter pricing?

Fuel, insurance, basic provisioning (ice, water), and all safety equipment. Guest provisions (food, alcohol, sun protection) are additional. Many charter companies provide a provisioning pack option (€50–€100).

Can we anchor wherever we wish?

UNESCO-protected posidonia meadows prohibit anchoring in seagrass areas. Boat4You crews know safe anchorages; buoyed moorings exist in popular bays. Respect local regulations and environmental guidelines.

Is a skipper necessary if I'm an experienced boater?

Not mandatory, but recommended for Ibiza's specific conditions (posidonia awareness, strong afternoon gusts, crowded anchorages). Optional skipper hire (€80–€120 daily) provides local knowledge and insurance peace of mind.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
