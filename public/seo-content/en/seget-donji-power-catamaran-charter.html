The Blue Lagoon and Local Swimming Spots

Krknjaši, a small uninhabited islet in the Blue Lagoon, lies roughly 20 nautical miles southwest. The lagoon's luminescent qualities—caused by fine white sand and shallow water filtering sunlight—create an otherworldly swimming experience. Power catamarans reach this destination in 2.5 hours from Seget Donji, leaving ample time for swimming, snorkeling, and beach picnicking before returning to Seget Donji or continuing south to Hvar.

Closer swimming options include Čiovo island, just 6 nautical miles west. Slatine cove offers protected anchorages and a taverna serving fresh fish. Perfect for half-day outings or lazy afternoon anchoring between longer passages.

Comfort and Amenities Aboard Power Catamarans

Power catamarans prioritize comfort. Full-beam cabin designs—width matching the entire hull—mean spacious staterooms with proper headroom and generous berths. Galleys include modern stoves, refrigerators, and ample counter space. Heads (bathrooms) feature proper showers with hot water. Air-conditioning systems keep interiors cool during summer heat.

Helm stations feature modern navigation electronics: GPS chartplotters, autopilots, and VHF radio. Some vessels include entertainment systems—music, television, and streaming capability when near coastal WiFi. These amenities transform multi-day charters into floating accommodations rivaling boutique hotels, with the added benefit of waking to new islands each morning.

Best Seasons and Weather Considerations

Power catamarans operate comfortably year-round, though summer (June–August) and shoulder seasons (April–May, September–October) are most popular. Spring wind patterns favor quick island hops. Summer heat can intensify fuel consumption (air-conditioning increases engine load), but modern vessels handle it efficiently. Autumn combines warm water (21–23°C) with manageable crowds.

The Jugo (southeasterly) occasionally brings larger swells, but power catamarans' dual hulls provide exceptional stability. Most crews find power catamaran trips more comfortable in rough conditions than sailing or smaller motorized vessels.

FAQ: Power Catamaran Charters from Seget Donji

Do I need a skipper's license for a power catamaran?

Most power catamaran charters require an International Certificate of Competence (ICC) or equivalent national boating license. Boat4You's intake process verifies your credentials. If you lack certification, arranges a professional skipper for your charter.

What's the fuel cost for a week-long power catamaran charter?

Fuel costs depend on distance traveled and speed. A typical week covering 150–200 nautical miles (mixing day trips and longer passages) runs approximately €400–600 in diesel fuel.

Can power catamarans anchor safely in typical Adriatic conditions?

Yes. Power catamarans feature dual anchors and excellent holding ground in sandy/muddy bottoms (typical for Adriatic island anchorages). The twin-hull design provides exceptional stability and reduced swing diameter compared to monohulls. Experienced crews report confidence anchoring power catamarans in moderate wind and swell.

Is AC (air-conditioning) standard on power catamarans?

Most modern power catamarans include AC systems, though operating them increases fuel consumption. the charter fleet includes vessels with and without AC. Specify your preference when booking.

What's the maximum distance a power catamaran can cover in one day?

A power catamaran cruising at 12 knots covers roughly 120 nautical miles in a 10-hour day. From Seget Donji, this opens the entire Adriatic coast from Zadar (north) to Korčula (south). Most day-trip itineraries cover 30–60 nautical miles, leaving time for swimming, exploring, and meals ashore.
