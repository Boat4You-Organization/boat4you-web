Brijuni National Park: Your Primary Destination

Brijuni National Park—occupying 14 islands 3–5 nautical miles north-west of Fažana—represents the region's premier motorboat destination. The protected archipelago showcases Byzantine fortress ruins, Venetian villas, and dense pine forests interspersed with rocky coves. Motorboats depart Fažana mid-morning, reaching Brijuni's primary anchorage (Veliki Brijun's sheltered bay) within 45 minutes. Afternoon departure schedules permit maximum island exploration time before sunset return.

National park regulations permit private motorboat exploration: anchoring in designated bays, dinghy access to secondary islands, and snorkelling in restricted zones. The shallow approaches and narrow passages suit motorboats' responsive handling. Many visitors undertake guided island tours (booking through park facilities) or self-explore using provided navigation charts. Fažana's proximity means flexible scheduling: early departure catches calm morning seas; afternoon charters suit crews preferring leisurely mornings.

Alternative Destinations from Fažana

Kamenjak peninsula, southernmost Istria, lies 20 nautical miles (approximately 60 minutes motorboat cruising) from Fažana. This nature reserve offers dramatic limestone scenery, protected anchorages, and pristine beaches. Half-day or full-day charters access Kamenjak's distinctive geography: steep cliffs dropping to turquoise waters, hidden coves sheltering afternoon swimmers, and unusual botanical communities found nowhere else along the Adriatic.

Rovinj, Istria's most picturesque waterfront town, lies 35 nautical miles (90 minutes) north-west. Multi-day motorboat expeditions from Fažana culminate with Rovinj overnight mooring, enabling waterfront dining and medieval town exploration. The passage north delivers panoramic coastal views; arrival at Rovinj's distinctive harbour—terracotta rooftops cascading toward turquoise waters, 13th-century church spire dominating the skyline—rewards the motorboat journey.
