Gulets are traditionally-built wooden sailing and motor yachts, typically 60–120 feet, crewed by professional captains, deckhands, and chefs. Boat4You offers 4 hand-picked gulets from Athens, each beloved for timber elegance, generous deck spaces, gourmet galley cuisine, and the unhurried pace of authentic Greek cruising. Gulets embody slow travel at sea. Your professional crew handles sailing, anchoring, weather routing, and provision shopping; you simply relax on teak decks, swim in crystal bays, dine on fresh-caught fish and Greek mezze, and soak in the Aegean's timeless beauty. This is how Mediterranean sailing should feel: unhurried, luxurious, and entirely yours.

Leisurely Saronic Loops and Overnight Cruises

Gulet charters from Athens typically favour gentle, 3–7 day Saronic Gulf itineraries. A classic weeklong route departs Alimos Marina, cruises southeast to Aegina (4–5 hours), continues to Poros (another 4 hours), then to Hydra (3 hours), and returns to Athens via the western Saronic (2–3 days of leisurely anchoring and day explorations). Each stop allows 1–2 days anchored, swimming, exploring villages, and enjoying dinner ashore or prepared aboard.

Gulet sailing prioritizes comfort and discovery over passage-making speed. Your crew will consult your preferences each morning, suggesting anchorages, nearby hiking, snorkeling, and dinner options. Winds are immaterial—a gulet combines sail and motor, guaranteeing arrivals and departures regardless of meltemi gusts or flat calms.

Aegina, Poros, and Hydra: The Iconic Three

These three Saronic islands are purpose-built for gulet exploration. Aegina, famous for pistachio groves and pebble beaches, anchors in protected harbours where you can walk to tavernas or shop for local almonds and honey. Poros, separated from the mainland by a narrow strait, offers dramatic landscape views, excellent fish restaurants along the waterfront, and evening walks through whitewashed streets. Hydra—car-free, cliffside, steeped in bohemian artist history—is perhaps Greece's most romantic island, with cobblestone alleys, family-run tavernas, and stunning sunsets over the Saronic.

A gulet's shallow draft and powerful aux-engine allow access to lesser-known anchorages between these islands: Methana's volcanic peninsulas, Aegina's southern coves, and Poros' eastern coast all reward unhurried exploration. Your crew knows secret lunch spots, hidden beaches, and family-run tavernas where guidebooks rarely reach.

Gourmet Provisioning and Culinary Experiences

Gulet charters include professional chef services. Your skipper and chef will discuss dietary preferences, allergies, and cuisine styles before departure; most chefs source fresh ingredients daily at island markets, combining Greek staples (feta, olives, local fish, seasonal vegetables) with international touches. Breakfasts feature Greek yoghurt, honey, fresh fruit, and pastries; lunches are light Mediterranean spreads; dinners are multi-course affairs with wine pairings.

The culinary experience reflects Greece's regions: Aegina offers pistachios and almonds; Poros brings exceptional fish and seafood; Hydra's emerging foodie scene influences creative menus. Many gulet chefs are trained in Greek cuisine traditions; they'll teach you to prepare mezze, explain local wine regions, and share stories of family recipes passed through generations. This gastronomic journey is often the most cherished memory of a gulet charter.

Marinas, Check-In, and Crewing

Gulets depart from Alimos Marina, Athens' premier crewed yacht facility. The marina has dedicated crews-changing areas, provisioning support, fuel docking, and full rigging services. Athens International Airport lies 45 minutes north; most clients arrange private transfers or taxis to Alimos. Upon arrival, you'll be welcomed aboard by your captain and crew; they'll brief you on cabin layouts, safety procedures, and the next days' itinerary.

Your crew—typically captain, one deckhand, and one chef—are professionals selected for seamanship, hospitality, and cultural knowledge. Boat4You vets all crews; they speak English, understand international guests' expectations, and are trained in emergency procedures. Most crews are delighted to share sailing knowledge, Greek history, and local recommendations; many become lifelong friendships.

Seasons and Weather for Relaxed Sailing

Gulet season runs April through October. May–June and September–October offer ideal conditions: moderate winds, calm seas, and comfortable water temperatures (20–24°C). July–August are hotter, windier (meltemi), but still manageable for relaxed gulet sailing—your crew's experience and dual sail-motor capability ensure comfort even in challenging conditions.

Spring sailing is perfect for those prioritizing comfort and avoiding summer crowds. Early autumn (September–October) combines warm seas, gentle breezes, and an intimate atmosphere once August crowds disperse. Winter gulet charters are rare from Athens; most crews operate elsewhere or service their vessels.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in a gulet charter price?

Gulet charters typically include boat, crew (captain, deckhand, chef), fuel, insurance, and meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner). You pay separately for alcohol, port fees (minor), and gratuities. Boat4You provides detailed pricing breakdowns during booking; expect €4,000–10,000+ per week depending on boat size, season, and destination.

Can I customize the itinerary with a gulet?

Absolutely. Your captain will discuss your interests—beach swimming, hiking, cultural sites, fine dining, nightlife—and tailor the itinerary daily. Weather and sea state may require flexibility, but your preferences drive the experience. This bespoke planning is a defining gulet charter advantage.

What is the typical crew-to-guest ratio?

Most gulets carry 1 captain and 1 deckhand per 6–8 guests, plus 1 chef. Smaller gulets (70 feet) may have 1 captain + 1 chef for 4–6 guests. This ratio ensures personalized attention without feeling crowded. Boat4You can advise on specific crew sizes during booking.

Is gulet sailing suitable for non-sailors?

Perfect. Gulet charters require zero sailing knowledge; your professional crew handles everything. Guests simply relax, swim, explore islands, and enjoy meals. This is ideal for clients who love the sea but dislike technical responsibility.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
