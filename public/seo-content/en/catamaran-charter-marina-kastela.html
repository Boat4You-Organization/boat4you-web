Marina Kaštela: Your Catamaran Starting Point

Marina Kaštela sits in the heart of the Kaštela riviera, just 3–5 minutes from Split Airport and perfectly positioned between Split (15 min) and Trogir (10 min). If you're chartering a catamaran from Marina Kaštela, you're choosing convenience without compromise. Marina Kaštela Facilities for Catamaran Charters

The marina offers approximately 400 berths with modern utilities, fuel availability, and professional staff experienced in catamaran operations. Freshwater, electrical hookups, and waste management are standard. On-site restaurants and shops serve the charter community. The marina's location in Kaštel Gomilica, within the historic Kaštela settlements, means you're rarely far from provisioning, dining, or local services.

For longer stays, Marina Kaštela provides secure storage, repair facilities, and winter hauling. Catamaran maintenance is straightforward here—charter companies maintain active support networks with local technicians.

Sailing Routes from Marina Kaštela

Your catamaran opens up multiple itineraries. The central Dalmatian circuit hits Brač (Bol's dramatic pebble beach), Hvar Town (wine bars and lavender fields), Vis (unspoiled and wild), and Šolta (quiet anchorages). A week easily covers this golden triangle with leisurely anchorages and gourmet stops.

Longer routes push south to Korčula or north toward the Dalmatian mainland. The calm, predictable Adriatic—especially May through September—makes catamaran sailing safer and more enjoyable than monohulls for most charterers.

Local Provisions and Dining

The Kaštela riviera's seven historic castle settlements (Kaštel Kambelovac, Lukšić, and others) offer local wines, fresh seafood, and traditional peka (slow-roasted meat in clay domes). The region is famous for Kaštelanski crljenak—the Zinfandel ancestor—so wine lovers will find unexpected depth in local reds.

Split's restaurant scene (15 minutes away) rivals any Mediterranean destination. Combine quiet island anchorages with sophisticated shore meals. That range is Marina Kaštela's competitive advantage.

Catamaran charters from Marina Kaštela typically cost 15–25% less than Split center berths for comparable vessels. Summer weeks (July–August) command peak rates; shoulder seasons (May–June, September) offer the best value-to-weather ratio. Multi-week charters negotiate discounts. Best Season for Catamaran Sailing

May through September is prime catamaran season. May offers calm water, fewer tourists, and reliable wind (10–15 knots). July and August are busier but warmest. September gives you summer water temperatures with clearer conditions and lighter crowds. April and October can work if you're flexible; winter catamaran sailing in the Adriatic requires experience and isn't recommended for leisure charters.

FAQ: Catamaran Charter from Marina Kaštela

Do I need sailing experience to charter a catamaran from Marina Kaštela?

No. Many charterers hire professional skippers through Boat4You. A crewed catamaran gives you comfort and local expertise. Bareboat charters require certification (ICC/ASA recommended) and navigation familiarity.

How far is Marina Kaštela from Split Airport?

Marina Kaštela is 3–5 minutes from Split Airport—the closest marina possible. This makes it ideal for short charters and minimizes ground time.

Can I provision food and fuel at Marina Kaštela?

Yes. The marina has fuel docks, provisioning access, and nearby shops. For larger provisioning runs, Split's supermarkets (15 min) offer greater selection.

What islands can I visit from Marina Kaštela on a catamaran?

Brač, Hvar, Vis, Šolta, and Drvenik are all within easy catamaran range. A week-long itinerary easily covers these islands with multiple anchorages and activities.

Is a catamaran more comfortable than a monohull for non-sailors?

Yes. Catamarans have less heel, wider cabins, and steadier platforms. For families and those prone to seasickness, they're the superior choice.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.
