Motorboat Charter at Agia Anna Paraga Anchorage

Boat4You operates a premium motorboat charter base at Agia Anna Paraga, a serene anchorage nestled in the same bay as Platis Gialos but offering a quieter, more intimate departure point. With pontoon facilities and direct access to Mykonos's best day-sailing routes, Agia Anna Paraga is perfect for travelers seeking a more relaxed alternative to bustling Platis Gialos beach clubs while retaining full access to island amenities.

Why Charter from Agia Anna Paraga?

Agia Anna Paraga is a hidden gem for motorboat charters. Located just minutes from Platis Gialos by boat, it offers a quieter atmosphere with the same stunning Aegean views. The anchorage features a modern pontoon facility making boarding and disembarking easy, with our dedicated Boat4You team typically ready to assist. The shallow waters (6–10 meters) and sandy holding make it an ideal mooring spot.

This location is perfect for travelers avoiding crowded beach-club scenes while maintaining easy access to Mykonos's restaurants and amenities. Agia Anna's small village atmosphere provides authentic Greek experience compared to cosmopolitan Platis Gialos, while offering excellent provisioning and dining options nearby.

The pontoon infrastructure with weather protection makes Agia Anna excellent for multi-day charters or those wanting added security and convenience. It's the perfect balance between remote island experience and accessible facilities.

Pontoon Facilities and Port Infrastructure

The pontoon at Agia Anna Paraga provides direct boat access with professional staff on site during operating hours. You'll find freshwater taps, fuel availability, and waste disposal facilities to keep your motorboat ready for extended exploration. The facility is well-maintained and designed specifically for small to medium motorboats.

Unlike Platis Gialos's beach anchorage, Agia Anna's pontoon offers dedicated mooring infrastructure with weather protection. This makes it excellent if you're chartering for multiple days or want added security. Boat4You staff arrange provisioning, equipment rental, and guidance on local attractions directly from the pontoon.

Day Routes and Island Exploration

From Agia Anna Paraga, you have access to the same day-sailing routes as Platis Gialos, with the added benefit of a quieter departure point. Head south to explore Mykonos's dramatic coastline, or venture to nearby Delos (2 nautical miles away) to visit one of Greece's most significant archaeological sites. The journey takes about 30 minutes each way.

Venture eastward to the uninhabited islands of Rheneia and Tinos for exceptional snorkeling and secluded anchorages. The trip takes about 30 minutes by motorboat. The waters here are pristine and uncrowded, perfect for a peaceful day on the water. Sunset cruises from Agia Anna Paraga offer stunning photo opportunities with the sun descending over the Aegean.

Local Food and Provisioning Services

Agia Anna village has several tavernas and small markets within walking distance of the pontoon. Fresh seafood, local cheeses, bread, and Greek wines are readily available. Many restaurants offer waterfront seating where you can enjoy your meal while watching motorboats pass by.

Provisioning your motorboat is straightforward: local fishmongers supply fresh catch daily, and the village bakery provides warm bread each morning. Stock up on local Mykonos specialties such as kopanisti cheese and sun-dried tomatoes. Boat4You can arrange pre-provisioning so your motorboat is stocked with refreshments before departure.

Seasons, Weather Patterns, and Best Times

Agia Anna Paraga enjoys the same excellent sailing season as Mykonos—April through October, with peak conditions June through September. The anchorage provides good protection from most wind directions, making it suitable for motorboating even on days when nearby anchorages experience rougher conditions.

Summer water temperatures reach 24–26°C, warm enough for comfortable swimming. The Meltemia winds (northerly summer winds) blow predictably, making conditions ideal for motorboat exploration. Wind speeds typically range from 10–15 knots in summer. Winter is quieter with fewer charters and lower rates, but weather becomes less predictable.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Agia Anna Paraga compare to Platis Gialos?

Both are excellent, but Agia Anna offers a quieter atmosphere and dedicated pontoon facilities. Platis Gialos is busier with upscale beach clubs. Choose Agia Anna for peaceful experience, or Platis Gialos for vibrant beach culture.

Can we go to Delos from Agia Anna Paraga?

Yes, Delos is about 2 nautical miles away and easily reached by motorboat in roughly 30 minutes. We can arrange guided archaeological tours or self-guided visits. Note that Delos has no facilities, so bring water and sun protection.

Do you offer sunset cruises from Agia Anna?

Yes! Sunset charters from Agia Anna Paraga are spectacular. The sun sets over the Aegean with minimal light pollution, creating perfect conditions for photography. Book in advance, as these charters fill quickly during high season.

Are children welcome aboard?

Absolutely. All motorboats are family-friendly with safety equipment for children. Shallow anchorages like Agia Anna are ideal for families who want to swim and snorkel in calm, clear waters. Life jackets are available in all sizes.

What is the typical duration of a day charter?

Most day charters run 4-6 hours, departing morning or afternoon. Half-day charters and overnight charters are also available. customizes duration to suit your schedule and interests.

Accommodation and Travel Planning

Visitors typically stay in Mykonos Town (Chora), about 10 minutes away by bus or taxi. The village has hundreds of hotels and guesthouses catering to all budgets. Some luxury resorts overlook Platis Gialos; budget options offer excellent value in less touristy areas.

Consider staying 3–4 nights to fully experience the area. Dedicate two days to motorboat charters, leaving time for exploring Mykonos Town's nightlife, museums, and restaurants. Book accommodation and charters in advance during summer, as both fill quickly.
