Understanding Meltemi Conditions for Serious Sailors

The meltemi wind, dominant from May through September, is a thermal phenomenon that shapes all sailing in the region. Heated air over inland regions rises, drawing cooler air from the Aegean Sea northward. This circulation creates consistent winds typically blowing from the north, with average velocities of 12-18 knots during peak season.

Experienced sailors appreciate meltemi's predictability. Unlike Atlantic storm systems that develop unpredictably, meltemi follows reliable patterns. Morning sailing often finds light winds; by mid-afternoon, the breeze strengthens. Early evening typically brings moderation. This cycle allows experienced skippers to schedule challenging passages during optimal conditions and plan more relaxed sailing during lighter wind periods.

Meltemi typically strengthens through May and June, peaks during July-August, and gradually declines through September. September is often preferred by experienced sailors: winds remain strong enough for excellent sailing but less crowded anchorages mean greater freedom of movement and more choice in overnight locations.
