Marina Frapa: Central Location and Facilities

The marina's 462 wet berths and 150 dry berths accommodate catamarans up to 35 metres with dedicated pontoons. A 50-ton travel lift handles maintenance and repairs. Full-service fuel dock, water systems, and shore-powered berths operate 24/7. The on-site hotel, pool, sauna, and fitness facilities support crew relaxation while vessels are provisioned or serviced.

Rogoznica itself sits roughly 25 minutes from Šibenik and 30 minutes from Trogir—both UNESCO-listed historic towns rich in cultural attractions. Split Airport (35 kilometres, 35 minutes by road) provides convenient crew arrivals. Zadar Airport (1 hour northeast) offers an alternative for northern-crew deployments. Most charterers arrange ground transfers through Marina Frapa's concierge, eliminating logistics stress.

Newer-Build Catamaran Specifications

Typical modern catamarans from Marina Frapa (40–50 feet) feature three or four cabins with en-suite bathrooms, air-conditioning, hot-water systems, and modern galley kitchens with refrigeration and cooking appliances. Saloon spaces include dining areas, entertainment systems, and comfortable seating. Flybridge decks span the vessel's full width with sun loungers, dining tables, and 360-degree views.

Navigation equipment is state-of-the-art: dual chartplotters, GPS, radar, and autopilot systems. Dual engines provide redundancy and reliable cruising (10–12 knots). Advanced stabiliser systems minimise motion at anchor. Solar panels and sizeable water tanks enable extended anchoring away from civilisation. Battery capacity supports electric winches, heating, and electronic systems. Many newer models feature outdoor saloon configurations—partially covered outdoor seating areas combining fresh-air comfort with weather protection.

Ideal Routes for Family Catamarans from Marina Frapa

Kornati National Park Expedition (3–5 days): Depart Marina Frapa, cruise north to the Kornati's limestone islands (1 hour). Anchor at designated park berths, explore secluded channels by tender, snorkel in crystalline water, and hike abandoned stone settlements. The shallow draft enables approach to protected bays inaccessible to larger vessels. Provisioning stops at Zadar or Šibenik ensure fresh supplies. Perfect for nature-loving families.

Wine Island Loop (7 days): Motor south from Marina Frapa through Šolta's sheltered bays (Maslinica anchorage, renowned for clarity). Continue southwest to Hvar Town, exploring the Pakleni islands and exploring lavender-focused shore excursions. Sail further west to the remote island of Vis—dramatic coastal passages, Blue Grotto snorkelling, and excellent wine bars. Return via Brač's Bol and Zlatni Rat beach to Rogoznica. 140 nautical miles; ideal for culture-immersive crews.

Coastal Exploration (4–5 days): Remain within 30 nautical miles of Marina Frapa. Visit Trogir's UNESCO old town, explore Čiovo island's remote coves, anchor at Šolta's quiet bays, and venture to Primošten wine region. Daily distances are brief (2–4 hours cruising), maximising swimming and snorkelling time. Ideal for families with young children or groups prioritising relaxation over ambitious sailing.

Seasonality and Wind Patterns

May through October offers optimal catamaran cruising. June and September deliver steady Maestral winds (10–15 knots), cooler evenings, and manageable crowds—ideal for families. July and August are warmest and most crowded; early-morning departures before anchorages fill enable peaceful exploration despite peak season.

Spring (April–May) brings mild weather and dynamic sailing conditions. Autumn (September–October) delivers warm water (22–23°C), gentle winds, and vibrant local culture. Winter sailing is possible but demands caution during Bura events (northeast, powerful, occasional). Most commercial charters suspend December–February; March and November offer shoulder-season deals with fair weather.

Provisioning and Galley Logistics

Modern catamarans feature full-service galleys: electric refrigerators, stoves, ovens, and dishwashers. Provisioning is straightforward—Marina Frapa's chandlery stocks fresh groceries, wines, spirits, and specialty items. If you arrange a chef, provisioning becomes transparent: provide dietary preferences and cuisine preferences, and the chef sources accordingly. Self-catering crews appreciate the flexibility to provision daily at local markets (Rogoznica's waterfront fish market, Hvar's colourful open market, Brač's village shops).

Water is unlimited at Marina Frapa dock and ample (300–400 litres) aboard most catamarans. Fuel consumption is predictable: a 45-foot catamaran burns 30–40 litres per 8-hour day of mixed motor-sailing, enabling week-long charters on 200–250 litres (approximately €250–€350 at current Adriatic diesel prices).
