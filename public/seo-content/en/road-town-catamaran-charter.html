Essential Island Routes from Road Town

Virgin Gorda's The Baths is the BVI's most iconic snorkelling destination. Depart Road Town, reach The Baths under sail in 3–4 hours, anchor off the beach, and swim through submerged granite formations framing pools of crystalline turquoise. The snorkelling extends north into Valley anchorage and south toward Copper Mine Point, each offering coral gardens and tropical fish populations.

Cooper Island lies southeast, a 90-minute reach from Road Town. Manchioneel Bay provides protected overnight anchorage, excellent snorkelling, and hiking trails. Nearby Carrot Rock and Cistern Rock are renowned dive sites. From Cooper Island, continue southeast to Norman Island, inspiration for Robert Louis Stevenson's "Treasure Island." The island's caves and the moored Willy T bar barge provide entertainment, swimming, and provisioning.

Jost Van Dyke, northwest of Road Town, hosts legendary Foxy's beach bar. Accessible by 2-hour sail, the anchorage at Great Harbour provides lively social scenes, excellent dining, and authentic Caribbean culture. White Bay on the island's north side offers pristine sandy beaches and the aforementioned Soggy Dollar Bar, famed for painkiller cocktails and swimming access from the water.

Anegada, the BVI's northernmost island, presents navigation challenges (shallow barrier reef, complex passes) but rewards with remote anchorages, fresh lobster dinners, and authentic island character. Experienced sailors view Anegada passages as professional navigation challenges—reading the water, identifying safe channels, and timing tidal flows.

Seasonal Conditions and Best Times to Sail

The BVI peak sailing season runs November through May. December–February offers the strongest trade winds (15–22 knots), most stable weather systems, and ideal sailing conditions for performance-focused sailors. March–May brings slightly lighter winds, calmer seas, and fewer crowds. Easter holidays (March–April) attract many families; book early if planning a spring holiday.

Autumn sailing (September–November) provides variable winds but stunning seascapes, fewer charter crowds, and reduced rates. October–November presents occasional tropical showers but excellent sailing conditions between systems. Summer (June–August) features lighter trade winds, higher humidity, and occasional tropical waves, but dedicated sailors appreciate the emptier anchorages and lower prices.

Hurricane season is June–November, but the BVI lies south of typical hurricane tracks. Tropical systems are rare; most weather systems pass north or south of the islands. maintains updated hurricane protocols, alternative routes, and evacuation procedures if severe weather approaches. In practice, hurricane impacts to the BVI are infrequent and minimal.
