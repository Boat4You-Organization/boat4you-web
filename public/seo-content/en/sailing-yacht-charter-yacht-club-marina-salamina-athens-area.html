The Saronic Gulf: Your Sailing Playground

From Salamina, you're positioned at the heart of the Saronic Gulf, one of Greece's most accessible and beautiful sailing grounds. Protected waters, moderate winds, and island hopping distances of 8–20 nautical miles make the Saronic ideal for varied itineraries. Highlights include Aegina (Temple of Aphaia), Poros (pine-forested narrowly separated from the Peloponnese), and Hydra (car-free island with stone mansions and artist culture).

The Saronic's typically calm summer winds (10–16 knots, mostly northerly) suit sailors of all skill levels. Water temperatures reach 26°C by August, making swimming in crystalline coves a daily ritual.

The Perama–Salamina ferry runs every 20–30 minutes from early morning to late evening. Journey time is 15 minutes; passengers and cars are welcomed. Athens International Airport (ATH) is approximately 45 minutes from Perama by car or shuttle. Most Boat4You guests arrange airport transfers directly; we can assist with recommendations.

Sailing Seasons and Weather

The Saronic enjoys mild winters (air: 12–15°C, water: 14–16°C) and warm summers (air: 28–32°C, water: 24–26°C). The prime sailing season runs May through September. July and August can see occasional thermal afternoon breezes (Etesian winds from the north), which energize sailing but require aware seamanship. Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer perfect temperatures and consistent winds—ideal for longer cruises.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a sailing license or ICC to charter?

For bareboat charters, yes: an ICC (International Certificate of Competence) or equivalent national license is required. Boat4You arranges professional skippers for those without certification at no extra hassle.

What's the best time to visit Yacht Club Marina Salamina?

May–June and September–October offer ideal conditions: warm temperatures, moderate winds, and fewer crowds than peak summer. July–August is busy but excellent for families. Winter (November–March) is calm but cooler.

How far is Salamina from Athens city center?

Perama (ferry point) is about 30 km northwest of central Athens. The ferry itself is 15 minutes; total travel from the airport is roughly 45–60 minutes depending on traffic.

Can I arrange a professional skipper for my bareboat?

Absolutely. coordinates professional, English-speaking skippers experienced in Saronic navigation and charter operations.

Are there nearby hotels and restaurants?

Salamina has modest hotels and numerous tavernas on the waterfront. For more variety, the nearby Perama and Agia Paraskevi areas (on the mainland) offer additional lodging. Many guests stay aboard their charter vessel, which the yacht club marina's calm waters make very comfortable.
