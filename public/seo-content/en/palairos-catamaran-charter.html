Understanding Catamaran Performance & Comfort

Modern catamarans typically cruise at 8–10 knots under motor, reaching 6–8 knots sailing in moderate winds. Their dual hulls eliminate rolling common in monohulls, reducing seasickness and improving meal enjoyment. Interior spaces feel voluminous compared to same-length monohulls, with separate cabins allowing privacy even for large groups. Multiple heads mean minimal bathroom conflicts during morning routines.

Disadvantages include slightly reduced sailing responsiveness and greater expense compared to equivalently-sized monohulls. However, for family charters and guests prioritizing stability and comfort, these tradeoffs prove acceptable. matches your priorities—performance sailing or relaxation focus—with appropriate vessel selections.

What's the difference between a catamaran and a sailboat?

Catamarans feature two hulls providing greater stability and interior volume; sailboats (monohulls) use a single hull with keel and offer enhanced sailing performance. Catamarans excel for families seeking comfort and shallow-water anchorages; monohull sailors prioritize traditional sailing feel and cost savings. Both traverse the Ionian beautifully—the choice depends on your priorities.
