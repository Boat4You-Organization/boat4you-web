Top Sailing Routes from Marina Delta Kallithea

The Saronic Gulf offers a tapestry of islands and sailing conditions. From Marina Delta Kallithea, you can explore Hydra with its picturesque harbor, Spetses renowned for its maritime tradition, and Aegina famous for its pistachio farms and stunning beaches. Day sails to the nearby Islets of Fleves provide sheltered anchorages and crystal-clear waters perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

For longer passages, consider the classic week-long circumnavigation: departing toward Hydra, then onward to Spetses and back via Aegina, with opportunities to anchor in small coves away from crowds. The summer Etesian winds (northerly, typically Force 3–4) make these waters ideal for recreational sailing between June and September.

Best Seasons for Sailing from Marina Delta Kallithea

Late April through October offers the most reliable sailing conditions. May and September provide moderate winds and excellent visibility, with water temperatures climbing to a pleasant 23–26 degrees Celsius by July–August. The peak summer season (July–August) brings stronger Etesian winds and crowded anchorages, while shoulder months offer a better balance of favorable weather and tranquility.

Provisioning and Local Flavors

The Athens Riviera location means access to excellent provisioning. Local supermarkets (including a Carrefour just minutes from the marina) stock everything from fresh produce to Greek wines and olive oils. Waterfront restaurants in nearby Alimos and Glyfada offer traditional tavernas where you can enjoy fresh fish, mezes, and local specialties. Don't miss the famous saganaki (fried cheese), locally caught octopus, and Greece's distinctive Retsina wine if you wish to explore local maritime culture.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
