Why Catamarans for the Maddalena and Lavezzi

The Maddalena Archipelago and nearby Lavezzi Islands feature numerous shallow-draft anchorages—pristine coves where sand meets granite, often under 3 metres deep. Catamarans draw just 1.2–1.5 metres, allowing you to anchor where traditional monohulls (drawing 1.8–2.2m) risk grounding. Spargi's eastern shore, Budelli's famed pink beach, and the secret coves of Razzoli become genuinely accessible. Stability is equally crucial: multihull platforms reduce heel, making movement below decks and on deck safer for children, elderly crew, or anyone with mobility concerns.

Crossing the Strait of Bonifacio Safely

The 11-kilometre crossing between Corsica and Sardinia is straightforward in fair weather but demands respect. Currents can reach 3 knots, and seasonal winds (Mistral, Libeccio) accelerate through the narrow passage. Catamarans' lower wetted surface and reduced motion make these crossings far more comfortable than monohulls. In beam swell, a cat's shallow heel keeps children calm and crew confident. Round-trip time is 3–4 hours; proper timing with tide and weather forecasting ensures a smooth passage.

Typical Catamaran Itineraries

A 7-day catamaran charter typically departs Ajaccio, crosses to the Maddalena (Day 2–3 exploring anchorages), visits Spargi and Budelli (Day 4–5), and explores smaller Lavezzi islands before returning via Bonifacio town. A 10-day version extends to Sardinia's northeastern granite coastline (Cala Spinosa, Costa Smeralda perimeter) or south to the Emerald Coast. Catamarans' fuel efficiency and spacious cabins make these multi-day passages genuinely pleasant, not endurance tests.

Technical Notes for Catamaran Skippers

Catamarans operating in the Maddalena and Lavezzi islands maintain extreme shallow-draft advantages: 1.2-1.5 metre hulls penetrate anchorages unreachable by deeper vessels. However, navigators must respect rising underwater topography and karst limestone features creating irregular seabeds. Some anchorages shelve gradually; others drop dramatically. Electronic charts combined with visual survey techniques ensure safe anchoring. crews carry detailed pilot guides documenting holding characteristics, swing radiuses, and emergency shelter options for each region.

Engine efficiency becomes critical during uncertain wind periods; catamarans typically motor at 7-8 knots, consuming roughly 15-25 liters/hour depending on hull design and sea state. Multi-day passages benefit from fuel-efficient cruising speeds. Generator systems charge batteries for evening comfort; solar panels supplement power during extended anchorages. catamarans feature modern diesel engines with comprehensive spares availability at main ports. Professional crews understand fuel consumption patterns and plan accordingly, ensuring adequate reserves for contingency passages.

Anchoring technique demands respect for holding characteristics: sand and mud provide excellent anchoring; rocky bottoms require vigilance. Catamaran windage necessitates adequate scope—typically 7:1 ratio in moderate conditions, increasing to 10:1 in strong winds. Swing areas are generous; adequate room separation from other vessels prevents nighttime collisions. skippers carry multiple anchor types (Danforth, CQR, Rocna) to address diverse seabed conditions, with practiced retrieval procedures ensuring reliable anchor handling throughout your charter duration.

How do catamaran fuel tanks compare to monohull equivalents?

Most catamarans feature dual fuel tanks (typically 300-600 liters total) positioned in each hull for weight distribution and balance. FAQ

Are catamarans slower than monohulls in Corsica?

Not meaningfully. Both achieve 6–8 knots in typical seasonal winds. Catamarans tack more gently and accelerate faster in light winds; monohulls have a slight edge in strong breezes. For cruising (not racing), speed differences vanish when factoring relaxation and exploration time.

How many cabins do catamarans offer?

Most 38–45-foot catamarans feature 3–4 double cabins, with some offering convertible saloon berths. Cabin ventilation is excellent in Mediterranean climates.

Is catamaran anchoring stable in swell?

Catamarans' wide beam and shallow draft provide exceptional holding and minimal swing. Even in moderate swell, motion is far less pronounced than monohulls. Anchorages in the Maddalena and Lavezzi are largely swell-protected, offering glassy conditions.

What's included in catamaran provisioning?

charters typically include basics (spices, oils, pasta, rice, coffee, tea, some canned goods). Fresh provisions—fish, vegetables, bread, meat—are sourced locally in port stops. We provide shopping lists and can pre-arrange provisioning in main towns if desired.

Can we charter a catamaran bareboat if inexperienced?

If you hold an RYA Day Skipper or equivalent certificate, bareboat is possible. Otherwise, arranges professional skippers (typically €150–250/day). Many clients opt for a skipper for their first charter in unfamiliar waters, then bareboat subsequent visits.
