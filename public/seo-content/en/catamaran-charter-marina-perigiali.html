Timing Your Perigiali Charter

May through September provides reliable sailing conditions for catamaran charters from Perigiali. May and June offer warm seas (21-23°C), light-to-moderate Maistro breezes, and fewer boats throughout the Ionian—perfect for first-time charterers or crews seeking less congestion. July and August deliver peak sunshine, warmest water (25-26°C), and the most stable forecasts, though booking well in advance is essential for popular summer dates. September remains excellent—warm water, reliable winds, and noticeably fewer charter crews than peak summer months.

Each season offers distinct advantages: early-season cruising features spring flowers and warmer air temperatures with calm mornings ideal for swimming and snorkeling. Peak summer delivers maximum sunshine for deck entertainment and water activities. Autumn sailing provides legendary clarity and stable conditions favored by photographers and nature enthusiasts exploring the Ionian's marine ecosystems and island villages.

The south-coast position means Perigiali experiences slightly more direct sun exposure and occasionally warmer air temperatures than Nikiana, particularly in shoulder seasons. This minor advantage can extend your season both directions, making May and September particularly pleasant for catamaran sailing with comfortable weather conditions for deck time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I book?

For peak season (July and August), reservations 4–6 months ahead secure the best fleet selection and pricing. Shoulder months (May, June, September) typically allow booking 6–8 weeks out.

What does the charter price include?

Base pricing usually covers the vessel, insurance, and marina fees at the home base. Fuel, provisioning, skipper service, end-cleaning, and tourist tax are generally added separately. Check the specific listing on Boat4You for a full breakdown.

Do I need a sailing licence?

For bareboat charter, a valid skipper licence plus a VHF certificate are typically required, and rules vary by country. If you don't hold one, hiring a professional skipper is straightforward — pricing runs roughly 150–220 euro per day plus meals.
