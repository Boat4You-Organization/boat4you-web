Nea Peramos Marina: Facility Overview

Nea Peramos Marina is a smaller but meticulously maintained facility featuring 60–80 protected berths. The harbour is sheltered by natural headlands, providing excellent protection in summer conditions. Modern infrastructure includes dedicated fuel berths, fresh water points, 24-hour power supply to each berth, waste management (grey water pumping), and recycling systems.

Reception services operate daytime; out-of-hours assistance is available via radio. The marina's smaller scale means personalised service: staff know every regular charterer and respond quickly to berthing requests or emergency maintenance needs.

Why Nea Peramos for Catamaran Charter?

Catamarans excel in Nea Peramos' setting. The shallow-draft multihull design allows you to access Megara Bay's smaller anchorages and tight island approaches that deeper monohulls cannot reach. The marina's protected water makes embarkation and disembarkation effortless—no wave surge, straightforward tie-up, and minimal fender wear. Our 19-vessel catamaran fleet ranges 38–48 feet, sleeping 6–10 guests.

Nea Peramos' quieter setting, compared to busier Lavrion or Alimos, is ideal for families and groups seeking relaxation combined with direct sailing access. The marina sits in a real village, not a commercial port, so pre-charter meals and exploration feel authentic.

Catamaran Specifications & Comfort Standards

Our Nea Peramos catamarans are predominantly Lagoon and Fountaine Pajot models, built for comfort, stability, and efficiency. All feature modern navigation systems (chartplotter, autopilot, GPS), air-conditioned cabins with private ensuite facilities, full galley systems with cookers and refrigeration, hot water showers, and generous saloon space.

Stabilizer technology, shallow draft (under 1.5 metres for most vessels), and dual engines provide confidence and control. Tender dinghies, snorkelling kits, fishing rods, and entertainment systems are standard issue.

Berth Allocation & Marina Access

Catamarans at Nea Peramos are allocated dedicated berths in the inner harbour, protected from swells and swell-driven movement. Berth depths typically 2.5–3.5 metres accommodate all our vessels comfortably. Power pedestals are positioned for easy connection; fuel berths are separately designated to streamline turnaround. Most crews refuel on departure morning and proceed directly to sea, saving half a day versus dockside refuelling delays.

Routes: Megara Bay, Saronic Gulf, Ionian Gateway

Shallow-draft catamarans from Nea Peramos access Megara Bay's protected inner reaches—perfect for shorter voyages (1–3 days) or families seeking minimal sea time. Eastward routes reach the Saronic Gulf proper: Aegina, Poros, Hydra, and Spetses are all within comfortable day-sail distances. Westward, the Corinth Canal entrance sits 3–4 hours away, opening Ionian passage for week-long adventures.

The catamaran's flat saloon provides panoramic views during transit; the spacious foredeck is ideal for families or larger groups to gather safely.

Seasons & Weather at Nea Peramos Marina

Summer (June–August) brings steady northerly Etesian winds (10–16 knots average), warm air temperatures (26–29°C), and calm afternoon conditions perfect for families. May and September–early October offer gentler conditions, fewer boats, and pleasant temperatures (18–22°C). The protected marina anchorage ensures safe overnight mooring even during windier periods.

Winter sailing is possible but less popular due to colder water (14–16°C), occasional strong southerlies, and reduced daylight. Most catamaran charters operate May through October.

Village Provisioning & Local Dining

Nea Peramos village retains authentic character. Thursday/Friday open markets stock produce, local cheese, bread, and wines at fair prices.

The village is small enough that crew members can explore, dine, and feel genuinely welcomed without touristy pressure. This authenticity is part of the Nea Peramos charter experience.
