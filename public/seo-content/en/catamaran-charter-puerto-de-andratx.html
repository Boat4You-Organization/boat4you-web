Puerto de Andratx Marina: Facilities and Infrastructure

Puerto de Andratx accommodates superyachts and modest cruising catamarans alike, with 180+ berths ranging from 7 to 50 metres. The marina features modern shore-side pedestal connections (220V/380V power, high-speed water), waste pump-out systems, and 24-hour security with CCTV monitoring. Fuel delivery is available daily from professional bunkering services, and the reception operates year-round with multilingual staff trained in charter vessel protocols.

Dedicated catamaran berths cluster near the main entrance, minimizing manoeuvring distance and offering convenient access to provisioning areas. The marina's fuel quality is consistently monitored, and diesel/petrol prices align with European standards. Winter storage facilities are available for vessels requiring off-season maintenance.

Why Choose Puerto de Andratx for Your Charter Base

Competing with Spanish mainland marinas like Barcelona or Valencia, Puerto de Andratx uniquely combines world-class facilities with dramatic Mediterranean scenery. The marina's sheltered position within the Andratx inlet provides protection from Atlantic swells, whilst the nearby Tramuntana mountain range channels consistent winds ideal for sailing. Unlike crowded summer ports, Andratx maintains exclusivity: it's popular among discerning charterers and yacht owners seeking sophisticated alternatives to Palma Bay's tourist masses.

The location eliminates lengthy motor-sailing requirements. Within 30 minutes of casting off, you're anchored in remote coves or sheltered by Sa Dragonera island. This efficiency appeals to time-limited charterers and those prioritizing sailing over transit time.

Departure Routes and Sailing Itineraries

From Puerto de Andratx, a classic week-long itinerary heads north to Sa Dragonera (4nm), then to Estellencs (8nm), Valldemossa (12nm), and the dramatic Port de Sóller (18nm). This route showcases the Tramuntana's highest peaks and most inaccessible villages. Alternatively, sail south to Calvia, Portals Vells, and Cabrera National Park's pristine beaches, totalling 35 nautical miles of exploration within a single week.

Weekend charterers favour the "Andratx Triangle": Sa Dragonera, Estellencs, and Andratx itself, easily completed in two days with leisurely anchorages. International charters often combine Andratx as a base with passages to Ibiza (40nm), Formentera (50nm), or even mainland Spain's Costa Brava, extending the Balearic experience beyond single islands.

Weather Patterns and Sailing Seasons from Puerto de Andratx

Summer (June-August) brings the northeasterly Tramuntana, averaging 12-20 knots, creating perfect sailing conditions and glassy anchorages in southern bays. Afternoon thunderstorms are rare but possible; they originate over the mountains and dissipate quickly. Spring (April-May) features lighter, more easterly breezes, ideal for families learning catamaran handling. Autumn (September-October) rivals summer: warm water (23-24°C), sunshine, and manageable crowds.

Winter sailing (November-March) is possible but demands caution. December-February bring the Tramuntana at gale force (30-40 knots), making February-March shorter cruises around protected southern bays more prudent than open-water passages.

Andratx Village: Dining, Provisioning, and Culture

Port-side restaurants range from casual fish taverns to Michelin-starred establishments. Es Portitxol, the village's heart, buzzes with yacht crew and islanders alike, serving fresh grouper almojábanas (battered and fried) at waterfront tables. Upscale options like Yachts Club Andratx offer fine dining overlooking your catamaran. Weekly markets (Wednesdays, Sundays) supply fresh produce, cheese, jamón, and Mallorcan wines at reasonable prices.

The village maintains an artisanal spirit despite luxury development: small fishing boats continue daily operations, local co-operatives control fish sales, and centuries-old stone architecture frames modern amenities. Evening paseos (walks) through the village square offer authentic island culture before you motor back to your catamaran under stars.

FAQ: Puerto de Andratx Catamaran Charters

How far in advance should I book a catamaran at Puerto de Andratx?

Summer charters (July-August) require 3-6 months advance booking; winter and shoulder seasons allow 4-8 weeks flexibility. Early bookings (January-February for summer) secure preferred vessel sizes and departure dates.

What's included in the catamaran rental price?

Standard inclusions: mooring, insurance, navigational equipment, linens, and basic life safety gear. Optional: skipper hire (€180/day), provisioning service (€500-€1,000 depending on preferences), and water sports equipment rental.

Is there a minimum sailing certification required?

Yes. At least one crew member must hold an internationally recognized certificate (IYC, ICC, RYA Day Skipper, or national equivalent). Beginners are welcome with a hired skipper providing instruction throughout the week.

Can I overnight at Puerto de Andratx marina or must I sail immediately?

You can spend your first and last nights moored at the marina. Many charterers do, using the time to provision, meet their hired skipper, and become familiar with the catamaran before setting sail.

What happens in bad weather or if the catamaran needs repairs?

Boat4You's insurance covers weather delays and mechanical issues. If unsailable conditions develop, charters are rescheduled or refunded. The marina's repair facilities ensure any technical problems are resolved within hours.
