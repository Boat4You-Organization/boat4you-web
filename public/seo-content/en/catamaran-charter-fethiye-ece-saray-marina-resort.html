Signature Catamaran Itineraries from Ece Saray

Departing Ece Saray Marina Resort, catamarans access the entire Lycian coast and Gulf of Gökova. A typical itinerary begins with an afternoon departure, reaching Ölüdeniz Blue Lagoon (15 kilometres south) by evening. The lagoon's protected anchorage provides excellent swimming, paddleboarding, and spectacular sunset viewing. Many guests dine aboard their catamaran, enjoying customised menus prepared by professional chefs using ingredients sourced that morning from Fethiye markets.

Day two typically reaches Butterfly Valley, where dramatic cliffs plunge into turquoise water. Tender excursions explore the narrow riverside gorge leading to natural pools and freshwater cascades. The endemic Jersey Tiger butterflies gather in summer—their striking orange and black patterns creating unforgettable natural photography. Your catamaran's superior stability makes tender operations safer than monohull vessels, particularly important for guests with limited mobility or elderly passengers.

Days three through six explore the Lycian coast's greatest treasures. Tlos and Xanthos ancient cities feature remarkable rock tombs, Greek and Roman structures, and landscape archaeology spanning millennia. Gemiler Island (Saint Nicholas Island) hosts Byzantine monastic ruins and exceptional snorkelling in crystalline waters. Kayaköy ghost village—the abandoned Greek settlement—offers poignant history and outstanding photography. Your catamaran's spacious layout allows full groups to remain aboard without crowding, and the stable platform makes photography from the deck far easier than aboard monohull vessels.

Culinary Excellence and Turkish Traditions

Your catamaran's chef prepares all meals aboard using fresh local ingredients. Turkish breakfasts—fresh bread from daily bakery runs, local cheese, honey from apiaries in nearby valleys, yoghurt, cucumbers, tomatoes, and strong Turkish coffee—set the tone for each day. Mezes—roasted peppers, stuffed grape leaves (yaprak dolma), yoghurt-based dips, cured anchovies, fresh feta cheese—appear throughout the day. Raki, the traditional anise spirit, accompanies evening mezes, often diluted with water and ice creating the traditional "lion's milk."

Dinner menus frequently feature fresh sea bass, sea bream, calamari, and octopus prepared simply—grilled with lemon and olive oil, pan-fried with garlic, or prepared in traditional Turkish styles using tomato, onion, and spices. Boat4You pre-coordinates all dietary requirements: vegetarian, vegan, kosher, halal, or allergy-conscious menus are executed with equal expertise and care. Wines complement each dinner, sourced from Turkish producers in nearby regions or international selections matching your preferences.

Season Planning and Optimal Conditions

The catamaran sailing season from Ece Saray runs June through September, with May and October offering shoulder-season alternatives. Summer months guarantee warm water (24–28 degrees Celsius), stable meltemi winds, and excellent visibility. Daytime temperatures reach 28–32 degrees Celsius; evening temperatures rarely drop below 18 degrees. The meltemi wind cools days while providing ideal sailing conditions. Spring and autumn offer fewer crowds, excellent weather, and slightly gentler conditions—many experienced sailors prefer these periods.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a catamaran more stable than a monohull yacht?

Yes. Twin hulls create exceptional stability, reducing motion dramatically—particularly beneficial for guests prone to seasickness or those seeking ultimate comfort. Catamarans also offer wider deck areas and more interior space than comparably-sized monohulls.

Are catamarans as fast as motor yachts?

Modern catamarans cruise at 8–12 knots under sail or power, slightly slower than dedicated motor yachts (12–15 knots). However, their stability, spaciousness, and comfort often outweigh modest speed differences. helps you select the vessel type matching your priorities.

Can we access Ece Saray's spa and restaurants?

Yes. On embarkation and disembarkation days, and during most midpoint returns to the marina, guests enjoy complimentary access to resort facilities. Your crew can arrange spa appointments, restaurant reservations, and any special services. Many guests enjoy pre-charter pampering or post-charter relaxation.

What happens during rough weather?

Modern catamarans possess excellent stability and powerful engines allowing safe navigation in conditions challenging monohull vessels. Your crew monitors weather continuously; if conditions deteriorate, you divert to nearby sheltered harbours. Alternative dates without charge apply if cancels due to extraordinary circumstances.

Can we customise our itinerary?

Absolutely. Discover an enchanting cove? Extend your stay. Prefer additional swimming time? Reduce destination visits. Your charter, your preferences.
