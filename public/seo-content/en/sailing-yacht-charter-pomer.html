ACI Marina Pomer: The Facility

ACI Marina Pomer occupies a protected inlet approximately 30 kilometers south of Pula, the largest city in Istria. The marina's 294 berths are distributed across several pontoons, each accessible via secure gangways. Every berth includes shore-power connections (220V, standard European), water supply, and waste-disposal access. The facility operates year-round with full-time professional staff—reception, maintenance crew, fuel service, and security personnel who ensure safe, efficient operations 24/7.

The reception building houses modern facilities: comfortable waiting areas, Wi-Fi connectivity, shower blocks, laundry services, and restrooms. A chandlery stocks essential sailing supplies, charts, and provisions. An onsite café serves coffee, pastries, and light meals, creating a natural gathering point where sailors plan routes, exchange advice, and build community. Parking for crew vehicles is available, though many charterers rely on airport transfers or public transport for access.

The Sheltered Basin: Design & Benefits

The inlet surrounding ACI Marina Pomer functions as a natural protected basin. Stone hills encircling the water filter wind, reduce swell, and create calm conditions even when the open Adriatic beyond experiences variable winds. This geography makes Pomer ideal for sailing schools—instructors can focus on teaching fundamentals in forgiving conditions rather than managing weather complications. For families, the calm anchorages enable young sailors to develop water confidence without anxiety about excessive boat motion or demanding conditions.

The basin also enables safe overnight mooring with minimal windage or swell impact. Crews departing at dawn encounter sheltered-water sailing for the first 2–3 hours, allowing further confidence-building before transitioning to open-water conditions. Return passages benefit from similar protection, reducing fatigue on final days of charters.

Sailing Routes & Operational Area

From ACI Marina Pomer, diverse sailing experiences are accessible. The Kamenjak cape (8 nm south) offers rocky, pristine anchorages and natural reserve exploration. The Brijuni Islands (12 nm northwest) provide multi-day expeditions with national-park scenery. The Rovinj area (20 nm northwest) introduces more experienced crews to open-water sailing and the famous Lim channel. Weekly itineraries might encompass: Day 1–2 sheltered-water confidence-building and day sails; Day 3–4 multi-day expeditions to Brijuni or Kamenjak; Days 5–7 return passages with possible extensions to Rovinj or Mali Lošinj.

Seasons & Sailing Conditions

Spring (April–May) offers warm days (18–22°C), moderate winds, and calm seas—perfect for sailing schools and family introduction charters. Water temperatures rise from 14°C to 18°C. Summer (June–August) brings reliable afternoon thermal winds (12–18 knots), hot sunny days, and peak demand. Water reaches 24–26°C. Autumn (September–October) combines warm water, gentler winds, and comfortable sailing conditions. Winter is possible but less commercial due to shorter days and occasional rough weather. Most charters operate April through late October.
