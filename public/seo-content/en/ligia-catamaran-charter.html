Catamaran Charter in Ligia, Lefkada

Ligia is a small, authentic fishing village on Lefkada's east coast, nestled between Lefkada Town and Nikiana. Unlike crowded charter bases, Ligia offers peace, tradition, and a genuine connection to Ionian life. A catamaran from Ligia is your platform for exploring the region with family stability and comfort that monohulls simply cannot match.

Why Choose a Catamaran in Ligia

Catamarans excel where comfort matters most. Double hulls mean no heel, spacious cabins, and a galley big enough for serious cooking. Boat4You catamarans in Ligia are ideal for families with young children, groups mixing sailors and non-sailors, or anyone prioritizing comfort over racing-yacht aesthetics. Ligia: A Quiet Harbor with Growing Nautical Culture

Ligia has remained relatively untouched by mass tourism, yet the catamaran presence is growing. Family-run tavernas line the waterfront, serving grilled fish and local wine within meters of your mooring. Fishermen still work these waters at dawn. The village's authenticity—narrow lanes, local shops, genuine hospitality—makes it a refreshing base compared to Nidri's hustle or Lefkada Town's crowds.

Catamaran Handling in Ionian Conditions

The summer Maistro wind runs 10–18 knots, and a catamaran's buoyancy and twin-engine setup make light winds manageable. No heel means galley crew won't tire, and children sleep undisturbed below deck. The wide beam provides excellent stability in the open Ionian, where sea state can surprise you. Catamarans also motor more efficiently, reducing fuel costs on calm days.

Ligia Marina and Mooring

Lygia Marina is a small municipal facility with approximately 80 berths, professional harbor watch, and basic amenities: fresh water, electricity, and showers. The harbor is well-protected year-round, and the catamaran's shallow draft means berthing is straightforward even in low-season water levels. Unlike exclusive clubs, the atmosphere is welcoming and informal.

Cruising from Ligia: Your Route Options

Sail north 30 minutes to Nikiana for sheltered anchorage and excellent tavernas. South to Nidri (40 minutes) opens access to the Meganisi strait and the islands beyond. East toward the mainland coast takes you past green hills and hidden bays. West, the open Ionian calls for longer passages to Kalamos, Kastos, and eventually the Peloponnese. Catamarans handle all these routes with steady confidence.

Catamaran Amenities and Layout

Charter catamarans from Ligia typically offer 3–5 cabins, shared heads and showers, and dual galleys. The saloon is spacious—ideal for games, evening gatherings, or rainy-day sheltering. The wide platform aft of the hulls is perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and towing a tender. Onboard systems are modern and redundant: dual engines, dual generators, ample water and fuel tanks.

Ligia's Fishing Heritage and Market Culture

Ligia's identity remains rooted in fishing. Daily at dawn, boats depart for the day's work; by late afternoon, the catch arrives at the waterfront. Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I book?

For peak season (July and August), reservations 4–6 months ahead secure the best fleet selection and pricing. Shoulder months (May, June, September) typically allow booking 6–8 weeks out.

What does the charter price include?

Base pricing usually covers the vessel, insurance, and marina fees at the home base. Fuel, provisioning, skipper service, end-cleaning, and tourist tax are generally added separately. Check the specific listing on Boat4You for a full breakdown.

Do I need a sailing licence?

For bareboat charter, a valid skipper licence plus a VHF certificate are typically required, and rules vary by country. If you don't hold one, hiring a professional skipper is straightforward — pricing runs roughly 150–220 euro per day plus meals.
