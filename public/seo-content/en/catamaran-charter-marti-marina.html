Kız Kumu Sandbar — Nature's Remarkable Creation

The Kız Kumu sandbar dominates Orhaniye's landscape and sailing experiences. This narrow two-kilometer formation of pristine white sand emerges from turquoise waters following predictable tidal patterns, creating a natural phenomenon visible from satellite imagery and attracting nautical enthusiasts worldwide. Walking barefoot across warm sand with Mediterranean waters lapping from both sides delivers an unmistakably magical experience transcending conventional vacation activities.

The sandbar's visibility varies seasonally—typically most prominent from May through September when sediment transport reaches equilibrium. Early morning visits avoid afternoon heat shimmer and crowds, enabling undisturbed exploration and exceptional photography opportunities. Local fishermen claim visibility patterns follow traditional calendars refined across generations. Geological scientists attribute formation to complex sediment transport mechanisms influenced by seasonal river discharge and longshore current patterns. Whatever scientific explanation, Kız Kumu rewards personal exploration and contemplation of natural forces creating such remarkable features.

Hisarönü Bay Anchorages and Coastal Villages

Hisarönü Bay encompasses multiple distinctive anchorages within short sailing distances of Marti Marina. Ekincik Cove provides dramatic limestone cliffs with ancient Lycian tomb facades carved into vertical rock faces—a geological and archaeological combination few locations match globally. The cove's northern shores provide excellent holding with sandy bottom and protective wind breaks. Local legends associate tombs with ancient maritime prosperity, and many visitors arrange guided historical tours explaining Lycian civilization's maritime heritage.

Türkbükü Cove offers shallow, calm waters ideal for families with children and individuals seeking undemanding recreation. Maximum depths rarely exceed 3 meters, providing safety for young swimmers while maintaining sufficient water for catamaran anchoring. Beach access enables shore exploration and sand recreation. Gökkaya Cove, located northwestward from Marti Marina, features pristine sandy beaches surrounded by pine forests descending steeply to waterline. These anchorages remain relatively unknown to mass tourism, offering authentic Turkish experiences beyond resort-based activities.
