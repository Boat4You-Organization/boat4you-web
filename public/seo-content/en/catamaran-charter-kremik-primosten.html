Primošten Town: A Walking Exploration Between Passages

Primošten is one of Dalmatia's most picturesque villages, renowned for its Red Wine of Primošten (Crljenak Kastela), cultivated in terraced vineyards that cascade down the hillsides. The compact old town, perched on a peninsula, features narrow stone alleys, a 17th-century church, and a working fishing harbor that feels suspended in time.

Walking uphill through the village rewards you with panoramic views across the Adriatic toward the Kornati islands. Local restaurants serve fresh seafood—pašticada, brancin (sea bass), and local mussels—paired with the region's distinctive red wines. The beach at the village's edge offers a quick swim before returning to your catamaran at Marina Kremik.

Access to the Kornati National Park & Šibenik Channel

Marina Kremik is positioned approximately 15 nautical miles south of Šibenik by sea. From here, the Kornati National Park entrance is roughly a 3–4 hour sail northeast—ideal for a full-day excursion or overnight passage. The Kornati's 140 islands form a labyrinth of channels, shallow anchorages, and pristine beaches that catamaran crews find endlessly rewarding.

The Šibenik channel itself, a narrow waterway flanked by medieval fortresses and limestone cliffs, offers dramatic scenery accessible within 2 hours of departure from Marina Kremik. Passages through the channel require attentive piloting but reward careful seamanship with unforgettable views and protected anchorages.

Routes & Island Itineraries

A typical week-long charter from Marina Kremik might begin with a passage to the Kornati, where you anchor in sheltered bays like Telašćica or Vrh, then sail onward to Dugi Otok (Long Island) for its striking Sakarun Beach. Returning west, many crews explore the islands near Šibenik—Zlarin, Kaprije, and Prvić—each offering unique charm and protected mooring.

Longer charters venture further south toward the islands of Brač and Hvar, or northwest toward the Dalmatian coast. The catamaran's fuel efficiency means you can venture farther without anxiety about refueling, and the stable platform makes the passages themselves a pleasure rather than an endurance test.

Best Seasons for Catamaran Charters

Spring (April–May) offers warm days, calm mornings, and water temperatures around 17–19°C. The Adriatic is less crowded, and Primošten's vineyards are in bloom. Summer (June–August) brings consistent northwest winds (the Maestral), sunny skies, and warm water (23–26°C), though crowding peaks mid-July through mid-August.

Autumn (September–October) combines warm water, manageable winds, and the harvest season in the Primošten vineyards. Winter sailing is possible but brings shorter days and occasional rough conditions. Most commercial charters operate April through October.

Local Flavors & Provisioning

Primošten's food culture centers on seafood and local wine. The Red Wine of Primošten is the star attraction—a robust, full-bodied red that pairs perfectly with grilled fish and local lamb stews. Fresh markets in the village stock mussels, scampi, squid, and seasonal vegetables. Shops near Marina Kremik supply standard provisioning, but a morning walk through the village market yields far richer rewards.

Local restaurants offer set menus featuring grilled brancin (sea bass), scallops, and traditional pašticada (slow-braised beef). Most welcome sailors and offer reasonable prices for groups. Wine tastings at local producers can be arranged for rest days.

FAQ: Catamaran Charters from Marina Kremik Primošten

How far is Marina Kremik from Šibenik?

Marina Kremik lies approximately 15 nautical miles south of Šibenik by water. By road, the drive from Šibenik is about 30 minutes. The marina is also roughly 70 km from Split Airport, making it accessible for international charter guests.

Are there enough facilities for a full week at Marina Kremik?

Yes. Marina Kremik's comprehensive infrastructure—fuel, water, electricity, provisions, repairs, and laundry—supports charters of any duration. The nearby village of Primošten adds restaurants, shops, and cultural attractions for off-boat time.

What's the wind pattern around Primošten?

The Maestral (northwest wind) is the dominant summer wind, typically 10–15 knots in May and June, strengthening to 15–20 knots July–August. The Sirocco (southeast wind) occasionally brings stronger gusts. Mornings are usually calm, making early departures ideal for passages to the Kornati.

Can we visit Kornati National Park from Marina Kremik?

Absolutely. The Kornati entrance is approximately 3–4 hours' sail from Marina Kremik. provides park permits and navigation briefings. Many crews spend 2–3 days exploring the Kornati's islands and anchorages before returning or continuing elsewhere.

Do I need a skipper for a catamaran charter?

If you hold an International Certificate of Competence (ICC) or equivalent, bareboat charters are available. Otherwise, can arrange a professional skipper. The choice depends on your experience and comfort level navigating the Adriatic's channels and anchorages.
