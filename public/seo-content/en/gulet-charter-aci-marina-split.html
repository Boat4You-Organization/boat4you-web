Island Routes & Anchorages for Gulets

Brač lies 15 nautical miles south. Gulets moor in Bol's sheltered harbor, where Zlatni Rat beach dominates the landscape. Traditional provisions include local wine, cheese, and fish—all available at harborside markets.

Hvar, the Adriatic's sunniest destination, sits 30 nautical miles southwest. Gulet crews drop anchor in Hvar Town's spacious harbor, where nightlife, restaurants, and chandleries cluster near the waterfront. Many gulets sail the island's north coast, discovering hidden bays inaccessible to conventional charters.

Vis represents the ultimate passage: 40 nautical miles offshore. Its dramatic approach unveils the island's character—fortifications, monasteries, and hidden coves. Gulet charters often explore the cave systems near Komiža and the dramatic southern cliffs.

Šolta and Drvenik Veli/Mali offer quieter anchorages. Pine-backed beaches and minimal development make these islands ideal for crews seeking slower-paced exploration and swimming in crystalline bays.

Seasons for Gulet Charters

Spring (April–May) brings calm mornings, warm days, and reliable sailing breezes. Water reaches 18–19°C. Summer (June–August) offers consistent winds, long daylight, and vibrant anchorages—though July–August sees peak tourism and higher rates.

Autumn (September–October) delivers warm water (22–23°C), gentler sailing conditions, and fewer crowds. Winter gulet charters operate but involve shorter days and occasional rough conditions. Most gulet operators focus on April through October.

The Gulet Experience: Local Culture & Cuisine

Gulet charters emphasize cultural immersion. Crews familiar with Split's traditions guide passengers toward local flavors: pašticada (slow-braised beef), soparnik (chard pie), and fresh catches from the Peškarija. Local wines—Plavac Mali and Kaštelanski crljenak—pair naturally with the journey.

Marjan hill, accessible from the waterfront, offers sunset hikes with panoramic views of Split's harbor and your gulet's evening mooring. Evening strolls along the Riva reveal centuries of maritime tradition, from Byzantine ruins to working fishermen.

What's the difference between a gulet and a motoryacht?

Gulets are traditionally built wooden sailing vessels, designed for the Adriatic with hand-crafted hulls and teak interiors. They emphasize atmosphere and cultural experience. Motoryachts prioritize speed and modern amenities. Gulets typically include crew; motoryachts are often bareboat.

Is prior sailing experience required for a gulet charter?

No. Gulet charters include professional crew who handle navigation, sailing, and day-to-day operations. Passengers enjoy the journey without technical responsibility. Optional hands-on sailing instruction is available through selected partners.

Are gulets suitable for families with children?

Yes. Gulets offer spacious cabins, calm anchorages, and crew attentive to family needs. Shallow-water swimming, snorkeling, and island exploration suit children of all ages. Water sports equipment is often included.

What provisioning options are available mid-charter?

Crews stop at harbors every 1–3 days, allowing fresh provisioning at local markets. The islands from ACI Marina Split—Brač, Hvar, Vis—all host supermarkets, fish markets, and specialty shops. crews coordinate with local suppliers for meals tailored to dietary preferences.
