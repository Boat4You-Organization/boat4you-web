Why Charter a Catamaran from Punat

Catamarans are the charter category of choice for stability and comfort. The twin-hull design eliminates much of the heeling and rolling found in monohulls, making them ideal for families and guests prone to seasickness. Punat's location in Kvarner Bay—a naturally protected area bounded by Krk, Cres, and the mainland—ensures calm waters for your sailing days.

Marina Punat is one of Croatia's oldest and largest marinas, with over 850 berths, modern facilities, and immediate access to sheltered Punat Bay. The marina sits just 3 kilometers from Krk town and benefits from excellent road access via the Krk bridge, making arrival and crew changes seamless. Boat4You's catamaran fleet in Punat ranges from entry-level cruisers to premium multihulls, each maintained to exacting standards.

Punat itself is a quiet, authentic Adriatic village. Its deep natural harbor has attracted sailors for centuries, and today it remains unpretentious and welcoming. You are immediately connected to island culture, local restaurants serving Krk lamb and šurlice pasta, and the slower pace of traditional maritime life.

Prime Sailing Routes from Punat

A catamaran from Punat opens three distinct cruising zones:

Northern Kvarner: Day sail to Cres island (1.5 hours), then south toward the Osorčica peninsula. Overnight at Mali Lošinj, a picturesque port town with excellent restaurants and galleries. Return to Punat via Martinšćica on Cres—a natural anchorage lined with pebble beaches.

Southern Krk & Vrbnik: Sail the east coast of Krk from Punat to Baška (1 hour). Baška is home to Vrbnik vineyards and Žlahtina wine tasting rooms, accessible by dinghy and a short walk from the beach. The Vela plaža beach in Baška is one of Croatia's longest and most photographed. Anchor overnight and explore inland.

Mainland Passage: A longer itinerary heads southwest toward the Vinodol coast and Novo Marof, then loops back. These waters are less crowded and offer excellent holding ground for catamaran anchoring. Nearby Krk Airport facilitates crew changes mid-charter.

The Košljun islet, visible from Punat Bay, hosts a Franciscan monastery and is accessible by dinghy. The monks welcome visitors and the islet provides a unique cultural excursion and photo opportunity.

Seasonal Conditions & Best Times

May to September is the active sailing season. May brings moderate temperatures (18–22°C) and light winds, ideal for skill-building and relaxed cruising. June sees water temperatures reach 18°C and warmer air (22–25°C). July–August are warmest (28–30°C) and busiest, with stronger winds (Force 4–5 from the northwest, locally called Maestral). September mirrors June in climate, with fewer tourists.

April and October are viable shoulder months, though water temperatures (14–16°C) demand wetsuits for extended swimming. November to March is quieter and cheaper, but winter Bora winds can be fierce and unpredictable. Most charter companies reduce activities in winter; Boat4You maintains limited winter availability for experienced sailors seeking solitude.

The Maestral wind (northwest) blows in summer afternoons, providing excellent sailing conditions for catamaran day trips. The Bora (northeasterly, winter) is stronger and gustier, demanding respect and shelter.

Sample Itinerary: 7-Day Catamaran Charter

Day 1: Arrival and briefing at Marina Punat. Provisioning and crew assignment. Brief shake-down sail to nearby Vela plaža beach, return by sunset.

Day 2: Full day sail north to Mali Lošinj (18 nautical miles). Explore galleries and waterfront restaurants. Overnight anchorage in the southern bay.

Day 3: Early departure to Cres island's east coast. Visit Martinšćica (a secluded anchorage). Afternoon beach time and swimming.

Day 4: Return sailing via the Kvarner strait. Visit Košljun islet and monastery in the afternoon. Overnight at Punat.

Day 5: South along Krk's east coast to Baška. Dinghy ashore to explore Vrbnik vineyards and wine tasting. Dinner at a waterfront restaurant. Overnight in Baška.

Day 6: Relaxation day in Baška. Long beach walk, swimming, and local food (Krk lamb šurlice). Optional land excursion to nearby hilltop villages.

Day 7: Return sail to Punat (1.5 hours). Final afternoon on the water, cleaning and documentation, evening return to Marina Punat. Crew departs the following morning.

Is Punat suitable for families with young children?

Absolutely. Catamarans are exceptionally stable, and Punat Bay is nearly waveless. Nearby anchorages (Vela plaža, Martinšćica) have shallow, warm, clear water ideal for children. Marina Punat has restaurants and shops within walking distance. However, ensure your catamaran has adequate safety netting and a dinghy for tender excursions.

What is the best time to charter a catamaran in Punat?

May through September is the standard season. May and September offer fewer tourists, stable winds, and comfortable temperatures. June and July are warmest and busiest. August can be crowded. For experienced sailors, April and October are excellent value and still suitable for active cruising.
