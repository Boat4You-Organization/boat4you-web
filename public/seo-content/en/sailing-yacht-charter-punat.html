Exceptional Sailing Routes from Punat

Northern Passage to Lošinj: Depart Punat and sail northwest across open Kvarner waters toward Mali Lošinj (18 nautical miles). Summer Maestral winds provide excellent sailing conditions on a beam reach. The passage tests sailor skills while delivering memorable seascapes. Mali Lošinj offers sophisticated anchorage and cultural exploration. Return via the scenic Cres channel.

Cres Sailing Circuit: Navigate the Kvarner strait toward Cres island, sailing through variable wind patterns and current. Anchor at Martinšćica on Cres's east coast, an anchorage surrounded by limestone cliffs and crystal-clear water. The Cres passage offers technical sailing challenges and rewards with isolation and natural beauty.

Krk East Coast Exploration: Sail south from Punat along Krk's windward coast. Weather permitting, this passage offers dynamic sailing conditions. Anchor at Baška, a large protected bay ideal for overnight mooring. From Baška, explore quieter coves on Krk's southern coast, accessible by dinghy. Include an optional shore excursion to Vrbnik vineyards and Žlahtina wine tastings.

Extended Open-Water Expedition: An ambitious itinerary for experienced sailors. Depart Punat, sail southwest toward the Vinodol coast, exploring open-water passages and lesser-known anchorages. This extended cruise (5–7 days) demands good seamanship and confident navigation but rewards with solitude and authentic sailing experience.

Košljun & Bay Sailing Circuit: A shorter, skills-building itinerary suitable for less-experienced crews. Sail from Punat to nearby Košljun islet (1 hour, short passages), then explore protected bay anchorages and beaches. Visit the Franciscan monastery by dinghy. This route builds crew confidence and sailing proficiency.

Seasonal Sailing Conditions

May: Ideal early-season sailing. Water 18–20°C, air 20–23°C. Light to moderate winds (Force 2–3), variable directions. Excellent for skill-building and relaxed cruising.

June: Strengthening winds. Water 20–23°C, air 23–26°C. Reliable Maestral winds (Force 3–4) begin appearing consistently in afternoons. Excellent sailing conditions paired with comfortable water temperatures.

July–August: Peak sailing season. Water 24–27°C, air 27–30°C. Strong afternoon Maestral winds (Force 4–5) provide dynamic sailing and excellent sailing wind. Early mornings often light wind; plan passages accordingly. Anchorages crowded; book early.

September: Exceptional for sailing. Conditions match June-early July, winds moderate, tourists decline. September is often considered the finest sailing month in Kvarner Bay. Water remains warm (23–25°C), air comfortable (24–26°C).

April & October: Shoulder months. Water 14–18°C, air mild (16–20°C). Suitable for experienced sailors. Variable winds and occasional unsettled weather. Fewer services available on islands.

Winter (November–March): Challenging sailing. Unpredictable Bora winds (northeast, Force 6–8+ gusts), cold water (10–14°C), and rough seas limit operations. Only experienced winter sailors should charter; conditions demand constant vigilance and expert seamanship.

Day 6: Return sail toward Punat. Sail through variable conditions, practicing crew skills and tactical sailing. Brief stop for swimming, then continue sailing toward home port. Sunset approach to Punat.

Is a sailing yacht suitable for non-sailors?

Yes, absolutely. Non-sailors can participate in sailing education, relax on deck, or simply enjoy the seascape and anchorages. The crew adjusts pace and activities to guest abilities and interests.

What happens if there is no wind?

Modern sailing yachts include auxiliary engines for motoring when wind conditions are unfavorable. However, keel-dependent sailing yachts sometimes underperform in strong motor conditions (higher fuel consumption, less speed). If prolonged calm or adverse wind patterns develop, the captain may adjust the itinerary or suggest shorter passages using motor, prioritizing guest comfort and schedule adherence.

Can families with children sail a charter yacht?

Yes, with appropriate precautions. Sailing yachts are stable and secure in protected waters. Younger children require supervision and adequate safety netting. Confirm the yacht has child-appropriate life jackets, safety features, and age-friendly sleeping berths. can tailor vessel selection to family needs and sailing experience.

What is the difference between a sailing yacht and a motorsailer?

A pure sailing yacht prioritizes sail efficiency and performance; the engine is auxiliary and relatively modest. A motorsailer emphasizes motoring capability with sufficient engine power to cruise efficiently in light wind or calm conditions. Sailors typically prefer pure sailing yachts for engagement and performance; families or less-committed sailors often prefer motorsailers for operational flexibility and reduced wind dependence.
