Catamaran Charter from Marina Trogir (ex.SCT) in Trogir

Marina Trogir (formerly Split City Tourist, now simply Marina Trogir) sits on the western edge of Trogir town, across a narrow channel from the UNESCO Old Town. It's smaller than Yachtclub Seget Marina Baotić (roughly 150-200 berths compared to 700), more locally-focused, and quieter—qualities that appeal to charterers who want authentic harbor pace rather than mega-marina scale. A five-minute walk connects the marina to the Old Town's cathedral square, narrow cobblestone lanes, and harborside restaurants where locals eat, not tourists alone.

Catamarans from Marina Trogir access the same Šolta-Brač-Hvar archipelago circuit as their Baotić counterparts, but the experience differs subtly. Marina Trogir's smaller footprint means fewer mega-yacht crowds, more chance to anchor alongside fishing boats rather than charter-fleet siblings, and deeper connections to Trogir town's rhythm. The fuel dock, crane, and essential services remain; the casual Mediterranean atmosphere is the difference.

Marina Facilities and Local Integration

Marina Trogir operates with full-service essentials: fuel dock (no 24-hour guarantee, but available during daylight and early evening), crane (for emergency haul-outs), and reception staff who understand local conditions intimately because they live and work in Trogir year-round. The marina occupies a sheltered basin protected by Trogir's town structures from north and east wind. The western exposure (toward the sea) catches afternoon Maestral, but the breakwater and town buildings provide reasonable protection compared to exposed anchorages.

Walking distance to the Old Town is the unique asset. Five minutes on foot connects the marina to Konzum supermarket, the farmers market (Wednesday and Saturday mornings), and a dozen family-run konobas. Konoba Kapetanović, directly opposite the marina (five-minute walk), serves traditional Dalmatian pašticada and brodet nightly. Unlike marinas isolated on industrial outskirts, Marina Trogir integrates into authentic town life.

Provisioning is more convenient here than at Baotić. The Trogir market's seasonal produce, local cheese, Plavac Mali wine, and fresh fish are 300 meters from your moored catamaran. Overnight provisioning runs: abandon the boat for dinner in town, return for nighttime watch. The marina's small size means fewer complications when you need to depart early or adjust mooring.

Why Catamaran from Marina Trogir Specifically

Catamarans' shallow draft (1.0-1.2 meters) and interior volume suit Marina Trogir's geographic position perfectly. The narrow channel connecting the marina to open water requires precise maneuvering for large monohulls; catamarans' twin-hulled design and separate engine control enable smooth exits and entries. The dual-cabin layout accommodates groups that might have slept cramped in other boat types but find the catamaran's divided cabins offer privacy and comfort.

The circuit from Marina Trogir mirrors Baotić's: Šolta Maslinica (five nautical miles, 30-minute cruise), Brač Bol (20 nautical miles from Maslinica, afternoon arrival), and Hvar Town (25 nautical miles total, requiring a two-day circuit with overnight at Brač). The catamaran's moderate speed (9-10 knots cruising) and stability in light-to-moderate Maestral make these passages feel like leisurely progress rather than urgent travel.

Catamarans from this base benefit from Marina Trogir's local knowledge. Reception staff often know exactly which harbors are crowded, which konobas are worth the dinghy ride, and which anchorages offer best protection if afternoon Maestral builds unexpectedly. This local expertise—available from experienced, on-site staff rather than distant charter operators—matters when real-time decisions shape your week.

Routes from Marina Trogir

The Three-Island Circuit (5–6 days)

Depart Marina Trogir mid-morning for Šolta Maslinica (30 minutes under power, light Maestral if available by late morning). Overnight, exploring olive groves and harborside restaurants. Next day, sail/motor to Brač Bol (4 hours northeast), anchor in the famous sand beach cove. Second overnight. Return via Supetar (Brač north coast) or direct to Maslinica, concluding with final evening back at Marina Trogir. Total: roughly 60 nautical miles, relaxed pace, suitable for mixed-experience crews.

The Hvar-Focused Week (7–8 days)

Maslinica (overnight), Brač Bol (overnight), Hvar Town (two nights: explore harbor, narrow lanes, cathedral square). Pakleni Islands optional day-trip (small undeveloped coves, accessible only by boat, minimal tourism). Return via Brač and Šolta to Marina Trogir. Total: roughly 110 nautical miles. Ideal for groups with moderate sailing/cruising experience.

Extended Visibility: Drvenik Veli Add-On (6–7 days)

Marina Trogir to Drvenik Veli (8 nautical miles, 45-minute cruise), explore the Blue Lagoon's turquoise water (shallow, stunning photography). Continue to Šolta Maslinica or Brač Bol depending on wind and time. Shorter passages mean more time at anchorages, less time underway. Suitable for families with young children or crews who dislike extended cruising days.

Technical Advantages: Catamarans Here

Marina Trogir's narrow channel (roughly 80-100 meters wide, 2.5-3 meters deep) demands a boat that responds instantly to helm and engine commands. Catamarans' independent engine control (one engine per hull) lets you pivot in tight spaces—essential when transiting between the marina's narrow northern entrance and the wider southern basin. Monohulls, lacking this dual-engine precision, require extensive maneuvering or outside assistance.

Shallow draft (1.0-1.2 meters) permits approach to the marina's northern mooring field, where only shallow-draft vessels can safely anchor. Deep-drafted boats must moor in the southern basin, farther from Old Town shops and restaurants. The catamaran's shallow keel solves this logistics problem entirely.

Dual cabins mean your crew maintains privacy while sharing vessel space. One cabin per hull creates natural separation—children sleep in the forward cabin of one hull, parents in the other, with saloon spanning both hulls. This layout eliminates galley-bed sleeping and cabin-sharing fatigue that smaller monohulls enforce.

Pricing and Value Proposition

Catamaran charter from Marina Trogir typically runs 450–800 euros per day—slightly lower than Baotić due to Marina Trogir's smaller operation and less mega-marina pricing pressure. June and September (shoulder season) sit toward the lower end; July-August peak season reaches the upper range. Early booking (8-10 weeks ahead) unlocks 10-15% discounts.

Fuel is separate and charged per consumption. Provisioning at Trogir market (Wednesday and Saturday) costs 40-50% less than packaged supermarket goods—savings that compound over a week. Paid harbor fees in main destinations (Hvar, Vis) run 10-15 euros per night. Damage waiver insurance typically adds 10-15% to the base rate.

Marina Trogir's value proposition isn't lowest price—it's authenticity and walkability. The five-minute walk to Old Town, the family-run konobas, the local harbor atmosphere, and the farmers market access create a more integrated week-long experience than larger charter marinas. Many repeat charterers prefer Marina Trogir for this exact reason.

Best Season for Catamaran Charter Here

June and September offer ideal conditions: Maestral winds 10-12 knots (steady, predictable), water temps 22-24°C (comfortable for swimming), and reasonable harbor crowding. July-August bring warmer water (26-27°C) but crowded anchorages (Hvar Town fills with 200+ boats by late July) and afternoon Maestral gusts (15-18 knots, adding boat motion and noise).

May and October are survivable but unpredictable. Spring cold fronts and autumn rain squalls are possible. Water temps drop to 18-20°C (cold for extended swimming). Winter (November-March) brings Bura and Scirocco winds; most charters are unavailable. Book peak-season catamarans 8-10 weeks ahead; shoulder months need 4-6 weeks.

Local Flavor and Trogir Town Dining

Trogir town's UNESCO Old Town sits minutes from Marina Trogir, integrating your catamaran charter into authentic medieval Mediterranean culture. The cathedral square (Trg sv. Ivana Evanđelista) dates to the 13th century; narrow lanes between stone buildings create a maze that changes character every 50 meters. Konoba Kapetanović, directly opposite the marina entrance, serves Dalmatian pašticada and brodet nightly to a mix of locals and visiting sailors.

Peka (slow-cooked under ceramic dome) defines Dalmatian cooking; the traditional version uses octopus, potatoes, and local white wine. Pašticada—beef braised in red wine with prunes—is heavier, better for cooler evenings. Brodet, a Mediterranean fish stew, varies by harbor; Trogir's version traditionally includes red mullet (barbunja), scorpion fish (skorpina), and local Pošip wine.

Plavac Mali (deep red) and Pošip (light white) are the region's principal wines. Both are cheap at source (8-12 euros per bottle at Konzum) and excellent with seafood. Wednesday and Saturday farmers markets sell seasonal vegetables, local cheese (particularly aged sheep's milk varieties), and cured meats. Buy fresh at the market; cook aboard and you'll eat better than restaurants offer at half the cost.

Getting There and Practical Information

Split Airport (25 kilometers north) sits 12-15 minutes by car from Marina Trogir. Confirm airport transfer when booking. Alternative: buses from Split town center to Trogir (30 minutes, €6-8), then walking or taxi to Marina Trogir (€8-12, roughly 3 kilometers). EU citizens need passport or ID card; non-EU nationals must confirm Croatian visa requirements.

Bareboat catamaran charter requires valid sailing certification (International Certificate of Competency or equivalent). If your crew lacks credentials, hire a professional skipper to command the catamaran while your group focuses on swimming and exploration.

Pets are welcome at Marina Trogir with advance notification; however, some harbors and Croatian islands restrict animals. Confirm your specific itinerary when booking. Provisioning for dietary restrictions requires advance planning—Trogir market has limited specialty items but ample fresh produce and fish.

FAQ: Catamaran Charter from Marina Trogir

How far in advance should I book a catamaran from Marina Trogir?

For July-August, book 8-10 weeks prior. June, September, and early October need 4-6 weeks. Marina Trogir's smaller operation (vs. mega-marinas) sometimes has last-minute availability at 10-15% discounts. Contact the operator directly if your dates are flexible.

What is the best month to sail from Marina Trogir to Hvar Town?

June and September offer steady Maestral (10-12 knots) and water temps around 22-24°C. The 25-nautical-mile circuit takes roughly 5-6 hours under normal conditions. July-August are warmer but crowded; May and October are unpredictable. June-September weekdays (Monday-Friday) are less crowded than weekends.

Can I visit the UNESCO Old Town of Trogir directly from my catamaran at Marina Trogir?

Yes, absolutely. The marina sits across a narrow channel from the Old Town; five-minute walk connects the mooring field to the cathedral square, narrow lanes, and medieval architecture. This is Marina Trogir's unique advantage over larger, more isolated charter bases.

Is Marina Trogir suitable for bareboat catamaran charter, or do I need a skipper?

Croatia legally requires International Certificate of Competency or equivalent for bareboat charter of any keeled catamaran. If your crew meets this standard, bareboat is fine. If not, hire a professional skipper (costs 250-350 euros daily but removes navigation stress and delivers local knowledge). Marina Trogir's staff can recommend trusted skippers.

What provisioning options exist at Marina Trogir?

Konzum supermarket is five-minute walk from the marina. Trogir farmers market (Wednesday and Saturday mornings, waterfront square) offers seasonal produce, cheese, wines, and fresh fish—typically 40-50% cheaper than packaged goods. No chandlery at the marina; essential spare parts and nautical supplies are available in Split (30 kilometers north).

Does Marina Trogir offer fuel 24/7 like larger marinas?

No. Marina Trogir's fuel dock operates daylight and early evening (typically 8 a.m.–6 p.m., confirm exact hours). Yachtclub Seget Marina Baotić (west side of the bay) offers 24-hour fuel dock if you need after-hours emergency fuel. Plan fuel stops during normal hours; the distance between marinas is short enough to minimize logistics friction.
