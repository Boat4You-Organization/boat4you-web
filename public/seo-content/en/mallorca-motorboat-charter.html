Motorboat Charter in Mallorca

Motorboats—open or cabin speedboats (18-35 feet) designed for velocity and maneuverability—appeal to thrill-seekers and families seeking rapid island access. Boat4You lists a modest selection of motorboats across Palma, Puerto Portals, and Alcúdia, catering to day trips, watersports, and tourists prioritizing speed over sailing comfort.

Motorboat Market in Mallorca

Mallorca's motorboat rental sector is smaller than its catamaran/motor yacht markets. The island's exceptional sailing conditions—reliable winds, protected bays—mean most charter operators emphasize sailing craft. However, motorboats thrive for specific use cases: family beach hopping, towed water sports, and groups uncomfortable with sailing dynamics.

Speed advantage: A 35-foot motorboat cruises 25-30 knots. Same journey by 40-foot catamaran (8-12 knot cruise) takes 3-4x longer. This suits tight holiday schedules and priority-based itineraries.

Motorboat Types & Specs

Open Speedboat (18-25 feet): 4-6 seating, minimal cabin, 20-35 knot cruise speed. T-top canvas for shade. Popular for day excursions. Rental: €400–€700/day skippered.

Cabin Motorboat (25-35 feet): Enclosed cabin, galley, head, 2-4 sleeping berths. 18-28 knot cruise. Suitable for 1-2 overnight trips. Rental: €600–€1,200/day bareboat (ICC required) or €800–€1,500/day skippered.

Performance Speedboat (30-40 feet): High-HP twins or triples (400-700 HP). 35-45 knot cruise, 50+ knot max. Luxury open designs. Rental: €1,200–€2,500/day. Thrilling, fuel-intensive (20-35 GPH), and not practical for week-long charters.

Why Motorboats Suit Mallorca's Goals

Island Hopping Efficiency: Palma to Cabrera National Park (45nm) takes 5-6 hours by catamaran cruise, 1.5-2 hours by fast motorboat. Maximize time on-island rather than underway.

Watersports Focus: Motorboats excel towing wakeboards, water skis, and tubes. Stable platform for jet ski launch/recovery. Many include dual ski towers and boarding ladders.

Family Flexibility: Young children and non-sailors tolerate motorboat speed and stability. No heeling, minimal motion sickness, more exciting than leisurely sailing pace.

Multi-Beach Days: Visit Es Trenc morning, Cala d'Or lunch, Cala Figuera afternoon. Constant motion keeps kids entertained.

Fuel & Operating Costs

Consumption: 8-35 GPH depending on speed and engine type. Cruise speed (16-22 knots) averages 12-18 GPH. Open speedboats are more efficient; twin-engine cabins burn more.

Fuel Pricing: Mallorca diesel marine fuel: €1.50–€1.80/liter (approx. €5.60–€6.80/gallon). A 25-footer on 250-gallon tanks, running 8 hours at 15 GPH, costs €1,200–€1,600 fuel alone.

All-In Day Cost: Skippered motorboat charter (€800–€1,200/day) + fuel (€200–€400/day) = €1,000–€1,600 daily. Groups of 4-6 share this; per-person cost: €170–€400.

Economics Note: Catamarans (€750–€1,100/day all-inclusive fuel) are cheaper for multi-day charters. Motorboats win for single-day high-speed excursions.

Menorca Day Trip (Advanced): Palma to Fornells Bay, Menorca (40nm, 1.5 hours fast). Explore Addaia, Alayor. Return (40nm, 1.5 hours). Minimal ferry time, maximum island immersion. Requires calm sea state; skip if swells exceed 1m.

Bases & Availability

Palma (Club de Mar, Real Club Náutico): 6-8 motorboats available. Popular, book ahead (especially July-August).

Puerto Portals: 3-4 boats, upscale clientele, premium pricing (€1,000–€1,500/day).

Alcúdia (north coast): 2-3 boats, budget-friendly (€600–€900/day), excellent for Pollença and Menorca access.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a license to operate a motorboat bareboat in Mallorca?

Yes. Spanish maritime law requires ICC (International Certificate of Competence) for bareboat motorboat charters over 24 feet. Below 24 feet, some operators waive licensing (verify in advance). Skippered charters bypass licensing entirely.

Can motorboats handle rough sea conditions?

Modest rough water only. Open speedboats are uncomfortable in swells over 1m; cabin cruisers tolerate 1.5-2m with reduced speed. Mallorca's May/September swell periods can cancel motorboat trips. Typically confirm sea state forecast before booking.

What is the fuel surcharge for motorboats?

Included in daily rates, or charged separately (€150–€300/day typical). Confirm with operator. High-speed runs (30+ knots) burn fuel fast; conserving speed extends range and reduces cost.

Can I tow water sports behind a motorboat?

Yes, most charter motorboats are equipped with ski towers, ballast bags, and boarding platforms. Wakeboarding, water skiing, and tube towing are standard. Crew operates boat while guests enjoy sports; no bareboat water-sports towing allowed.

How does motorboat comfort compare to catamarans on longer trips?

Motorboats vibrate more (engine resonance). Open designs have limited weather protection. Cabin cruisers offer shelter but tight sleeping quarters. Catamarans' dual hulls provide stability and spacious living. For 3+ days, catamarans are superior comfort; motorboats excel on day trips.
