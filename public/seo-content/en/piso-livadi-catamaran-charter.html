Top Sailing Routes from Piso Livadi

From Piso Livadi, you'll enjoy direct access to the central Aegean islands. A favourite route is the short sail to Naxos, approximately 20 nautical miles southeast, a voyage of roughly 3–4 hours. Naxos offers spectacular anchorages, vibrant beach bars, and the opportunity to explore both windward and leeward coastlines.

The islands of Antiparos and Despotiko lie within easy reach to the west, making for perfect day-sail destinations. Many charterers combine a crossing to the Naxos group with onward passages to Amorgos or smaller Cycladic outposts. The Aegean's prevailing northwesterly winds (the meltemia) typically blow May through September, providing consistent sailing conditions.

Those seeking a longer itinerary might plan a week-long circuit encompassing Paros, Naxos, Antiparos, and the quieter islets of Despotiko and Iraklia. Each island presents distinct character: Naxos for its marble quarries and mountainous interior, Antiparos for sea caves and protected coves, and the smaller islands for solitude and traditional village life.

Delos and Mykonos lie further southeast, requiring longer passages but rewarding adventurous crews with archaeology, nightlife, and cosmopolitan culture. Best Seasons for Catamaran Chartering

May through October represent peak sailing season in the Aegean, with reliable winds and warm, stable weather. Summer months (July and August) see the strongest meltemia—northwesterly winds typically 15–25 knots—providing excellent sailing but drawing larger crowds to popular anchorages.

Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer milder conditions with lighter winds, ideal for leisurely sailing and shore exploration. Water temperatures range from 18°C in April to 25°C by July, remaining comfortable for swimming through October.
