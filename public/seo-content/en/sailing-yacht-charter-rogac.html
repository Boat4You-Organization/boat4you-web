Wind Patterns & Sailing Conditions

Rogač's northern exposure captures afternoon thermal winds reliably during summer months. The pattern: calm dawn, light airs by 8 AM, Force 3–4 from the northwest by midday, occasional Force 4–5 gusts in afternoon, wind easing toward evening. This predictability suits sailing-education goals; crews experience consistent conditions for practicing sail-trimming, helmsmanship, and passage planning.

Spring sailing (May–June) brings lighter winds (Force 2–3) perfect for learning; summer (July–August) delivers robust winds ideal for experienced sailors; autumn (September–October) offers balanced conditions and warm water. Winter sailing is possible but challenging; gales and short daylight limit most charters.

Multi-Island Sailing Routes

From Rogač, sailing passages span from hour-long coastal explorations to week-long circumnavigations. Northwestward, Trogir lies four hours away; the Venetian old town rewards cultural day-sails and evening exploration. Eastward, Brač's southern coast offers dramatic cliffs and secluded anchorages; Bol harbor provides reliable shelter. Southward, Šolta's protected coves (Nečujam, Stomorska, Maslinica) offer overnight stops with taverna meals and swimming opportunities.

Extended voyages (seven days) extend toward Vis, Hvar, or the Drvenik islands. Boat4You provides detailed route planning during your pre-charter consultation; passages are customized to your sailing experience, interests, and available time.

Port Facilities & Mooring

Rogač harbor provides basic mooring services: berths available daily at €2–€3 per meter. The fuel dock operates summers (7 AM–6 PM); water filling is available year-round. Reception staff assist with route planning and local contacts. Fresh-water supplies are typically abundant; crews top tanks before departing on extended passages.

Nearby Maslinica harbor offers protected mooring and regatta-season social atmosphere. Nečujam bay (Šolta's eastern coast) provides natural anchorage with excellent holding and taverna access. Multiple options allow flexible mooring strategies across multi-day itineraries.

Provisioning & Local Suppliers

Rogač's harborside supermarket stocks provisioning basics: fresh bread, vegetables, cheese, and canned goods. Local fishermen sell daily catch directly from boats; morning walks along the waterfront reveal what's available. Taverna meals cost €12–€18 per person; most establishments welcome sailors and offer informal group rates.

Wine sourcing: local Posip (crisp white) and Rukatac (light red) appear in tavernas and at harbor shops. Quality is consistently good; prices are fair. Rakija (traditional brandy) rounds evening meals; most tavernas offer complimentary small glasses after dinner.
