Leisurely Routes: Motor Cruising Highlights

Brač Island (18 nautical miles south) is easily reached in two hours at a leisurely 9-knot cruise. Bol's protected harbor offers restaurants, pebble beaches, and cultural attractions (Dominican monastery) without the time pressure of sailing crews.

Hvar Island (32 nm) becomes accessible in a relaxed afternoon. Stop midway at Šolta's shallow anchorages for lunch ashore, then motor to Hvar Town's spacious harbor. Motor yachts anchor longer, permitting dinner reservations at clifftop restaurants with sunset views of your vessel at mooring.

Vis (42 nm) transforms into a comfortable overnight cruise. Depart Trogir early, anchor at Komiža for lunch and provisioning, then reach Vis Town by evening. A private motor yacht avoids crowded flotilla anchorages, permitting solitude in Green Cave or the Adriatic's quieter southern reaches.

Fuel Efficiency & Range Planning

Marina Trogir's motor yachts cruise at 8–12 knots, burning 5–8 gallons per hour. A full 300-gallon fuel tank yields 40–60-hour range—sufficient for week-long charters including day excursions and reef exploration. Fuel consumption logs help skippers optimize speeds and plan refueling stops.

Diesel engines are reliable but thirsty at higher speeds. Cruising at 8 knots yields 2+ nautical miles per gallon; 12 knots drops efficiency to 1.5 nm/gallon. Crews who slow down, relax, and enjoy sunsets economize fuel while enhancing the charter experience.

Comfort Amenities & Provisioning

On-deck lounging areas, full galleys, and multiple cabins ensure comfort for families and groups. Some vessels feature aft hot tubs and forward sun pads. Air-conditioning runs continuously if desired; diesel heating maintains warmth during off-season cruises (October–April).

Marina Trogir's provisioning team stocks vessels pre-departure with breakfast items, wine, and basics if requested. Trogir's market (Gradska Pijaca) opens Tuesday–Saturday mornings and supplies fresh fish, local Plavac Mali wines, and cheese. Restaurants like Konoba Kaleta and Tragos welcome motor-yacht crews and offer group dining discounts.

Seasonal Cruising & Weather Considerations

Spring (April–May): Calm anchorages, 16–18°C water, and leisurely exploration. Perfect for less-experienced crews and families with small children.

Summer (June–August): Reliable 12–18-knot winds, 23–25°C water, and crowded anchorages. Motor yachts navigate between flotillas, accessing quieter coves where sailboats struggle against light winds.

Autumn (September–October): Ideal cruising. 14–18-knot winds, 20–22°C water, and fewer charter vessels. Marina Trogir's motor yachts occupy premium anchorages at sunrise, departing by 10 AM for quieter afternoon mooring.

FAQ: Motor Yacht Charters from Marina Trogir

Do I need a boating license to charter a motor yacht?

Professional skippers are mandatory for bareboat motor-yacht charters at Marina Trogir. Boat4You arranges experienced captains who are licensed, insured, and familiar with Trogir's fleet. Crews may assume the helm under captain supervision.

What's included in fuel and generator runtime?

Fuel to full tanks and diesel for the generator are included in the charter rate. Excess consumption (if cruising at high speeds or running air-conditioning continuously in winter) may incur additional charges. Marina Trogir bills separately if supplemental fuel is required.

Can the motor yacht visit Istria or venture north toward Venice?

Yes. Longer charters (10–14 days) from Marina Trogir can reach Rovinj (Istria) or even Portorož (Slovenia). Fuel and provisioning logistics change over distance. Boat4You advises on multi-week itineraries and ensures your captain is experienced in longer transits.

Is air-conditioning available on all motor yachts?

Yes. All six motor yachts feature modern reverse-cycle air-conditioning. Running continuously draws approximately 25 amps per hour; generator fuel consumption increases in hot weather. Most crews balance air-conditioning with natural ventilation and open-air deck lounging to economize fuel.

What provisioning services does Marina Trogir offer?

Pre-charter provisioning (breakfast supplies, wine, basics) can be requested. Crew provisioning runs (local markets, fish vendors, butchers) are arranged by your captain. Restaurants near Trogir provide catering services for groups wishing formal dinners aboard.
