Motorboat Charter in Primošten: Fast Access to Central Dalmatian Islands

Not all Mediterranean sailing holidays demand weeks at anchor. Motorboat charters from Primošten appeal to travellers seeking action, variety, and the freedom to cover significant distances in a single day. Unlike sailing yachts (which prioritise wind response) or catamarans (which balance comfort with sail), motorboats deliver speed, flexibility, and instant access to far-flung anchorages. A motorboat from Primošten—whether a nimble RIB or a sporty speedboat—opens a day-trip circuit through the Kornati National Park, Brač island, and the Dalmatian coast that would require 3–4 days aboard a traditional sailing vessel. Boat4You offers motorboat charters from Primošten's Marina Kremik, ideal for active crews wanting maximum adventure in minimal time.

Motorboat Types Available from Primošten

Primošten motorboat charters include rigid-hulled inflatable boats (RIBs), ranging from 5–8 metres, and speedboats in the 6–10 metre range. RIBs typically carry 6–12 passengers plus a skipper and offer impressive speed (30–40 knots) with exceptional manoeuvrability. Speedboats vary: some are open-console designs emphasising speed, others are cabin cruisers balancing comfort with performance.

RIBs are favoured for day-trip itineraries: the shallow draft allows beach landings, the open design enables swimming directly from the vessel, and the speed permits covering 40–50 nautical miles comfortably in a half-day. Speedboats with cabins suit crews prioritising comfort for longer day trips or multi-day excursions.

All Boat4You motorboats from Primošten are equipped with modern navigation systems (GPS, depth sounder), safety equipment (life jackets, first-aid kits, flares), and communication systems (VHF radio). Professional skippers are typically included or available, particularly for RIB charters where local knowledge maximises exploration options.

Speed, Efficiency, and Itinerary Flexibility

A motorboat's primary advantage is speed. From Primošten, you can reach the outer Kornati islands (20–25 nautical miles) in 45 minutes—a journey requiring 3–4 hours aboard a sailing yacht. This speed fundamentally changes charter strategy: instead of sailing a full day to reach a single anchorage, motorboat crews can visit multiple islands, explore numerous anchorages, and return to Primošten for dinner the same evening.

This flexibility suits several traveller profiles: couples combining sailing holidays with exploring multiple locations in limited time, families with young children wanting shorter transit times, and active groups prioritising activity variety over leisurely sailing. A motorboat charter is less about the journey and more about the destination—and reaching as many destinations as possible within your available time.

Day-Trip Circuit: Kornati National Park Runs

The classic Primošten motorboat itinerary is a day run into the Kornati National Park. Departing Marina Kremik at 08:00, a RIB or speedboat reaches the outer Kornati islands (Mala Proversa, Levrnaka) by 09:30. You have the full day to explore multiple anchorages, swim in crystal-clear bays, and enjoy lunch at a waterside anchorage before returning to Primošten by 17:00–18:00.

This run is particularly popular with families: children enjoy the boat ride, swimming, and exploring wild anchorages is safer aboard a motorboat (where the skipper maintains full engine control) than aboard a larger sailing vessel. The return trip to Primošten is quick, allowing everyone ashore for dinner at a village restaurant by evening.

Variations on this itinerary include pushing further north to Silba or Premuda islands (1.5–2 hour runs from Primošten) or exploring the Šibenik coast northward. Multi-day motorboat charters (less common but available) allow crews to explore multiple islands over 2–3 days, anchoring overnight and enjoying the motorboat's speed advantage over longer ranges.

Brač Island, Šolta, and Southern Excursions

To the southwest, motorboat charters from Primošten can reach Bol on Brač (15 nautical miles, 30 minutes) and explore Zlatni Rat beach, a distinctive sand spit famous throughout Croatia. A motorboat allows quick trips to this iconic location combined with exploration of quieter Brač anchorages and small towns like Pučišća.

Šolta island lies further southwest (12–15 nautical miles from Bol) and offers impressive anchorages and hiking trails. The motorboat speed advantage means you can visit Šolta as part of a day-trip circuit that includes Brač and Hvar island (further southwest, 25 nautical miles from Primošten)—a itinerary impossible aboard a traditional sailing yacht in a single day.

Marina Kremik and Motorboat Logistics

Motorboats depart from Marina Kremik's dedicated speedboat pier. Unlike sailing yacht operations, motorboat charters typically involve no overnight stays aboard; you depart at dawn and return by early evening. This reduces operational complexity and appeals to crews combining motorboat adventures with land-based accommodation in Primošten's town-centre hotels or waterfront apartments.

Fuel is typically included in motorboat charter costs for day trips (though longer expeditions may add fuel surcharges). All safety briefings, navigation, and insurance are handled by the operator. Professional skippers are included on all Boat4You RIB charters and available on speedboat options, providing local knowledge and safety oversight.

Seasonality and Weather Considerations

Motorboat charters operate year-round, though May through September offers the warmest water and most reliable weather. July and August bring the most consistent Maestral winds—favourable for motorboat operation (though wind is less relevant for powered vessels than for sailing). Summer also means warmest water (26–27°C), ideal for swimming.

Motorboats are less affected by wind than sailing yachts—they have engine power to overcome conditions. This makes motorboat charters practical even during occasional Bura squalls (20–30 knot northeast winds), when sailing becomes challenging. A skilled skipper will adjust routes based on sea state but rarely cancels trips outright.

Water temperatures below 15°C (November–April) require wetsuits for swimming; many crews prefer the May–September season for maximum comfort.

Pricing and Booking Motorboat Charters

Motorboat day-trip charters from Primošten typically cost €400–€800 per day for groups of 4–8 people, depending on vessel size and fuel consumption. Multi-day motorboat charters (less common) run €1,500–€3,000 per day all-inclusive (fuel, skipper, provisions). These prices are significantly lower per-person than sailing yacht or catamaran charters, making motorboat rentals attractive for couples or small families.

Most motorboat charters include a professional skipper, all fuel, safety equipment, and basic provisions (water, snacks). Additional meals are either prepared aboard or arranged at island restaurants. Insurance is typically included; damage deposits are minimal (€500–€1,000) compared to sailing vessels.

Frequently Asked Questions About Primošten Motorboats

Do I need a boat licence to charter a motorboat?

Not if a professional skipper is provided (typical for Boat4You motorboat charters). For unguided motorboat rentals, licensing requirements vary by vessel size and your nationality. Contact Boat4You for specific details.

How fast can a motorboat go?

RIBs typically cruise at 25–35 knots (comfortable for passengers), with top speeds of 35–40 knots. Speedboats vary but often reach 30–45 knots depending on design. These speeds make 20–30 nautical mile day trips practical and fun.

Is a motorboat suitable for families with young children?

Yes, particularly for day trips. RIBs are stable, safety equipment is comprehensive, and skippers are experienced with families. Children typically enjoy the speed and active itineraries. For overnight voyages, larger speedboats with cabins offer more comfort.

What happens if weather becomes rough?

Professional skippers will adjust routes to calmer waters or shallower bays if necessary. In very rare cases of threatening conditions, trips may be rescheduled or refunded. Motorboats are generally less affected by weather than sailing vessels, but skipper discretion typically takes priority.

Can we arrange meals and provisioning aboard?

Yes. Most motorboat charters include basic provisions (water, soft drinks, snacks) and can arrange provisions for longer trips. Alternatively, crews often eat lunch at island restaurants, which motorboat speed makes practical.
