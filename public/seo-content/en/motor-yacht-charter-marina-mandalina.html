Seasons & Weather Optimization

Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) deliver optimal motor yacht conditions: moderate 10-15 knot winds (unobstructive for power vessels), water temperatures of 16–21°C, and manageable sea states. Summer (June–August) brings warm water (23–26°C) and predictable Maestral breezes; however, crowding and thermal glare require strategic timing. Winter (November–March) remains navigable but demands experienced captains and acceptance of occasional rough conditions; however, winter solitude attracts discerning clients seeking pristine anchorages without crowds.
