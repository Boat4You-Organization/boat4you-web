Why Choose a Power Catamaran?

Power catamarans combine the stability and space of twin hulls with reliable diesel engines, eliminating sailing skills and weather dependency. Unlike sailing yachts, which require constant sail trimming and seaworthiness, power cats allow crews to focus on exploring, swimming, and enjoying anchorages. Power Catamaran Routes & Daily Distances

Power catamarans excel at exploring diverse destinations in a single week. From Alimos, motor east to Aegina (14 nm, 90 minutes), then south to Poros (25 nm, 2.5 hours) and Hydra (40 nm, 4 hours). Spetses (55 nm, 5.5 hours) opens access to quieter islands and pristine anchorages. This range permits a different island each day—impossible with sailing yachts.

Venture further southeast around Cape Sounion (20 nm) to reach the Cyclades: Kythnos, Kea, and Serifos become day-trip destinations rather than multi-day sails. The Argolic coast—Porto Cheli, Ermioni, and Navplio—showcases Byzantine ruins and sheltered harbours. Corinthian Canal passages (12 hours from Alimos) access the Corinthian Gulf and historic sites near Delphi.

Power Catamaran Comfort & Amenities

Modern power catamarans are floating resorts. Air-conditioned saloons with premium furnishings, flat-screen entertainment systems, and spacious dining areas create a contemporary cruising experience. Galleys feature dual refrigerators, convection ovens, and marble countertops. Water makers (desalination systems) ensure unlimited fresh water—a luxury on extended charters.

Master suites rival hotel cabins: queen beds, private en-suite bathrooms with rainfall showers, and ample storage. Guest cabins offer comfort with bunk or double berths, individual air-con controls, and modern lighting. Tenders (small boats) hang from davits, enabling beach explorations, water skiing, and visits to anchorages inaccessible to larger vessels.

Wide sterns feature swim platforms with showers, direct-feed diving gear, and water toys. Flybridge sun decks with lounge areas, tables, and Bimini shade create alfresco relaxation zones. GPS, chartplotters, autopilot, VHF, and SSB radio ensure navigation confidence. Safety gear (life rafts, harnesses, flares) meets international standards.

Power vs. Sailing: Comparing the Experience

Sailing yachts demand skill, attention, and seaworthiness; power catamarans prioritize relaxation and distance. Sailors value the craft, trim, and connection to wind; power-cat crews value speed, flexibility, and comfort. Neither is superior—just different values and priorities.

Power catamarans suit non-sailors, large groups wanting kitchen comfort, those covering long distances daily, and crews with mixed sailing abilities. Sailing yachts attract purists, experienced sailors, romantic adventurers, and those seeking lower charter costs. Boat4You helps you choose: answer a few questions about sailing experience, group size, and itinerary, and we'll recommend the perfect vessel.

Operating costs differ: power cats burn diesel (typically €200–€400/week), while sailing yachts use wind. Charter prices reflect this: power cats cost more (€1,800–€5,000/week) than bareboat sailing yachts (€900–€3,000/week), but offer superior comfort and speed.

Power Catamaran FAQs

What's the difference between power catamarans and sailing cats?

Sailing catamarans use wind-driven sails; power catamarans rely on diesel engines for propulsion. Power cats are faster, more flexible, require no sailing skill, and suit non-sailors. Sailing cats offer lower fuel costs and traditional seafaring experience. recommends power cats for first-timers and groups prioritizing relaxation.

How much fuel does a power catamaran consume?

Typical power cats (40–50 feet) burn 15–25 litres per hour at cruising speed. Weekly costs range €200–€400 depending on distance travelled and fuel prices. Fuel is usually added to your final invoice.

Can non-sailors bareboat a power catamaran?

Yes, if they hold a valid boating licence (RYA Power Boat Level 2 or equivalent) and have basic seamanship. Charter companies require a briefing and confirm competence before departure. Many non-sailors hire a skipper for peace of mind; arranges this seamlessly.

What's included in a power cat charter?

Standard inclusions: mooring at Alimos, kitchen equipment, safety gear, GPS/chartplotter, life jackets, and welcome briefing. Fuel, skipper, and provisioning are typically extra. Premium charters include water maker, tender, diving equipment, and crew. Check your listing for exact inclusions.

Are power catamarans faster than motor yachts?

Power cats (18–22 knots cruising) and motor yachts (15–20 knots) are comparable in speed, but cats offer superior stability and flatter decks. Motor yachts are sleeker and often faster at top speed. helps you compare based on your priorities: comfort (cats) vs. style (motor yachts).

Power catamaran bareboat charter requires RYA Power Boat Level 2 or equivalent national certification. Proof of boating experience and basic seamanship suffice—no sailing skills required.
