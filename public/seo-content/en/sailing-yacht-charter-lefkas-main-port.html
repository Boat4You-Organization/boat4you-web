Wind Patterns and Sailing Angles

The Ionian Islands' Maistro wind typically builds by mid-morning, peaks in the 1400–1700 window, and eases toward evening. This pattern rewards early departures, midday sailing intensity, and protected evening anchorages where crews enjoy sunset aperitivos. Wind shifts around major islands create interesting tactical challenges; thermal effects along mountainous coastlines demand constant attention.

Autumn (September–October) introduces occasional Sirocco southeasterlies that challenge wind forecasting. Experienced racing crews favour these unpredictable conditions; cruising yachts benefit from the reduced charter-fleet congestion and clearer anchorages.
