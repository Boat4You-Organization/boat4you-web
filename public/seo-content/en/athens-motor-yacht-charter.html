Flisvos Marina: Athens' Superyacht Hub

Flisvos Marina, nestled on Athens' southwestern coast, is home to approximately 400 yachts, many over 50 metres. The facility offers world-class infrastructure: deep berths accommodating mega-yachts, 24/7 security, fuel and water refueling, ice production, and specialized provisioning services catering to international crews and luxury clientele. Flisvos is 20 km southwest of central Athens and 50 minutes from Athens International Airport; ground transport is straightforward via private car service (which Boat4You can arrange).

Flisvos' location is strategically perfect: quick access to the Saronic Gulf for leisure cruising, proximity to the Athens Riviera for cosmopolitan dinners and beach-club soirées, and straightforward departure toward the Cyclades for week-long luxury voyages. Most international clients prefer Flisvos over Alimos Marina for its exclusive atmosphere and VIP services.

Saronic Gulf and Cycladic Voyages

Motor yachts from Athens typically undertake 7–14 day charters. A week-long itinerary might depart Flisvos, cruise the Saronic Gulf islands (Aegina, Poros, Hydra) over 2–3 days with leisurely anchorages and gourmet meals, then venture toward the Cyclades (Kea, Kythnos, Mykonos, Delos) for 4–5 days of exploration, island hopping, and nightlife. The speed of motor yachts (15–20 knots) allows ambitious routing: circumnavigate multiple island clusters, base yourself in different harbours each night, and maximize geographic diversity.

Alternatively, some clients prefer loitering—anchoring in one remarkable bay for days, diving, snorkeling, swimming, and dining ashore each evening. Motor yachts' reliability and amenities make this flexibility possible. Your captain will propose daily itineraries based on weather, your wishes, and emerging opportunities; no two charters are identical.

Water Sports and Recreational Activities

Motor yachts 60+ feet typically carry tenders (small motorboats), jet skis, diving equipment, fishing tackle, and stand-up paddleboards. Your crew facilitates water sports: sunrise paddleboarding in isolated bays, afternoon diving on Greek reefs, evening jet-ski excursions, or casual snorkeling off the transom. Many clients spend mornings exploring islands on foot (crew can arrange car rentals or guide service) and afternoons at sea, balancing activity with relaxation.

The Saronic and northern Cyclades offer excellent snorkeling; fish stocks are healthy, and visibility is typically 15–30 metres. Fishing is possible; your captain can coordinate fishing charters or provide tackle for casual fishing off the transom. Entertainment systems, satellite internet, and movie libraries ensure that rest days aboard are rarely boring.

Seasonal Charter Patterns and Weather

Motor yacht season in Athens runs April through October. Peak season (June–August) commands premium rates and offers reliable fair weather, albeit with summer crowds and meltemi winds. Shoulder seasons (May–June and September–October) offer excellent value, milder weather, and more intimate island atmospheres. Winter charters (November–March) are rare; most yachts are in maintenance or operate in warmer Mediterranean regions.

Motor yachts' speed and reliability make summer meltemi winds manageable; passages that would challenge sailing yachts are routine for motorsailers. Spring sailing is ideal for romance and relaxation; early autumn (September–October) combines warm seas, gentle breezes, and sophisticated clientele returning from summer holidays.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cost of a motor yacht charter from Athens?

Prices vary dramatically by boat size, season, and charter duration. A 50-foot crewed yacht typically runs €8,000–15,000 per week in shoulder season, rising 50–100% in peak summer. Mega-yachts (150–200 feet) command €30,000–80,000+ weekly.

Can motor yachts charter for shorter periods than a week?

Yes. Shorter charters (3–5 days) are possible, though premium rates apply (daily rates exceed weekly averages). can discuss short-notice or flash-sale opportunities during lower-demand periods.

Is it customary to tip the crew on a motor yacht?

Yes. A gratuity of 10–15% of the charter fee is customary and appreciated by professional crews. Tips may be pooled (crew divides equally) or individual, depending on charter agreement. will advise on tipping customs for your specific vessel.

Can motor yachts accommodate large groups (16+ guests)?

Absolutely. can arrange larger yachts (130–200 feet) or multiple yachts in flotilla formation for corporate groups, extended families, or special celebrations. Your charter team will coordinate timing, activities, and logistics across vessels.

What is the difference between a motor yacht and a gulet?

Motor yachts are modern, speed-focused, and luxury-appointed; gulets are traditionally-built, slow-paced, and rooted in maritime heritage. Motor yachts excel for ambitious itineraries and contemporary amenities; gulets suit clients seeking romance, slow travel, and timeless charm. Both offer crewed service and gourmet dining.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
