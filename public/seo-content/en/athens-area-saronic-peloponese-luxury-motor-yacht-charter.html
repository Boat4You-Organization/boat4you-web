Activities and Water Sports

Luxury yachts anchor in the Saronic's premier spots: Mandraki (Hydra), Vidi (Spetses), Chinitsa (Porto Cheli), and Kounoupitsa (Ermioni). Each anchorage offers distinct experiences. Tenders facilitate island exploration without docking; jet skis extend activity radius. Snorkeling and scuba diving at Minor Epidavros sunken city or Dokos wreck site access the Saronic's underwater archaeology. Yacht-organized activities include sunset cocktails on the flybridge, stargazing decks, and themed dinners (Greek night, Mediterranean tapas, Michelin-inspired tasting menus).

Many superyachts partner with local guides for Palamidhi fortress climbs, Nafplio old-town tours, and day excursions to Mycenae. For water-sports enthusiasts, towed wakeboards, water skis, and paddleboards are standard.

The Athens Riviera and Luxury Marina Bases

Flisvos Marina (southern Athens coast) is the superyacht hub, hosting 30+ vessels 20m+ in length. Pristine facilities, dedicated superyacht support, and waterfront restaurants cater to high-end clientele. Proximity to Athens airport (30 km) facilitates crew changes and guest transfers.

Alimos Marina handles some superyachts; larger vessels (~40m+) moor at dedicated superyacht berths with concierge services.

Piraeus (Mikrolimano and Zea): Historic ports with traditional waterfront tavernas and convenient ferry access to islands.

Seasonality and Climate for Superyacht Charter

May through October is peak season. June and September offer moderate 8–15 knot winds and perfect weather for leisurely cruising. July and August bring warmer seas (26°C), stronger Meltemi winds (15–25 knots), and crowded anchorages—ideal for those seeking adventure and don't mind company. April and November are shoulder seasons with occasional deals and fewer crowds; weather is less predictable.

Modern superyachts weather summer Meltemi with ease: powerful engines ensure schedule reliability, stabilizers minimize motion, and air-conditioning maintains comfort. Crews thrive in logistical challenges; crewed yachts are least vulnerable to unexpected wind shifts.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in a crewed superyacht charter price?

All prices include captain and crew salaries, fuel, mooring at premier bases, full provisioning, insurance, and water-sports equipment. Excluded: guest gratuities (10–15%), bar/wine upgrades beyond standard provisioning, and day excursions requiring paid entrance fees or guide services.

How far in advance should I book a luxury superyacht?

Premier vessels are often booked 6–12 months ahead, especially for July–August peak season. Last-minute bookings (4–8 weeks) may yield discounts on less-popular vessel types or dates. Spring and autumn offer more flexibility.

Can the crew customize menus for dietary preferences?

Absolutely. Professional chefs accommodate vegan, gluten-free, kosher, halal, and allergen-specific diets. Notify during pre-booking consultation; crew sources ingredients locally, minimizing compromises in quality or authenticity.

Are superyachts stable enough for elderly guests or those prone to seasickness?

Yes, powerful engines and marine stabilizers ensure minimal motion even in summer Meltemi. Saronic anchorages are relatively sheltered; crews position to maximize comfort. Medication (Dramamine, scopolamine patches) is available on request.

What happens if a guest or crew member becomes ill during a charter?

All superyachts carry first-aid kits and satellite communications for emergency consultation with shore-based medical professionals. Greece has excellent hospitals in Piraeus, Nafplio, and Poros; medical evacuation helicopters are available if necessary. Crew includes basic first-aid training.

Can a crewed superyacht accommodate religious or cultural requirements during the charter?

Yes. coordinates with crew well before departure to accommodate observances: Sabbath meal preparation, halal/kosher provisioning, prayer space designation, or dietary practices. Professional crews routinely manage diverse guest backgrounds and cultural practices. Advance notification ensures seamless integration of guest preferences with onboard routines.

What is the difference between bareboat superyachts and fully crewed vessels?

Bareboat superyachts (rare; typically 20–30m max) require licensed operator and demand full guest involvement in navigation, provisioning, and housekeeping. Fully crewed superyachts (35m+) provide complete hospitality: captain, chef, hostess, deckhand, and logistics management. Crewed is standard for Saronic superyacht charters, eliminating guest workload and enabling total relaxation.

Are superyacht charters subject to VAT or other taxes in Greece?

EU and Greek residents typically pay VAT (24% standard rate) on charter prices. Non-EU visitors may be eligible for VAT exemptions (refundable at airport departure) if requirements are met (non-resident status, purchase for export). Professional crew wages, fuel, provisions, and insurance may have different VAT treatments. clarifies VAT applicability and itemization during pre-booking consultation; final invoices detail all charges transparently.

Can a superyacht charter be customized for diving, fishing, or water sports expeditions?

Yes. Many superyachts carry dive equipment, fishing rods, towed wakeboards, and jet skis. Charter packages can emphasize diving (day trips to Dokos wreck or Minor Epidavros), deep-sea fishing (Greek waters support Mediterranean species), or water sports. Crew coordinates with local dive shops, fishing guides, and water-sports operators. Equipment costs (beyond onboard items) and guide fees are typically additional.

What communications options exist aboard a superyacht (satellite phone, internet, television)?

Modern superyachts carry satellite communications systems: Iridium/VSAT for phone, email, and emergency contact; some offer streaming capability (weather-dependent, limited bandwidth). Television service is limited to pre-loaded entertainment or satellite reception (variable at sea). Mobile phone coverage exists near islands and coasts but not reliably offshore. clarifies communication capabilities during booking; guests with urgent connectivity needs should discuss expectations.
