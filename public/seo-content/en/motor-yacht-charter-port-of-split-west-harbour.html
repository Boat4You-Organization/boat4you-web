Island Routes: Speed & Flexibility

Brač (15 nm, 2 hours motor cruising) offers Bol's iconic Zlatni Rat beach, fish-rich restaurants, and crystal-clear waters. Motor yachts arriving mid-morning beat sailing competitors to the best anchorages. A motor yacht departure from West Harbour at 08:00 finds you moored at Bol by lunchtime.

Hvar (30 nm, 4 hours) becomes a dinner destination. Depart West Harbour after breakfast, arrive Hvar Town by late afternoon, dine at harborfront establishments with local wine. The town's Venetian harbor and cosmopolitan scene suit motor yacht clientele. One-night or multi-night stays explore lavender plantations and mountain villages.

Vis (40 nm, 5–6 hours motor) showcases the Adriatic's most pristine anchorage: the Green Lagoon. Motor yachts reach Vis Town for late lunch, then motor to the lagoon's sheltered waters for sunset swimming. Komiža's fish restaurants serve pašticada and fresh buđola (the local silverfish) to crews returning from open-water passages.

Šolta and Drvenik islands (6–10 nm, 1–1.5 hours) offer day-trip flexibility: breakfast at West Harbour, snorkeling at Drvenik's pine-backed coves by mid-morning, return by evening. Motor yachts excel at rapid positioning for multiple daily experiences.

Seasons, Weather & Fuel Planning

Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer ideal conditions: calm mornings, afternoon Maestral to 12 knots, and manageable fuel consumption (lower wind = steady cruising rather than fighting gusts). Summer fuel costs rise with demand; winter sailing demands shorter days and rougher-water experience.

Charter scheduling around fuel prices is strategic. Winter rates (November–March) attract budget travelers; spring and autumn offer the best wind-fuel balance. July–August premium pricing reflects high demand.

How quiet are motor yachts compared to sailing vessels?

Modern diesel engines (common in charter fleets) produce 70–75 decibels at cruising RPM—roughly equivalent to highway traffic. Engine rooms are soundproofed; cabins are remarkably quiet at 8–10 knot cruising. Full-throttle (20+ knots) increases noise and fuel burn; most charters favor efficient cruising for comfort and economy.
