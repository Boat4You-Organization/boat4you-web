Saronic Gulf Sailing from Kallithea

From Marina Delta Kallithea, the Saronic Gulf's greatest hits are 12–25 nautical miles away. Typical outbound routes take you southeast toward Aegina (6–8 hours), east toward Poros (7–9 hours), or further to Hydra (10–12 hours). The journey out of Kallithea itself involves navigating the busy Athens Riviera shipping lanes—the marina's professional staff brief charterers on the safest, clearest passages.

Once beyond the marina approaches, the Saronic opens up: calm anchorages, clear waters, moderate winds, and Greek island charm. Summer thermal winds from the north rarely exceed 15 knots, making the Saronic one of Greece's most forgiving sailing regions—perfect for building confidence or teaching crew members to sail.

Getting There and Ground Logistics

Athens International Airport (Eleftherios Venizelos, ATH) is the main international gateway. From ATH, Marina Delta Kallithea is roughly 30–40 minutes by car (depending on traffic). Most Boat4You guests arrange airport transfers directly; we can recommend reliable shuttle services. Highway A6 and local roads (E94) make navigation straightforward. Parking is available at the marina for multi-day stays or guest vehicles.

Best Times to Charter at Marina Delta Kallithea

The Saronic enjoys a Mediterranean climate: warm, dry summers (June–September, 28–32°C air, 24–26°C water) and mild winters (October–May, 12–20°C). The prime sailing season runs May through October. July–August attract families and tourists; expect warmer water, longer daylight, but also more traffic. April–May and September–October offer ideal sailing conditions: comfortable temperatures, consistent winds, and fewer crowds on island anchorages.
