Sailing Routes & Destinations from Olbia

The classic week-long catamaran charter explores the Maddalena Islands (roughly 30–35 nautical miles), the dramatic granite peaks of Caprera, the pristine beaches of Budelli (pink sand), and the sheltered anchorages of Spargi and Santa Maria Maddalena islands. Morning sail times are typically 3–5 hours, leaving afternoons free for snorkeling, beach exploration, and water sports.

Southern itineraries from Olbia head toward the Costa Smeralda's exclusive Porto Cervo, anchor in Cala di Volpe, and explore hidden bays such as Liscia di Vacca. The winds here are remarkably consistent, allowing Boat4You captains to plan daily sailing that balances exhilaration with relaxation.

For longer charters, extended routes venture west toward the island of Asinara or south to the Tyrrhenian coast. Most sailing occurs in the 8–16 knot range, ideal for both experienced sailors and those seeking a gentler pace. Seasonal patterns favor April–May and September–October, when winds are steady and temperatures perfect (22–26°C).

Local Sardinian Cuisine & Culture on Your Charter

Olbia's gastronomic tradition centers on seafood fresh from the Tyrrhenian Sea. Bottarga (dried mullet roe) features prominently in pasta dishes, while malloreddus (Sardinian gnocchi) pairs with rich meat sauces. Aboard your catamaran, simple meals transform into celebrations when prepared with local ingredients sourced from Olbia's markets: San Pietro fish, Casu Marzu (if you're adventurous), local Cannonau wine, and pecorino sardo cheese.

The culture around Olbia blends Sardinian tradition with cosmopolitan European influence, reflecting centuries of maritime trade and modern tourism. Sailors here respect the sea, celebrate evening aperitivo time (6–7 PM), and understand that the best meals are shared at sunset, anchored in a protected bay.

Wind Patterns & Sailing Conditions

Olbia and the surrounding northern Sardinian coast experience the Maestrale wind (northwest), typically flowing May–September at 12–18 knots, occasionally gusting to 25 knots during afternoon hours. The Scirocco (southeast) brings warmer but less predictable conditions, while winter months see stronger systems. skippers monitor these patterns daily, adjusting routes to match conditions and guest comfort levels.

The Tyrrhenian and Ionian currents sweep past Sardinia's northern tip, creating excellent wind hold and moderate swell. Tidal ranges are minimal (under 1 meter), simplifying navigation. Night sailing is safe and rewarding, with full moonlight illuminating granite cliffs and starscapes free from terrestrial light pollution.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need sailing experience to charter a catamaran from Olbia?

No. All catamarans come with a professional skipper included. If you wish to actively sail, our captains provide hands-on instruction. Non-sailors simply relax while the crew handles navigation and propulsion.

What is the best time to sail from Olbia?

April–May and September–October offer ideal conditions: 18–22°C air temps, 16–19°C water temps, and steady 12–16 knot winds. July–August are busier (more crowded anchorages) and hotter. Winter offers solitude but stronger, less predictable winds.

Can I extend a charter beyond one week?

Yes. accommodates 10 and 14-day itineraries. Longer charters allow deeper exploration—perhaps sailing to the southern Tyrrhenian coast (Costa Verde, Carloforte) or the protected Arcipelago di Bocche (between Sardinia and Corsica).

Are meals and provisioning included?

Provisioning is not included but can be pre-arranged through our partners's concierge. We coordinate shopping, restaurant reservations at anchor-out venues, and can arrange for a private chef if you prefer gourmet dining aloft.

What happens if weather turns severe?

Our skippers monitor weather constantly. If a strong system approaches, we shelter in protected harbors (Olbia, Porto Rotondo, or Maddalena's Cala Gavetta) and adjust itineraries. Your safety and comfort are paramount.

What should I pack for a Sardinian catamaran charter?

Essentials include sun protection (high SPF sunscreen, hat, sunglasses), lightweight clothing (cotton and linen preferred in summer), a light jacket for evening breezes, comfortable deck shoes with non-slip soles, and a reusable water bottle. Bring personal medications, a minimal first-aid kit, and any dietary supplements. Most guests find reef shoes helpful for snorkeling; some prefer bringing personal snorkeling gear rather than relying on shared equipment.

Can I combine catamaran sailing with land-based activities?

Yes. Many multi-week Sardinia visits combine week-long catamaran charters with shore-based accommodation in Olbia, Portisco, or Costa Smeralda. can coordinate these hybrid itineraries, allowing sailing adventures alongside archaeological exploration, wine tastings, and cultural experiences in medieval villages accessible only by land.
