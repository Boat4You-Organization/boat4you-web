Sailing Conditions: Seasons & Wind Regimes

Maestral dominates May through September: steady 10-18 knots from the northwest, most reliable between 11 AM and 5 PM. Spring mornings (April–May) see flat glass before thermal wind builds; autumn evenings (September–October) often feature sudden 25-knot squalls requiring attentive sail handling. Winter Bura (November–March) delivers cold, dry 20-35 knot bursts from the northeast, occasionally stronger in narrows; these dramatic systems require experienced crews and proper reefing discipline.

Jugo (southeasterly) appears 2-3 times monthly, typically lasting 12-18 hours. It brings warm, humid conditions and wave generation that tests sailing yacht stability. Lulls between major wind systems allow for motoring or drifting—rare in the Adriatic but possible during August heat highs.
