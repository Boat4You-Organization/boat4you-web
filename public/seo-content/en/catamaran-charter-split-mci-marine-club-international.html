Island Routes from Central Split

Brač (15 nautical miles) offers accessible introduction sailing with protected harbors and beach culture. Šolta (10 nautical miles) provides quieter anchorages ideal for first nights. Hvar (30 nautical miles) brings legendary nightlife and lavender-covered hillsides. Vis (40 nautical miles) rewards longer passages with dramatic landscapes and authentic village life.

The Drvenik islands (20 nautical miles) suit day-sail crews seeking sandy beaches and minimal infrastructure. Multi-week itineraries loop the Adriatic, touching Montenegro or returning to Split via the Kornati archipelago.

Provisioning & Split Market Culture

Split's Peškarija (fish market) showcases daily catches of brancin (sea bass), orada (sea bream), and squid. The Green Market supplies fresh produce and local cheese. Many catamaran crews shop each evening, cook aboard, and enjoy communal dinners on deck—a Mediterranean ritual that grounds the sailing experience.

Wine selection emphasizes local Dalmatian reds (Plavac Mali, Crljenak) and whites (Posip). Restaurants cluster along the waterfront; many offer group discounts and accommodate dietary restrictions for visiting sailors.

Best Sailing Seasons from MCI

Spring (April–May) brings mild temperatures, building afternoon thermals, and manageable crowds. Summer (June–August) offers reliable westerly winds (maestral) ideal for catamarans but brings heat and congestion. Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (22–23°C), gentle sailing conditions, and September serenity.
