Catamaran Charter from Marina Trogir

Marina Trogir (historically known as SCT) sits on Čiovo island, directly opposite the medieval town of Trogir—a UNESCO World Heritage site. With 70 catamarans and deep-water berths, this full-service facility is one of the Adriatic's premier catamaran bases. Located approximately 25 km from Split Airport, Marina Trogir combines convenient access with comprehensive shore support for bareboat and skippered charters.

Marina Trogir: Full-Service Facilities & Deep-Water Access

Marina Trogir distinguishes itself with full-service infrastructure. Deep-water berths accommodate vessels up to 60 meters; a 30-ton travel lift enables haul-out and maintenance between charters. Fuel dock, potable water, shore power (16 and 32 amp), and professional reception operate daily 7 AM–8 PM. The workshop complex supports electronics, engine, and rigging repairs—crucial if you've chartered a vessel and encounter mid-voyage issues.

The marina sits on Čiovo's eastern shore, positioned so that Trogir's old town (just 200 meters across the bridge) is visible from your berth. Provisioning is straightforward: the Konzum supermarket and local market at Trogir's Riva offer fresh produce, local cheese, and fish. Walk-ashore time is minutes—you'll sleep aboard yet dine in town restaurants.

Trogir's Historic Appeal & Nearby Routes

Trogir itself merits a day ashore. The old town's Venetian architecture, cathedral, and bell tower crowd narrow streets. Restaurants like Kantun and Tragos blend traditional Dalmatian pašticada with seafood. Return to your catamaran at sunset and anchor in nearby coves on Čiovo's southern side for a quiet night.

Brač Island lies 18 nautical miles south. Bol's Zlatni Rat beach and the island's wine region are easily reached in a day sail. Hvar, 32 nm southwest, offers social mooring in Hvar Town and quieter anchorages at Jelsa on the north shore. Vis, 42 nm distant, remains an overnight passage but rewards with dramatic coves and excellent local restaurants.

Charter Turnaround & Crew Briefings

The marina excels at Saturday turnaround protocols. Crews arrive by 4 PM Thursday, attend a comprehensive 90-minute briefing covering catamaran handling, navigation aids, weather forecasting, and emergency procedures. Friday is a day-sail shakedown; Sunday evening, departing crews clear by 6 PM. This rhythm has perfected crew handovers and minimizes delays.

Boat4You coordinates all briefings with Marina Trogir staff, ensuring your crew receives updated weather bulletins and route advice tailored to your week's plans. Charts are modern, navigation systems are GPS-enabled, and flotilla support is available if you prefer guided sailing.

Pricing & Seasonal Rates

Catamaran weekly rates from Marina Trogir range from €2,600–€5,000 in shoulder seasons (April–May, September–October), rising to €4,200–€6,800 in peak July–August. Smaller 34-footers are less expensive; 42–46-footers command premium rates. Search Boat4You's catalog for real-time availability and pricing.

Fuel, mooring fees (€1.80–€2.20/meter/night), and provisioning are separate. Insurance and damage waiver are included. Off-season (November–March) charters are discounted 30–40% but require experienced crews familiar with winter Adriatic conditions.

Best Seasons & Wind Patterns

Spring (April–May) brings calm mornings and 10–15-knot afternoons; water temperature 17–18°C. Summer (June–August) offers reliable Mistral and Bura winds, 12–18 knots typical, and warm 23–25°C water. Autumn (September–October) is ideal: 16–20-knot winds, fewer charter fleets, and water still 21–22°C.

Trogir's enclosed harbor provides sheltered overnight mooring even in moderate blow. The nearby Drvenik islands offer shallow-draft anchorages if you seek protection during a Bura squall.

Local Provisioning & Dalmatian Cuisine

Trogir's market (Gradska Pijaca) operates Tuesday–Saturday mornings and stocks everything: fresh fish, local Plavac Mali red wines, Dalmatian prosciutto, and pungent Trogir cheese. Restaurants like Pasike (fish) and Konoba Kaleta (meat stews) cater to sailors. Wine enthusiasts should sample Trogir's own Vinarija Trogir—boutique producers operate just inland.

What's included in the crew briefing?

90-minute session covering catamaran systems, sail trim, weather assessment, anchor techniques, radio protocols, marina etiquette, and emergency procedures. A paper chart and digital chartplotter guide follows. Optional skippers provide additional confidence.

Are catamarans easier to sail than monohull yachts?

Yes. Catamarans have minimal heel, larger cockpits, and simpler rig configurations. Newer crews often find them forgiving compared to monohulls, though experience typically helps.

Can I rent a catamaran with a professional skipper?

Yes. Marina Trogir maintains a roster of licensed skippers available for bareboat charters. Check availability on Boat4You or contact the team directly for skipper rates.
