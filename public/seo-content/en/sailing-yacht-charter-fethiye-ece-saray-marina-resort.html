Iconic Sailing Routes from Ece Saray Marina Resort

Departing Ece Saray Marina Resort, your sailing yacht reaches Ölüdeniz Blue Lagoon (15 kilometres south) within hours of leaving the marina. The lagoon's protected anchorage offers perfect swimming, paddleboarding, and sunset viewing. Your crew prepares gourmet dinners using fresh ingredients, served either aboard your yacht under the stars or in beachfront tavernas if you prefer dining ashore.

Sailing further east reaches Butterfly Valley, where dramatic cliffs plunge into turquoise water. The narrow river gorge accessible by tender leads to natural pools and freshwater cascades. The endemic Jersey Tiger butterflies gather in summer—their striking orange and black patterns covering flowers create stunning natural beauty. Your sailing yacht's performance platform makes approaching shallow-water anchorages accessible to larger vessels impossible—pure advantage of canvas sailing.

Multi-day itineraries explore the Lycian coast's greatest treasures. Ancient cities Tlos and Xanthos feature remarkable rock tombs, Greek and Roman structures, and archaeological remains spanning two thousand years. Gemiler Island (Saint Nicholas Island) hosts Byzantine monastic ruins and exceptional snorkelling. Kayaköy ghost village—the abandoned Greek settlement—offers poignant history and outstanding photography. The meltemi typically provides excellent sailing conditions for reaching each destination efficiently, leaving ample time for exploration and leisure.

Seasonal Conditions and Optimal Sailing

The sailing season from Ece Saray runs June through September, with May and October offering excellent shoulder-season conditions. Summer months guarantee warm water (24–28 degrees Celsius) and stable meltemi winds ideal for sailing. Daytime temperatures reach 28–32 degrees Celsius; evenings cool to 18–22 degrees. The meltemi wind provides consistent, reliable sailing conditions—typically 12–20 knots—allowing predictable planning and excellent sailing performance.

Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer fewer crowds, warm sunshine, and slightly gentler wind conditions. Many experienced sailors prefer these periods for their ideal balance between sailing conditions, weather stability, and crowd levels. Winter sailing remains possible with appropriately equipped vessels and experienced crews; however, conditions become less predictable.
