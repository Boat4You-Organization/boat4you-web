Island Routes & Sailing Destinations

Ugljan, the larger of the two islands flanking Zadar, stretches 23 kilometres long and offers sheltered bays, pine-backed beaches, and the charming village of Preko. A typical sail takes 45 minutes from Borik. Olive groves and local restaurants serving fresh fish make Ugljan a quieter alternative to busier Hvar or Brač.

Pašman, directly across the channel from Ugljan, mirrors its neighbour in beauty but attracts fewer crowds. The village of Pašman Town and several anchorages in the Pašman Channel provide natural mooring points for sailing yachts. Many charter sailors spend two or three days exploring these twin islands, sailing between them and enjoying the consistent thermal winds of the Zadar region.

The Kornati National Park, 30 nautical miles south, represents the crown jewel for sailing yachts from Borik. This protected archipelago of uninhabited limestone islands, discovered by geology and loved by sailors, offers crystal-clear anchorages, dramatic rocky coastlines, and a sense of wilderness rare in the Mediterranean. A typical Kornati expedition takes 4–5 days; you'll sail through narrow straits, anchor in hidden coves, and experience the Adriatic largely undisturbed.

Shorter day-sail options include the northern islands near Nin (a historic salt-making town) and the quieter bays of the Zadar coastline—ideal for families or those seeking gentler sailing conditions.

Best Sailing Seasons & Weather

Spring (April–May) brings warm days, calm mornings, and water temperatures climbing to 18–19°C. Wind is typically light, favouring motor-sailing, but the crowds are minimal. Summer (June–August) brings reliable Maestral winds from the northwest, ideal for sailing yachts, though July–August sees higher temperatures and more charter activity.

Autumn (September–October) is widely considered the best season: water warm at 22–23°C, wind consistent but manageable, and fewer boats. Winter sailing is possible but demands respect for occasional Bura squalls sweeping down from the northeast.

Local Food & Dalmatian Culture

Zadar's culinary scene centres on fresh fish, octopus preparations, and traditional Dalmatian fare. The city market offers daily fresh produce, local cheese (especially Pag cheese from the island of Pag), and cured meats. Bakeries sell fresh bread daily, perfect for provisioning your sailing yacht before departure.

Zadar Old Town, minutes from Borik marina, hosts Roman ruins (the Forum), medieval churches, and the iconic Sea Organ—an installation producing haunting tones as waves pass through. Sunset walks along the Riva promenade and drinks at waterfront cafés are essential Zadar experiences.

FAQ: Sailing Yacht Charters from Borik

What's the typical yacht size for couples or small families?

A 32–36-foot sailing monohull with two or three cabins suits two to four people comfortably. D-Marin Borik's fleet includes several vessels in this range, ideal for intimate sailing adventures.

Do I need sailing experience to charter a monohull from Borik?

Yes. Bareboat sailing yacht charters require an International Certificate of Competence (ICC) or equivalent national sailing qualification. reviews your credentials during booking; if you lack formal certification, a professional skipper can be arranged. See how we work for details.

Can I reach the Kornati in one day from Borik?

Yes. Under sail, the Kornati National Park entrance lies roughly 30 nautical miles south. With a morning departure and favourable wind, you can reach the outer islands by early evening, perfect for overnight mooring.

What's the mooring cost at other Adriatic harbours?

Harbours near Zadar (Ugljan, Pašman) typically charge €20–€40 per night for a 30-foot yacht. The Kornati, being a national park, require permits (€10–€20 daily) and designated mooring balls rather than marina berths.

Is motor-sailing common from Borik?

Absolutely. Variable summer winds and the need to reach distant islands on schedule mean most charter sailors blend motor and sail. Modern sailing yachts carry reliable diesels, ensuring you make your connection even in flat calms.
