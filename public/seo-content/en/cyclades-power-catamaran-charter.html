Motor Catamarans for Island-Hopping Freedom

Power catamarans represent a niche but expanding segment of Cyclades charters. With only 9 vessels in our Boat4You fleet, they offer an alternative to sailing: speed, stability, and the luxury of schedules independent of wind conditions. Unlike monohulls that tack against the meltemi, power cats carve direct routes between islands, cutting journey times in half and delivering you to anchorages with minimal fuss.

The appeal is obvious for mixed-ability crews. No sail handling, no experience required. Modern motor catamarans feature twin diesel engines, planing hulls, spacious saloons, and fuel capacity to venture beyond typical day-trip limits. They excel at the Cyclades' shortest runs—Mykonos to Delos (5 nm, 20 minutes), Paros to Naxos (8 nm, 30 minutes), Mykonos to Tinos (10 nm, 40 minutes)—where pure motoring comfort beats wrestling with sails.

Speed & Efficiency Through the Islands

Power cats cruise at 8–12 knots, compared to monohulls' 5–7 knot average against the meltemi. A journey that takes a sailing yacht 10 hours becomes a 5-hour run. This advantage multiplies on island-hopping itineraries: you can anchor in three different bays in a single day, enjoy taverna lunch without time pressure, and still catch sunset cocktails at your final destination.

Fuel consumption is the trade-off. Twin engines and planing efficiency mean you'll budget for diesel more carefully than sailors, but modern power cats are engineered for reasonable burn rates (8–15 liters per hour at cruise). Boat4You specifies fuel range for each catamaran—typically 200+ nautical miles—easily spanning any Greek island combination.

Comfort & Luxury Amenities

Power catamarans in the Cyclades are premium platforms. Saloons are larger than monohull equivalents, with ample headroom and natural light. Galleys are equipped for serious cooking; heads are spacious. Wide side decks mean no climbing over rigging, and the broad beam creates exceptional motion comfort in choppy seas—a massive advantage over narrow monohull hulls during meltemi passages.

Many Boat4You power cats offer air conditioning, modern entertainment systems, and generator power to run laptops and phones indefinitely. Diving families often prefer motor cats because you can moor safely in shallower bays and deploy tenders without the chaos of active sails overhead.

Day Cruises & Sunset Charters

Power catamarans dominate the premium day-charter market from Mykonos and Santorini. A sunset cruise departs at 5 pm, motors to a secluded bay in 30 minutes, anchors for swimming and aperitifs, and returns by 8 pm—impossible under sail with the meltemi. Dive sites are easier to reach: Delos's submerged columns (Rheneia), the volcanic rocks near Anafi, and the Antiparos sea caves are all power-cat distance from major ports.

Extended cruises (4–7 days) benefit equally. You control timing, skip windward islands if conditions worsen, and navigate narrow passages (like the Donousa strait) without tacking anxiety.

Marinas & Anchorages

Power cats fit Mykonos (New Port), Santorini (Amoudi), and Paros harbors easily. Their shallow draft (1.3–1.8 m) grants access to small anchorages where monohulls draw too much water. Naxos's sandy bays, Milos's volcanic inlets, and Koufonisia's tight moorings welcome motor cats that monohulls cannot safely enter.

Boat4You power cats typically anchor in sand or mud—ideal holding for stable, reef-free drops. The twin engines provide excellent station-keeping with bow and stern anchors, a setup preferred by crews planning extended stays.

Couples seeking luxury and minimal effort also gravitate to power cats. You can cruise for seven days making no sail decisions, focusing purely on exploration, local food, and relaxation. Boat4You's smaller motor-cat fleet ensures personalized service and careful matching to your needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are power catamarans more stable than sailing yachts?

Yes, absolutely. The wider beam distributes weight better, and the lack of heel means no sliding across deck or cabin. In choppy meltemi seas, power cats roll gently; sailing monohulls heel 15–25°. Families and less-experienced cruisers appreciate this stability immensely.

How much does fuel cost?

Diesel in Greece costs roughly €1.10–1.30 per liter. A week's cruise burns 50–100 liters depending on speed and distance, totaling €55–130. Factor this into your budget.

Can we anchor safely, or do we need marinas?

Power cats anchor beautifully in sand and mud. The Cyclades are dotted with excellent natural anchorages: Antiparos's lagoon, Iraklia's sheltered bay, Koufonisia's twin harbors. Marina usage is optional for occasional weather protection or provisioning—most charters anchor 80% of the time.

What if we want to sail occasionally?

Some power catamarans come with auxiliary sails (steadying sails or jibs), but primary propulsion is typically engine-driven. If sailing performance is essential, choose a sailing catamaran (multihull monohulls) instead—though these are rarer in the Cyclades.

How many people can a power catamaran sleep?

Typical power cats sleep 6–10 people across 3–4 cabins, each with ensuite head. Confirm berth count in your listing—larger catamarans offer exceptional sleeping comfort compared to compact sailing yachts.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
