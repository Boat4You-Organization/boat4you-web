Orhaniye's Famous Sandbar and Exceptional Natural Beauty

Orhaniye's primary claim to fame is its walking-across-water sandbar—a seasonal natural phenomenon where underwater ridges create water so shallow and transparent that swimmers appear to walk on liquid turquoise. This remarkable sight has garnered international attention, yet the village itself remains unspoiled. From your catamaran anchored at Martı Marina, accessing this wonder takes minutes via tender. The experience—literally walking on crystalline water with transparent bottom visible beneath your feet—is utterly extraordinary.

The sandbar's appearance depends on season and tide. It's most pronounced April through October, most dramatic at low tide during calm, sunny conditions. Photography is remarkable; the images barely capture the reality. Snorkelling beneath and around the sandbar reveals abundant marine life—fish, corals, and sea urchins inhabiting the underwater ecosystem. The contrast between the shallow, warm water and deeper channels creates habitats supporting diverse life. ensures you visit during optimal conditions.

Beyond the sandbar, Orhaniye's waters are extraordinarily clear—visibility often exceeds 20 metres. The anchorages near the village feature forested hillsides descending to water's edge, creating beautiful backdrops and natural protection. Beaches are small and rarely crowded. Mountains in the distance form the Lycian coast's dramatic spine. Every view from your catamaran is exceptional. Sunset is particularly magical: mountains fade into silhouette; the water turns gold then purple; restaurant lights flicker to life along the waterfront. This natural beauty, combined with cultural authenticity, makes Orhaniye uniquely compelling.

The Meltemi Wind and Sailing Conditions

The Meltemi wind—a consistent northwesterly breeze dominant from May through September—shapes Turkish sailing rhythms. From Martı Marina, understanding Meltemi patterns is crucial for optimal sailing. The wind typically strengthens from mid-morning, peaks in afternoon (12–18 knots, occasionally stronger), then eases toward evening. This predictability allows purposeful planning: leisurely morning departures in light winds, energetic midday sailing, calm evening approaches. Catamarans handle these conditions beautifully; their stability makes fresh wind enjoyable rather than challenging.

Martı Marina's protected anchorage provides strategic advantage. Morning passages to wider sailing occur in light winds—navigating the bay entrance with attentive seamanship but without struggle. By midday, stronger Meltemi fills seaward-facing waters, providing excellent sailing conditions for longer passages. Evening returns occur in easing winds, allowing relaxed approaches back into the marina's shelter. This rhythm is remarkably civilised, allowing sailors of all abilities to enjoy meaningful sailing without excessive challenge or fatigue.

Seasonal variation is significant. Peak season (July–August) brings strong, consistent Meltemi—ideal for sailors enjoying spirited sailing. Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer gentler conditions with fewer crowds; weather is less predictable but often more interesting. Winter charters are possible but demand flexibility; rewards include dramatic natural beauty and solitude. helps select your season based on weather preferences and desired sailing intensity.
