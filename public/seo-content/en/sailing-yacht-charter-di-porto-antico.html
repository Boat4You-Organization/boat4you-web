Sailing Yacht Charter at Marina di Porto Antico, Genoa: Modern Marina in Genoa's Historic Heart

Marina di Porto Antico in Genoa represents the perfect fusion of history and contemporary maritime excellence. Located in the heart of Genoa's UNESCO-recognised Old Port—redesigned in the 1990s by renowned architect Renzo Piano—this marina offers state-of-the-art facilities, direct access to Genoa's attractions, and an ideal launch point for sailing throughout the Ligurian and Tuscan coasts. Boat4You regularly bases sailing yachts at Porto Antico, making it a premier choice for guests seeking convenience, comfort, and world-class infrastructure.

Marina di Porto Antico: Facilities and Infrastructure

Porto Antico is among Italy's most modern marine facilities. The marina accommodates yachts from 8 to 80 metres and features 520 berths equipped with 16-amp electricity, fresh water access at each berth, and Wi-Fi throughout the facility. The reception office operates 24/7, providing assistance with customs, immigration, provisioning, and any technical support your yacht may require.

Fuel services are available via dedicated fuel dock, with diesel and petrol on tap and credit-card payment accepted. The marina's pump-out station ensures environmental compliance, and waste disposal services (oil, batteries, rubbish, recycling) are comprehensive and eco-conscious. Professional security, surveillance cameras, and gated access create a safe, secure environment for your yacht.

Location and City Access

Unlike marinas on the periphery, Porto Antico sits directly in Genoa's city centre, just metres from the Aquarium of Genoa (Europe's largest), boutique shops, galleries, and award-winning restaurants. You can tie off your yacht, walk into the old medieval alleys (caruggi), and dine on Ligurian pesto pasta within ten minutes. This central location transforms your yacht into a floating hotel with the entire city as your backyard.

The marina is also the primary berth for Boat4You's Ligurian fleet, meaning guest yachts benefit from local ground support, provisioning partners, and experienced staff familiar with every detail of the facility. Handover and departure procedures are streamlined, allowing you to maximize time on the water rather than in logistical discussions.

Why Choose Porto Antico for Your Sailing Holiday

Porto Antico is more than a parking lot for yachts; it is a living, breathing extension of Genoa's culture. The marina district includes restaurants, aperitivo bars, and cultural events throughout the year. On summer evenings, the waterfront hosts outdoor film screenings, live music, and food festivals. Sailors staying at Porto Antico often spend their first evening exploring Renzo Piano's architectural masterpiece, wandering the revitalised waterfront, and soaking in the Mediterranean atmosphere.

From a practical standpoint, Porto Antico offers the easiest provisioning in Liguria. Fresh produce, seafood, wine, and local specialities are available within walking distance. The marina's provisioning concierge can pre-stock your yacht with groceries, ensuring you depart stocked and ready to explore.

Gateway to Ligurian and Tuscan Sailing Routes

Porto Antico serves as the springboard for endless sailing adventures. North along the coast lie the dramatic cliffs of Cinque Terre, Monterosso, and the exclusive village of Portofino—all reachable in day-sail distances of 3–6 hours. Directly south lies the Tuscan coast, with the Tuscan Archipelago and islands (Elba, Capraia, Giglio) typically 8–14 hours sailing. For longer passages, Corsica, the French Côte d'Azur, and Spain are all within reach for week-long or extended charters.

The wind patterns in summer—reliable northwesterly Mistral flows—provide steady sailing conditions from Porto Antico toward the south and southeast, making Tuscany and the archipelago particularly appealing for a week-long adventure starting from Genoa.

Arrival and Departure Information

Porto Antico is easily reached via Cristoforo Colombo Airport (6 km), which offers flights to major European cities. A taxi or car rental takes 15–20 minutes. The marina is also accessible by train; Genoa's main railway station (Stazione Centrale Brignole) is 3 km away, with shuttle bus and taxi services available.

For those driving, parking is available at Porto Antico itself or in surrounding garages, though Boat4You recommends arranging valet parking or car storage through the marina office to simplify your departure day. Upon arrival, check-in at the marina reception; Boat4You's local representative will be present for yacht orientation, safety briefing, and departure briefing.

Best Times to Charter at Porto Antico

Porto Antico operates year-round, but the sailing season peaks from May through September when temperatures are warm (25–28°C) and winds are reliable. April and October offer excellent spring and autumn conditions with fewer crowds and lower prices. Winter sailing is possible, though less predictable; storms can complicate departures, and some charter guests prefer warmer months.

For business travellers or those combining sailing with city exploration, spring (April–May) and early autumn (September–October) are ideal—the city is vibrant, weather is perfect, and the pace feels less frenzied than peak summer.

Dining and Entertainment Near Your Yacht

Genoa's food scene is world-renowned. Within steps of your berth, you'll find casual pizzerias, fine-dining establishments with Michelin stars, traditional trattorie serving fresh fish, and casual aperitivo bars. Pesto, focaccia, fresh pasta with local seafood, and Ligurian wines (particularly Vermentino and Pigato) are staples. Many restaurants offer outdoor seating with views of Porto Antico's waterfront.

Beyond dining, Genoa offers museums (the Palazzo Ducale, the Maritime Museum), galleries, live music venues, and cultural events. Summer festivals bring theatre and classical concerts to the waterfront, creating an enchanting backdrop to your sailing holiday.
