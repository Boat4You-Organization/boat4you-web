Technical considerations

Sailing yachts at ACI Marina Pomer handle the region's conditions well when properly prepared. Check reefing points, engine hours, and safety gear during the check-in briefing — marinas in Croatia run careful handover procedures and the briefing is the right moment to raise questions.

Anchoring and berthing

Anchor tackle on charter sailing yachts is generally sized adequately for Croatia's typical bay depths (6–15 metres). Med-mooring stern-to is standard in marinas; the briefing at ACI Marina Pomer covers the local conventions.

Provisioning and logistics

ACI Marina Pomer sits within reasonable range of supermarkets and fresh markets. Plan a provisioning run for the first evening rather than day zero — the fleet turnaround day is not the moment to load the galley.

Sample 7-day itinerary from ACI Marina Pomer

Day 1 — Departure and Medulin

Embark after midday, provision, and sail or motor the first short leg to Medulin. Evening dinner aboard or ashore at a family-run restaurant.

Day 2 — Unije

Morning departure, mid-day anchor stop for swimming, and evening mooring at Unije. Explore the old town on foot.

Day 3 — Susak

A longer passage today — roughly 20–30 nautical miles depending on wind and conditions. Arrive mid-afternoon, drop anchor in a protected bay, and enjoy an early swim.

Day 4 — the Brijuni islands

Short hop followed by a long lunch at anchor. Dinner at a local taverna if the harbour offers good options.

Day 5 — Return leg

Begin the return voyage with a lunch stop at a favourite anchorage. Aim for a marina or well-protected bay within a short distance of base.

Day 6 — Final exploration

Use the final full day for a highlight the group missed — a wine tour ashore, a hike to a viewpoint, or a long swim stop.

Day 7 — Return to base

Short morning passage, fuel top-up, clean-up, and formal disembarkation before noon.
