Island-Hopping Routes from Kos Marina

The Dodecanese cluster offers endless cruising possibilities. From Kos, you can reach Rhodes (60 nm south), Symi (50 nm), Patmos (45 nm), and Leros (35 nm)—each with distinct character and protected anchorages. The catamaran's shallow draft and beam allow you to anchor in quiet bays unreachable by monohulls.

Summer Meltemi winds (N 10-15 kts in the southern Dodecanese) provide steady sailing without extreme conditions. The network of islands creates natural waypoints, with harbor villages, taverns, and historical sites at nearly every stop.

Bareboat vs. Skippered Catamaran Charters

Bareboat charters suit confident sailors who wish to command their own vessel. Kos Marina welcomes bareboat clients with briefings, local charts, and 24/7 support from Boat4You.

Skippered charters pair your group with an experienced captain and crew. They navigate, manage logistics, and share insider knowledge of secret anchorages and beach taverns. Ideal for mixed-experience crews or those wanting a stress-free holiday.

Summer Conditions & Sailing Season

The Dodecanese enjoys a long sailing season from April through October. July-August brings peak Meltemi winds (12-20 kts), creating ideal sailing with manageable sea states. June and September offer calmer conditions and fewer tourists.

Water temperatures peak at 27°C in August; spring and autumn averages range from 19-23°C. Pack light layers and UV protection regardless of season.

Cross-Border Adventures: Kos to Bodrum

Kos lies just 4 nm from the Turkish coast. Bodrum, a historic harbor town with vibrant nightlife and Crusader castle, is a popular day trip or overnight detour. International waters between Greece and Turkey are straightforward; ensure your documents are in order and check current border regulations.

Many catamaran charters combine Dodecanese and Turkish Riviera itineraries for added cultural richness.

Dining & Nightlife on Kos Island

Kos town's harbor promenade is lined with tavernas serving grilled fish, lamb, and authentic Greek salads. Local wines and ouzo are staples. The old town, a short walk from the marina, blends Ottoman architecture with Venetian fortifications and palm-shaded squares.

Beach bars and clubs cater to younger travelers, while quieter villages inland offer traditional hospitality and farm-to-table dining.

FAQs

Do I need a sailing license to charter a catamaran from Kos Marina?

Bareboat charters require proof of sailing competence (IYT or equivalent certificate). Skippered and crewed charters have no such requirement. can advise on specific credentials needed.

What is included in a catamaran charter?

Mooring fees, fuel, insurance, safety equipment, tender, and snorkeling gear are typically included. Skippered charters add crew costs. Provisioning, fuel top-ups, and extras are client-paid.

Can we visit Turkey from Kos Marina?

Yes, Bodrum is a short sail. Ensure passports, visas, and vessel papers are current. Check bilateral regulations before departing Greek waters.

What is the catamaran advantage in the Dodecanese?

Twin hulls offer stability, spacious living, and shallow draft for anchorages. Families and less-experienced crews prefer catamarans over monohulls for comfort and ease of handling.

Are children welcome on catamaran charters?

Absolutely. Catamarans are ideal for families. Calm anchorages, tender access to beaches, and spacious cabins make multi-generational trips enjoyable.
