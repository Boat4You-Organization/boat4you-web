Trade Wind Sailing Routes from Radazul

The Canary Islands sailing zone benefits from the Azores High-pressure system, which drives the northeast trade winds most reliably from October through April. These winds create natural sailing corridors connecting Tenerife, Gran Canaria, La Gomera, La Palma, and El Hierro in a logical sequence.

A classic week-long itinerary departs Radazul, sails westward to La Gomera's Valle Gran Rey—a dramatic anchorage beneath towering 1,200-meter cliffs—then continues to the pristine waters between La Gomera and Tenerife. On the return passage, you'll round Tenerife's northern tip, enjoying close-up views of Garachico's volcanic formations and historic harbor town architecture.

For experienced sailors, the eastern passage to Gran Canaria and Las Palmas traverses the main sailing route for the Atlantic Rally for Cruisers (ARC). This passage offers exposure to offshore conditions while remaining within reasonable reach of protected harbors. Las Palmas, hosting the ARC's starting line each November, offers excellent provisions, repair facilities, and sailing community networking.

Longer two-week charters can venture south toward the Maspalomas dunes and pristine anchorages near Gran Canaria's southern coast, where Playa del Inglés provides beach access and Playa de las Américas offers dining and social opportunities for crew breaks.

Navigation and Seamanship in the Atlantic

Sailing from Radazul demands competent coastal navigation and offshore seamanship skills. The Canary Islands region experiences significant Atlantic swells, particularly on the north and west coasts where 2-4 meter swells are common even in calm wind conditions.

Modern GPS and electronic charting systems have simplified navigation considerably, yet understanding traditional chartwork, current flow, and visual piloting remains valuable when approaching remote anchorages. Boat4You provides detailed navigation briefings, current charts, and emergency communication protocols for every charter.

Seasonality, Weather, and Sailing Conditions

October through May represents the prime sailing season. Trade winds blow steadily from the northeast, typically 10-18 knots, with higher velocities (15-25 knots) during winter months. These conditions suit experienced sailors comfortable with rolling passages and overnight watches.

December through February brings the strongest winds and largest Atlantic swells. Passages can be vigorous but exhilarating, particularly on the open-water run from Tenerife to Gran Canaria or westward to La Gomera. Visibility remains excellent; water temperatures range 16-18°C, requiring wetsuits for extended swimming.

Spring (March-April) brings slightly lighter winds but remain favorable. Summer months (June-September) experience much lighter trade winds, occasional calms, and warmer water (22-24°C), suitable for sailors prioritizing comfort and leisurely progression over challenging seamanship.

Canary Islands Anchoring and Mooring Guide

Radazul's departure position grants rapid access to the Canaries' finest anchorages. Valle Gran Rey (La Gomera) shelters even in moderate Atlantic swells due to high surrounding cliffs. Garachico's historic harbor offers rolly but secure overnight holding in sandy bottom and historical atmosphere.

Southern anchorages include Maspalomas (shallow, sandy, excellent for water sports), Playa del Inglés (reef-protected, busy but safe), and numerous unnamed coves between Tenerife and Gran Canaria featuring pristine white sand and minimal human impact. Many remote anchorages remain unreported in cruising guides, discovered through local knowledge or other sailors' recommendations.

Overnight mooring in protected harbors typically costs €15-30 per night, including water and basic amenities. Anchoring in designated free anchorages around the islands provides cost-free overnight stops in scenic locations.
