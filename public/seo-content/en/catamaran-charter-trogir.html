Catamaran Charter in Trogir: Spacious Island Cruising in Dalmatia

Why Choose a Catamaran for Your Trogir Charter

A catamaran offers unmatched stability and interior space—qualities that define the Trogir charter experience. Unlike monohull sailing yachts, catamarans provide shallow draft, rapid anchoring, and cabins that maximize headroom and light. From the charter fleet, catamarans range from 35 feet (family-friendly cruisers) to 50+ feet (luxury cruising platforms), with price points from €2,500–€8,000 per week depending on season and specifications.

Trogir is ideally positioned as a catamaran base. The three marinas—ACI Marina Trogir, Marina Trogir (ex SCT), and Trogir Yachtclub Seget—handle multihulls routinely, and the Split Airport connection (just 5 km away) makes logistics seamless. The shallow waters around Šolta, Brač, and Drvenik Veli are forgiving for catamaran keels, while the UNESCO old town serves as a cultural anchor after days on the water.

Stability and Comfort at Anchor

Catamarans' twin hulls eliminate roll, critical when anchoring off Trogir's quieter coves. Morning coffee on the trampoline while watching Kamerlengo Fortress backlit by sunrise—this is catamaran life.

Easier Docking and Mooring

Many charter catamarans feature bow thrusters and shallow draft, reducing maneuver stress in Trogir's compact marina basins and narrow Dalmatian harbors.

Routes and Destinations from Trogir

Trogir functions as a natural hub for Dalmatian island exploration. A typical week divides between Adriatic passages and protected anchorages.

Šolta Island Loop (8 nm)

Northeast of Trogir, Šolta offers Maslinica harbor for lunch, plus olive groves and quiet anchorages. Boat4You charters often pair Šolta with a Brač crossing for a two-day micro-circuit.

Brač and Hvar Circuit (12–18 nm)

The classic Dalmatian triangle: Trogir to Bol (Brač), famous for Zlatni Rat beach; then west to Hvar Town, where marble-clad harbor and nightlife contrast sharply with village charm. Catamarans anchor comfortably off both, and multihull stability matters when Jugo wind picks up.

Vis and Drvenik Loop (22–28 nm)

For week-long adventures, Vis—the westernmost inhabited island—justifies the passage. Drvenik Veli sits between Trogir and Vis, offering provisioning and protected anchorages. Budget two overnight legs and plan around wind forecasts.

Čiovo Island Escape (2 nm)

Connected to Trogir by bridge, Čiovo's eastern shore hosts restaurants and beaches accessible via dinghy. Ideal for families seeking minimal overnight passages.

Technical Specifications for Catamaran Charters

Trogir-based catamarans typically span 35–50 feet, with diesel engines, modern navigation, and air-conditioned cabins. Most carry 4–8 guests across 2–4 cabins. Engine specs average 2 × 75 hp, providing 8–10 knot cruising and fuel economy valuable on longer Adriatic crossings.

Shallow Draft Advantage

Catamaran draft ranges from 1.1 to 1.5 meters, permitting access to Trogir's eastern shallows and Drvenik anchorages where deeper monohulls ground.

Fuel and Range

Twin engines consume roughly 15–20 liters per hour combined at cruising RPM. With 500-liter fuel tanks, most Boat4You catamarans achieve 200+ nautical mile range—sufficient for Trogir to Vis and back with refueling at Brač or Hvar.

Pricing and Seasonal Rates

Catamaran charters from Trogir follow Mediterranean seasonality. High season (July–August) commands €6,000–€8,000 per week for 35-foot family boats; shoulder season (May–June, September) ranges €3,500–€5,000; winter lows (November–February) drop to €2,000–€3,200 but require skipper experience and willingness to motor.

All-inclusive Boat4You packages typically bundle mooring fees (€50–80/night), skipper option (€150–200/day), provisioning assistance, and pre-arrival briefings. Fuel, provisioning, and optional crew are separate.

Mid-Season Sweet Spot

May and September offer ideal conditions: warm water (18–20°C), steady Maestral winds, fewer crowds, and 30–40% rate reductions versus peak summer.

Best Season for Catamaran Sailing from Trogir

May through October represents the viable window. July and August guarantee sunshine and calm anchorages but attract flotillas and raise prices. June offers excellent weather with lighter tourism. September remains warm, wind remains predictable, and rates fall. April and October suit experienced skippers; November–March demand sheltered harbors and coastal piloting.

Wind patterns favor Trogir: northwesterly Maestral (April–October, 5–12 knots) provides reliable sailing, while occasional Jugo (southeasterly) brings rougher seas but swift westbound passages.

Provisioning and Local Logistics

Trogir's mainland side hosts a large fresh market daily. The Konzum supermarket chain stocks provisions. For gourmet supplies, ACI Marina's chandleries source Croatian wines—Kaštelanski crljenak is a local must. Trogir's restaurants (waterfront Riva district) serve Dalmatian peka (slow-roasted meat and vegetables) and grilled fish.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a catamaran easier to sail than a monohull?

Not inherently simpler, but different. Catamarans forgive sloppy anchoring, don't heel dramatically, and offer interior comfort. They require more awareness of shallow draft limitations and wind effects on the twin-hulled profile. Most Boat4You crews find catamarans rewarding after one passage.

Can I bareboat charter a catamaran without experience?

Croatian law requires valid sailing certification (e.g., ICC, Day Skipper) for bareboat. For guests without credentials, Boat4You's skipper service removes liability while preserving hands-on crew participation. Cost is €150–200 per day.

What's the typical fuel cost for a week?

Engine-based cruising from Trogir (mixing motoring and sailing) typically costs €300–500 in fuel weekly, depending on passage intensity and engine load. Sailing-heavy itineraries reduce fuel by 40–50%.

Are there family-friendly anchorages near Trogir?

Absolutely. Čiovo's beaches, Šolta's Maslinica harbor, and Drvenik Veli all suit families. Depths range 4–8 meters in typical spots, holding is good, and dinghy access is straightforward.

How far in advance should I book a catamaran?

High season (July–August) books 4–6 months ahead. Shoulder season (May–June, September) typically secures charters 6–10 weeks out. Winter and early spring offer late availability but require flexibility.
