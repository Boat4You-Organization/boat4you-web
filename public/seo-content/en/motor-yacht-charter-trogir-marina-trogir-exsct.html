Motor Yacht Charter from Marina Trogir (ex.SCT) in Trogir

Marina Trogir offers motor-yacht charter with a rare combination: full-service infrastructure integrated directly into an authentic Mediterranean town. The small marina (roughly 150-200 berths, formerly Split City Tourist) sits on Trogir's western edge, its fuel dock and crane serving charter yachts departing into the Adriatic while the UNESCO Old Town stands five minutes by foot from your mooring. This is not isolation on an industrial waterfront; it's harbor-based luxury with town life surrounding you.

Motor yachts from Marina Trogir reach the same Šolta-Brač-Hvar circuit as larger marinas, but the departure experience differs fundamentally. You're leaving from a genuine Mediterranean harbor where locals fish, tourists walk medieval lanes, and dinner remains a social ritual. The motor yacht provides reliable schedule freedom; Marina Trogir provides authentic atmosphere. Together, they define a week-long experience rather than a logistics challenge.

Marina Facilities and Town Access

Marina Trogir operates with essential services: fuel dock (daylight and early evening hours, typically 8 a.m.–6 p.m.; confirm exact times), crane for emergency haul-outs, and on-site reception staffed by year-round residents who understand local conditions intimately. The sheltered basin provides reliable protection regardless of wind direction. The western exposure (toward open sea) allows afternoon Maestral to enter, but the town's buildings and natural breakwater provide reasonable protection against heavy weather.

Walking distance to the UNESCO Old Town is the defining asset. Five minutes on foot connect the marina to the cathedral square (13th century, restored after WWII), narrow cobblestone lanes spiraling outward, Konzum supermarket, and the farmers market (Wednesday and Saturday mornings, offering seasonal vegetables, local cheese, Plavac Mali and Pošip wines, and fresh fish from dock suppliers). Konoba Kapetanović, directly opposite the marina entrance, serves Dalmatian pašticada and brodet nightly to a mix of locals and boat crews.

The marina's small size means fewer mega-yacht complications and more personalized service. Reception staff know each boat and crew, anticipate questions, and arrange provisions or fuel with minimal bureaucracy. This personal touch, standard at independent marinas, disappears at larger charter operations focused on volume over experience.

Why Motor Yacht from Marina Trogir Works

Motor yachts eliminate wind-forecast anxiety. Want to reach Hvar Town by dinner? Your motor yacht departs Marina Trogir at 10 a.m., cruises 25 nautical miles in 5-6 hours at 9-10 knots (regardless of wind direction), and moors in Hvar harbor by late afternoon. This reliability suits groups on fixed schedules, families with young children, and crews who prioritize relaxation over sailing drama.

Marina Trogir's narrow channel entrance (80-100 meters wide, 2.5-3 meters deep) requires precise maneuvering. Motor yachts' dual engines enable independent control of each propeller, permitting short-radius turning and smooth docking in tight spaces. The shallow-draft hulls (1.2-1.8 meters) permit safe navigation through channels where deeper boats cannot venture without grounding risk.

The Šolta-Brač-Hvar circuit's attraction lies in the anchorages themselves, not the passages connecting them. Motor yachts let you reach each island on schedule, maximize time at each stop (swimming, snorkeling, dining ashore, exploring), and arrive at the next harbor without fatigue from navigating or sail changes. This priority perfectly matches what groups seek from Mediterranean charter: experience the islands, not the transit.

Technical Advantages: Motor Yacht Design

Modern motor yachts designed for the Adriatic feature twin diesel engines offering redundancy—if one requires maintenance, the other keeps the yacht mobile. Twin propellers enable precision docking and short-radius turning impossible for single-prop vessels. Semi-displacement hulls balance fuel efficiency with comfortable cruising speeds. At 9-10 knots, a 45-foot motor yacht consumes roughly 25-35 liters per hour—reasonable for the range and comfort offered.

Air-conditioned cabins (standard on 40+ footers) transform summer cruising. When July temperatures exceed 30°C on deck, retreating to climate-controlled cabin space becomes essential for comfort and fatigue management. Full-size galleys with gas stoves and electric ovens let your crew prepare multi-course meals. Separate staterooms (typically 2-4 cabins on 40-50 foot yachts) provide privacy and sleeping space without the galley-bed compromises that sailing yachts demand.

Shallow-draft design (1.2-1.8 meters) permits anchoring in 2.5-3 meter water safely, accessing pristine coves and remote islands. The hull shape also reduces excessive heeling when turning, making the motion smooth and comfortable for passengers who experience motion sickness.

Pricing and Value Proposition

Motor yacht charter from Marina Trogir typically runs 500–1000 euros per day for 40-45 foot yachts, with larger vessels (50-60 feet) reaching 1200–1800 euros daily. These rates are typically 10-20% lower than Baotić due to Marina Trogir's smaller operation and absence of mega-marina pricing premiums. June and September (shoulder season) sit toward the lower end; July-August peak season reaches the upper range.

Fuel is separate and charged per consumption. Budget 25-35 liters per hour at cruising speed; a week-long charter burning 25 liters/hour at 8-10 daily cruise hours = roughly 2000-2500 liters per week. Provisioning at Trogir market (Wednesday and Saturday) costs 40-50% less than packaged supermarket goods. Paid mooring in main harbors: 10-15 euros per night. Skippered charter (operator provides licensed captain) adds 250-400 euros daily but removes navigation responsibility.

Marina Trogir's value is authenticity combined with reasonable rates. You're not paying mega-marina premiums; you're gaining direct access to Old Town dining, local provisioning, and a harbor where genuine Mediterranean harbor life, not charter tourism, defines the pace.

Best Season for Motor Yacht Charter

June and September offer ideal conditions: temperatures 22-28°C (comfortable without excessive heat), Maestral wind predictable (8-12 knots, adding visual appeal without threatening comfort), water temps 22-24°C (swimmable, pleasant), and harbor crowding manageable. July-August bring warmer water (26-27°C) but crowded anchorages (Hvar Town fills with 200+ boats by late July) and afternoon Maestral gusts (15-18 knots, adding boat motion and noise).

May and October are survivable but unpredictable. Spring can feature sudden cold fronts; autumn brings scattered rain. Water temps drop to 18-20°C (cold for extended swimming). Winter (November-March) brings Bura and Scirocco gales; most charters are unavailable. Book peak-season motor yachts 8-10 weeks ahead for best selection and rates. Shoulder months typically need 4-6 weeks.

Local Flavor and Adriatic Dining Culture

Trogir's UNESCO Old Town surrounds your motor-yacht charter with 13 centuries of Mediterranean culture. The cathedral square (Trg sv. Ivana Evanđelista) sits at town center; narrow lanes spiral outward through residential quarters where daily life—market shopping, afternoon drinks on stone steps, evening strolls—continues unchanged by tourism. Your motor yacht serves as home base for exploring this living cultural landscape, not as transport between tourist destinations.

Dalmatian cuisine centers on fresh Mediterranean seafood and slow-cooked meat tradition. Peka (slow-cooked under ceramic dome, often octopus with potatoes and white wine) and pašticada (beef braised in red wine with prunes) define regional meals. Brodet, a Mediterranean fish stew, appears on nearly every konoba menu; Trogir's version traditionally includes red mullet (barbunja) and scorpion fish (skorpina), simmered in white wine and finished with fresh herbs.

Plavac Mali wine (deep red, from nearby vineyards) and Pošip wine (light white, from the Neretva valley south of Trogir) pair naturally with seafood. Both cost 8-12 euros per bottle at Konzum and offer exceptional value. Wednesday and Saturday farmers markets sell seasonal vegetables, aged sheep's milk cheese, cured meats, and fresh fish from dock suppliers. Buy at the market; cook aboard your motor yacht; enjoy meals exceeding restaurant quality at half the cost.

Practical Information and Logistics

Split Airport (25 kilometers north) is 12-15 minutes by car from Marina Trogir. Confirm airport transfer when booking. Alternative: buses from Split town center to Trogir (30 minutes, €6-8), then walk or taxi to Marina Trogir (€8-12, roughly 3 kilometers). EU citizens need passport or ID card; non-EU nationals must confirm Croatian visa requirements before travel.

For bareboat motor-yacht charter, Croatia requires valid boating certification (International Certificate of Competency or equivalent) demonstrating navigation and boat-handling competence. If your crew lacks certification, hire a professional skipper to command the yacht while your group focuses on leisure. Marina Trogir staff can recommend trusted skippers.

Water supply is abundant at the marina (minimal limitation aboard). Diesel fuel available during operating hours. Provisioning for dietary restrictions requires advance planning—Trogir market has limited specialty items but ample fresh produce and fish. Pets welcome with advance marina notification; however, some harbors and Croatian islands restrict animals—confirm your itinerary when booking.

FAQ: Motor Yacht Charter from Marina Trogir

Do I need a boating license to charter a motor yacht from Marina Trogir?

Croatia legally requires an International Certificate of Competency (or equivalent) for bareboat charter of any powered vessel exceeding 24 meters or 9.9 kilowatts engine power. Most modern motor yachts meet these thresholds. If your crew lacks certification, hire a professional skipper to command the vessel while you focus on relaxation and swimming.

What is included in the motor yacht charter price, and what is separate?

Typically included: the yacht, basic insurance (damage waiver), linens, navigational equipment. Separate: fuel (budget 25-35 liters/hour at cruising speed), paid harbor mooring (Hvar, Vis: 10-15 euros/night), provisioning (100-150 euros/day for four people), and crew gratuities. Early booking discounts (10-15%) typically apply for 8-10 weeks lead time.

Can I visit Trogir Old Town directly from my motor yacht at Marina Trogir?

Yes, absolutely. Five-minute walk from the marina's mooring field connects you to the cathedral square, narrow medieval lanes, and harborside restaurants. This direct access to authentic town life is Marina Trogir's unique advantage over isolated charter bases.

What is the difference between skippered and bareboat motor yacht charter?

Bareboat: you (or a certified crew member) navigate and dock the yacht. Skippered: a licensed professional captain commands the boat, handles navigation and docking, delivers local knowledge. Skippered adds 250-400 euros daily but removes navigation responsibility entirely. Ideal for groups without navigation experience or those prioritizing pure leisure.

What happens if the motor yacht engine needs repairs during my charter?

Marina Trogir has on-site reception and limited technical support. For major repairs, Split (30 kilometers north) has full shipyard facilities. Minor issues (fuel filter, belt, electrical) are often fixed quickly on-site. Your charter insurance covers mechanical breakdown—contact reception immediately if problems develop. The marina can arrange emergency parts or technician dispatch.

Is the fuel dock at Marina Trogir open 24 hours?

No. Marina Trogir's fuel dock operates daylight and early evening (typically 8 a.m.–6 p.m., confirm exact hours when booking). Plan fuel stops during these windows. For emergency after-hours fuel, Yachtclub Seget Marina Baotić (west side of the bay) offers 24-hour dock; the distance is short enough for minimal diversion.
