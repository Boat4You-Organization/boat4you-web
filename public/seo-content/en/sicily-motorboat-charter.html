FAQ

What experience level is required?

Bareboat charters require RYA Day Skipper or equivalent certification demonstrating navigation and seamanship competency. Beginners hire professional skippers (€150–250 daily) managing sailing operations. All-inclusive crewed charters eliminate experience requirements entirely—professional crew manages everything.

What is included in charter pricing?

Standard pricing covers vessel, crew salaries, fuel, meals, beverages, snorkeling equipment, and dinghy excursions. Premium wine, specialized activities (helicopter tours, ROV expeditions), and enhanced services incur additional fees detailed in itemized quotes provided before booking confirmation.

How flexible are itinerary changes?

Professional crews adjust itineraries based on weather, guest interests, and local conditions offering flexibility impossible on rigid cruise ship schedules. Last-minute destination changes, extended stays in beloved locations, and spontaneous cultural discoveries characterize the charter experience advantage.

What are typical weather conditions?

May–October charter season experiences minimal severe weather. Occasional autumn Sirocco winds (20–25 knots) prompt strategic anchorage repositioning but rarely prevent sailing. Professional captains prioritize guest comfort and safety maintaining protective harbor positioning when conditions warrant conservative approaches.

How far in advance should charters be booked?

Peak July–August demand requires 12–18 months advance booking due to limited premium inventory availability. June and September accommodate 8–12 week lead times. Shoulder months (May, October) permit flexible 6–8 week scheduling. Occasional last-minute cancellations surface through broker networks offering substantial discounted rates.

Snorkeling and Water Activities from Motorboats

Modern charter motorboats include enclosed snorkeling platforms with integrated rinse showers, drying racks, and organized equipment storage. Egadi snorkeling excursions visit multiple submerged reefs, white-sand lagoons, and protected coves ensuring 8–12 meter visibility and abundant Mediterranean fish. Sea turtles frequent summer waters; responsible operators maintain safe distance, prioritizing marine life protection. Paddleboarding from motorboat swim platforms enables exploration of shallow lagoons and beach access points unreachable by larger vessels.

Underwater photography (Go-Pro, underwater cameras) captures Mediterranean marine ecosystems—colorful parrotfish, sea urchins, octopuses, seagrass meadows—creating lasting visual memories. Snorkeling instruction (free orientation for beginners, €30–50 private lessons) builds confidence in younger guests or inexperienced swimmers. Professional crews maintain safety protocols: buddy systems, time limits, depth restrictions, and immediate response procedures ensuring guest well-being throughout water-based activities.

What is the typical motorboat group size?

Charter motorboats (20–35 ft) accommodate 6–12 guests depending on vessel size and configuration. Ideal group size balances social cohesion with intimate experience: 8 guests optimize snorkeling dynamics, allow individual attention during guidance, and support spontaneous activity adjustments. Larger groups (12+ people) require multiple boats, introducing coordination logistics and pricing complexity. recommends motorboat daytrips for small groups, families, or couples; larger groups benefit from catamaran or gulet multi-day charters offering superior group accommodation and crew service ratios.

How quickly can speedboats reach distant Aeolian Islands?

Motorboats cruise 20–35 knots depending on model and sea conditions. Palermo to Lipari (60 nm) requires approximately 2–3 hours; Capo d'Orlando to Stromboli (50 nm) takes 1.5–2 hours. Fast-boat services (40+ knots) achieve Lipari passage in 90–120 minutes, though fuel consumption and sea-state sensitivity limit high-speed operation to calm summer months. Professional captains balance speed against fuel economy and passenger comfort, typically cruising 15–22 knots to optimize destination time within safety margins.
