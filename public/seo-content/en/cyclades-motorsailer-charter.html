For mixed crews, motorsailers eliminate hierarchy tension. Fuel Economy vs. Pure Motorboats

Motorsailers burn fuel more frugally than pure motor yachts (15–25 liters/hour vs. 30–40), because sails share the propulsion load on favorable days. A week combining 40% sailing and 60% motoring might consume 400 liters total (vs. 800–1,000 for a motor yacht), saving €240–300 in fuel costs. This efficiency, combined with authentic sailing moments, makes motorsailers economical for budget-conscious charter planners.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the sailing experience requirement?

None—motorsailers welcome complete beginners. However, previous sailing experience is advantageous (you'll advance faster and enjoy sail handling more). can arrange professional skippered charters or mentorship arrangements if your crew is new to sailing.

Will my crew get seasick on a motorsailer?

Motorsailers heave less than narrow sailing monohulls—their wider beam (relative to length) and moderate motion reduce seasickness rates. Motoring in rough seas provides exceptionally stable platforms. The worst scenario (sailing into headwinds under power and canvas) rarely occurs because you'll simply motor if conditions prove uncomfortable.

Can we motor for an entire week and just use sails occasionally?

Absolutely. Your charter, your rules. Motor the entire time if preferred—no judgment. Many charter groups purchase motorsailers precisely for this flexibility: day-tripping to multiple islands without worrying about wind direction or sailing complexity, then unfurling sails for sunset pleasure cruises if mood strikes.

Are motorsailers significantly more expensive than bareboat sailing yachts?

Generally comparable or slightly higher. motorsailers command modest premiums over bareboat sailing yachts because they're rarer, typically well-maintained, and offer superior all-weather capability. The value proposition (sailing versatility + weather confidence) justifies costs for most charter groups.

What if my crew wants pure sailing performance?

If your crew is highly experienced and seeks racing-yacht performance, our bare-boat sailing yachts (519 monohulls) are superior. Motorsailers are designed for comfort and versatility, not speed or tactical complexity. will steer you toward appropriate vessel types during consultations.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
