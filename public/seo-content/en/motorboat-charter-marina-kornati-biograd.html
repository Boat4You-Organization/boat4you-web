Motorboat Routes: Day Trips & Multi-Day Itineraries

Kornati National Park, 5 nautical miles south (20–30 minutes by motorboat), offers dramatic island scenery and protected anchorages. Popular stops include Sipan, Katić, and Žut—each accessible by 6-meter RIB or larger cruiser. Motorboats navigate shallow approaches and narrow passages where sailboats struggle, expanding your exploration options significantly.

Pašman Island, 8 nautical miles east (25 minutes), provides quieter anchorages and village hospitality. Multiple coves and beaches punctuate the island's shoreline. Motorboats enable efficient exploration of both Pašman's windward and leeward coasts in a single day.

Ugljan Island, 12 nautical miles northeast (35 minutes), offers provisioning stops at Zaglav and Sutomičan villages. Popular for fishing, snorkeling, and casual cruising, Ugljan suits families seeking relaxation over adventure.

Dugi Otok's southern beaches—Sakarun, Velja Prapratna, and Soline—lie 15–20 nautical miles away (45–60 minutes). These pristine, golden-sand beaches are motorboat-accessible and attract fewer crowds than Adriatic resort towns. Day trips are common; multi-day charters often anchor in Sakarun's sheltered bay.

Best Seasons for Motorboat Charters

Spring (April–May) offers calm seas, warm days (18–22°C), and excellent visibility for exploring underwater reefs and coves. Summer (June–August) brings reliable weather and warm water (23–24°C), though July–August sees peak crowds and higher rates. Autumn (September–October) delivers warm water (20–22°C), calm seas, and fewer boats—ideal for relaxed family charters. Winter motorboat charters are possible but less common; the season typically closes November–March.

From Marina Kornati to Sipan island in Kornati National Park takes 20–30 minutes by RIB or cruiser at typical planning speed (15–20 knots). Faster passage is possible but reduces fuel efficiency.
