Motorboat Fleet: Speed and Comfort

Day-charter motorboats are typically 24–32 feet with twin diesel engines (twin outboards for smaller models), delivering 20–30 knot cruise speeds—fast enough for beach-hopping, leisurely enough for snorkelling. Onboard amenities include helm seating, passenger shading (canvas or hardtop), basic galley (cold drinks, snacks), and snorkelling equipment. Some vessels have portable heads (toilets); larger models have full cabins for comfort on rougher days.

Operating costs are modest: a full day charter (6–8 hours) typically costs €600–1,200 depending on vessel size and fuel burn. Fuel is often charged separately (€80–150/day, depending on distance and sea state). Multi-day motorboat charters allow overnight mooring at quiet bays, blending the freedom of independent boating with minimal technical skill requirement.

Classic Day-Trip Routes: Riviera and Cape Sounion

The most popular motorboat itinerary departs Alimos in the morning and heads south along the Riviera. Glyfada (8 nm, 20 minutes) offers upmarket beach clubs and a working fishing harbour. Vouliagmeni (12 nm) is famous for geothermal-heated lagoons and sheltered bathing. Varkiza (18 nm) provides quieter beaches and authentic tavernas.

Extending to Cape Sounion (28 nm, 60 minutes from Alimos) takes you to the Temple of Poseidon perched on a 60-metre cliff. The approach by water reveals the temple's majesty from the sea, inaccessible to road tourists. Anchor in nearby Anavyssos Bay and spend the afternoon swimming, snorkelling, and exploring. The return journey by sunset paints the sky in reds and oranges—magical viewing from a motorboat's helm.

Saronic Island Escapes by Motorboat

Motorboats extend your range: Aegina (15 nm, 45 minutes) is easily reachable for a half-day or full-day visit. Perdika Harbour is perfect for lunch; the Temple of Aphaia nearby is a short hike. Poros (28 nm, 90 minutes) allows afternoon swimming and evening taverna dining before returning to Alimos under stars.

For overnight charters, Poros and Hydra (40 nm, 2+ hours) become accessible. A two-day motorboat charter covers Friday evening to Sunday afternoon, maximising weekend time and minimising commute frustration from Athens city. The combination of speed (reaching distant islands quickly) and comfort (spacious cabins, cruising rather than scrambling) appeals to time-constrained holidaymakers.

Snorkelling and Swimming Highlights

The Athens Riviera and Saronic Gulf offer world-class snorkelling: underwater reefs, colourful Mediterranean fish, and occasional sea turtles. Popular snorkelling stops include Varkiza's rocky outcrops, Poros's reefs, and isolated Dokos Island (north of Hydra). Most motorboats carry snorkelling gear; some include a brief safety briefing and guidance on underwater wildlife.

Shallow anchorages allow swimming for all levels; children enjoy protected bays, while confident swimmers explore deeper reefs. The warm summer water (25–28°C, June–September) makes extended snorkelling comfortable. Autumn water temperatures (20–24°C, September–October) are still swimmable but may require wetsuits for extended time in the water.

Seasons and Weather Considerations

Summer (June–September) dominates motorboat charter season: predictable weather, warm water, and reliable business hours (8 am–7 pm departures). Spring and autumn offer calmer waters and fewer crowds, though weather is slightly more variable. Winter (November–March) is possible for sheltered Riviera cruising, but Mediterranean swells can make long-distance passages uncomfortable; most operators suspend day charters during winter storms.

FAQ

Do I need a skipper's licence to charter a motorboat?

No. Most day-charter operators include a professional skipper or offer captained options. If your group includes an experienced powerboat skipper with valid credentials (RYA Powerboat Level 2 or equivalent), some operators allow you to helm; most, however, recommend professional captaincy for safety and insurance.discusses skipper arrangements during booking.

What's included in a motorboat day-charter?

Typically: vessel hire, skipper (if booked), basic navigation equipment, snorkelling gear, and safety gear (life jackets, first aid). Fuel is often charged separately (€80–150/day, depending on distance). Food, beverages, and fishing gear are additional extras.'s pricing tool itemises all inclusions.

Can I book a motorboat charter for a wedding or special event?

Yes. Many operators specialise in event charters: catering, champagne service, photography support, and custom itineraries. Advance booking (2–4 weeks) is recommended to secure your preferred date and arrange catering. Typical event motorboat charters (10–12 hours for 8–12 guests) cost €1,500–2,500 plus catering and beverages.

How many people fit on a motorboat?

A typical day-charter motorboat (24–32 feet) accommodates 4–10 passengers comfortably. Smaller boats suit 2–4; larger models (35+ feet) accommodate 12+. Overcrowding is uncomfortable; Boat4You recommends 1 person per 3–4 feet of vessel length for optimal enjoyment.

What happens if weather turns bad during my charter?

Your captain monitors forecasts continuously. The Riviera's protected bays provide shelter in light weather; extended bad weather forces itinerary changes or charter postponement. Most operators have flexible rebooking policies: if weather forces cancellation, you reschedule for another date at no additional charge (operational fees may apply).

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your Boat4You consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
