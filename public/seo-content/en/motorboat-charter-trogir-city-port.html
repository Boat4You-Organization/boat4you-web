Day-Trip Routes from Trogir City Port

The Šolta route departs Trogir at 9 AM, reaching Maslinica bay (10 nautical miles, 25 minutes) by 9:45 AM. The protected anchorage offers pristine swimming, excellent snorkeling, and waterfront dining. Morning snorkeling or beach exploration, lunch at a taverna, and afternoon village wandering fill the day comfortably. Departure by 3:30 PM returns you to Trogir City Port by 4:45 PM, leaving the evening for exploring the old town or dining harborside.

The Brač day-trip departs early (8:30 AM) to maximize Bol exploration and beach time. Arriving by 9:30 AM, you have full access to the Zlatni Rat phenomenon—whether swimming at the beach, windsurfing spectating, or exploring the hilltop Bogumil's monument offering panoramic views. Lunch at a Bol waterfront restaurant, afternoon in-water time, and 4 PM departure returns you to Trogir by 5:15 PM. This single-island approach suits those prioritizing depth over breadth.

The Pakleni islands route (less common, for experienced crews or those with professional skippers) weaves through the scenic coves scattered between Trogir and Hvar. Multiple anchorage stops, snorkeling at each, and island exploration consume a full day. Departure by 8 AM enables return by 6 PM, with the motorboat's speed allowing three or four distinct cove visits impossible by sailboat.

Extended itineraries targeting Drvenik Veliki (remote, pristine) or Vis island (outermost, dramatic) are available for multi-day charters; Trogir City Port staff coordinate mooring and provisioning logistics at destination harbors.

Snorkeling and Water Activities

Waters surrounding Trogir are excellent for snorkeling. Šolta's reefs support diverse fish populations; Brač's southern coves hide underwater formations. The motorboat's accessibility means you can visit multiple snorkeling sites in a single day—a flexibility sailboats lack.

Most Trogir City Port motorboats carry basic snorkeling equipment (masks, fins); bring your own wetsuit if comfort in cool water matters. Morning water temperatures (May–June: 16–18°C; July–August: 24–26°C) determine comfort levels. Visibility typically ranges 10–18 metres, sufficient for engaging snorkeling experiences.

Provisioning: Market to Boat

Trogir's Green Market operates daily in the old-town square, 2 minutes from Trogir City Port. Fresh vegetables, fruit, cheese, bread, and local specialties are available minutes before departure. Fish markets near the waterfront stock morning catches. This immediate market access enables last-minute provisions—a luxury unavailable at remote marinas.

Most day-trips include lunch stops at island tavernas, reducing onboard meal preparation. A simple cooler with water, soft drinks, and snacks supplements harborside dining. Return to Trogir by early evening leaves time for exploring restaurants offering pašticada (slow-braised beef), fresh fish prepared as gregada (traditional stew), or soparnik (chard pie).

Multi-day discounts (10–15% off daily rates) apply to charters of 3+ consecutive days. Peak season (July–August) sees modest price increases; shoulder seasons (May–June, September–October) offer best-value-to-experience ratios.

Optimal Seasons for Trogir City Port Charters

May through October is the primary charter window. Late May and early June deliver warm days (24–26°C), fresh morning winds, manageable crowds, and cultural events happening throughout the old town. July and August bring intense heat, thermal predictable winds, and busy anchorages—book early for preferred dates. September and October combine warm water (22–24°C), declining crowds, and varied wind conditions keeping sailing engaging.

April and November are possible but marginal: shorter daylight, occasional Bura squalls from the northeast, and water temperatures below 18°C. Winter operations largely pause; year-round operations resume May through October.
