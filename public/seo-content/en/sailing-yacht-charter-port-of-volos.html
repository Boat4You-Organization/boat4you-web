Summer Sailing Conditions & Seasons

The Sporades summer season (May–September) delivers consistent Meltemi winds, crystal-clear waters, and reliable weather. Early June and late August–September offer the best balance of wind, warmth, and fewer crowds.

The Pagasetic Gulf and surrounding areas remain sheltered from extreme conditions thanks to the Pelion peninsula and northern Sporades geography. Water temperatures peak at 26–28°C by August, ideal for swimming and snorkeling.

Frequently Asked Questions

What sailing experience is required for a bareboat charter from Port of Volos?

Most bareboat charters require proof of sailing competence or a valid ICC (International Certificate of Competence). Skippers must have practical experience sailing monohulls in similar conditions. If uncertain, opt for a skippered charter where our professional crew ensures safety and optimal enjoyment.

What is the best time of year to charter a sailing yacht from Volos?

June through September offers ideal conditions. Early season (May–June) provides moderate winds and fewer crowds; peak summer delivers warm weather but busier anchorages; late season (August–September) combines pleasant conditions with reduced tourist density.

How long does it take to sail from Port of Volos to Skiathos?

Under typical summer conditions with Meltemi winds, the passage takes 4–6 hours depending on wind direction and yacht specifications. Morning departures allow afternoon arrival, providing time to explore Skiathos town and find your evening anchorage.
