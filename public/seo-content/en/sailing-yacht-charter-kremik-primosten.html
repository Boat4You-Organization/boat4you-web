Sailing Conditions in the Šibenik Region

The Adriatic's dominant summer wind is the Maestral (northwest breeze), typically 10–15 knots in May and June, strengthening to 15–22 knots in July and August. Morning conditions are often calm, rewarding early departures with glassy-smooth sailing by mid-morning. The afternoon builds to peak wind—ideal for longer passages.

Mornings at Marina Kremik typically see sailors departing by 0600 to catch the Maestral's build. The channel toward Šibenik and the Kornati offers steady winds with protected anchor points every 10–15 nautical miles, making it perfect for learning crews and experienced sailors alike. Autumn brings variable wind patterns and occasionally strong southeasterly Sirocco passages.

Routes & Regatta-Friendly Waters

Marina Kremik is the unofficial headquarters for regional regattas, particularly during the summer months. The waters between Primošten, Zlarin, and the Kornati islands provide ideal racing courses: protected from major swells yet open enough for consistent wind. Local sailing clubs organize weekly racing events, and visiting charter crews frequently join informal divisions.

Beyond racing, the cruising itineraries from Marina Kremik encompass the Kornati's 140 islands (3–4 hours' sail), the dramatic Šibenik channel with its medieval fortresses (2 hours), and farther passages toward Brač, Hvar, and Vis for multi-week adventures. Shorter day sails explore nearby islands like Zlarin, Kaprije, and Prvić, each featuring unique anchorages and village atmospheres.

The Primošten Village Experience

Primošten is a living monument to Dalmatian seafaring heritage. The compact old town perches on a rocky peninsula, connected by a narrow causeway lined with small taverns and fishmongers. Winding stone alleys lead uphill to a 17th-century church and a viewpoint overlooking the Adriatic and your anchored yacht.

The village is famous for its Red Wine of Primošten (Crljenak Kastela), cultivated in stone-walled terraced vineyards that cascade down the limestone hills. Local restaurants serve fresh catches—brancin, scallops, octopus—often grilled over charcoal and accompanied by local wine. Many establishments cater to sailors and offer group discounts.

Access to Kornati National Park & Protected Anchorages

The Kornati National Park, comprising 140 islands and islets over 89 square kilometers, is approximately 3–4 hours' sail northeast from Marina Kremik. The park's narrow channels, shallow anchorages, and pristine beaches make it a destination of choice for sailing crews. Boat4You provides park entry permits and detailed navigation charts ensuring safe passage through the islands.

Protected anchorages like Telašćica Bay, Vrh, and Mala Proversa offer sandy bottoms and excellent holding. Shore facilities are minimal—no shops, no fuel, no restaurants—which preserves the Kornati's wilderness character and rewards crews with solitude and starlit anchorages.

Best Seasons & Weather Patterns

Spring (April–May) offers warm days, calm mornings, and water temperatures of 17–19°C. Crowds are minimal, making it ideal for crews seeking peace. Summer (June–August) brings consistent Maestral winds, sunny skies, water temperatures of 23–26°C, and active social anchorages—but also increased boat traffic and crowding, especially mid-July through mid-August.

Autumn (September–October) combines warm water, moderate winds, and stunning light. Winter sailing is possible but brings shorter days and occasional rough conditions. Most commercial sailing charters operate April through October, with regatta weeks typically July–August.

Local Provisions & Culinary Highlights

Provisioning from Marina Kremik is straightforward: supermarkets near the marina stock standard groceries, and the village market offers fresh seafood, produce, and local specialties. The region is famous for its Red Wine of Primošten, mussels, scallops, and grilled fish.

Traditional Dalmatian dishes include pašticada (slow-braised beef in a wine reduction), soparnik (chard pie), and brodet (fish stew). Fresh brancin (sea bass), scallops, and squid are staples at waterfront restaurants. Many establishments welcome sailors with special menus and group rates.

Is the Kornati accessible for first-time Adriatic sailors?

Yes, with proper planning and navigation. The Kornati's channels are narrow but well-charted, with numerous protected anchorages along the route.

When is regatta season at Marina Kremik?

Regional regattas occur throughout summer, with peak activity July–August. Weekly racing events are organized by local sailing clubs. Visiting charter crews can often join informal divisions. The marina's reception desk provides regatta schedules and participation details.

What's the average water temperature throughout the year?

April–May: 17–19°C. June: 20–22°C. July–August: 23–26°C. September: 22–24°C. October: 20–21°C. Winter temperatures drop to 10–12°C. Most sailors find May–October comfortable for swimming and water sports.

How advanced must I be to sail to the Kornati?

Intermediate sailing skills are sufficient for the passage from Marina Kremik to the Kornati. The channels are narrow but navigable, and many anchorages offer protected holding. First-time visitors benefit from detailed charts and a weather briefing from our fleet's team or the marina staff.
