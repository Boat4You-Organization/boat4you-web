Sailing Routes and Destinations from Tivat

Marina Solila's location offers exceptional sailing opportunities in every direction. The Bay of Kotor—just 10–15 nautical miles north—is accessible within an hour, providing ample time for day sails or overnight anchorages. Perast's iconic twin islands, Our Lady of the Rocks, and Herceg Novi are all within comfortable reach.

A typical week-long catamaran charter from Marina Solila circumnavigates the Bay, visiting Kotor's medieval harbour (20 nautical miles), Perast and the sanctuary (25 nautical miles), Herceg Novi and Lazure Marina (30 nautical miles), and returning via quiet anchorages and fishing villages.

Beyond the bay, adventurous sailors venture south to Budva (35 nautical miles), the Riviera coast, and eventually Dubrovnik (60 nautical miles)—a classic three-day passage. Extended charters explore the Dalmatian islands: Hvar, Korčula, Mljet, and the Elaphiti chain. Each offers distinct character, pristine beaches, and authentic villages untouched by mass tourism.

To the north, Montenegrin and Croatian waters blend seamlessly. Coastal passages take catamarans to hidden coves, secluded anchor spots, and small fishing communities where tourism is light and traditions remain strong.

Tivat: Gateway to the Bay and Beyond

Tivat is a working port and growing sailing hub, less touristy than Kotor but increasingly sophisticated. The waterfront features restaurants, boutiques, and provisioning shops. Nearby Gospa od Škrpjela (Our Lady of the Rocks), reachable by short ferry, is one of the Adriatic's most photographed landmarks.

Tivat has a rich maritime heritage. The local shipyard, active for centuries, still builds and repairs wooden vessels. Observing traditional craftsmanship provides cultural depth. Several museums document the town's naval and commercial history.

Podgorica International Airport lies 60 kilometres away (45 minutes' drive). Ground transportation is straightforward: car rentals, shuttle services, or taxi transfers coordinate easily. Many sailors arrive by car, enjoying the scenic drive along the coastal highway.

Provisioning from Marina Solila is simple. Fresh markets operate near the waterfront. Local producers supply cheese, cured meats, fresh produce, and wine. Marina restaurants source ingredients locally, ensuring restaurant meals reflect genuine regional flavours.

Seasonal Conditions and Best Times to Sail

The Bay of Kotor and surrounding waters enjoy Mediterranean conditions year-round, with distinct seasonal character. May through September offers warm temperatures, afternoon thermal winds, and reliable weather patterns ideal for catamaran family sailing.

June and September are particularly favourable—warm but not intensely hot, with moderate winds and fewer crowds than July and August. Spring (April–May) features variable conditions and occasional fresh winds that challenge sailors. Autumn (September–October) combines pleasant temperatures with increasing wind variability.

Winter sailing is feasible but less predictable. Occasional strong northeasterly winds (bura) funnel down the mountains, creating exciting but challenging conditions. Summer is safest for families; spring and autumn reward experienced sailors with invigorating sailing and dramatic landscapes.

Current and tidal flows are minimal—rarely exceeding 0.5 knots—making navigation straightforward for catamarans of any experience level. Shallow anchorages are plentiful; the enclosed bay forgives navigational errors.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Marina Solila different from Porto Montenegro?

Marina Solila is quieter, more intimate, and eco-focused, hosting modest sailing and fishing craft. Porto Montenegro is a mega-yacht destination with upscale amenities and international clientele. Solila feels authentically Mediterranean; Porto Montenegro is more exclusive and cosmopolitan. Both offer excellent facilities, but Solila suits traditional sailors and families.

Can bareboat sailors operate catamarans from Marina Solila?

Yes, provided they hold valid sailing credentials (national certificate, RYA, or demonstrated offshore experience). The Bay of Kotor is sheltered and forgiving, making bareboat catamaran sailing accessible to competent sailors. also offers skippered charters for those preferring professional captaincy.

What activities are available beyond sailing from Tivat?

Land-based activities include hiking the fortress and ramparts around Kotor and Herceg Novi, exploring museums, kayaking in quiet coves, snorkeling in turquoise waters, visiting wineries, and enjoying spa services at nearby resorts. The region combines active adventure with relaxation.

Is Marina Solila's eco-focus reflected in pricing?

Marina Solila's rates are comparable to conventional marinas. The eco-friendly practices are operational initiatives rather than cost premiums. 's environmental commitment is reflected in transparency and carbon offset programmes available to all sailors.

How far south can I sail a catamaran from Tivat?

Sailing south from Tivat, you can reach Budva (35nm, one day), Petrovac (45nm), Ulcinj (65nm), and Dubrovnik (60nm south-southeast). A week-long charter easily includes a Dubrovnik passage and exploration of the Riviera. Longer expeditions reach the Dalmatian islands and Croatian Adriatic.
