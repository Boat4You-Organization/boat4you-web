Palm Cay One: Facility Overview and Amenities

The marina occupies a gated community on New Providence Island's southeastern shore, roughly 25 kilometers east of Lynden Pindling International Airport (LPIA). Access is restricted to residents and authorized marina guests, meaning security is professional and reliable. The facility maintains 194 berths arranged on modern floating docks with adequate space for catamarans up to 60 feet. Maximum draft reaches 11.5 feet — sufficient for most charter vessels. Every berth features shore power (110V and 220V available), ensuring you can charge batteries, refrigeration systems, and personal electronics before departure.

The fuel dock operates during standard business hours (8 AM–5 PM daily), allowing full refueling before early morning departures. Potable water is available at every berth; laundry facilities support longer charters. A well-stocked chandlery provides spare parts, provisioning supplies, and basic repairs. The adjacent supermarket and provisioning stores mean you're rarely more than 5 minutes' walk from comprehensive shopping.

Reception staff manage check-in, provide route planning assistance, and handle documentation. Wireless internet connectivity allows you to file passage plans, communicate with Boat4You support, and research anchorages before departure. The marina's professional management ensures that unexpected mechanical issues are addressed promptly — critical when you're departing for remote waters where service facilities don't exist.

Routes from Palm Cay One Marina

The Exuma Cays Loop (7–10 days) remains the most popular itinerary. Depart mid-afternoon, anchor overnight near Athol Island, then sail progressively south and west into the Exuma Banks' pristine anchorages. Thunderball Grotto, Compass Cay, and Cat Island dominate most charters, though experienced crews explore south of the main tourist destinations into largely untouched cays.

Nassau to Eleuthera (10 days) involves a 50–60 nautical-mile crossing of the Tongue of the Ocean — best completed over two days with an overnight stop. Eleuthera's east shore offers Harbour Island (pink sand beach), Spanish Wells (fishing community culture), and quiet anchorages along undeveloped coastline. Return passages loop through the Abacos or retrace the outbound route.

Nassau to Bimini (5–7 days) heads west 45 nautical miles to Bimini, a smaller island community famous for deep-sea fishing but offering excellent sailing access. This route suits shorter charters or clients wanting to combine sailing with sport fishing.

A week of island-hopping from Palm Cay One — combining sailing with motor-sailing and anchoring — typically costs €250–€400 in fuel, depending on wind conditions, passage distances, and engine hours.
