Ploče City Port Facility Overview

Ploče City Port manages commercial shipping traffic, fishing operations, and charter guest needs within a single harbor authority. The approach channel, dredged to 4 meters, accommodates vessels up to 50 feet safely. Mooring berths are allocated within designated charter zones, separated from cargo-handling areas. Boat4You coordinates all facility arrangements, documentation, and guest services through the port authority's charter office.

Approach Channel & Navigational Practicalities

The Ploče harbor entrance is marked by a lighthouse and buoy system; the approach is straightforward in daylight and well-lit for dawn or dusk transits. Holding ground (mud and sand mix) is excellent; charter crews typically anchor without incident. The Neretva delta's northern reaches provide protected shallow-water anchorages for day sails and island approaches.

Cargo Terminal Proximity & Port Operations

Ploče's commercial character—container cranes, bulk carriers, working fisheries—creates an unfiltered Adriatic experience. Charter crews witness authentic port operations: early-morning fish auctions, cargo handling, and the bustle of regional commerce. This environment appeals to sailors seeking genuine Mediterranean sailing culture beyond resort aesthetics. Boat4You coordinates around commercial schedules, ensuring safe mooring and departure windows.

Local Navigational Considerations

The Neretva delta's shallow northern reaches (2–3 meters depth) suit dinghies and shallow-draft vessels for exploring agricultural lands and bird-watching sites. Charter crews navigate marked channels carefully; Summer currents are minimal; winter storms occasionally create challenging exit conditions.

Provisioning at Ploče City Port

The Ploče market, a 5-minute walk from the port, supplies fresh fish, regional produce, and local wines. Supermarkets stock provisions; restaurants along the waterfront offer affordable meals and fresh seafood. The Neretva valley's citrus and agricultural wealth ensures excellent produce quality and fair prices compared to island shops.

Pricing & Mooring Rates

Mooring fees at Ploče City Port run €1.00–€1.50 per meter daily—substantially lower than larger resort marinas. Charter rates for 40–48-foot sailing yachts range €1,800–€3,500 weekly (shoulder to peak season).

FAQ: Ploče City Port Sailing Yacht Charters

Is the working-port environment safe for charter families?

Absolutely. Commercial and charter operations coexist peacefully; charter berths are designated in safe zones away from cargo handling. Families appreciate the authentic port atmosphere and lower costs than resort-based charters.

What are the best times to depart Ploče due to tide and current?

Tides in the Adriatic are minimal (0.5–1.2 meters). Currents within the Neretva delta are strongest in spring (winter runoff); summer currents are negligible. advises on seasonal conditions and optimal departure windows.

Can we fuel up at Ploče City Port on short notice?

Yes. The fuel dock operates Monday–Saturday, 8 AM–5 PM. Emergency fuel is available through our partners's contact network outside regular hours. Most charters depart with full tanks, minimizing mid-passage fueling needs.

Are there shower and laundry facilities at Ploče City Port?

Limited facilities exist at the port; arranges guest access to nearby facilities. Most crews prefer island showers and waterfront swim breaks during multi-day passages.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.
