Multi-Day Gulet Itineraries

A typical 7-day gulet charter departs Split early morning, sailing southwest toward Brač (12 nautical miles). First-night anchorage may be Supetar's protected harbor or Zlatni Rat's eastern approaches, depending on conditions and crew preference. Mornings begin with breakfast on deck; afternoons combine leisurely sailing with coastal exploration and swimming in crystalline coves.

Day three often targets Hvar island (28 nautical miles), where gulet flotillas cluster in the town harbor or anchor off smaller villages like Vrbovska or Jelsa. Your crew navigates social anchorages with practiced ease, and shoreside explorations unfold at your pace—vineyard visits, fortress hikes, or simple tavern dinners with island families.

Vis (38 nautical miles) represents the voyage's crescendo: Vis Town's authentic fishing harbor, the hidden lagoon at Stiniva, and Komiža's bohemian charm. Multi-week gulet charters often base themselves here for full-day island explorations, snorkeling at submarine caves, and dining at family-run konobas featuring local fish and wines.

Return passages zigzag northward, incorporating Šolta's quiet anchorages, the Drvenik islands, and a final night at an island haven before returning to Split West Coast.

FAQ: Gulet Charters from Split West Coast

What's included in the gulet charter rate?

The all-inclusive rate covers vessel, crew (captain, mate, chef), provisioning, fuel, insurance, and mooring at Croatian ports. Guests provide only alcohol preferences and dietary requirements. Optional paid services include water sports, guest activities, and above-standard provisioning.

Can the chef accommodate dietary restrictions?

Yes. Advise at booking—vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, allergies—and your chef designs menus accordingly, sourcing local alternatives daily.

How many cabins does a typical gulet have?

West Coast gulets offer 5–7 cabins. Each cabin sleeps 1–2 guests. Confirm your exact cabin layout when booking; Boat4You's search shows deck plans.

What happens in bad weather?

Your captain alters itineraries to prioritize guest safety and comfort. Protected harbors and motor propulsion ensure progress even in choppy conditions. Bad-weather days become cultural days: market visits, museum tours, or longer tavern lunches ashore.

Is there Wi-Fi connectivity aboard?

Most West Coast gulets carry satellite or cellular connectivity. Speed and reliability vary by location and provider. Expect reliable email but unpredictable streaming. Many guests embrace the digital respite gulet sailing offers.
