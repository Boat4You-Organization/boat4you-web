Pula & the Istrian Peninsula: Strategic Location

Pula lies 140 kilometers south of Venice, 85 kilometers northwest of Rovinj, and 110 kilometers west of Rijeka. The city's deep natural harbor—protected by the Verudella Peninsula—has served as a naval stronghold for two millennia. Today, Marina Tehnomont Veruda occupies the site of the former Austro-Hungarian naval arsenal, maintaining that legacy of maritime excellence.

Arriving crews find Pula Airport (Pula International, 7 km north) well-connected to European hubs. Parking and car rental serve those driving. The marina's position on the peninsula's eastern flank offers shelter from the Bura wind (the cold northeasterly) while remaining open to the gentler Maestral summer breezes.

Island Routes & Day-Sail Destinations

Brioni Islands: Just 8 nautical miles south of Pula, the Brioni National Park contains 14 islands offering protected anchorages, archaeology, and wildlife. Catamarans anchor in Verige Strait or off Grande (main island), where a short dinghy ride reaches shore-based trails and historical sites from Austro-Hungarian times.

Rovinj & the Istrian Coast: Sailing southeast 25 nautical miles brings you to Rovinj, Istria's jewel. Pastel townhouses cascade toward the harbor; local restaurants serve istrian truffle pasta and award-winning wines. Catamarans moor in the outer harbor or at nearby Lone Bay. The approach suits the catamaran's draft perfectly.

Cres & Lošinj (Kvarner gateway): A 35-nautical-mile passage northeastward opens the Kvarner Gulf. Lošinj, famous for its marine-biology station and protected Adriatic monk seals, offers sheltered anchorages and outstanding provisioning. Cres, the larger northern island, features pine-backed bays and the dramatic Orlandin anchorage.

Umag & Northern Istria: Northbound day-sails reach Umag (18 nm), where sandy beaches and modern resort marinas contrast with Pula's Byzantine atmosphere. Excellent provisioning and water-sports facilities make it a popular lunch stop.

Marina Facilities & Operational Services

Marina Tehnomont Veruda operates 270 berths across multiple quays, with 40+ designated for charter catamarans. Facilities include a 60-ton travel lift, covered workshop, fuel dock (available 7:00 AM–6:00 PM daily), freshwater supply, and shore-powered berths. The marina restaurant serves breakfast, lunch, and supplies cold provisions for provisioning packs.

Reception staff speak English, German, and Italian—essential for international crews. Wi-Fi covers the entire facility. Laundry, showers, and waste-disposal points support extended charters. A chandlery stocks basic spares; major repairs are handled in-house or coordinated with nearby shipyards.

Seasons & Weather

Spring (April–May): Warm days (18–22°C), calm mornings, reliable 10–14 knot winds by afternoon. Water 16–18°C. Ideal for confidence-building.

Summer (June–August): Hot, consistent Maestral 12–18 knots, water 22–25°C. Crowded marinas, premium pricing. July–August are peak.

Autumn (September–October): Warm (20–24°C), gentler winds, manageable crowds. Excellent balance of comfort and value.

Winter (November–March): Bura wind dominates, water 10–14°C. Commercial charters rare but possible for experienced crews.

Istrian Culture & Provisioning

Istria is Mediterranean Europe's underrated gem. The hinterland produces award-winning Malvazija (white wine) and Teran (robust red). Coastal towns serve fresh pasta with shaved black truffles, grilled squid, and scampi risotto. Olive oil and local cheese feature at every table.

Pula's Central Market (Pijaca) and supermarkets within 500 meters of the marina provide excellent provisioning. Rovinj and Lošinj offer specialty shops and farmers' markets. Most crews stock their catamaran galley before departure and resupply at island harbors.

Can I charter a catamaran one-way (drop-off elsewhere)?
