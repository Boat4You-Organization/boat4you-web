Island Routes: Kornati, Pašman & Beyond

Kornati National Park, 5 nautical miles south, is a 30-minute sail or motor. This UNESCO-protected archipelago of 140 islands and islets attracts charterers seeking dramatic limestone formations, underwater visibility exceeding 25 meters, and anchorages accessible only by water. Popular anchorages include Sipan, Katić, and Žut, each offering restaurants serving traditional brudet and fresh lobster.

Pašman Island, 8 nautical miles southeast, provides quieter anchorages and village charm. The narrower straits between Pašman and Ugljan afford protected sailing, making this area ideal for less experienced crews. Fishing villages offer provisioning stops and authentic Adriatic hospitality.

Telašćica Bay, 18 nautical miles southwest, is a natural wonder—a protected anchorage sheltering the only saltwater lake in the Mediterranean (Mir). Catamaran charters spend two days here exploring limestone cliffs, swimming in turquoise waters, and anchoring in one of the world's most stunning natural harbors. From Marina Kornati, Telašćica is a rewarding overnight passage.

Closer day-sail options include Dugi Otok's southern beaches and anchorages, accessible in 3–4 hours, and the Drvenik islands' shallow, sandy bays, perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

Best Seasons for Sailing

Spring (April–May) brings calm mornings, warm days (18–22°C), and minimal crowds. Water temperatures reach 17–18°C. Summer (June–August) delivers reliable afternoon Maestral winds and sparkling skies, though mid-July to mid-August brings peak congestion and heat. Autumn (September–October) offers warm water (20–22°C), moderate winds, and fewer boats—a charterer's favorite. Winter sailing is possible but requires experienced crews and brings shorter daylight and occasional rough passages.

Local Provisioning & Adriatic Dining

Biograd's culinary tradition emphasizes fresh fish, squid, mussels, and local olive oil. The town's fish market (Riblja Tržnica) supplies daily catches. Local specialties include pašticada (slow-braised beef), soparnik (Swiss chard pie), and crni rižot (squid-ink risotto). Wines from nearby vineyards—Plavac Mali and Babić—complement seafood beautifully.

Waterfront restaurants in Biograd and Pašman island villages welcome sailors and often offer group discounts.
