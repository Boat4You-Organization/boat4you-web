Motor Yacht Fleet Characteristics

D-Marin Dalmacija-based motor yachts typically range 45–65 feet, with sleeping capacity for 6–12 guests (depending on cabin configuration) plus a crew of 2–4. Modern designs feature flybridge (upper-deck) helm stations offering panoramic views, spacious saloons with dining areas, professional galleys, and dedicated crew quarters. Cabins are appointed with en-suite bathrooms, air-conditioning, and modern amenities rivalling luxury hotels. Many yachts carry tenders (small boats) for island exploration, watertoys (snorkelling gear, paddleboards), and entertainment systems.

Engine systems are typically twin diesel, offering redundancy and sufficient power to cruise at 12–18 knots with multi-day range. Navigation electronics include chartplotters, radar, depth sounders, and VHF communication. Water systems support extended cruising; battery capacity and solar panels extend periods between marina refuelling. Modern motor yachts maintain professional standards in cleanliness, provisioning, and guest service comparable to five-star resort hospitality.

Best Times for Motor Yacht Charters

May–October offers reliable weather, warm water (18–27°C), and accessible provisioning. July–August are the busiest and most expensive. June and September provide excellent conditions (reliable winds, fewer crowds, lower prices). May and October are possible but bring variable weather and occasional provisioning challenges at remote island villages.

Winter operation (November–April) is feasible for those seeking solitude and mild conditions, but Bura squalls can disrupt itineraries, and many island restaurants/services close seasonally. Professional crews mitigate weather challenges; contingency planning during booking ensures realistic expectations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need sailing experience to charter a motor yacht?

No. The captain and crew handle all navigation and maneuvering. Guests can participate in helm operation (if desired and crew-approved), but sailing or boating experience is not required. This accessibility makes motor yachts ideal for non-nautical travellers.

How many crew accompany the yacht?

Typically 2–4, depending on yacht size and guest numbers. A captain is typically present (required for navigation and safety). Additional crew might include steward, cook, or deckhand, handling provisioning, meals, maintenance, and guest services. Crew composition is detailed during booking.

What happens if the itinerary needs to change?

Motor yachts offer flexibility. If weather deteriorates, fuel requirements emerge, or you discover a destination you prefer, the captain adjusts the itinerary with minimal fuss. Unlike sailing yachts (where wind patterns dictate routes), motor yachts' engine independence enables real-time adaptation.

Are meals included in the charter price?

Basic provisioning is typically included, though customization (premium spirits, specialty foods) incurs additional costs. Crew prepares meals from provisioned ingredients, or you dine ashore at restaurants (crew makes reservations). Pre-charter consultation clarifies meal arrangements.

Can motor yachts anchor in the same places as sailing yachts?

Yes, and often with greater comfort. Motor yachts typically draw 1.5–2.2 metres—sufficient for Kornati anchorages and Northern Dalmatian bays. Anchoring is engine-assisted (no sail management required), making positioning easier. The spacious cabins and professional crew enhance comfort compared to sailing vessels.
