Seasonal Conditions & Optimal Planning

May & June: Water 18–20°C, northerlies 8–14 knots. Warm, longer daylight, spring flowers on islands. Ideal season for comfortable cruising; lower tourism than summer peak. Occasional afternoon thunderstorms inland; coastal weather excellent.

July & August: 22–25°C water, consistent 12–18 knot northerlies. Hot, sunny, crowded anchorages near tourist destinations. Best season for families and heat-seeking crews. Book 6–8 weeks ahead for premium vessels; last-minute availability rare.

September & October: 18–20°C water, declining winds (12–16 knots early September, 8–12 knots late October). Exceptional sailing and cruising conditions combined with reduced tourism. September offers ideal balance; October brings variable conditions and occasional Scirocco wind.

Winter (November–April) involves unpredictable Bora/Scirocco winds and rough seas; most luxury charters pause. Spring (March–May) brings rebirth to island communities and increasingly stable conditions.

Can motor yachts anchor safely in the Kornati Islands?

Yes. Modern motor yachts carry high-holding-power anchors and advanced anchor management systems. Alternative anchorages feature excellent sand/mud holding; typical scope is 5:1 (five parts rope to one part depth). Experienced crews anchor confidently; nervous charterers benefit from professional skipper support.
