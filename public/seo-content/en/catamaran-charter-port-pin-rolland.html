Port Pin Rolland: Catamaran-Dedicated Facilities

Port Pin Rolland occupies a distinct niche in the Mediterranean marina landscape. Designed and operated specifically for catamaran operations, every aspect of the facility reflects catamaran-specific requirements. Berth dimensions are optimised for multi-hulled vessels; mooring configurations accommodate catamaran-specific beam (width) and anchoring systems. The slip (cale) is equipped with modern mechanical lifts and support frames capable of hauling and servicing catamarans up to 20 tonnes. In-water maintenance facilities (boat-wash, hull inspection) are configured for catamaran requirements. The facility's technical specialists—mechanics, riggers, canvas workers—possess specialised catamaran knowledge, a rarity among Mediterranean marinas.

This specialisation translates to practical advantages. Catamaran-specific berths eliminate the awkward compromises that occur when multi-hulled vessels occupy traditional finger-pier monohull berths. The support community—other catamaran sailors, specialist technicians—creates an environment where catamaran-specific questions are answered knowledgeably. Provisioning and fuel delivery are coordinated with understanding of catamaran-specific needs. The result is a sailing experience optimised for multi-hulled excellence, distinct from generalist marinas where catamarans are accommodated as an afterthought.

Berth Layout and Catamaran-Specific Infrastructure

The facility comprises a central basin (approximately 15,000 square metres) with a series of dedicated finger piers and mooring fields. Six charter catamaran berths are clustered in a dedicated section, allowing for convenient coordination, provisioning, and crew briefings. Individual berths feature standardised dimensions suitable for 35–45 foot catamarans (roughly 10–14 metres length overall, 6–8 metres beam). Spring-line and bow-line cleats are positioned appropriately for catamaran geometry; modern fenders and protective systems prevent hull damage during mooring.

Water depth in the dedicated catamaran berths ranges from 3.5 to 5.0 metres, sufficient for any charter catamaran. The bottom composition (sand with occasional rock) provides excellent anchor-hold characteristics, important for overnight mooring in exposed positions. The berth layout allows adequate swing-space for vessels to rotate with wind or current without contacting neighbours. Pedestrian access from berths to shore facilities is direct and convenient; provisioning trucks and fuel barges can access berth zones without congestion or logistical complications.

Fuel, Water, Power, and Catamaran-Specific Services

Port Pin Rolland offers comprehensive utility services optimised for catamaran operations. Diesel fuel is dispensed from a dedicated pontoon; modern metering equipment ensures accurate charges and rapid filling. Fuel quality meets all EU specifications; delivery is reliable and continuous throughout the season. Fresh water is supplied dockside from standardised connections at each berth; water pressure and quality are municipal standard—clean, reliable, and suitable for drinking and cooking.

Electrical power (220V, 16A) is provided at designated berths via pedestal outlets; modern electrical infrastructure minimises voltage fluctuations and ensures safe charging of catamaran-specific systems (larger battery banks, dual engine-starting systems, solar-charging regulators). Generator berths (for vessels requiring extended independent power) are available upon request, important for catamarans with sophisticated electrical systems requiring intensive charging.

Catamaran-specific services set Port Pin Rolland apart. Engine servicing by certified technicians specialising in dual-engine catamaran systems (common to modern charter catamarans) is available for routine maintenance or emergency repairs. Hull and canvas services include catamaran-specific sail repair, dacron patching, and catamaran-optimised rigging adjustments. Haul-out and in-water services (hull cleaning, inspection, antifouling) are performed by technicians with catamaran expertise.

Saint-Mandrier: The Peninsula Village Advantage

Port Pin Rolland's location on the Saint-Mandrier peninsula offers unmatched village proximity and cultural authenticity. The village centre—restaurants, market, provisioning shops—sits immediately above the marina, accessible by a 5-minute walk. The morning fish market (Cours Jules Ferry) operates daily from 06:00, supplying fresh Mediterranean catch: sea bass, sea bream, grouper, local mussels, and crab. Vegetable vendors offer seasonal Provençal produce; wine merchants specialise in local Côtes de Provence rosé and regional reds. Local bakeries (boulangeries) produce fresh bread daily.

Restaurants in the village range from simple fishermen's tables to contemporary Mediterranean establishments. Chez Michel, Le Relais de la Passe, and smaller family-run auberges serve grilled fish, local vegetables, and uncomplicated Provençal preparations. Prices are reasonable—€25–€40 for dinner with wine at unpretentious establishments. The atmosphere is authentically Mediterranean: loud, convivial, centred on good food and maritime conversation. This is not resort dining; it is working village hospitality, a rare commodity on the increasingly commercialised Côte d'Azur.

Sailing from Port Pin Rolland: Protected Waters and Extended Routes

Port Pin Rolland's immediate surroundings offer some of the Mediterranean's finest protected sailing waters. The strait separating Saint-Mandrier from the mainland (Le Passe) provides calm, clear water ideal for day sailing, swimming, and exploration. Numerous mooring buoys and small anchorages within 2–3 nautical miles of the village create options for overnight stops without extended passage-making. Catamaran performance in these sheltered waters is exceptional: the shallow draft permits exploration of tiny coves and rocky anchorages unreachable by deeper-keeled vessels; stability ensures comfort throughout the day's sailing.

Extended passages reach the Îles d'Hyères national park (20 nautical miles, 4–5 hours), Saint-Tropez (30 nautical miles, 6–7 hours), and Cannes (50 nautical miles, 9–10 hours). A week-long charter from Port Pin Rolland might allocate four days to local exploration (Passe, nearby rocky coves, day sails) and three days to an extended passage toward the Îles d'Hyères or Saint-Tropez. Two-week charters explore the full Côte d'Azur from this versatile base.

Seasons, Weather, and Catamaran-Optimised Sailing

Port Pin Rolland operates year-round. The sailing season runs April–October, with May–September offering optimal conditions. Summer brings warm, stable weather and the Mistral wind (northwestern)—a dry flow descending from the Rhone valley that develops mid-morning, strengthens through afternoon (15–25 knots), and eases at sunset. This rhythm is catamaran-optimised: morning departure under light winds, midday sailing in moderate conditions (catamarans thrive in steady breeze), and evening arrival at anchorages as winds ease. The Mistral is consistent and predictable—ideal for catamaran sailing.

June and September offer warm weather, fewer crowds, and more variable winds. May and October deliver warm water and pleasant air, with occasional weather surprises. Spring (April) brings warming conditions and spring wildflowers; winter (November–March) is possible but less popular due to shorter daylight and occasional Atlantic-born storms.
