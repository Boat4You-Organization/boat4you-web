Motorboat Routes: Islands, Beaches & Day Trips

Kornati National Park, 20 nautical miles south (50–70 minutes by motorboat), offers dramatic limestone islands and protected anchorages. RIBs navigate shallow approaches and narrow channels where larger vessels struggle. Popular stops include Sipan, Katić, and Žut. Motorboats enable efficient island-hopping: visit multiple anchorages, explore beaches, and return to Sukošan for dinner.

Pašman Island, 8 nautical miles northeast (25 minutes), provides quieter anchorages and village hospitality. Multiple coves and beaches punctuate the shoreline. Motorboats efficiently explore both windward and leeward coasts in a single day, accessing family-friendly villages and excellent provisioning.

Ugljan Island, 12 nautical miles northeast (35 minutes), offers provisioning stops and relaxed village culture. Popular for fishing, snorkeling, and casual cruising, Ugljan suits families and crews seeking laid-back pace. Several villages provide restaurants, bakeries, and fresh-produce markets.

Dugi Otok's southern beaches—Sakarun, Velja Prapratna—lie 15–20 nautical miles west (50–75 minutes). These pristine golden-sand beaches attract motorboat day-trips. Popular for swimming, snorkeling, and picnicking, they remain far less crowded than Adriatic resort beaches. Motorboats anchor in shallow, sheltered waters.

Zadar city (15 km north, 30 minutes) offers historic attractions, museums, waterfront dining, and cultural experiences. Day-trip motorboat visits combine beach exploration with urban sightseeing.

Best Seasons for Motorboat Charters

Spring (April–May) offers warm days (18–22°C), calm seas, and excellent underwater visibility for snorkeling. Summer (June–August) brings reliable weather and warm water (23–24°C), though July–August sees peak crowds. Autumn (September–October) delivers warm water (20–22°C), calm conditions, and minimal congestion—ideal for relaxed family cruising. Winter motorboat charters are possible but less common; the commercial season typically runs April through October.

Do I need a boating license?

Day motorboat charters under 40 HP typically require no license in Croatian waters. Larger vessels may require basic boating certification or insurance. clarifies requirements during booking; professional skippers are available if preferred.

Is fuel included in the price?

Weekly charter rates typically include fuel. Day rentals often provide a fuel allowance or split costs. Boat4You's search specifies fuel inclusion for each vessel. Contact our team to confirm before booking.

How fast can we reach Kornati?

From D-Marin Dalmacija to Kornati islands takes 50–70 minutes by motorboat at typical cruising speed (15–20 knots). Faster passage is possible but reduces fuel efficiency. Many charters plan multiple-island days, visiting Kornati, Pašman, and Ugljan in sequence.

Are half-day motorboat rentals available?

Yes. Pricing is prorated; shoulder seasons offer more availability than peak July–August.

Can children participate?

Absolutely. Motorboats are family-friendly, offering safe, supervised island exploration. Life jackets and safety equipment are mandatory and provided. RIBs and cabin cruisers suit families with children of all ages. Water sports (with proper supervision) are popular with active kids.
