Catamarans are exceptionally family-friendly compared to monohulls. Wide decks make movement safe for children; twin-hull stability means less heel and motion sickness. Catamaran Performance and Sailing Characteristics

Modern catamarans sail faster than comparably-sized monohulls, cruising comfortably at 8–10 knots with genoa and mainsail. Electric winches make sail handling simple—even novice crews manage. Catamarans point slightly higher into the wind than monohulls but offer superior downwind performance. The broad beam (width) provides exceptional stability.

Seasickness is minimized aboard cats: the two hulls absorb wave energy differently than monohulls, creating gentler motion. Families and nervous first-timers universally appreciate this comfort advantage.

Best Season and Weather for Tourlos Catamarans

May through September offers reliable conditions: warm days (25–30°C), moderate meltemi winds (Force 3–5), and calm anchorages. July and August see peak crowds; June and September offer excellent weather with fewer tourists. Tourlos' protected harbor makes even windy days comfortable.

FAQ

Do catamarans heel (tilt) as much as monohulls?

No. Catamarans heel minimally due to wide beam and twin-hull design. Even experienced sailors notice the comfortable, level feeling aboard a cat. This is one of their primary advantages for families and nervous sailors.

Are catamarans fast?

Yes, modern catamarans cruise at 8–10 knots under sail, making longer passages quicker than monohulls. Motor performance is excellent too—some cats motor at 10–12 knots, useful for slow-wind conditions.

Can children sleep safely in a catamaran cabin?

Absolutely. Cabins are tucked between the two hulls, far from rocking motion. Children sleep soundly. Wide companionway stairs (not steep ladders) make movement safe, and spacious cabins (not cramped) are comfortable for families.
