Motorboat Charter in Ornos, Mykonos

Why Choose a Motorboat from Ornos?

Ornos Bay sits on Mykonos' south-west coast, offering a sheltered anchorage and quick access to the island's most glamorous beach clubs. The bay is known for its sandy beach, calm waters perfect for families, and a vibrant row of beachfront venues. Renting a motorboat in Ornos means zero stress—your boat is minutes from town, yet you escape the hustle within moments of departure.

A motorboat gives you total freedom: throttle out to neighboring islands, speed across to Delos for afternoon ruins-gazing, or idle along Mykonos' dramatic coastline. Fuel consumption is predictable, and the open design lets you feel the Aegean wind while staying in control.

Top Routes and Day Sail Ideas from Ornos

From Ornos Bay, the best route is a straight shot east to sacred Delos (30-40 minutes), home to ancient temples and archaeological wonders. Combine it with a visit to Rhenia's sandy beaches for swimming. Head north to neighboring Tinos for whitewashed villages and local tavernas, or explore the tiny islets of Tragonissi and Ftenopoula—perfect for secluded snorkeling.

Many guests prefer the relaxed "beach-hopping" itinerary: depart Ornos in the morning, explore Platys Gialos and Psarou beaches on Mykonos' south coast, stop for lunch at a waterfront taverna, and return by sunset. The distances are short (3-10 km), making it ideal for families, first-timers, or those who just want gentle cruising without long passages.

Marinas, Facilities, and Logistics in Ornos Bay

Ornos Bay is a natural, sheltered anchorage with sandy bottom and good holding. A small mooring field has been developed, and local operators maintain tender access to the beach. The bay has no formal fuel dock, so plan refueling at nearby Mykonos Town marina if needed. Water taxi services and local beach clubs provide supplies, cold drinks, and lunch for anchored boats.

Transfer from Mykonos Town is seamless—just a 5-minute boat ride or 15-minute coastal walk puts you at Ornos Beach. The resort hotels lining the bay offer convenient parking and quick boat access. No paperwork required for EU nationals holding a valid ID; non-EU renters need a passport.

Best Seasons for Motorboat Charters in Ornos

May through September is peak season, with reliable northerly "meltemi" winds (Force 3–5) and average water temperatures of 22–26°C (72–79°F). July and August see the most tourists and boat traffic, so book early. June and September offer the sweet spot: warm weather, moderate crowds, and slightly gentler wind patterns.

April and October are shoulder season—quieter beaches, cheaper rentals, and water around 20–22°C (68–72°F). Winter sailing in Ornos is possible but less popular due to shorter daylight and occasional rough sea conditions.

Local Flavor: Food, Nightlife, and Provisions

Ornos Beach is ringed with upscale beach clubs serving Mediterranean cuisine and cocktails. PST Club, Hippie Fish, and Kudo Beach Club are local favorites, each with a unique vibe. Moor your motorboat nearby and wade ashore for fresh fish, Greek salads, and ouzo. Most clubs offer reserved seating and table service for boat guests.

For provisions, Mykonos Town's main market (Enopoli) is a 5-minute drive from Ornos. Stock up on fresh fruit, cheese, local wine, and bread at the bakery. Supermarkets near Mykonos Town deliver to boats on request. Water is available at the marina; fuel contracts can be arranged through your rental company.

FAQ

Do I need a boating license to rent a motorboat from Ornos?

EU nationals over 18 with a valid ID are welcome to self-drive most boats under 7 meters. Larger boats require a recreational skippers' certificate (IYC or equivalent). We can arrange a professional skipper for a small fee if you prefer not to drive.

Is Ornos good for families with young children?

Absolutely. The bay offers shallow, sandy waters perfect for kids. Beach clubs provide shade and facilities, and the calm conditions make for comfortable motoring. Many families rent motorboats here for a single day of beach club hopping and gentle exploration.

What's included in the motorboat rental?

Rental includes the boat, mooring, basic safety kit, GPS, and fuel allowance. Insurance, skipper service, and additional equipment (sunshade, snorkel gear) are available for a supplement.

How far is Ornos from the airport?

Mykonos Airport is about 15 km (9 miles) from Ornos, roughly a 20-minute drive by car or taxi. Car rental and transfer services are readily available; most rental companies offer direct delivery to the boat.

Can I take a motorboat from Ornos to other Greek islands?

Yes. Day trips to Delos, Tinos, and the Sporades are all feasible. Overnight passages to Paros or Naxos are also possible, though you'll need to arrange mooring at those islands' marinas. Check weather conditions with your captain before planning a longer journey.

What water sports are available from Ornos Bay?

Snorkeling, swimming, and diving are all popular. The bay's calm waters are perfect for beginners and families. We provide basic snorkel kits with most rentals, and advanced divers can arrange dive site guidance. Paddleboarding and towable water sports are also available on request.

Is fuel expensive in Greece, and what are typical consumption rates?

Diesel and petrol fuel at Greek marinas typically cost €1.20–1.60 per liter. A 24-foot motorboat with a 150 hp diesel engine burns roughly 15–20 liters per hour at cruising speed, translating to €20–30 per hour of use. Your rental includes an initial fuel allowance, and refueling fees are charged separately at cost.

What happens if the motorboat breaks down or I damage it?

Mechanical breakdowns are our responsibility—we'll arrange repairs or provide a replacement motorboat at no extra cost. Damage caused by operator error, negligence, or adverse weather is subject to insurance deductibles (typically €300–500). Collision and theft are covered by full insurance if you've opted for zero-deductible coverage, available for a small daily surcharge.
