Catamaran Charter from Tourlos Marina

Tourlos Marina: The Superyacht Corridor Gateway

Tourlos is Mykonos' new port and the entry point for superyachts transiting the Aegean. Tourlos Marina offers deep-water berths (6–8 meters), breakwater protection, and full-service facilities. A catamaran from Tourlos combines stability and comfort with access to prime sailing territory and world-class provisioning infrastructure.

Unlike remote anchorages, Tourlos offers immediate marina services: fuel, water, shore power, waste disposal, and 24/7 security. The protected basin keeps water flat even during strong meltemi winds. Deep berths accommodate larger catamarans (50+ feet) with confidence.

Marina Facilities and Deep-Water Berthing

Tourlos Marina features 150+ berths ranging from 20 to 80 meters. Deep-water access (6–8 meters) allows large cruising catamarans to moor securely. Fuel is provided via hose-to-boat delivery; water and shore power are instant. The harbor master's office handles all documentation, customs clearance, and logistical coordination.

Provisioning is exceptional: supermarkets, fish markets, bakeries, and chandleries are adjacent to the marina. Chandlery staff speak English and understand yacht needs. Same-day provisioning delivery to your berth is standard for advance orders.

Dinghy Access and Beach Club Integration

Catamarans' shallow draft (2–3 feet) allows access to shallow bays and secluded beaches inaccessible to larger monohulls. Included tenders make beach exploration simple. Swim platforms on the stern facilitate direct water entry for snorkeling and swimming. Some beach clubs (Psarou, Platys Gialos, Elia) offer mooring fields where catamarans can secure while guests enjoy facilities.

Dinghy operations are safe and simple. Crews learn quickly; skippers supervise for first-time users. Boat4You provides comprehensive briefings on tender handling, safety protocols, and local procedures.

Multi-Day Itineraries and Sailing Routes

From Tourlos Marina, typical itineraries include: Day 1—Tourlos to Delos/Rhenia (30 nm) for archaeological exploration and swimming; Day 2—Delos to Tinos (25 nm) for traditional villages and tavernas; Day 3—Tinos to Andros (20 nm) for windmills and beaches; Day 4—Andros to Paros (25 nm) for nightlife and restaurants; Day 5—return to Tourlos (30 nm).

Catamarans cruise at comfortable 7–9 knots under sail; motor performance (10–12 knots) handles light wind periods. Twin hulls provide excellent stability; minimal heel ensures passenger comfort even in Force 4 winds.

Seasonal Conditions and Weather Patterns

May through September offers warm weather (25–30°C), reliable meltemi winds (Force 3–5), and excellent sailing conditions. July–August see peak tourists and boats; June and September offer comparable weather with fewer crowds. Tourlos Marina's protection makes even windy days comfortable and safe.

April and October are shoulder season with milder winds, lower prices, and shorter daylight. Winter charters (November–March) are rare but possible for well-equipped boats and experienced crews.
