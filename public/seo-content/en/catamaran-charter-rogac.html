Ferry Terminal & Logistics Hub

Split-to-Rogač ferry service runs year-round: typically four crossings daily in summer, two in winter. Arriving in Rogač, you step directly into the harbor where your catamaran awaits. The ferry terminal complex includes ticket offices, a small supermarket, and a café serving coffee and traditional pastries. No shuttle, no waiting—harbor access is immediate.

Rogač's waterfront hosts several tavernas, a basic pharmacy, and a post office. Fresh-fish markets operate seasonal—summer mornings bring local fishermen selling overnight catch. Provisioning here costs less than Split or tourist islands; Boat4You staff often assist with provisioning advice and supplier contacts.

Immediate Passage Planning

From Rogač, your catamaran can depart immediately upon briefing completion. Eastward, Brač lies roughly 12 nautical miles; Lovrecina beach and Bol harbor offer shelter within two-hour sails. Southward, Šolta's protected coves (Nečujam, Stomorska, Maslinica) provide calm anchoring for overnight stays. Westward passages toward Trogir's medieval old town take four hours under sail; the walled city and Venetian architecture reward cultural exploration.

Rogač's harbor itself anchors comfortably in northeast conditions; working with harbormaster ensures secure mooring during your charter's first and final nights. Most crews depart Rogač by late morning, allowing dawn travel from Split and early provisioning time.

Marina Facilities & Services

Rogač harbor operates a fuel dock (daily 7 AM–6 PM during summer); reserve fuel in advance if departing at dawn. Potable-water filling stations are available; crew typically top tanks before departing on multi-day passages. Shore power (220V, 50Hz) is available at select berths. Basic provisioning supplies—fresh bread, vegetables, cheese, canned goods—are available at the harborside supermarket and from local residents selling seasonal produce.

Laundry facilities are limited; most crews plan laundry stops in Maslinica (which has a small communal facility) or wait until return to Split. Medical facilities are minimal; the nearest hospital is in Split (40-minute ferry).

Provisioning & Local Eating

Rogač's harbor tavernas serve simple seafood-focused meals at reasonable prices (€12–€18 per person). Fresh fish appears daily, prepared grilled or baked with local herbs. Wine selections feature Šolta's Posip and Rukatac; local rakija (traditional brandy) is offered as digestif after meals.

For catamaran provisioning, shopping happens in Rogač's supermarket or at Split before ferry departure. Local fishermen often sell small portions of fresh catch directly from boats; morning walks along the harbor waterfront reveal what's available daily.

Seasonal Conditions & Weather

Rogač's northern exposure means occasional summer thermal winds from the northwest (Force 3–4). These winds suit catamaran sailing perfectly: predictable, steady, and ending by evening. Spring (May–June) brings lighter conditions and calm mornings; summer peaks with reliable breezes and occasional afternoon gales; autumn (September–October) offers gentle, consistent winds and warm water (22–23°C). Winter sailing is rare but possible for experienced crews; November–February see occasional rough conditions and strong northern winds.

FAQ: Catamaran Charters from Rogač

How do I arrive at Rogač with my catamaran charter?

Fly into Split airport (25 km from the city); take a taxi or bus to Split's ferry terminal. Board the ferry to Rogač (40 minutes, €8–€12 per person). Boat4You coordinates your arrival with catamaran briefing, typically scheduled for the afternoon you arrive.

Can I provision my catamaran in Rogač before departing?

Yes. Arrive in Rogač by early morning (first ferry from Split departs 7 AM) to shop at the harborside supermarket, visit local fishermen, and purchase fresh provisions. Most crews complete provisioning within 90 minutes and depart by late morning.

Is Rogač suitable for first-time catamaran sailors?

Absolutely. The calm waters, short passages, and straightforward navigation make Rogač ideal for learning crews. Boat4You's process includes thorough briefing and optional skipper assistance for multi-island passages.

What if I need to leave my catamaran overnight in Rogač?

Rogač harbor provides mooring services; daily rates are typically €2–€3 per meter. Coordinating with the harbormaster ensures secure berths. Most crews moor Rogač for their final night and take the morning ferry to Split.

Are there fuel and water services?

Yes. The fuel dock operates summers 7 AM–6 PM; water filling is available year-round. Reserve fuel in advance if departing at dawn to ensure availability.
