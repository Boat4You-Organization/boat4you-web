No License Required—For Small Boats

Greek maritime law permits unlicensed operation of motorboats under 10 meters (33 feet) within territorial waters. Most Boat4You motorboats fall into this category, meaning you can captain your own vessel without credentials—ideal for travelers who've rarely held a boating license. Larger motorboats (12–14 meters) may require proof of basic competence or a licensed skipper, but Boat4You staff clarifies requirements upfront and can arrange professional crews if needed.

This democratization of access is liberating. Rent a motorboat, grab a chart or GPS unit, and explore at your own pace. Anchoring in a bay near Milos for lunch, then motoring to Ios for dinner, becomes a realistic day's agenda.

Day Cruises & Sunset Moments

Motorboat day charters typically depart at 10 am and return by 6 pm—perfect for the classic 2–4 hour itinerary: depart Mykonos harbor, motor 15 minutes to a secluded beach on Delos, snorkel 90 minutes, motor 20 minutes to a seafood taverna on Tinos, dine and relax, then motor home as the sun sets. Total time: 8 hours. Total distance: 30 nautical miles. Fuel cost: roughly €40–60.

Boat4You motorboats are equipped with basic navigation (GPS, charts), safety gear (life jackets, flares, first aid), and comfortable seating. Shade is limited on smaller craft—bring sunscreen and hats. Toilets are usually a chemical head (basic but functional) on vessels 8+ meters; smaller boats have none.

Inter-Island Hops & Multi-Day Rentals

For longer trips, motorboats excel at rapid island-to-island progression. Sleep ashore in village rooms, then motor from Paros to Antiparos (3 nm), to Naxos (8 nm), to Iraklia (12 nm), and back—a 5-day island sampler impossible with traditional sailing yachts, which would spend half the week fighting the meltemi.

This approach suits backpackers and budget travelers. Rent a small motorboat for €150–250/day, split costs among 4 friends (€40–60 each), anchor in free natural bays, cook simple meals aboard or eat ashore, and explore far more territory than bareboat sailing yachts permit in equal time.

Diving & Water Sports Access

Motorboats reach remote dive sites quickly. The Antiparos sea caves (6 nm from Paros), Delos submerged sculptures (5 nm from Mykonos), and Anafi's volcanic rock formations (25 nm from Mykonos) are easily reached in 30–60 minutes. This efficiency means more bottom time and less travel fatigue—crucial for divers planning multiple sites per day.

Families use motorboats for endless beach rotations: anchor in a quiet bay by 11 am, swim and snorkel for 2 hours, motor to another beach by 2 pm, repeat. Children stay entertained; parents enjoy simple logistics. Boat4You motorboats often come with basic snorkeling gear; confirm availability when booking.

Fuel Economics & Operating Costs

Motorboats burn 15–40 liters per hour depending on engine size and speed. At €1.20/liter, a day's 40-nautical-mile cruise costs €24–48 in fuel. Weekly rentals (€800–1,500) plus fuel and mooring (typically free in natural anchorages) total €1,200–2,000 for a group of 4—competitive with bareboat yacht costs and faster to execute.

Fuel tanks vary: small boats hold 50–100 liters (covering 200–400 nm at cruise speed); larger motorboats hold 200+ liters. specifies tank capacity and cruise range for each vessel, ensuring you plan refueling stops on Mykonos, Paros, or Naxos as needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need no boating experience?

For small motorboats (under 10 meters), many jurisdictions—including Greece—permit operation by unlicensed operators. However, recommends basic safety training (30-minute orientation) and common sense: stay in familiar areas, monitor weather, and rarely anchor in strong winds. Our staff provides briefings and GPS-assisted navigation tools.

What if weather turns rough?

Motorboat charters are flexible. If seas build unexpectedly, return to your origin harbor or a nearby shelter (Paros, Naxos typically have refuge). Unlike sailing yachts committed to multi-day passages, motorboats can abort quickly. Check weather forecasts before departure; if warnings are issued, postpone.

Can we charter a motorboat for just a few hours?

Yes—many motorboat partners offer 3-hour half-day rentals (€120–180), perfect for sunset cruises or quick beach runs. Confirm minimum rental periods when browsing.

Is towing available if we break down?

Yes. includes basic breakdown coverage; in the unlikely event of engine failure, recovery services operate from major ports. Confirm insurance and emergency contact details in your charter agreement.

Can we fish from a motorboat?

Yes—Greece permits recreational fishing from chartered vessels in most areas. Local species include grouper, sea bream, and mackerel. No commercial fishing license required; check local regulations for size/quantity limits. Our crew can suggest productive grounds.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
