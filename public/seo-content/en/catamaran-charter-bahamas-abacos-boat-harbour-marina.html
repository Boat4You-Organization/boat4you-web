Catamaran Charter from Boat Harbour Marina, Abacos

Boat Harbour Marina, anchored within the Abaco Beach Resort on Great Abaco Island, represents the modern face of Bahamas yacht charters. Purpose-built for cruising yachts and operating as a full-service marina facility, Boat Harbour hosts 21 catamarans available for bareboat and crewed charters. The marina's deep-water approach (no shallow-draft constraints on entry), full-service amenities, and resort infrastructure create an operational platform that marries traditional Caribbean hospitality with contemporary marine services.

Located at Marsh Harbour's eastern gateway, Boat Harbour Marina sits 8 kilometers from Marsh Harbour International Airport and operates as an alternative base to Conch Inn Marina. The facility offers complementary advantages: a quieter operational atmosphere, direct resort amenities (restaurants, accommodations for pre-charter nights, water sports), and deep-water berths accommodating larger catamarans and superyachts. Boat Harbour Marina: Deep Water and Full-Service Infrastructure

Boat Harbour Marina was designed with cruising yachts as a priority. The entrance channel maintains 6–8 feet of water at most tidal states, eliminating the shallow-draft anxiety that sometimes characterizes Bahamas marina approaches. The basin itself ranges 8–15 feet deep, accommodating 40–50 foot catamarans without grounding concerns or tidal restrictions. Berths are spacious, with modern cleats, fenders, and utility pedestals at each slip.

Facility infrastructure exceeds typical Caribbean marina standards. The fuel dock operates 7 AM–5 PM daily, dispensing diesel and gasoline at competitive Bahamas rates. Water fill-ups are instantaneous at each berth; electrical connections (30-amp 110V and 50-amp 220V) support modern catamaran battery and refrigeration systems. The reception building houses an office, weather briefing room, and Wi-Fi hotspot. Shower facilities, laundry services, and holding-tank pump-out complete the full-service picture.

Security is thorough. The marina entrance is gated, with controlled access during off-hours. Reception staff monitor the facility continuously. Vehicle parking is available throughout the resort property. The atmosphere is decidedly upscale—the Abaco Beach Resort adjoining the marina creates a resort-town vibe distinct from the working-port character of Conch Inn Marina.

Route Options and Navigation from Boat Harbour

Departing from Boat Harbour, crews immediately access the western Sea of Abaco—the protected lagoon system connecting Hope Town, Green Turtle Cay, Treasure Cay, and countless smaller islands. From Boat Harbour, Hope Town lies 14 nautical miles southwest (3-hour sail/motor); Green Turtle Cay is 28 nm northwest (4.5-hour transit); Treasure Cay is 45 nm north (6-hour passage).

The deep-water approach means crews depart Boat Harbour and can motor directly across the mouth of the Sea of Abaco toward outer islands or the Bahama Banks. Unlike Conch Inn Marina (located central to the harbor), Boat Harbour's eastern location offers slightly faster access to the Banks or northern Abacos. For crews prioritizing geography coverage over Abaco concentration, this advantage is meaningful.

The Boat Harbour-to-Treasure Cay Route: Most direct passage covers 45 nm north along the western sound boundary. Departure mid-morning reaches Treasure Cay by late afternoon. The passage offers excellent snorkeling stops en route—Guana Cay and nearby reefs are accessible day-sail destinations.

The Boat Harbour-to-Banks Route: Motor or sail west 50+ nm from Boat Harbour toward the Bahama Banks. The Banks represent some of the Caribbean's most pristine waters—shallow (8–20 feet), crystalline clarity (200+ foot visibility), and virtually uninhabited. 10–14 day charters can spend 3–4 days on the Banks exploring pristine cays and unvisited anchorages, returning to Boat Harbour for final provisioning and checkout.

Week-long charters averaging 40–50 engine hours consume 320–600 gallons. Current Bahamas fuel prices ($5.50–$6.50 per gallon) mean fuel surcharges of $1,760–$3,900 weekly. Weather and Operational Seasons

Winter (December–April): Consistent northeast trade winds (12–20 knots), comfortable temperatures (75–80°F), calm seas inside the reef. Peak season for charters and most competitive pricing. Book 2–3 months in advance.

Spring and Fall (May–June, September–October): Lighter winds (8–15 knots), warmer water (78–82°F), 20–30% lower pricing. Fewer charter boats, quieter anchorages, excellent sailing for wind-comfortable crews. Recommended for first-time Abaco visitors.

Summer (July–August): Hot, humid, lighter winds, occasional tropical rain squalls. Hurricane season officially begins June 1. Statistically, the Abacos sit south of typical hurricane tracks, but weather vigilance is essential. Most commercial operations prioritize winter bookings.

Sample 10-Day Itinerary from Boat Harbour Marina

Day 1: Arrival and Departure — Arrive Marsh Harbour, taxi to Boat Harbour Marina (8 km, 15 minutes). Check-in, provisioning, safety briefing. Depart mid-afternoon to nearby Hope Town (14 nm, 3-hour sail). Overnight Hope Town Harbour.

Days 2–3: Hope Town Circuit — Day sails along Elbow Cay's eastern shore, snorkeling, relaxation. Overnight Hope Town Harbour. Explore the cay on foot.

Day 4: Hope Town to Green Turtle Cay — Motor-sail northwest 25 nm. Navigate the cut into Green Turtle's harbor. Explore colonial village, Jolly Roger tavern dinner, overnight anchorage.

Days 5–6: Green Turtle Exploration — Day sail to Guana Cay (12 nm east). Snorkel pristine reefs, swim, beach exploration. Return to Green Turtle or anchor at Guana overnight.

Day 7: Northern Push — Green Turtle to Treasure Cay (15 nm north). Resort marina facilities, resupply fuel and fresh provisions, relax at beach.

Days 8–9: Treasure Cay Region — Day sails to nearby northern cays. Explore the Abaco Banks approach. Evening return to Treasure Cay for final resort dinner.

Day 10: Return to Boat Harbour Marina — Motor-sail 45 nm south to Boat Harbour. Check-out procedures, engine wash-down, shower, return taxi to airport for evening flights.
