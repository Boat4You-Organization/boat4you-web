Catamaran Charter from Lavrion Main Port

Boat4You offers 16 premium catamarans moored at Lavrion Main Port, one of Attica's most accessible and practical departure points for Saronic Gulf sailing. Located just 60 kilometres south-east of central Athens, Lavrion blends commercial port energy with growing leisure charter infrastructure.

Why Choose Catamaran Charter from Lavrion Main Port?

Lavrion Main Port is home to established charter fleets, particularly catamarans that benefit from the port's robust commercial quay facilities when Olympic Marina reaches capacity. The double-hulled design offers unmatched stability, shallow draft capability, and spacious cabins—ideal for families and groups exploring the Saronic islands.

The port's ferry infrastructure (both domestic and international services) means excellent ground access. Visitors arrive via Highway A2 from Athens, or via shuttle from Athens airport, making embarkation straightforward. Unlike finger-pontoon marinas, quayside mooring at Lavrion allows walk-on access even for larger multihulls.

Fleet & Catamaran Specifications

Boat4You maintains 16 catamarans at Lavrion Main Port, ranging from 38-foot to 48-foot vessels. All are fully equipped with modern navigation, galley, hot water systems, and stabilizer technology. Sleeping capacity runs 6 to 10 berths per vessel. Each catamaran carries tender dinghies and snorkelling gear as standard.

Catamaran hull design maximises internal volume without sacrificing performance. Compared to monohulls of similar length, catamarans offer 30-40% more interior space, translating to larger cabins, more comfortable saloons, and superior living conditions for week-long charters. The multi-hulled design also reduces rolling motion, making cats ideal for families and less experienced crews who prioritise comfort and stability.

Sailing Routes from Lavrion

The Saronic Gulf is a sailor's dream: sheltered by Attica's coastline and the Peloponnese, with consistent northerly winds in summer. Day-sail destinations include the island of Hydra (2 hours), Spetses (3 hours), and the shallow anchorages of Dokos. Multi-day routes venture to the Argolic Gulf or loop around Aegina, Poros, and back to Lavrion.

Catamarans' shallow draft lets you access many hidden bays and sandy beaches that monohull sailors cannot reach. The flat saloon of a catamaran gives ideal views as you cruise past Peloponnesian cliffs.

Lavrion Main Port Facilities & Infrastructure

The port offers 24-hour reception, fuel berths, fresh water points, and provisioning shops within walking distance. Lavrion town itself (a historic mining-to-tourism town) has tavernas, supermarkets, and pharmacies. For overnight pre-departure meals, we recommend the waterfront restaurant cluster near the ferry terminal.

Waste disposal, recycling, and grey water pumping-out stations are available. The port authority is cooperative with leisure charterers and maintains good signage for navigation.

Best Season & Weather

May through September offers the most reliable sailing: average July temperatures of 29°C, northerly Etesian winds of 10–20 knots, and minimal rainfall. April and October are still pleasant, with 20–22°C air temperature and slightly lighter winds—perfect for first-time sailors.

Winter (November–February) sees occasional squalls and stronger southerlies; we recommend these months for experienced crews only.

Local Colour: Provisioning & Dining

Lavrion's waterfront is lined with fish tavernas serving the day's catch: grilled octopus, sea bass, and mussels are staples. For provisioning, the open-air market (Thursdays and Fridays) offers local produce, cheese, and wine. Board your catamaran with fresh bread, olives, and feta—your crew will love it.

The town's history as a mining centre means interesting architecture and local wine producers. Many charterers take a half-day ashore to explore Lavrion Museum or walk the waterfront before setting sail.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I charter a catamaran from Lavrion with no sailing experience?

Yes. Boat4You offers professional skippers for hire, or crew-taught bareboat options with on-the-water training. Most first-time renters choose a skipper for at least the first few days.

What is the typical catamaran charter cost from Lavrion?

Costs vary by season and vessel size. Summer peak-season rates for a 40-foot catamaran typically range €3,000–€4,500 per week. Shoulder seasons (May, September–early October) offer 20–30% discounts. Check current availability and pricing on Boat4You.

How far is Lavrion Main Port from Athens airport?

Approximately 70 kilometres by road (Highway A2), roughly 90 minutes depending on traffic. arranges airport shuttle pickups and drop-offs. Alternatively, take the regional KTEL bus directly to Lavrion town.

Are provisioning and fuel included in the charter price?

Fuel is typically charged separately at cost. Provisioning is not included, but coordinates with local suppliers to pre-stock your catamaran with groceries, drinks, and essentials if you arrange it in advance.

What is the typical water temperature in the Saronic?

Summer water temperatures range 24-28°C (perfect for swimming and snorkelling). Spring and autumn see 18-22°C; winter drops to 14-16°C. Most swimmers and snorkellers find summer ideal; wetsuits optional in shoulder seasons.

Can we visit the Temple of Poseidon on a catamaran charter?

Yes. Cape Sounio's Temple of Poseidon (470 BC) sits dramatically above the water. Day trips from Lavrion take 1-1.5 hours sailing; the temple is 90 minutes walk from the nearest beach anchorage. Many crews combine sailing and cultural exploration.
