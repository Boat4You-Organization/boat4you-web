Why Choose a Catamaran for Mahé Sailing

Catamarans excel in the Seychelles environment. Their shallow draft allows navigation into tight anchorages and coral-laden passages where monohulls would struggle. The wide beam creates expansive cabin layouts—cabins that don't feel cramped after days at sea. The reduced heel (angle) means fewer seasick mornings and more stability for enjoying meals, reading, or simply relaxing on deck. For families with young children, snorkelers, or those new to sailing, a catamaran's roominess and gentle motion are transformative.

A catamaran also carries more water, fuel, and provisions, reducing the need for frequent port stops and maximizing your time in remote anchorages. The dual-engine design provides added safety and maneuverability in tight mooring situations, essential when anchoring near rocky reefs or approaching crowded harbors.

Multi-Island Itineraries: A Week of Discovery

A typical week-long catamaran charter from Mahé follows a classic inner-island loop. Day one sails northeast to Praslin (approximately 40 nautical miles), where you'll anchor in sheltered Baie Ste Anne. Days two and three explore Praslin's treasures: the otherworldly Vallée de Mai, a UNESCO World Heritage Site harboring the endemic Coco de Mer palm; pristine beaches at Anse Lazio; and snorkeling reefs teeming with parrotfish, groupers, and sea turtles.

Days three and four push south to La Digue, home to the iconic Anse Source d'Argent—a dreamlike beach of pink sand and sculptural boulders. The island's quiet villages, bicycle-powered transport, and unpretentious charm captivate many visitors. Your catamaran provides an ideal base for exploring tide pools, kayaking in crystalline shallows, and dining on fresh grilled fish at island restaurants.

Days five and six typically route westward to Silhouette Island, a dramatic, largely undeveloped wilderness rising steeply from the ocean. The handful of resorts and private estates on Silhouette contrast sharply with its pristine forests, hiking trails, and untouched beaches. A catamaran's stability and shallow draft make Silhouette's anchorages—often protected by rocky outcrops—thoroughly accessible and safe.

Days six through seven return toward Mahé, with stops at smaller islands like Curieuse (a giant tortoise sanctuary), Cousin (a strict bird reserve), or other gems depending on wind and conditions. Your final night might be spent at Eden Island Marina or Victoria Port, celebrating a successful expedition with shoreside dinner.

Snorkeling and Water Adventures

Catamarans are platforms for immersive ocean experiences. The Seychelles boasts world-class coral reefs, home to countless tropical fish, octopus, rays, and reef sharks (harmless to snorkelers). Most Boat4You catamarans carry snorkeling gear and dinghy tenders, enabling daily reef explorations. Popular snorkeling sites include the reefs surrounding Praslin, the channels between La Digue and Félicité Island, and the pristine reefs fringing Silhouette's western coast. Many locations feature both vibrant coral gardens and deeper drop-offs—something for every skill level.

Weather and Optimal Sailing Windows

April to October brings the southeast trade winds (12–18 knots), cool, dry conditions, and calm-to-moderate seas. These months are prime for catamaran chartering, with reliable wind for sailing and comfortable temperatures. November to March features the northwest monsoon—lighter, more variable winds with occasional afternoon squalls. The payoff: fewer tourists, lush island verdancy, and excellent charter rates. Many experienced captains prefer the southwest shoulder season (March–April) for its balance of favorable winds and pleasant weather.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics.
