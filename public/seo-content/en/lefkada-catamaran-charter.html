Why Choose a Catamaran from Lefkada

Catamarans bring unmatched comfort and stability to Ionian sailing. The wide hull separation creates a spacious deck, twin engines provide redundancy and easy maneuvering, and the shallow draft (just 1.0–1.2m) opens shallow anchorages around Meganisi and Kalamos that monohulls cannot reach. Families love the flat, secure cockpit; children can move freely without fear of heeling.

Lefkada's position is geography's gift. Just 25 minutes from Preveza airport, the island sits at the gateway to week-long Ionian loops. The Maïstro wind, a light afternoon NW breeze averaging 10–15 knots, arrives like clockwork from June through September—ideal for novices and anyone seeking relaxed passages rather than hard sails. Unlike the meltemi-dominated Cyclades to the east, where 30-knot gusts can intimidate, the Ionian asks less of crew and gear.

Popular Routes & Island Itineraries

The classic Lefkada week unfolds in a gentle arc. Day one: sail northeast to Meganisi, a lush island with olive groves and quiet coves. Anchor at Spilia Beach or Katomeri village. Day two: island-hop to Kalamos, untouched and wind-sheltered, then Kastos. Day three: make the longer passage to Ithaca, home of Homer's Odysseus; moor at Vathi or explore the quieter Kioni village on the north coast.

Days four and five extend south to Kefalonia, where Argostoli marina offers full supplies and restaurants. From there, day-sail to the red-sand Myrtos Beach or anchor in Assos under its Venetian castle. By mid-week, crews feel at ease; the catamaran's stability and easy handling make longer passages stress-free. Days six and seven loop back via Lefkada's own west coast—Porto Katsiki's limestone cliffs and Egremni's dramatic golden sand appear at sunset. Arrive Nidri or Lefkada Town for final nights.

Marinas & Bases in Lefkada

Nidri village, on Lefkada's sheltered east coast, is the charter-boat capital. Several thousand yachts and catamarans pivot through its waters annually. Nidri marina offers berths, fuel, water, and a village full of tavernas, shops, and chandlers. It's chaotic in August but welcoming and efficient. Most Boat4You catamaran charters either depart or return here.

Lefkada Town marina, operated by D-Marin at the north end, caters to larger yachts and catamarans with concrete floating pontoons, 24-hour security, and modern facilities. It's less crowded than Nidri, with easier road access and a charming Old Town 500 meters away. Preveza airport is 25 minutes by car; the nearby Preveza waterfront town has additional marinas and provisioning.

Smaller villages like Vasiliki (south coast, windsurfing hub) and Vlicho (sheltered bay) offer quieter bases for lighter traffic weeks or season shoulders.

Sailing Season & Wind Patterns

The sweet spot runs June through September. July and August are busiest (and priciest), but also most reliable for the Maïstro breeze. June and September offer fewer crowds and pleasant 18–22°C air; wind is slightly lighter and less predictable, but still gentle. May and October are shoulder months—viable for experienced crews, but afternoon breezes can be thin.

The Maïstro typically builds around noon, peaks mid-afternoon at 10–15 knots, and dies at sunset. Mornings are often glassy, ideal for motoring to next anchorage or a early-day swim stop. This rhythm suits families who prefer relaxed passages: depart after breakfast, anchor by 4 p.m., dine ashore. Storms are rare; when they do occur, the Ionian's numerous sheltered bays offer refuge within hours.

Provisioning, Food & Local Flavours

Nidri and Lefkada Town have full supermarkets (Lidl, Alpha, local grocers) and excellent fish markets. Produce is cheap and abundant—tomatoes, feta, olives, and local wines. Tavernas dot every cove; expect grilled octopus, gemista (tomato-baked fish), and locally-caught grouper at 12–18 EUR per main. Kefalonia's Argostoli is the Ionian's larder; stock up on wine and provisions here if you're doing a longer charter.

Ice is available at Nidri and Lefkada Town marinas; fresh bread appears at village bakeries each morning. Many villages have no shops—plan accordingly. Restaurants are informal; taverna owners rarely take credit cards, and English-speaking staff are common but not reliable. The ritual of provisioning—haggling over fish prices, tasting local wine—is part of the Ionian sailing experience.

Practical Information & Certifications

Most Boat4You catamaran charters are bareboat; a valid ICC (International Certificate of Competence) is required, though some operators accept log-book proof of experience or offer skipper-placement. Children of any age are welcome; the catamaran's stability and spacious layout suit families with toddlers as well as teenagers. Pet policies vary; confirm when booking.

Fuel costs roughly 1 EUR per liter; budgeting 200–300 EUR for a week covers engine running and generator use. Provisioning and food typically run 300–500 EUR per week for a four-person crew. Mooring fees at larger marinas are 25–50 EUR per night; anchorages are free. Wi-Fi is available at main marinas but unreliable in remote coves.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a sailing license to charter a catamaran in Lefkada?

An ICC (International Certificate of Competence) is typically required, though can connect you with skippers if you prefer to hire professional crew. Some operators accept logbook evidence of experience or offer introductory lessons.

What is the best time to visit Lefkada for catamaran sailing?

June through September is ideal. July and August are warmest and windiest (reliable Maïstro), but also crowded. June and September offer fewer crowds, milder temperatures, and still-pleasant sailing conditions. May and October are viable for experienced crews but less predictable.

How far is Preveza airport from Lefkada's main marinas?

About 25 minutes by car. Nidri marina is 30 km south; Lefkada Town (D-Marin) is slightly closer. Ground transport is easy to arrange; can coordinate rental cars or taxi transfers.

Can families with young children sail a catamaran in Lefkada?

Absolutely. The Ionian's gentle winds, spacious cabin layouts, and protected anchorages make catamarans ideal for families. The catamaran's stability is superior to monohull yachts, and the shallow-draft design reaches child-friendly, sandy beaches that bigger vessels cannot.

How much does it cost to charter a catamaran in Lefkada for a week?

Prices range from 1,800–2,500 EUR for a 32-footer in shoulder season to 4,500–7,000+ EUR for larger, luxury catamarans in peak summer. Extras (fuel, mooring, provisioning, skipper hire) typically add 500–1,500 EUR. lists true all-in pricing with no surprises.

What type of catamaran is best for my crew?

A 38-40 footer suits four to six people comfortably with three cabins and two heads. Larger 45+ footers offer master suites, separate galley, and lounge spaces ideal for eight-person groups or extended family holidays. Smaller 32-35 footers are nimble, economical, and perfect for couples or three-person crews who value speed and ease of handling.

Deck Layout & Comfort Onboard

Modern catamarans in the Lefkada fleet boast expansive sunpads forward, shaded helm stations with full instrumentation, and built-in galley access. Cabins feature queen berths with quality linens, climate control, and en-suite heads with hot showers. The spacious saloon is perfect for evening meals, card games, or sheltering from rare squalls. Flybridge or hardtop canopies protect the cockpit and helm from sun and spray.

Tenders and water-sports gear—snorkels, kayaks, paddleboards—are standard on most vessels. The shallow draft and wide beam also mean easier anchoring and superior holding in sand and mud bottoms, critical in the Ionian where seagrass beds cover many popular coves.
