Technical considerations

Motor yachts at Marina Lav handle the region's conditions well when properly prepared. Check reefing points, engine hours, and safety gear during the check-in briefing — marinas in Croatia run careful handover procedures and the briefing is the right moment to raise questions.

Anchoring and berthing

Anchor tackle on charter motor yachts is generally sized adequately for Croatia's typical bay depths (6–15 metres). Med-mooring stern-to is standard in marinas; the briefing at Marina Lav covers the local conventions.

Provisioning and logistics

Marina Lav sits within reasonable range of supermarkets and fresh markets. Plan a provisioning run for the first evening rather than day zero — the fleet turnaround day is not the moment to load the galley.

Sample 7-day itinerary from Marina Lav

Day 1 — Departure and first bay

Embark after noon once the cabin preparation is complete, stock provisions, and motor to a nearby sheltered bay for the first night. Swim, rig checks, and a quiet dinner aboard set the tone.

Day 2 to Day 3 — Short hops along the coast

Cover 15–25 nautical miles per day between protected harbours and anchorages. Lunch stops at lunch-only anchorages, evenings in towns with konoba dining and a morning bakery run.

Day 4 — Longer crossing

Pick a day with favourable forecast for the longest passage of the week. Target 25–40 nautical miles of sailing or motoring depending on wind.

Day 5 to 6 — Return legs

Work back toward base with cultural stops — old town walk, a local winery, and an anchorage-only overnight if weather allows.

Day 7 — Return to base

Early morning passage back, fuel top-up, a quick galley clean, and formal disembarkation by late morning.

Local food and culture

expect peka (slow-cooked lamb or octopus under an iron bell), gregada (Adriatic fish stew), local Pošip and Plavac Mali wines, and konobas (family taverns) on nearly every islanded harbour. Meals aboard tend to work well for breakfast and lunch; evenings ashore deliver the local character — ask marina staff for current recommendations rather than relying on online ratings alone.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I book a motor yacht from Marina Lav?

For peak July–August departures, 4–6 months ahead secures the best fleet. For shoulder months, 6–8 weeks is usually enough. Last-minute availability is tracked on the Boat4You search.

Is a licence required for bareboat charter in Croatia?

For bareboat motor yacht charter, a recognised skipper licence and VHF certificate are typically required. If you don't hold them, a professional skipper from the Marina Lav network runs roughly 150–220 euro per day plus meals.

What is included in the charter price?

Base pricing covers the motor yacht, insurance, and berth fees at Marina Lav. Fuel, provisioning, transit fees (country-specific), end-cleaning, and skipper (if hired) are typically added on top. The final pre-departure summary from the broker lists every line item.

How is Marina Lav reached from the nearest airport?

Taxi or pre-booked transfers from the closest airport handle logistics well. Most charter companies at Marina Lav can arrange the transfer as part of the booking.
