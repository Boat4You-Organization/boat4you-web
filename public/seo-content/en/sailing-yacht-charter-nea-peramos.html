Marina Facility & Berthing Specifications

Nea Peramos Marina features 60–80 protected berths, meticulously maintained by a dedicated, smaller team. Natural headlands shield the inner harbour from summer swells. Each berth includes dedicated 220V power pedestals, fresh water connection, dedicated fuel berths (to avoid congestion), waste management systems, and ice-maker access. The reception office operates daytime hours; out-of-hours radio assistance is available 24/7.

Berth depths average 2.5–3.5 metres—comfortable for monohulls with typical 1.8–2.2 metre drafts. The protected inner harbour means calm water even during breezy conditions, making berthing and departure straightforward regardless of wind direction.

Access & Departure Logistics

Berthing at Nea Peramos Marina is straightforward: contact the reception office, receive your berth assignment, and proceed at your own pace—no commercial port bureaucracy. Most crews arrive morning-of, brief on local conditions, provision lightly (or pick up pre-arranged groceries), and sail by early afternoon. Fuelling can occur on departure day morning, taking 30 minutes turnaround for smaller vessels.

Ground transport: Nea Peramos is 70 kilometres west of central Athens (75–90 minutes by road). Boat4You arranges airport transfers; the KTEL regional bus also serves Nea Peramos directly.

Sailing Routes from the Marina

Westward (toward the Ionian): A 3–4 hour reach brings you to the Corinth Canal entrance. Transit the Canal (a thrilling once-in-a-lifetime passage between towering white cliffs) and emerge into the Ionian Sea. From there, Kefalonia, Ithaca, Lefkas, and the broader Ionian island chain are within reach. Week-long Ionian loops departing Nea Peramos are popular with experienced crews.

Eastward (toward the Saronic): explore the western Saronic's quieter anchorages, then venture toward Aegina, Poros, Hydra, and Spetses. These routes are gentler and ideal for less experienced crews or those seeking leisurely pacing.

Wind & Water Conditions from Nea Peramos

Summer Etesian winds blow northerly at 10–16 knots average, with occasional stronger blows reaching 18–20 knots. The marina's protected water ensures calm overnight mooring. Megara Bay and the western approaches see slightly lighter air than the open Saronic, ideal for learning sailors or those wanting relaxed passages.

Water temperatures: summer (June–August) 24–28°C, spring/autumn (May, September–October) 18–22°C. Winter (November–February) is cold (14–16°C) and sees occasional gales; most yachts depart the region during winter months.
