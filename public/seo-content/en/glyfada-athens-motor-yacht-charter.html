What does "all-inclusive" mean in motor yacht charter pricing?

All-inclusive covers vessel lease, professional crew (captain, chef, hostess, deckhand), fuel, standard provisioning, cabin amenities, and most entertainment equipment. Excluded typically are gratuities (expected at charter end), alcohol beyond standard selections, specialized water sports (diving, fishing), and private shore excursions. specifies exactly what's included during booking consultation—no hidden surprises.

Absolutely. Your charter briefing includes detailed dietary consultations: allergies, religious restrictions, cuisine preferences (vegetarian, Greek specialties, international flavors), and ingredient sources. chefs source ingredients ahead of charter, preparing menus you review and approve. Last-minute modifications are accommodated if discussed early in the process.

How many cabins and guests can a typical motor yacht accommodate?

Motor yachts range considerably. Intimate 40-footers typically feature one spacious master suite plus one or two guest cabins, perfect for couples or small families (4–6 people). Larger 60–80 footers offer three to four en-suite cabins accommodating 8–12 guests. helps match yacht size to your group composition and desired privacy levels. Crew accommodates separately, ensuring guest comfort and professional service boundaries.

What happens if we want to extend the charter or modify the itinerary during our trip?

Motor yacht flexibility is a primary advantage. Your captain and crew coordinate With us support to adjust itineraries, extend dates if yacht availability permits, or pivot to alternative destinations based on weather or guest preferences. Extensions are billed at daily rates transparent during initial booking. The crew's expertise enables spontaneous discoveries and pivots impossible with rigid sailing schedules.

Is seasickness a concern on a motor yacht cruise?

Motor yachts' massive displacement hulls provide excellent stabilization, and modern autopilot technology reduces pitching and rolling compared to smaller vessels. Motoring (versus sailing, which can involve heel and motion) further minimizes discomfort. Your crew carries pharmaceutical remedies and can modify speed/course to comfort levels if any guest experiences sensitivity. Historically, motion-sensitive guests report significantly improved comfort aboard motor yachts.
