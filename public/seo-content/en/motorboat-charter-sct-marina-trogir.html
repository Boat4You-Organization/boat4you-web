Day-Trip and Multi-Day Motorboat Routes from Trogir

The classic day-trip departs SCT Marina at 9 AM, targeting Šolta island (10 nautical miles, 20-minute RIB journey). Maslinica bay offers a protected anchorage, excellent snorkeling, and waterfront restaurants serving fresh fish. By 11 AM you're swimming and exploring; by 1 PM lunch is underway at harborside; by 4 PM you depart for Brač (another 15 minutes by RIB), arriving with daylight for an evening aperitif overlooking Bol and Zlatni Rat beach. Return to Trogir by 8 PM. This itinerary suits groups seeking cultural stops and culinary experiences mixed with water time.

The island-hop route targets the Pakleni islands—a string of shallow, pristine coves between Trogir and Hvar. Departing at 8 AM, you reach the first anchorage by 9:30 AM, spending 2–3 hours snorkeling, swimming, and exploring before motor-sailing to a second cove. A picnic lunch aboard, followed by more water time, extends to late afternoon. This route suits families and groups prioritizing water activities over cultural exploration.

The extended itinerary stretches to Hvar island (30 nautical miles, 45 minutes by RIB). The motorboat speed makes a day-trip viable: depart 7 AM, arrive Hvar town by 8:45 AM, explore the harbor and narrow streets, lunch at a waterfront restaurant, visit the cathedral, and depart by 4 PM for a 6 PM return to Trogir. Seasons and Weather Considerations

Motorboat charters operate year-round, though May through October offers the most reliable conditions. Summer (June–August) brings warm water (24–26°C), stable afternoon winds, and busy anchorages. Spring (April–May) offers warm days, calmer seas, and manageable crowds. Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (22–24°C), gentler conditions, and fewer boats—ideal for families and less experienced crews.

Winter (November–March) is possible but brings shorter daylight hours, occasional rough conditions, and cold water (10–14°C). Most recreational motorboat charters pause December through February, resuming fully by April. Bura squalls (strong northeast winds) occasionally strike January–February; monitors forecasts and can recommend shelter or suggest alternative itineraries.

Frequently Asked Questions About Motorboat Charters from SCT Marina

Do I need a boat license to rent a motorboat from SCT Marina Trogir?

Croatian regulations require a Skipper's Ticket or national boat license for motorboats over 10 kW. Most charter motorboats exceed this threshold. If you lack a license, arranges a professional skipper (€120–€180 per day) or guides you toward obtaining temporary certification.

What's the fuel cost for a day-trip motorboat charter?

Expect €50–€100 for a RIB exploring Šolta and Brač; €80–€150 for a longer island-hop extending to the Pakleni islands or Hvar. Fuel surcharges are calculated after your charter at actual consumption rates. Motor-sailing (brief periods with engines off) reduces consumption and extends range.

Are motorboats safer than sailboats in unpredictable weather?

Motorboats offer advantages in sudden wind shifts because engine power is typically available—you're not dependent on sails. However, safety depends on seamanship, equipment, and judgment. 's motorboats carry modern safety gear (life jackets, flares, VHF radios) and navigation systems. Professional skippers are recommended for first-time charterers or during uncertain conditions.

Can motorboats explore the same anchorages as sailboats?

Yes. Motorboats access all sailboat anchorages plus several shallow coves where sailboats struggle due to deep keels. The flexibility and speed mean you can visit multiple anchorages in a single day, expanding exploration compared to sailboat pace.

What's the typical motorboat speed, and how far can I travel in a day?

RIBs cruise at 25–35 knots (45–65 km/h); speedboats reach 30–40 knots. From Trogir, you can reach Hvar (30 nautical miles) in 45 minutes, Vis (40 nautical miles) in 75 minutes. A full-day itinerary easily covers 100+ nautical miles with multiple stops, snorkeling, and meals included.
