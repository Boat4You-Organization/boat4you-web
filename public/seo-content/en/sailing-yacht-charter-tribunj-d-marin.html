Sailing Routes: Kornati & Beyond

The Kornati National Park—approximately twelve nautical miles northeast—represents the quintessential passage from Tribunj D-Marin. A dawn departure reaches the southern islands (Kornat, Žut) by late morning, allowing full-day anchorage exploration. Uninhabited islands, dramatic limestone cliffs, and pristine swimming mean sailing experiences impossible in crowded summer resorts.

Seven-day itineraries encompass the complete Kornati loop: southern islands with their dramatic approaches, Telašćica bay (surrounded by 100-meter cliffs and excellent shelter), northern islands accessible via technical passages, and return via Murter or direct to Tribunj. Sailing conditions vary: spring brings gusty mistral winds (exhilarating for performance enthusiasts), summer offers reliable thermals, autumn combines steady breezes with warm water temperatures.

Best Seasons for Sailing Charters

Spring (April–May) brings warming air (18–22°C) and variable wind—challenging but rewarding for experienced sailors. The mistral (northwest wind) arrives suddenly, creating exhilarating sailing but demanding respect. Summer (June–August) offers reliable thermals, warm water (23–25°C), and social anchorages; mid-July crowding is pronounced but manageable. Autumn (September–October) delivers ideal sailing: consistent northeasterly breeze, warm water, uncrowded islands, and longer daylight hours. Winter sailing is possible but brings short days and occasional rough passages; most commercial operations run April through October.
