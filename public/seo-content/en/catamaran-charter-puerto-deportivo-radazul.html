Charter Routes and Sailing Destinations

From Puerto Deportivo Radazul, typical week-long itineraries explore the northwest Canary Islands sailing zone. The trade winds, predominantly from the northeast, create favorable conditions for reaching La Gomera in a single day, typically 4-6 hours depending on sea state.

Popular multi-day routes include circumnavigating Tenerife, with stops at Garachico (historic town on the north coast with dramatic volcanic cliffs), Los Cristianos (southern resort hub offering provisioning and water sports), and Playa de las Américas (vibrant beach destination with excellent restaurants and sheltered anchorage).

For adventurous sailors, the westerly route toward La Gomera Island opens access to pristine anchorages rarely visited by larger vessels. The journey showcases emerald cliffs, endemic Canarian palm forests, and opportunities for swimming in crystal waters far from cruise ship traffic.

The southern route bypasses Mount Teide's rain shadow, leading to Gran Canaria's Maspalomas dunes and the sheltered marina at Las Palmas, the largest yacht harbour in the Canary Islands and the traditional start point for the Atlantic Rally for Cruisers (ARC). Day trips to Playa del Inglés offer white-sand beaches and modern amenities.

Marina Facilities and Logistics at Radazul

Puerto Deportivo Radazul provides comprehensive support for catamaran charters. The marina boasts over 600 berths protected by modern breakwater infrastructure. Facilities include 24-hour security, shower facilities with hot water, laundry services, restaurants, and a well-stocked chandlery.

Fuel and water services operate throughout daylight hours, with advance booking recommended during peak charter seasons (December through April). The marina's location on the north coast offers direct access to the open Atlantic, minimizing the need for lengthy motorsailing in light conditions.

Road access is straightforward: the marina lies 45 minutes by car from Tenerife North Airport (TFN), making transfers convenient for international charter guests. Car rental services operate from the airport, though Boat4You can arrange transfers upon request.

Provisioning at Radazul is excellent. The nearby town of Icod de los Vinos offers supermarkets, fish markets, bakeries, and local wine producers famous throughout the Canaries. For gourmet provisions, the marina's concierge team can coordinate deliveries to your catamaran before departure.

Best Seasons and Weather Conditions

The optimal charter season at Radazul runs from October through May, when the trade winds blow steadily from the northeast at 12-18 knots. Winter months (December-February) see the strongest and most consistent breezes, ideal for sailors seeking reliable conditions and challenging passages.

Summer months (June-September) bring lighter winds and warmer water temperatures, though Atlantic swells can remain significant. These months suit families with young children and those prioritizing comfort over performance sailing. Visibilities are consistently excellent year-round, with water temperatures ranging from 17°C (winter) to 23°C (late summer).

Hurricane season (June-November) is generally outside the Canary Islands' primary storm belt, positioned south and west of the Caribbean. The Canaries experience easterly trade winds year-round, making them among Europe's most reliable sailing destinations.

Local Provisioning and Island Cuisine

Canarian gastronomy emphasizes fresh seafood, local cheeses, and traditional dishes rooted in African and Spanish culinary traditions. Catamaran galley provisioning can include wrinkled potatoes (papas arrugadas) with mojo sauce, fresh dorado and grouper from local fisheries, and Canarian wine from nearby vineyards.

The marina's proximity to Icod de los Vinos places you near the historic Dragon Tree (Drago Milenario), a thousand-year-old botanical landmark and cultural symbol of Tenerife. Local markets sell Canarian rum, honey, tropical fruits, and artisanal products ideal for onboard entertaining.
