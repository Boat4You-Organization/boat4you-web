Navigation, Weather, and Operational Advantages

Sardinia's northeast coast exhibits Mediterranean conditions: warm, dry summers (June–September) with sea temperatures reaching 24–28°C. The prevailing Maestrale wind (northwest, 8–18 knots) provides pleasant breezes; motorboats operate independently of wind patterns, enabling flexible passage planning and reliable arrival scheduling. Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer comfortable conditions with fewer crowds.

Motorboats excel during challenging weather when sailboats may be unable to operate efficiently. The stable twin-hull design (or deep-keeled monohull) minimizes rolling and pitching, enhancing comfort for seasick-prone passengers. Winter passages (November–March) are navigable—motorboats proceed safely despite variable winds or occasional mistral storms, provided appropriate caution and weather routing are applied.

Our team monitors gale warnings, swell forecasts, wind patterns, and tidal streams. Mobile weather apps synchronized with vessel navigation systems offer 10-day outlooks. Emergency communication systems and 24/7 support ensure assistance if weather deteriorates unexpectedly during your charter.

Do I need boating experience or licensing for motorboat charter?

Italian recreational boating regulations require a license for vessels exceeding 10 meters length. assists international guests by arranging temporary permits or recommending captain-skippered charters. EU citizens often qualify for reciprocal license recognition. Inexperienced boaters benefit from our professional captain program—experienced skippers handle navigation and mechanical oversight while you enjoy the freedom of commanding the vessel.

What's the difference between motorboats and power catamarans?

Motorboats (traditional monohulls) offer superior speed (often 15–20+ knots) and maneuverability, ideal for rapid island-hopping or time-constrained itineraries. Power catamarans provide superior stability, spacious living areas, and shallow draft—better for extended cruising and larger groups. helps match your priorities: high-speed adventure favors motorboats; comfort and stability favor catamarans.

Can motorboats access shallow-water anchorages?

Most motorboats feature shallow draft (1.0–1.5 meters), enabling access to pristine anchorages throughout the Gulf of Arzachena and La Maddalena archipelago. However, some high-performance speedboats have slightly deeper draft. Our team matches your vessel to desired anchorages and routing.
