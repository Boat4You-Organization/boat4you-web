Island Routes and Catamaran Destinations

Brijuni National Park, 8 nautical miles north-west, remains the flagship catamaran destination. Polesana's fleet regularly explores Velika Brijun's protected bay, Pine Island's secluded anchorages, and the archipelago's historical Byzantine ruins. The catamaran's responsive diesel engines ensure timely arrival; spacious saloon layout encourages leisurely exploration. Afternoon departures return charterers to Polesana by dusk—an ideal introduction to catamaran cruising.

Fažana village, 5 kilometres north, serves as supplementary provisioning hub and secondary catamaran anchorage. Waterfront restaurants cater specifically to sailors; many establishments offer mooring discounts for dining customers. The village's working-harbour atmosphere—fishing boats unloading daily catches, local vendors manning market stalls—provides authentic Istrian cultural immersion unavailable at larger commercial marinas.

Kamenjak peninsula, southernmost Istria, presents dramatic scenery accessible within two hours from Polesana. The catamaran's stability and shallow draft permit exploring protected inlets, translucent anchorages, and pristine beaches. Multi-day catamaran charters often include Kamenjak overnight mooring, combining exploration with leisurely evening aperitivo aboard deck as Mediterranean sunsets illuminate limestone cliffs.

Rovinj, 35 nautical miles north-west, attracts multi-day catamaran expeditions. The passage delivers panoramic coastal views, island anchorages en route, and arrival at Istria's most captivating waterfront. Rovinj's narrower medieval streets, upscale dining, and artistic community justify overnight mooring. Professional skippers frequently recommend Rovinj as the pinnacle multi-day catamaran experience from Polesana base.

Local Provisioning and Culinary Traditions

Pula's markets deliver comprehensive catamaran provisioning: Green Market fresh vegetables, Peškarija fish market catches, and specialty shops offering Istrian wines (Malvasia, Teran), olive oils, and artisanal cheeses. Fažana adds supplementary provisioning convenience; Rovinj offers upscale provisions for extended charters. Most charterers enjoy combining boat-prepared meals with occasional restaurant stops ashore.

Istrian cuisine celebrates seafood and truffles. Pašticada (slow-braised beef), grilled octopus, fuži pasta with white truffle, and fresh mussels characterize waterfront dining. Local Malvasia wines (crisp, mineral-driven) and Teran (bold, earth-toned) accompany meals naturally.
