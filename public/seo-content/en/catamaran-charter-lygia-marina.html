Catamaran Charter at Lygia Marina, Ligia

Lygia Marina sits in the heart of Ligia, Lefkada's charming fishing village. Modern catamaran charters from this intimate facility offer families and groups the space, stability, and ease that make cruising joyful rather than exhausting. The marina's position on the calm east coast and immediate proximity to tavernas and local life create an unbeatable combination for a relaxed Ionian week.

Lygia Marina: Modern Facilities, Village Charm

Lygia Marina is a professionally maintained municipal facility with 80 berths, each equipped with water and electrical connections. The harbor is well-protected by breakwaters, ensuring secure mooring in any weather. Harbor watch operates 24/7. The marina sits steps from waterfront tavernas, a small market, bakery, and laundry—everything you need without leaving the village's authentic atmosphere.

Catamaran Layout and Amenities

Charter catamarans typically feature 3–5 cabins, dual heads and showers, and a spacious saloon ideal for games, meals, and gathering. The galley is well-equipped for both simple daily cooking and ambitious entertaining. The wide aft platform is perfect for swimming, tenders, and water sports. Dual engines, redundant electrical systems, and ample water tanks mean reliability and comfort underway.

Sailing Conditions from Lygia Marina

The east coast's protected position means calm mornings and moderate Maistro winds (10–18 knots) in summer. Catamarans handle light air well with twin engines; stronger conditions are straightforward with their buoyancy and power. The shallow-draft advantage means anchoring options abound: tucked bays, tiny islands, and protected shallows where monohull cruisers cannot venture.

Cruising Routes from Lygia Marina

North to Nikiana (30 minutes) offers sheltered anchorage and excellent tavernas. South to Nidri (40 minutes) opens the Meganisi strait and access to Skorpios and the mainland coast. East toward the mainland curves past green hills and hidden coves. West into the open Ionian reaches Kalamos and Kastos in 3–4 hours. Each route suits different moods: short hops for relaxed cruising, longer passages for more ambitious sailors.

The Catamaran Advantage in Ionian Waters

Catamarans' shallow draft, wide beam, and speed under power make them ideal for the Ionian's variable conditions. Light-wind days are less stressful—twin engines motor efficiently. No heel means the galley crew stays comfortable, children sleep undisturbed, and evening meals don't require anticipation of violent heel. Safety lines and broad decks make movement easy for young children and older guests.

How far in advance should I book?

For peak season (July and August), reservations 4–6 months ahead secure the best fleet selection and pricing. Shoulder months (May, June, September) typically allow booking 6–8 weeks out.

What does the charter price include?

Base pricing usually covers the vessel, insurance, and marina fees at the home base. Fuel, provisioning, skipper service, end-cleaning, and tourist tax are generally added separately. Check the specific listing on Boat4You for a full breakdown.

Do I need a sailing licence?

For bareboat charter, a valid skipper licence plus a VHF certificate are typically required, and rules vary by country. If you don't hold one, hiring a professional skipper is straightforward — pricing runs roughly 150–220 euro per day plus meals.
