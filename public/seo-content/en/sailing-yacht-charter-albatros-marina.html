Meltemi Wind and Seasonal Sailing Excellence

The meltemi—the Aegean's famous northwest thermal wind—defines summer sailing character. Blowing consistently at 12-20 knots from the northwest during afternoon hours, the meltemi provides ideal sailing conditions for prepared sailors. Morning calm periods offer glassy-water conditions perfect for photography and snorkeling. experienced skippers understand meltemi patterns intimately: they forecast daily intensities, predict wind shifts, and plan itineraries maximizing sailing pleasure while respecting the wind's power.

Spring (April-May) brings moderate winds of 8-12 knots, water warming to 20-24°C, and minimal rainfall. Wildflowers bloom along island shorelines creating stunning landscapes. Autumn (September-October) offers perhaps the ideal sailing window: warm temperatures, moderate winds, excellent visibility, reduced crowds, and entirely predictable weather patterns. Winter (November-March) presents variable conditions requiring experienced seamanship, though dramatic seascapes and significant charter discounts appeal to cold-weather sailors.
