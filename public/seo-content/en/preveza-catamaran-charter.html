Explore the Ambracian Gulf – Europe's Secret Anchorage

The Ambracian Gulf is one of Europe's most overlooked sailing treasures. Enter via the narrow channel at Aktion and you unlock a vast, sheltered inland sea bordered by mountains and wetlands. Few international charterers venture here; the result is tranquility, abundant marine life, and authentic Greek hospitality.

Anchor off Messolonghi, Vonitsa, or tiny Paleros and experience genuine isolation. The gulf's waters are calm year-round, making catamaran sailing stress-free even in July and August when the Ionian gets crowded. Dolphins are common; birdwatchers report raptors and wetland species rare elsewhere in Greece.

Ionian Island-Hopping from Preveza

Exit the Ambracian and sail south into open Ionian waters. Lefkada (5 nm, 30 min) is your springboard. From there, classic routes branch to Meganisi (dramatic limestone cliffs), Kalamos and Kastos (remote, pine-covered), Ithaca (Odysseus's mythical home), and the larger islands of Kefalonia and Zakynthos.

The catamaran's breadth and shallow draft mean you can anchor in Nidri Lagoon, explore Meganisi's tucked-away anchorages, and reach small islets unreachable by monohull. Preveza-based charters enjoy 5–7 day itineraries with no back-tracking.

Preveza: A Venetian Waterfront & Modern Convenience

Preveza's old town is a walker's dream—cobbled streets, Venetian architecture, family tavernas where sardine festivals are still celebrated seasonally. After days at sea, your crew will appreciate the town's café culture, fresh seafood restaurants, and laid-back atmosphere.

The waterfront (Preveza Main Port) sits at the city's heart, a working harbour where fishing and commercial vessels share space with charter yachts. This authenticity is part of Preveza's charm; unlike purpose-built marinas, you experience Greece as it is.

Catamaran Advantages: Stability, Space & Shallow Draft

Catamarans offer three critical advantages in Greek waters: exceptional stability (important for younger sailors and non-sailors), generous cabin and deck space (ideal for multi-family charters), and shallow draft (access to anchorages monohulls cannot reach).

For Preveza charters, this means calm motion in the Ambracian, easy anchoring in 3–4 metres of water off secluded isles, and comfortable accommodation for groups of 8–12 guests. Boat4You catamarans come with full galley facilities, air conditioning, and modern heads.

Preveza Sailing Season: May to September

The Ionian and Ambracian Gulf see stable conditions May through September. Summer brings the reliable Maistro thermal breeze (NW, 10–18 knots), building through the day and easing evening. June and September offer the most consistent wind; July and August can be lighter, favoring motor-sailing.

Water temperatures reach 27°C by August. Weather windows are frequent; Aegean-scale storms are rare in the Ionian. Boat4You monitors conditions and advises on itinerary adjustments.
