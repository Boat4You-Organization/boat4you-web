Supported Sailing Routes from Marina Drage

Pašman island, directly across the Pašman Channel, offers immediate sailing. The 15-minute passage to Pašman Town introduces you to the village's medieval heritage, working fishing culture, and fresh-fish restaurants. Sailing around Pašman's eastern shore reveals quiet anchorages, pine-backed beaches, and the kind of peaceful swimming opportunities that define Adriatic sailing.

Ugljan, the longer and more developed of the twin islands, stretches 23 kilometres and combines accessible tourist services with genuine local character. The village of Preko hosts accommodation and restaurants; sailing from Marina Drage reaches Preko in 45 minutes. Many charter yachts spend two to three days exploring Ugljan's bays, provisioning at Preko's supermarket, and enjoying leisurely anchorages.

The Kornati National Park represents the pinnacle of Adriatic sailing—140 limestone islands, dramatic rocky formations, anchorages of crystalline clarity, and an almost primordial landscape. Marina Drage's position (30 nautical miles north) makes Kornati expeditions accessible within a 4–6 day charter. Sailing yachts depart early morning, reach the outer Kornati islands by midday, and anchor in protected bays perfect for swimming, snorkelling, and evening reflection.

For shorter charters, Sakarun Bay and Telašćica (both within the Kornati region) offer natural harbours, spectacular scenery, and mooring facilities without the full archipelago commitment. Many sailors plan 5–7 day itineraries balancing Pašman/Ugljan exploration with one or two nights in the Kornati.

Best Seasons & Sailing Conditions

Spring (April–May) delivers mild weather, warm days, and calm mornings—ideal for families and cautious sailors. Water temperatures reach 17–19°C; winds are light and variable. Summer (June–August) brings reliable afternoon Maestral winds (15–20 knots) and warm water (22–25°C), perfect for sailing yachts and confident passage-making.

Autumn (September–October) is celebrated as the ideal sailing season: warm water (22–23°C), consistent wind, and fewer boats. Winter sailing is possible but demands respect for occasional Bura squalls and shorter daylight hours. Most charter operations run April through October; winter bookings are available by special arrangement.

Local Dining & Provisioning at Marina Drage

Restaurants at Marina Drage serve catch-of-the-day from local fishermen—gregada (traditional fish stew), grilled sea bass, octopus salads, and pasta with local shells. Portions are generous, prices reasonable, and atmosphere genuinely welcoming. Many marina staff can recommend favourite dishes or facilitate reservations if your party is large.

Provisioning combines local markets (at Drage and nearby Pakoštane/Biograd) with onboard pantry space. Fresh bread, cheese, and produce are sourced daily. Many sailing yacht charters feature a mix of dinners ashore and meals prepared aboard—economical, flexible, and often delicious.
