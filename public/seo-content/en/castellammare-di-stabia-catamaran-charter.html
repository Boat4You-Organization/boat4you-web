Sorrento & Capri: 18 & 20 Nautical Miles Away

From Castellammare di Stabia, Sorrento lies just 18 kilometers to the south, accessible in under two hours by catamaran. This dramatic clifftop town, famous for its fragrant lemon groves and charming piazzetta, offers world-class dining and boutique shopping within steps of our mooring positions.

Capri, the legendary Blue Grotto island, sits 20 nautical miles southwest. Your catamaran's shallow draft makes exploring Capri's famous sea caves and limestone cliffs effortless. Overnight anchorages in Marina Grande provide safe harbor while you savor fresh seafood and local wines.

Positano, the jewel of the Amalfi Coast, lies approximately 25 nautical miles west and makes for an unforgettable overnight excursion. The town's pastel-colored vertical buildings cascade down to pristine beaches, creating one of Italy's most photogenic destinations.

Local Cuisine & Cultural Experiences

Castellammare di Stabia's culinary heritage runs deep. Sample pastiera napoletana, a traditional Easter cake with ricotta and wheat berries, at family-run bakeries in the historic center. Spaghetti alle vongole—fresh pasta with tender littleneck clams—reflects the region's maritime bounty.

The area's volcanic soils produce Lacryma Christi, a white wine said to be "tears of Christ." With Vesuvius as backdrop, evening wine tastings aboard your catamaran become transcendent experiences. Boat4You arranges cellar visits and sommelier-guided tastings throughout your charter.

Vesuvius & History Meets Modern Comfort

Ancient Stabiae thrived as a Roman resort town before Vesuvius's eruption in 79 AD buried it beneath ash and pumice. Today, archaeological sites like the Stabia Villas showcase remarkably preserved frescoes and mosaics. Charter clients frequently combine morning excavation tours with afternoon sailing.

Castellammare's waterfront marina district has evolved into a modern leisure destination while honoring its Greco-Roman heritage. Modern catamaran infrastructure meets ancient maritime traditions in this uniquely layered setting.
