Motorboat Charter from Marina Kaštela

Marina Kaštela: Gateway for Motorboat Adventures

A motorboat charter from Marina Kaštela offers the simplest entry into Adriatic boating. Marina Kaštela, just 3–5 minutes from Split Airport and positioned between Split (15 min) and Trogir (10 min), provides immediate access to coastal exploration without overnight complexity. Whether you're planning a day trip to Brač's Zlatni Rat beach, a sunset cruise, or a 3-day island-hopping adventure, motorboats deliver accessible, affordable boating on one of Europe's most stunning coastlines.

Why Choose a Motorboat from Marina Kaštela?

Motorboats—typically 15–30 feet—combine simplicity with capability. A motorboat charter requires minimal experience, operates on demand (no wind-dependent delays), and focuses on accessible, lightweight exploration. Marina Kaštela's protected anchorages, calm waters, and 15–25% lower berth rates than Split center make it ideal for casual boating. Many motorboats feature small cabins (sleeping 2–4), basic galleys, and basic navigation—sufficient for day cruises and short overnights.

Motorboats suit couples, small families, and those learning to boat. No skipper certification is required for smaller vessels; larger ones may need basic documentation.

Marina Kaštela Facilities for Motorboat Charters

Marina Kaštela accommodates approximately 400 berths with fuel docks, freshwater hookups, and basic utilities. The nearby Kaštel settlements (Gomilica, Kambelovac, Lukšić) provide rapid provisioning, fuel refills, and repair access. Restaurants on-site and in nearby villages cater to motorboat charterers. For day-trip operations, Marina Kaštela offers secure parking, equipment storage, and friendly staff assistance.

The marina's proximity to Split (15 min) ensures access to larger repair facilities and premium provisioning if needed.

Motorboat Routes and Day-Trip Options

Half-day trips from Marina Kaštela reach nearby beaches (Bol on Brač, local swimming spots), coves, and fishing grounds. Full-day trips push to Hvar Town, Vis, or quiet anchorages on Šolta—returning before dusk. Multi-day charters (3–4 days) cover the central Dalmatian circuit: Brač (Zlatni Rat, white stone quarries), Hvar (island culture, dining), and Vis (remote anchorages).

Motorboats excel at agile, spontaneous exploration. No sail setup, no wind wait—turn the key and depart.

Fishing and Water Sports

Many motorboats include fishing gear (lines, rods) for those seeking Adriatic fish. The waters around Brač, Hvar, and Vis offer excellent opportunities. Snorkeling is straightforward—drop anchor in clear coves and dive. Some charter companies offer water skis or towing services; confirm before booking.

Motorboat Pricing and Rates

Motorboat charters from Marina Kaštela typically cost €300–€800 per day or €1,200–€2,500 per week, depending on vessel size and season. Fuel is additional (expect €100–€300 per day depending on cruising distance). Peak summer (July–August) commands premium rates; shoulder seasons (May–June, September) offer 20–30% savings. Day-trip rentals are common; multi-day discounts apply. Compare motorboat options on Boat4You to find vessels matching your budget and adventure goals.

Best Seasons for Motorboat Boating

May through September suits motorboat operations. May offers calm water (15–18°C), comfortable air temperatures, and minimal crowds. June–September provide warmth (24–27°C) and long daylight, though July–August peak with tourists. September delivers summer warmth with lighter pressure and excellent swimming conditions. April and October work for flexible boaters; winter (November–March) is less common due to colder water and shorter daylight.

FAQ: Motorboat Charters from Marina Kaštela

Do I need a license to rent a motorboat?

For smaller motorboats (under 7m or specific horsepower thresholds), no license is required in most cases. Larger boats may require basic certification or documentation. Your charter company specifies requirements.

How much fuel will I use?

Typical motorboats burn 8–20 gallons per hour at cruising speed. A full day of boating costs roughly €80–€200 in fuel. Budget accordingly or confirm all-inclusive fuel pricing.

Can I overnight on a motorboat?

Most motorboats include small cabins with bunks and basic heads. They're suitable for occasional overnight stays but lack the space and comfort of larger yachts. Multi-night charters work for adventurous crews.

What safety equipment is included?

Life jackets, first-aid kits, fire extinguishers, and signaling equipment are standard. Your charter company briefs you on safety protocols before departure.

Can I fish from a motorboat?

Yes. Many motorboats include basic fishing gear. Local species (sea bass, grouper, squid) are abundant. Confirm gear availability and any fishing regulations with your charter company.
