Marina Zenta Facilities & Location

Marina Zenta is a small, customer-focused operation. The facility offers basic but essential services: potable water, electricity at berths, and a welcoming reception. The marina's eastern position means direct access to Bačvice beach via a short walk, plus proximity to local taverns serving fresh fish and octopus. Provisioning is straightforward—a supermarket and fruit market sit nearby.

Unlike central marinas, Zenta bay provides calmer waters and fewer ferry movements. Departure and return are relaxed affairs, andstaff coordinate directly with the marina to ensure smooth check-ins and guest briefings.

Island Routes from Marina Zenta

Brač lies 15 nautical miles south. The catamaran's shallow draft allows you to enter smaller harbors and anchor in hidden coves. Bol beach and the island's protective coastline make Brač an ideal first-night stop.

Hvar, 30 nautical miles southwest, remains the Adriatic's social hub. Catamaran charters enjoy generous deck space for sunset drinks while anchored off Hvar Town. The island's lavender-covered hills and vibrant harbor culture justify the passage.

Vis stands at 40 nautical miles, rewarding longer passages with dramatic anchorages at Komiža and the secret Green Cave. Vis's wine bars and authentic village character feel world away from Split.

Bačvice Beach & Local Life

Bačvice is Split's heart. Just uphill from Marina Zenta, this pebble beach hosts swimmers, paddleboarders, and a thriving beach-bar culture. After a day at sea, crew members can wade into the cool Adriatic or grab a macchiato at one of the beachfront cafés. Local sports teams play picigin (a traditional ball game) in the shallows each evening—a quintessential Split scene.

The adjacent Old Town is a 20-minute walk uphill. Diocletian's Palace dominates the skyline, surrounded by narrow lanes, fresh-juice stands, and family restaurants serving traditional Dalmatian fare.

Seasonal Sailing & Best Times

Spring (April–May) brings consistent thermals and warm days. Water temperatures hover around 17–18°C, suiting early-season explorers. Summer (June–August) delivers reliable westerly winds (maestral) ideal for catamaran passages, though July and August crowds intensify.

Autumn (September–October) offers the best combination: warm water (22–23°C), gentle sailing conditions, and manageable traffic. Winter sailing is possible but sporadic and short-dayed.

Dalmatian cuisine centers on seafood and slow cooking. Pašticada (marinated beef stew), soparnik (chard pie), and fresh grilled fish define the regional taste.
