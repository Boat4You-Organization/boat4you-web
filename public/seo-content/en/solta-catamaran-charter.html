Šolta's Anchorages & Pine-Backed Bays

Nečujam bay, on Šolta's eastern shore, ranks among the Adriatic's most serene natural harbors. Surrounded by fragrant Aleppo pines reaching down to white-sand beaches, Nečujam offers anchor-hold in soft sand and excellent holding in 4–8 meters. Tavernas line the waterfront; fresh fish dinners happen steps from your catamaran's swim platform.

Stomorska, to the north, serves as Šolta's principal settlement. A modest fishing village with a working harbor, Stomorska provides provisioning, fuel, and water. Its small marina accommodates visiting catamarans; the village's daily catch appears nightly at family-run restaurants overlooking your mooring.

Maslinica, on the island's northwestern point, commands attention. This deep, protected harbor opened centuries ago as a Venetian shipyard and remains one of the Adriatic's most picturesque anchorages. Cliff-side homes, stone cottages, and a narrow waterfront street create a postcard setting. Local olive-oil producers use centuries-old trees; tastings and sales happen informally at harborside stands.

The Quiet Sailing Alternative

While Brač and Hvar attract flotillas and party boats, Šolta remains refreshingly calm. Catamaran charters here emphasize slow sailing, reading on deck, swimming in transparent water, and genuine connection with island life. Winds tend gentle and predictable during summer months. Day sails to nearby Brač's quieter southern beaches (Lovrecina, Radonja) take just two hours. Evening sails to Trogir's Venetian old town lie within reach for those seeking a cultural day-trip.

Marina Facilities & Local Logistics

Stomorska harbor provides basic services: fuel dock, potable water, and seasonal mooring availability. Reception assistance coordinates anchoring permissions and local recommendations. The village supermarket stocks provisioning staples. Open-air markets in Stomorska appear seasonally; locals sell fresh vegetables, olive oil, and wine directly from their homes.

Maslinica marina, private but welcoming to Boat4You charters, offers protected berths and access to harbourside tavernas. Nečujam has no formal marina but centuries of successful anchoring history; local fishermen know the holding grounds intimately and often offer informal advice.

Provisioning & Island Flavors

Šolta's food culture centers on simplicity: grilled fish, homemade pasta with local seafood, and abundant olive oil. Taverna meals cost less than equivalent fare on Hvar or Vis. Local wines include Posip (dry white) and Rukatac (red blend), both grown on Šolta's sheltered slopes. Nečujam's waterfront restaurants prepare fresh catch for dinner; ordering at midday guarantees the day's best selection.

Maslinica's annual olive-oil festivals celebrate the island's primary crop. Visiting during autumn (September–October) lets you purchase certified-organic oil directly from producers—ideal for provisioning your catamaran's galley.

Seasonal Sailing & Weather

Spring (April–May) brings warm days and calm mornings; the Adriatic reaches 17–19°C. Summer offers reliable winds (Force 3–4 from the northwest) and blue skies, though July–August see peak heat and modest crowding. Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (22–23°C), gentler winds, and fewer visiting boats. Winter sailing is rare but possible for experienced sailors; November–February bring occasional gales and short daylight.

Most catamaran charters operate May through October. Boat4You recommends June–July and September–early October for the ideal balance of weather, temperature, and solitude.

FAQ: Catamaran Charters from Šolta

How far is Šolta from Split airport?

Split airport (Kaštela) lies 25 km from Split city center. Ferry to Šolta (40-minute crossing) departs from Split harbor; total travel time is roughly 90 minutes. Alternatively, a water taxi from Split takes 30 minutes and costs €60–€100.

Is Šolta suitable for first-time bareboat sailors?

Yes. The calm anchorages, light winds, and short passages between islands make Šolta ideal for learning catamaran sailing. Boat4You's process includes a thorough briefing and available skipper assistance if needed.

Can I provision my catamaran on Šolta itself?

Absolutely. Stomorska's supermarket stocks staples, fresh produce, and locally-produced wines and oils. Maslinica and Nečujam have smaller shops and direct sales from residents. Planning provisioning in Split before departure ensures maximum selection.

What's the best anchorage for families with children?

Nečujam bay. Its soft sand bottom, gradual approach, and sheltered beach make it perfect for families. The tavernas nearby welcome children; swimming and snorkeling happen safely in calm, clear water.

Are there day-sail destinations from Šolta?

Yes. Brač's quieter southern beaches, Trogir's medieval old town, and Drvenik island all lie within 2–4 hours under sail. provides detailed route suggestions during your charter briefing.
