Regional Sailing Routes & Wind Patterns

Kornati National Park, 20 nautical miles south (3–4 hours under sail), offers legendary sailing through 140 islands. The park's unique geometry—narrow channels, protected bays, limestone ridges—provides everything from sheltered passages to exhilarating open-water stretches. Popular anchorages include Sipan, Katić, and Žut. Sailing yachts benefit from steady afternoon winds, manageable current, and excellent holding in sandy bottoms.

Telašćica Bay, 25 nautical miles southwest (4–5 hours), rewards sailing crews with a natural saltwater lake and dramatic limestone cliffs. The passage requires navigation through narrow entrance channel, but protected deep-water mooring accommodates even sizeable yachts. Many multi-day itineraries anchor here, exploring the bay's unique marine environment.

Pašman and Ugljan islands (8–12 nautical miles northeast, 1.5–2.5 hours) provide sheltered passages and village anchorages. Narrow straits between islands create protected sailing grounds ideal for less experienced crews. Fishing villages offer provisioning, authentic hospitality, and excellent waterfront restaurants.

Dugi Otok's southern beaches (15–20 nautical miles west, 3–4 hours) feature pristine Sakarun sand beach and remote anchorages. Sailing yachts navigate coastal passages offering varied wind conditions and stunning seascapes. Longer passages toward Split remain possible for experienced crews.

Seasonal Sailing Conditions

Spring (April–May) brings warm days (18–22°C), calm mornings, and predictable afternoon Maestral winds of 10–14 knots—ideal for practicing crews. Water temperatures reach 17–18°C. Summer (June–August) delivers consistent 12–18 knot winds and brilliant sunshine, though July–August brings peak congestion. Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (20–22°C), moderate winds (8–14 knots), and fewer boats—many sailors' favorite season. Winter sailing is possible but brings shorter days and occasional stronger Bura winds.

recommends May and September for optimal wind consistency, comfortable temperatures, and reasonable anchorage availability. These shoulders seasons offer the best balance of conditions and pricing.

Local Winds & Sailing Characteristics

The Maestral (northwesterly) dominates May through September, typically building 11 AM–6 PM at 12–18 knots. Morning offshore Bura winds are less common but can strengthen to 15+ knots. Sirocco (southeasterly) winds appear occasionally, bringing warm, humid conditions. Tidal range is minimal (0.4 meters), so anchoring relies on holding rather than tidal effect. Water temperatures: 11°C (winter), 17–18°C (spring), 21–24°C (summer–autumn).
