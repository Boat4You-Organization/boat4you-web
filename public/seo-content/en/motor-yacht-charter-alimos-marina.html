Flisvos Coast and Athenian Riviera by Motor Yacht

The Flisvos coast—extending from Piraeus south to Cape Sounion—is a playground for motor yachts. Glyfada, Vouliagmeni, and Varkiza offer world-class beach clubs with reserved mooring; your crew coordinates parking and arranges transfers ashore. The coastal towns feature Michelin-starred restaurants, designer boutiques, and art galleries accessible by tender or short motorboat ride.

An alternative itinerary anchors at remote coves: Anavyssos Bay offers solitude and the Temple of Poseidon views. Lagonissi Island provides turquoise anchorages and pristine snorkelling. The Riviera's proximity to Athens (30 minutes by tender to downtown) allows mid-week cultural excursions: Acropolis visits, museum tours, and fine dining—all returning to your private yacht sanctuary by evening.

Cyclades Odyssey: Islands and Superyacht Culture

Longer charters venture into the Cyclades archipelago. Mykonos (90 nm from Alimos, 6–8 hours) is a superyacht hub; anchor at Paradise Beach or Psarou and experience legendary nightlife from your yacht's tenders. Delos (nearby) offers archaeological wonders accessible by morning exploration before afternoon beach club lounging.

Paros and Antiparos attract culture-seekers: wine tastings, marble workshops, and family-run tavernas serve fresh Mediterranean cuisine. Naxos is quieter, offering traditional villages, Byzantine monasteries, and golden-sand beaches. A 10-day Cyclades odyssey showcases island variety while allowing 2–3 nights anchored per location—sufficient for deep exploration without rushed itineraries.

Water Sports and Activities

Motor yachts carry inflatable tenders, water skis, kayaks, and snorkelling equipment. Crew members assist with launching and retrieval; some yachts have dedicated water sports staff. Swimming platforms (extending from the stern) allow easy water entry; hot freshwater showers on deck rinse salt away. Fishing enthusiasts can arrange deep-sea expeditions in summer; guides and specialist tackle are coordinated by crew.

Onboard entertainment includes satellite television, music systems, and game libraries. Some yachts have submarine viewers, underwater cameras, and drone photography systems. Seasons and Mediterranean Conditions

Summer (June–August) offers reliable sunshine, calm seas, and busy superyacht season. Spring and autumn are equally pleasant with fewer crowds. Winter (November–March) is viable for experienced skippers; variable weather and occasional gales require flexible itineraries, but empty anchorages and peaceful island stays reward adventurous operators.

FAQ

What's the minimum charter duration for a motor yacht?

Minimum charters typically range 3–7 days, though some operators accept shorter periods (2 days) at premium rates. Weekly charters (7 days) are standard; 10-day and 2-week charters are common for incentive trips and multi-destination odysseys. Boat4You's search tool filters by charter length and vessel availability.

What's the typical cost range for a motor yacht charter?

A 45-foot motor yacht (8–10 guests) costs €5,000–8,000/week in shoulder season, €8,000–12,000 in summer. A 60-foot yacht, €12,000–25,000/week. Superyachts (70+ metres), €30,000–150,000+/week depending on size, age, and amenities. Costs include crew, fuel, and mooring; beverages, special provisions, and water sports may incur extras. displays all-inclusive pricing.

Are crews included in the charter price?

Yes. All motor yacht charters include professional crew (captain, stewardess, chef, engineer). Guest-to-crew ratios vary by yacht size; luxury charters prioritise high ratios (1 crew member per 2–3 guests). Tipping (10–15% of charter cost) is customary; it's often distributed among crew members by the captain.

Can I customise the itinerary during my charter?

Absolutely. Your captain and crew are flexible partners in experience design. Want to extend time at an anchorage? Modify the route? Visit an unplanned island? Crew accommodates these requests (weather and fuel constraints permitting). The advantage of motor yachts is speed: you can cover vast distances, allowing spontaneous itinerary changes without missing planned highlights.

What happens in case of medical emergency?

Motor yachts maintain 24/7 communication with coast guards and medical services. Most Mediterranean regions offer helicopter evacuation within 30 minutes; larger superyachts carry advanced first-aid equipment and telemedicine systems. Crew are trained in first aid; the captain coordinates with nearest hospital if evacuation is needed. Medical travel insurance is recommended (often included in luxury travel packages).
