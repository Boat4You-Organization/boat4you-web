Top Routes and Destinations from Fethiye

The waters surrounding Fethiye offer countless memorable itineraries. The most popular route sails eastward along the Lycian coast, visiting the famous Ölüdeniz Blue Lagoon—a crescent beach of stunning white sand backed by a forested hillside, located just 15 kilometres south of Fethiye. This protected lagoon is perfect for swimming, snorkelling, and spotting sea turtles. Nearby, Butterfly Valley cuts into the cliffs, accessible only by sea or a steep mountain trail, creating a secluded sanctuary for the endemic butterflies that gather here during summer months.

Further along the coast lies the ancient city of Tlos, one of the most important Lycian settlements, with ruins perched dramatically above the sea. Xanthos, another major Lycian stronghold, offers remarkable rock tombs carved directly into the cliff face. These ancient sites provide extraordinary photo opportunities and a tangible connection to history spanning over two thousand years.

Saint Nicholas Island (also called Gemiler) is a magical overnight anchorage surrounded by the remains of Byzantine churches and monasteries. The island is uninhabited and protected, making it a peaceful retreat where your gulet becomes a floating base for swimming, kayaking, and exploring the underwater archaeology. Kayaköy ghost village sits on the hillside above nearby Ölüdeniz, its whitewashed stone houses frozen in time, offering a poignant glimpse into a 1920s Greek-Turkish exchange.

Best Seasons for Your Fethiye Gulet Charter

The optimal season for gulet sailing from Fethiye runs from mid-May through mid-October, with July and August offering the most stable winds and warmest water temperatures. The meltemi wind blows most reliably from June through August, creating ideal sailing conditions and keeping the vessel cool during daylight hours. Spring months (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer fewer crowds, warmer temperatures than northern Europe, and often gentler winds—perfect for guests seeking a more leisurely pace or those less experienced with rough sea conditions.

Winter sailing is possible but less common, as weather becomes less predictable and some local services reduce hours. However, December through February does attract sailors seeking warm-weather escapes, and can arrange winter itineraries for well-equipped vessels and experienced crews familiar with seasonal conditions.

Local Flavours, Cuisine, and Provisioning

One of the great pleasures of a gulet charter is the onboard cuisine. Turkish mezes—small appetisers including stuffed grape leaves, yoghurt dips, fresh cheese, olives, and roasted vegetables—feature prominently on your daily menu. Raki, the anise-flavoured spirit, is traditional with evening mezes, often diluted with water and shared among friends. Turkish breakfasts are legendary: fresh bread, honey, local cheese, cucumber, tomato, egg, and strong Turkish coffee set the tone for each day.

Fethiye's markets, located near the waterfront, overflow with seasonal produce—strawberries and cherries in spring, peaches and melons in summer. The fish market operates daily, allowing your gulet's chef to select fresh sea bream, sea bass, calamari, and mussels from local fishermen. can pre-arrange provisioning to match any dietary preferences, and many gulets prepare all meals onboard using fresh, local ingredients prepared in authentic Mediterranean style.

Activities and Water Sports

Beyond swimming and snorkelling, gulet charters offer opportunities for kayaking from the deck, diving at protected sites, paddleboarding, and fishing. Many vessels carry tenders (small motorboats) allowing exploration of narrow inlets and visits to remote anchorages. Underwater archaeology sites near Saint Nicholas Island appeal to certified divers, while casual snorkellers enjoy spotting octopus, grouper, and bream in crystal-clear coves.

Evening activities often include traditional Turkish folk performances in harbourside tavernas, sunset aperitifs on the gulet's deck, and stargazing in the quiet anchorages far from light pollution. Some itineraries include visits to local artisan villages where you can watch carpet-weaving and pottery-making—ancient crafts still practised much as they were centuries ago.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a gulet and a motor yacht?

Gulets are traditionally designed sailing vessels built from wood, with two or three masts and auxiliary engines. They prioritise authentic sailing experience, on-deck living, and slower cruising pace. Motor yachts are sleek, modern, fast-moving vessels ideal for covering long distances. Gulets suit those seeking romance and tradition; motor yachts suit those prioritising speed and modern amenities.

Is a gulet charter suitable for non-experienced sailors?

Absolutely. gulets come with professional crews handling all sailing. Your role is purely to relax, swim, eat, and explore. You rarely need to touch a rope unless you wish to. Families, elderly guests, and first-time sailors all thrive on gulet charters.

What is included in a typical gulet charter?

Charter fees cover the vessel, crew, all meals, water, fuel, and mooring in protected harbours. Not included: alcoholic beverages (unless pre-arranged), crew gratuities (typically 10% of total), diving certifications, or excursions requiring additional fees.

What about seasickness or rough weather?

Gulets sail primarily in summer when the Aegean is calmest. Sheltered anchorages and protected bays mean you're rarely far from calm water. If weather deteriorates, crews divert to nearby harbours. Over-the-counter remedies, ginger supplements, and anti-nausea bands help most travellers; discuss concerns With us before booking.

Can we bring small children or infants?

Yes. Many gulets carry families with young children. Discuss specific requirements (cots, high chairs, dietary needs) during booking. Calm summer anchorages and generous deck spaces make gulets ideal for families, though supervision near water is essential as it is everywhere.
