Sailing Routes from Alimos Marina

The Saronic Gulf is a catamaran paradise. Aegina lies just 15 nautical miles north-west; its pine-covered hills and sheltered harbour are reachable in 2–3 hours. Poros (28 nm) sits at the gateway to the Peloponnese, with narrow straits and postcard beaches. Hydra (40 nm) is legendary among sailors for its carless island and traditional stone mansions. Spetses (55 nm) offers a longer passage but rewards with golden beaches and maritime heritage.

For a classic one-week Saronic loop, depart Alimos, visit Aegina for overnight, push to Poros the next day, then Hydra, and return via Spetses and smaller anchorages. Moderate winds (12–16 knots in summer) and calm waters make this route ideal for intermediate sailors. In spring and autumn, longer itineraries to the Cyclades (Mykonos, Delos, Paros) are popular; the passage takes 18–24 hours, but the reward is iconic island culture and world-class provisioning.

Provisioning and Local Flavour at Kalamaki

Alimos Marina sits in the Kalamaki suburb, minutes from major supermarkets (Lidl and other chains) stocked with Greek produce, European wines, and international provisions. Weekend markets in Kalamaki offer seasonal fruits, vegetables, and artisanal products ideal for onboard meals.

Best Seasons for Catamaran Charter from Alimos

Summer (June–September) offers predictable northerly winds (Etesian pattern), calm seas, and reliable weather—perfect for family charters. Water temperatures reach 25–28°C, ideal for swimming and snorkelling. However, popular islands are crowded and mooring fees are at peak rates.

Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) provide gentler winds, fewer crowds, and lower costs. Water remains warm enough for daily swims (20–24°C). Early June and late September are the sweet spot: wind is still reliable, weather windows are generous, and you'll find peaceful anchorages and attentive taverna owners. Winter (November–March) brings variable weather and occasional gales, best suited to experienced skippers.

Bareboat vs. Crewed Catamaran Charters

Bareboat catamarans suit sailors with 2+ years Mediterranean experience and a valid skipper's licence. You navigate, cook, and manage the vessel independently—a liberating, immersive holiday. Crewed options pair your catamaran with a professional captain and often a chef, ideal for groups wanting relaxation, gourmet meals, and expert knowledge of hidden anchorages. FAQ

Do I need a sailing licence to charter a catamaran from Alimos?

Yes. Most bareboat operators require an IYT (International Yacht Training) Competent Crew or Day Skipper certificate, or equivalent national qualification (RYA Competent Crew, equivalent). If you lack formal certification but have substantial offshore experience, some operators conduct a practical assessment. Crewed charters have no licence requirement; the captain handles all navigation and helm.

What is the typical cost range for a one-week catamaran charter?

A 40-foot bareboat catamaran in summer costs €1,200–2,000 per week; luxury 50-footers, €2,500–4,500. Crewed catamarans (6–8 passengers) range €5,000–15,000+ per week depending on size, season, and crew experience. Boat4You's search tool filters by budget and vessel size to show exact pricing and availability.

Can I moor at Alimos Marina with my rented catamaran?

Yes. The charter company arranges your berth at Alimos before arrival. Mooring is included in most bareboat packages, though fuel, food, and end-of-charter cleaning are extras. Crewed charters typically include mooring but may charge separately for premium berths in peak season.

How far is Alimos Marina from Athens city centre?

Alimos is 45 minutes by car (taxi or rental) from Athens International Airport, and 20 minutes by metro or car from Piraeus port. For pre-charter sightseeing (Acropolis, museums), budget a day in Athens before heading to the marina; the metro connects Alimos to downtown in ~30 minutes.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
