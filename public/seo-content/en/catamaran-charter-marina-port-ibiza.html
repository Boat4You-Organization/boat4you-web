Catamaran Charter from Marina Port Ibiza

Ibiza's historic Marina Port lies in the heart of the Mediterranean's most vibrant sailing destination. Moored just steps from the UNESCO-listed old town of Dalt Vila, with its 13th-century fortifications overlooking turquoise waters, a catamaran charter from Marina Port Ibiza offers the perfect blend of cultural immersion and island exploration. Boat4You specializes in catamaran rentals that let you set sail toward Formentera's white-sand beaches, the mystical silhouette of Es Vedrà, and a chain of unspoilt anchorages only accessible by water.

Why a Catamaran from Marina Port Ibiza?

Catamarans are the ideal platform for Ibiza sailing. Their shallow draft means you can anchor in places where monohulls cannot—the turquoise shallows of Playa de Migjorn, the limestone caves of Cala d'Hort, and the serene beaches of Formentera's Cala Saona. The twin-hull stability eliminates the heel familiar to traditional sailing yachts, making them perfect for families, non-sailors, and anyone seeking a laid-back bareboat or skippered experience. Ibiza's moderate Embat breeze—a consistent northeasterly that picks up in morning and settles by evening—suits catamaran sailing perfectly, offering steady wind without the intensity that monohulls crave.

Marina Port's central location saves precious vacation time. Rather than motoring 12 nautical miles to reach open water, you leave the dock and reach the Balearic sailing lanes within 20 minutes. The marina itself sits in the Ibiza harbour basin, sheltered from Atlantic swell and home to a mix of working fishing boats, superyachts, and leisure craft from around the world.

Catamaran Routes from Marina Port Ibiza

A week-long catamaran charter opens the entire Ibiza archipelago. Most sailors make Formentera their first overnight destination—just 20 nautical miles south—arriving by late afternoon to drop anchor off Illetas and enjoy sunset drinks at Chiringuito beachclubs. From Formentera, you can island-hop to tiny Espalmador (uninhabited, fringed with white sand), then northward to Cala Saona's protected anchorage, where local red snapper and sea bass grace every evening's menu.

The island of Es Vedrà deserves a full sailing day. Approaching from the northeast, the red limestone monolith rises from the sea like a phantom, clearer on calm mornings. Anchor off neighbouring Cala d'Hort (5 fathoms sandy bottom) and explore hidden cave entrances by dinghy. Return to Marina Port for reprovisioning, then venture northwest toward the wild coast at Portinatx, where wind-sculpted cliffs meet pristine anchorages.

For the culturally curious, a day-sail to Dalt Vila itself (your catamaran secured to a mooring in the inner harbour) lets you climb the old town's winding streets, visit the Cathedral and Castle, and dine at harborside restaurants while your boat rocks gently beneath you. No other sailing destination offers such seamless integration of history and hedonism.

Marina Port Ibiza Facilities and Services

Marina Port has undergone significant upgrades in recent years. The facility offers 150+ berths, including dedicated catamaran moorings (ideal beam widths up to 8.5 metres). The reception office is open 08:00–20:00 daily, providing fresh water, 230V/400V shore power, and fuel (diesel and petrol). A pump-out station serves sewage, and the marina stocks basic provisions—ice, gas bottles, engine oil, fenders, and charts. For more serious reprovisioning, the Mercat Vell (Old Market) sits just 200 metres away, selling fresh vegetables, fish, and Spanish cheeses.

The marina's in-house chandlery carries essential spares and sailing gear. If you need a mechanic or electrician, the marina coordinates with local specialists who can reach your catamaran within two hours. WiFi coverage is reliable throughout the basin, essential for checking weather windows and communicating with home.

Sailing Seasons and Weather

The ideal catamaran season in Ibiza runs from May through October. June and September offer the best combination of steady winds (12–16 knots from the northeast) and warm, swimmable water (24–26°C). July and August can bring stronger thermal winds and more crowded anchorages, though the water peaks at 27°C. Spring and autumn are excellent for those willing to sail in lighter winds and accept occasional rainy days; the landscape is lusher, provisions fresher, and the islands quieter.

Winter gales are rare but possible. Storm systems track from the Atlantic toward Gibraltar, occasionally bringing southwesterly gales. If you're sailing November–March, ensure your catamaran charter includes a comprehensive weather briefing and contingency planning.

Local Character and Provisioning

Ibiza's reputation precedes it—the island is famous for nightlife, but the sailor's Ibiza is quieter, more ancestral. The whitewashed villages of San Rafael and Santa Agnes retain traditional agriculture and artisan workshops. Local fishermen still use traditional methods, and conversations at the marina bar often turn to recent sightings of dolphins, tuna schools, and the occasional sunfish. Boat4You's catamaran charters include detailed briefings on where to eat—family-run beachclubs that serve the day's catch, Formentera's famous seafood paella, and the island's surprising craft-beer and natural-wine scenes.

Stock your galley at Marina Port, or—if you prefer ease—arrange provisions via Boat4You before departure. Fresh bread, cured jamón, Ibiza's renowned goat cheese, and Spanish canned goods (conservas) transform simple boat meals into Mediterranean feasts. Most anchorages have small grocery stores reachable by dinghy, and Formentera's waterfront shops sell ice, fresh fish, and cold wine until sunset.

Bareboat vs. Skippered Charter

Marina Port caters to both experienced sailors and newcomers. If you hold a valid sailing certificate (IYC, RYA Coastal Skipper, or equivalent), Boat4You offers bareboat catamarans—you captain the boat, make the decisions, and experience Ibiza's waters at your own pace. The marina provides a detailed briefing on local hazards (a few rocky outcrops near Cala d'Hort, strong current near Es Vedrà), VHF channels, and refuge harbours.

First-time sailors and those wanting local knowledge should book a skippered charter. Your professional skipper navigates the shoals, handles mooring etiquette, and steers you toward secret anchorages known only to locals. They'll teach you basic seamanship, share island stories, and handle the boat while you relax, swim, and soak in the landscape.

Frequently Asked Questions

What experience do I need to charter a catamaran from Marina Port Ibiza?

For a bareboat charter, Boat4You requires a valid sailing qualification (RYA Coastal Skipper, IYC, or equivalent) and typically a minimum of 100 hours of sailing experience. If you don't meet these requirements, a skippered charter is perfect—your experienced skipper handles all navigation and seamanship while you relax and enjoy the sailing.

What is the best time to visit Ibiza for a catamaran charter?

May through October is optimal. June and September deliver steady northeast winds (12–16 knots), warm water, and manageable crowds. July and August are busier but offer peak water temperatures. Spring and autumn sailing is excellent if you prefer solitude and cooler weather.

Can we take children on a catamaran charter from Marina Port?

Absolutely. Catamarans' spacious decks, shallow draft, and gentle motion make them ideal for families. can provision the galley with kid-friendly foods, arrange shorter sailing days to quieter anchorages, and pair your charter with a professional skipper if you prefer to supervise rather than navigate.

How far is Marina Port Ibiza from the airport?

Ibiza International Airport is just 8 kilometres (5 miles) south of Marina Port. A taxi or rental car takes 15–20 minutes in normal traffic. Many charter clients combine airport arrival with a waterfront lunch at the marina before boarding their catamaran.

Do I need insurance to charter a catamaran?

Yes. includes comprehensive boat insurance in the charter fee, covering hull, third-party liability, and equipment damage (excess applies to bareboat charters). Additional personal travel or cancellation insurance is recommended and available through your travel agent.
