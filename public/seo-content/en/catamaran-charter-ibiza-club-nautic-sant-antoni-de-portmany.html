Premier Routes and Day Sail Options

Your catamaran adventure from Sant Antoni opens access to some of the Mediterranean's most coveted destinations. The short sail to Formentera—just 30 nautical miles south—is a quintessential day trip. Crystal-clear waters, white sand beaches at Playa Migjorn and Playa Llevant, and excellent fish restaurants make Formentera an unmissable excursion.

For a longer voyage, plot a course southeastward to Cabrera Island, a protected national maritime-terrestrial park located 60 nautical miles away. The journey takes approximately six to eight hours under sail, but rewards you with pristine anchorages, dramatic cliffsides, and historic castle ruins. The Embat breeze—a gentle thermal wind from the south—typically kicks in by mid-morning, providing ideal sailing conditions for this route.

Closer to home, explore the northern Ibiza coast, including visits to Punta Xarraca and Cala Llentrisca, both renowned for snorkelling and clear visibility. Multi-day itineraries can combine Formentera with stops at small, exclusive anchorages in the Es Vedrà region, where you'll witness some of the Mediterranean's most dramatic sunset scenery.

Best Sailing Seasons and Wind Patterns

Ibiza benefits from a Mediterranean climate with distinct sailing seasons. Late May through September offers warm, stable weather with moderate winds—ideal for leisurely cruising. The Tramontana wind, a powerful northwesterly pattern, occasionally sweeps the region in summer but typically favours afternoon sailing with excellent conditions for more experienced sailors seeking dynamic passages.

Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) provide the finest all-round conditions: comfortable air temperatures (20–24°C), steady breezes, and reduced summer tourist congestion. Winter sailing is also possible for hardy adventurers, with occasional strong westerlies and Atlantic storm systems. Our crews monitor meteorological patterns daily and adjust itineraries accordingly to ensure your safety and comfort.

Local Cuisine and Provisioning

Ibiza's culinary scene blends traditional Balearic recipes with Mediterranean influences. Fresh seafood—grilled sea bream, langoustine, and local lobster—feature prominently in island cuisine. For your onboard provisions, request local cheeses, jamón ibérico, and Pan de Ibiza (a traditional seeded bread). Local wines from Do Pla and Do Binissalem complement your meals perfectly.

Sant Antoni's weekly market (Wednesday and Saturday mornings) offers exceptional fresh produce, fish, and artisanal goods. Your crew can arrange shore excursions to shop or dine at waterfront tavernas specializing in arròs caldoso (soupy rice with seafood) and caldereta de langosta (lobster stew). For dinner onboard, our optional chef service prepares bespoke menus using fresh local ingredients purchased daily.

Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifications do I need to charter a catamaran from Sant Antoni?

If sailing with a captain and crew, no sailing qualifications are required. Our professional captain handles all navigational and operational duties. If you wish to sail bareboat (without crew), you must hold an RYA Level 2 certification or equivalent. can arrange onboard training for less experienced sailors.

Is travel insurance recommended?

Yes, comprehensive travel insurance covering cancellations, medical emergencies, and personal accident is highly recommended. Our charter packages include yacht insurance, but your personal belongings and medical coverage are your responsibility. Consult your insurance provider regarding nautical activities.

Can I bring my family and young children aboard?

Absolutely. Catamarans are exceptionally family-friendly due to their spacious layouts and stable platform. Children as young as six months old sail comfortably with us. Life jackets in all sizes are provided, and our crew includes experienced guides trained in family-friendly anchorages and activities.

What happens if weather conditions deteriorate?

Your safety is paramount. Our captain monitors forecasts continuously and adjusts the itinerary to ensure comfortable, safe sailing. If severe weather threatens, we remain in port or return to Club Nàutic. Partial refunds or credit toward future charters apply in cases of significant itinerary modification due to weather.

Are gratuities included in the quoted price?

No, crew gratuities (typically 10% of charter cost) are customary but not obligatory. Many guests provide €10–15 per day per crew member. All other costs—fuel, mooring, crew wages, insurance—are fully included in 's all-inclusive pricing.
