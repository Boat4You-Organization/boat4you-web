Catamarans are the ideal vessel choice for exploring Castellammare's diverse coastline. The spacious dual-hull design provides exceptional stability in moderate seas, making them perfect for families and groups seeking comfort without sacrificing performance. Discover the Zingaro Nature Reserve

The Zingaro Nature Reserve, located just 7 kilometers from Castellammare del Golfo, represents Sicily's most pristine coastal ecosystem. From your chartered catamaran, you'll access isolated beaches, underwater cave systems, and Mediterranean pine forests clinging to dramatic cliffsides. The reserve's waters are protected, ensuring exceptional snorkeling opportunities and encounters with endemic marine species. Anchor near Cala Tonnarella or Cala Berretta for an unforgettable coastal experience that captures Sicily's raw natural beauty.

Tuna Fishery Heritage and Cultural Attractions

Castellammare del Golfo carries centuries of tuna fishing tradition. The tonnara, or historic tuna trap system, shaped local culture and remains visible along the coastline today. Explore the working harbor where traditional fishing boats still operate alongside modern vessels. The Arab-Norman tower-castle overlooking the harbor dates back centuries, offering stunning vistas of the walled gulf. Boat4You can arrange cultural excursions to local fish markets, traditional processing facilities, and waterfront restaurants specializing in fresh catch of the day.

Faraglioni Rock Stacks and Scopello

The distinctive faraglioni rock stacks near Scopello present one of Sicily's most photographed coastal landmarks. These towering limestone formations rise dramatically from azure waters, creating natural sculptures that seem impossible. Your catamaran provides the perfect platform for circumnavigating these geological wonders and capturing stunning photographs. The shallow anchorages nearby offer excellent conditions for swimming, snorkeling, and observing the abundant marine life that inhabits these protected waters.

Beach Hopping and Cala Rossa

Castellammare del Golfo offers numerous pristine beach options accessible only by sea. Cala Rossa, renowned for its dramatic red cliffs and golden sand, provides an idyllic daytime anchorage. Your catamaran's spacious deck areas are perfect for sunbathing, while the shallow waters near shore offer safe swimming and water sports activities. Bring snorkeling gear to explore underwater seagrass meadows and colorful Mediterranean fish species. The calm anchorages throughout the gulf create ideal conditions for both active water sports and tranquil relaxation.

Day 5 returns via Scopello, completing this quintessential Sicilian sailing loop that combines family comfort, cultural immersion, and dramatic Mediterranean seascapes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I book?

For peak season (July and August), reservations 4–6 months ahead secure the best fleet selection and pricing. Shoulder months (May, June, September) typically allow booking 6–8 weeks out.

What does the charter price include?

Base pricing usually covers the vessel, insurance, and marina fees at the home base. Fuel, provisioning, skipper service, end-cleaning, and tourist tax are generally added separately. Check the specific listing on Boat4You for a full breakdown.

Do I need a sailing licence?

For bareboat charter, a valid skipper licence plus a VHF certificate are typically required, and rules vary by country. If you don't hold one, hiring a professional skipper is straightforward — pricing runs roughly 150–220 euro per day plus meals.
