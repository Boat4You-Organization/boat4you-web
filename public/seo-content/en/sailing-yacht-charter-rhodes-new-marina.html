What Certifications Do I Need for Bareboat Sailing?

Most charter fleets require an ICC (International Certificate of Competence) or equivalent national sailing certificate demonstrating navigation, safety, and seamanship competency. Some operators accept RYA Coastal Skipper or ASA 104/105 credentials. If you lack certification, a crewed charter with a professional skipper removes this requirement—you can still sail hands-on under their supervision.

Navigation, Chart Plotting & Route Planning

Modern sailing yachts combine traditional navigation skills with contemporary technology. Electronic chartplotters (pre-loaded with detailed Eastern Mediterranean nautical charts) provide position awareness, route planning, and hazard identification. Automatic Identification Systems (AIS) alert you to nearby vessel traffic. Backup paper charts and compass skills remain essential—electronic systems occasionally fail. Your skipper (if crewed) or support team provides detailed route briefings: optimal passages given current forecasts, sheltered anchorage positions, and waypoint sequences. Waypoint navigation eliminates guesswork about hidden rocks or shallow approaches. Traditional piloting (visual navigation using landmarks) supplements electronic positioning, building navigation confidence.
