Passage Routing: Trogir, Brač, Hvar, Vis

Trogir passages depart Rogač northwestward; the Venetian old town's fortified gates, drawbridge, and medieval architecture reward cultural day-sailing and evening exploration. Nearby Kastela coast harbors smaller villages and archaeological sites. Most crews overnight in Trogir harbor and explore the old town on foot before departing the following morning.

Brač routes proceed southeastward through Šolta's eastern waters; typical passages approach Bol's spacious harbor or explore the island's quieter southern coast (Radonja, Lovrecina). These 12–15 nautical-mile passages suit learning crews and provide intermediate-difficulty navigation practice.

Hvar passages represent serious open-water sailing: 30+ nautical miles across deeper Adriatic channels. Most crews depart Rogač at dawn, progress via Šolta's eastern staging points, and arrive Hvar Town by late afternoon. This passage suits confident crews; navigation planning, weather interpretation, and sail-trimming skills matter substantively.

Infrastructure & Provisioning Logistics

Rogač harbor provides year-round services: summer fuel dock (7 AM–6 PM), water filling (year-round), basic mooring (€2–€3 per meter), and reception assistance. Provisioning occurs at the harborside supermarket and through seasonal open-air markets. Fresh-fish sales happen daily at the harbor; local fishermen sell directly from boats at fair prices.

Taverna meals cost €12–€18 per person; most establishments welcome sailing crews and offer informal group discounts. Wine selections feature local Šolta varieties (Posip white, Rukatac red); rakija (traditional brandy) concludes meals.
