Mikrolimano, also known as Mikrolímano, is a hidden gem among Piraeus harbours. Unlike the sprawling modern marinas, this intimate port retains authentic character with traditional fishing boats, family-run seafood tavernas lining the waterfront, and genuine Greek maritime heritage. Easy metro access makes Mikrolimano perfect whether you're arriving from central Athens, the airport, or nearby neighbourhoods. Berth your vessel and immediately immerse yourself in vibrant Piraeus nightlife or savour fresh-caught seafood at waterfront restaurants.

Sailing Routes from Mikrolimano

Your sailing adventure begins the moment you cast off from this historic port. The Saronic Gulf offers protected waters ideal for both beginners and experienced sailors. Common sailing itineraries include day trips to nearby Aegina island (40 minutes), where you'll find the majestic Temple of Aphaia and charming fishing villages.

Multi-day charters from Mikrolimano typically reach Poros, Hydra, and Spetses, each island offering unique culture, architecture, and natural beauty. The northern Saronic provides sheltered conditions even during summer meltemi winds (N/NE 15–25 knots July–August), making these waters significantly calmer than the Cyclades.

Practical Information for Mikrolimano Charters

Mikrolimano port operates year-round, with peak season May–September. Spring and autumn offer ideal sailing conditions: mild winds (8–15 knots), fewer crowds, and pleasant temperatures. Summer can bring stronger meltemi winds, but the Saronic's protection keeps conditions manageable.

Transportation is seamless. The Piraeus metro station is minutes away, making transfers from Athens airport or city centre straightforward. Port facilities include provisioning shops, fuel dock, and fresh-water stations. Boat4You handles all documentation, insurance, and pre-charter briefings on-site.

FAQ: Sailing Yacht Charter at Mikrolimano

What sailing experience is required for a bareboat charter from Mikrolimano?

We recommend at least Day Skipper certification or equivalent experience. Alternatively, choose our skippered option and sail alongside our expert captain who navigates and manages the yacht while you relax and enjoy.

Is Mikrolimano suitable for families with children?

Absolutely. The sheltered Saronic waters and short sailing distances (day trips to Aegina, Poros) make Mikrolimano ideal for families. Our fleet includes family-friendly vessels with safety features and spacious cabins.

What is the best time to sail from Mikrolimano?

May–June and September–October offer ideal conditions: mild winds and warm seas without July–August's intense meltemi. Year-round sailing is possible, but avoid December–February for stronger winter storms.

Are meals and provisioning included in charter packages?

Provisions are available separately. We recommend pre-ordering groceries delivered to your vessel, or purchasing fresh supplies at Mikrolimano's local shops and tavernas. Skippered charters can include prepared meals.

Can I combine a Mikrolimano charter with visits to other Saronic islands?

Yes. Most 7-day charters from Mikrolimano include island-hopping to Aegina, Poros, Hydra, and Spetses. Shorter 3–4 day charters typically cover Aegina and Poros.

Does Mikrolimano offer provisioning services?

Yes, with excellent local shops and waterfront tavernas. We coordinate pre-ordering groceries delivered to your vessel, or you can shop locally for fresh produce, wines, and provisions at competitive Greek prices. Taverna dining ashore is highly recommended and exceptionally affordable.
