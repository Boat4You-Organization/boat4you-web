Marina Mandalina Facilities & Infrastructure

D-Marin Mandalina accommodates vessels up to 240 meters, with deep-water berths ranging from 5.0 to 10.0+ meters depth. The facility features 400-amp electrical connections, high-pressure water supply, fuel dock with diesel and gasoline, marine gas oil (MGO), pump-out services, and comprehensive infrastructure for extended stays. 24-hour concierge and security services ensure guest safety and seamless logistics. The marina's repair yards include mobile cranes, haul-out capability, and specialized technicians for complex mechanical work.

Onsite amenities include waterfront restaurants and bars, boutique retail shops, laundry facilities, and high-speed Wi-Fi throughout the marina. Guest provisioning services arrange fresh produce, meat, seafood, and grocery deliveries directly to your catamaran. Professional crew recruitment, navigator assistance, and logistics coordination support bareboat and crewed charters. The marina's proximity to Šibenik's old-town attractions—museums, historical fortifications, traditional markets—creates a unique blend of maritime sophistication and Mediterranean culture.

Marina fees are premium (€1.00–€2.00+ per meter daily, depending on season and berth location), reflecting world-class facilities and 24-hour services. Mooring buoys for overnight anchorages cost substantially less; Mandalina secures guest reservations at approved anchorages throughout the Kornati Islands and surrounding coast.

Top Catamaran Routes from Marina Mandalina

The Kornati Circuit (3–5 Days): Depart Mandalina, motor/sail south 30 nm to the Kornati National Park's protected anchorages. Overnight at Opat, Vela Panitula, or Mala Proversa. Daily swimming, snorkeling, hiking, and waterfront dining create an unforgettable Mediterranean experience. Park permits (booked via Mandalina concierge) are mandatory. Return to Šibenik with time to explore the old town or relax at Mandalina's waterfront restaurants.

Krka River & Waterfall Gateway (2–3 Days): Motor upriver 9 nm to Skradin marina (navigable for most catamarans; confirm draft requirements). Board national park shuttles to the famous seven cascades (entry fee ~€15 per adult). Lunch at waterfront tavernas, then return to Šibenik or continue south to the Kornati Islands. River scenery rivals the waterfalls; this route suits families and photographers.

Northern Dalmatian Exploration (7–10 Days): Depart Šibenik, cruise north 45 nm to Zadar's ACI Marina. Explore Velebit Channel, visit Plitvice Lakes region (day excursion), anchor near Dugi Otok's dramatic eastern cliffs, and return via scenic passages. Distances total 180–220 nm; ideal for experienced crews seeking technical sailing and cultural immersion.

Central Dalmatian Grand Tour (10–14 Days): Ambitious crews complete a comprehensive coastal itinerary: Šibenik → Kornati (3 days) → Split (3 days exploring Adriatic sailing hub) → Brač & Hvar Islands (3 days) → Vis Island (2 days isolated island life) → Return via Korčula. Total distance: 250–300 nm. Premium dining, world-class beaches, and Venetian architecture enhance the experience.

Catamaran Performance & Handling at Mandalina

Modern catamarans excel in the Šibenik region's summer conditions. Dual hulls minimize heeling angle (often under 15 degrees even in 15+ knot winds), ensuring guest comfort and improved sailing efficiency. Shallow draft (1.0–1.5 meters typical) opens pristine anchorages inaccessible to monohulls. Spacious deck layouts and stable platforms ideal for families, elderly guests, and non-sailors.

Engine redundancy (twin diesel engines, 75–150 hp each) provides security for long-range cruising. Fuel consumption at cruise (8–12 knots) ranges 10–18 gallons per hour; weekly range easily exceeds 400 nautical miles. Advanced autopilot systems reduce crew fatigue. Modern catamarans carry water makers (producing 30–50 gallons fresh water daily), allowing extended remote anchorages without coastal provisioning.

Navigation from Mandalina benefits from excellent chart coverage, minimal maritime traffic (compared to Mediterranean crowding), and clear visual landmarks. Calm summer waters (June–September) reward cautious first-time bareboat operators; experienced crews enjoy technical sailing and varied conditions year-round.

Provisioning & Local Resources

Marina Mandalina's concierge arranges comprehensive provisioning. Šibenik's central market supplies fresh fish, produce, and local cheeses daily. Waterfront bakeries deliver bread for breakfast; wine shops feature Croatian coastal varietals (Babić from Šibenik, Plavac from Dalmatia) and international selections. Guest crews can request pre-provisioning (chef prepares shopping lists; Mandalina staff stocks your galley before charter departure) or self-provision using daily market visits.

Island anchorages and coastal villages offer varied dining and provisioning. Kornati National Park's island restaurants (Kornat, Opat, Vela Panitula) serve fresh grilled fish and traditional dishes; waterfront tavernas accept cards and advance radio reservations. Zadar (45 nm north) offers supermarkets, pharmacies, and extensive maritime services. Remote anchorages typically lack shops; plan provisions accordingly or rely on your catamaran's water maker and onboard storage.

Seasonal Conditions & Optimal Timing

May & June: Water 18–20°C, winds 8–14 knots from north. Longer daylight, spring flowers on islands, minimal tourism. Ideal for experienced crews building confidence in lighter conditions. Occasional afternoon thunderstorms inland; coastal protection excellent.

July & August: 22–25°C water, consistent 12–18 knot northerlies. Hot, crowded, predictable weather. Best for families and risk-averse crews; challenging for bare-feet dinghy landings due to heat and sun exposure.

September & October: 18–20°C water, variable winds. Exceptional sailing conditions, fewer tourists, warm days with cool mornings. September is often ideal for balanced sailing and comfort. October brings unpredictable Scirocco wind and deteriorating conditions; experienced crews manage successfully.

Winter (November–April) involves strong Bora/Scirocco winds, rough seas, and operational challenges; most charters pause. Spring (March–May) brings rebirth to island communities and stabilizing weather.
