Crewed vs. Bareboat Motor Yachts

Crewed motor yachts include professional captain and crew, handling navigation, maintenance, and often provisioning and cooking. Ideal for those prioritizing comfort and safety. Bareboat motor yachts require skipper certification and navigation experience; you captain the boat independently. Bareboat charters cost less but demand competence and responsibility. Marina Kaštela's staff help match your experience level to the right vessel type.

Best Seasons for Motor Yacht Sailing

May through September offers prime conditions. May brings calm seas (15–18°C water), steady wind, and minimal crowds. June–August provide warmth (24–27°C) and maximum daylight, though tourist density peaks. September delivers summer conditions with lighter crowds and excellent provisioning. April and October suit flexible schedulers; winter (November–March) is less ideal for recreational motor yachting due to cooler water and variable weather.

FAQ: Motor Yacht Charters from Marina Kaštela

What's the difference between a motor yacht and a motorboat?

Motor yachts are 30+ feet with full cabins, galleys, and navigation systems. Motorboats are smaller (15–30 feet) with basic facilities. Motor yachts offer overnight cruising comfort; motorboats suit day trips or short overnights.

Do I need a skipper's license for bareboat motor yacht?

Yes, most charter companies require documented boating experience or certification (ICC, ASA, or equivalent). Your charter company specifies requirements. Check Boat4You listings for details.

How much fuel does a motor yacht burn?

Typical 40-foot motor yachts burn 15–30 gallons per hour at cruising speed. A week-long charter costs roughly €1,000–€2,000 in fuel, depending on cruising hours and speed. Budget accordingly.

Can a motor yacht tow water sports equipment?

Yes. Many motor yachts support jet skis, wakeboards, and water skiing. Equipment availability varies by charter company. Request specifics when booking.

What if I need a crew but want a bareboat price?

Many charter companies arrange professional skippers on a daily rate, blending bareboat economics with professional navigation. Discuss hybrid options when inquiring.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.
