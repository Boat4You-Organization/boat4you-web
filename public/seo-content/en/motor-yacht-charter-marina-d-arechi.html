Marina d'Arechi: Superyacht Infrastructure & Services

Facility Infrastructure: Power, Fuel & Environmental Systems

Marina d'Arechi's electrical infrastructure features 400-amp shore power connections at superyacht berths—exceptional for Mediterranean facilities. This capacity supports air-conditioning, galley appliances, laundry operations, and guest amenities simultaneously without generator operation, reducing noise pollution and fuel consumption. Backup systems ensure uninterrupted power during utility fluctuations.

Fuel delivery operates via dedicated pump trucks; bunkering occurs at berths eliminating dock congestion. Water systems include unlimited fresh-water supply from municipal sources plus desalination capacity (water-makers) for extended offshore passages. Waste management protocols separate recyclables, hazardous materials, and routine refuse; environmental standards exceed International Maritime Organization (IMO) requirements.

Security systems include 24/7 CCTV surveillance, gated access control, and professional security personnel. Storm-shelter protocols utilize protected inner basins; Hurricane-force Mediterranean storms have been weathered without significant moored-vessel damage. Insurance partnerships expedite claims processing for incidental damage.

Motor Yacht Charter Routes: Amalfi, Islands & Cilento

Departing Marina d'Arechi, motor yachts command direct access to three premier cruising regions. The Amalfi Coast route operates northeastward: 15 km to Amalfi town (30-minute transit), 22 km to Positano (45 minutes), and 30 km to Ravello's surrounding coast. Luxury motor yachts anchor 300–500 meters offshore in protected waters; tenders deliver passengers to historic village piazzas. Overnight stays enable thorough exploration of clifftop villages, Michelin-starred restaurants, and artisanal shops.

Gourmet dining ashore highlights include Chez Black (Positano, beachfront, Michelin-recommended, reservations essential), La Gavitella (Positano, refined coastal cuisine), and Ristorante Pansea (Amalfi, contemporary Mediterranean). Shoreside excursions include hiking Faraglioni sea stacks (Positano), touring historic duomo cathedrals (Amalfi), and visiting artisanal lemon groves (Ravello)—all accessible via quick tender transits.

The Cilento Coast route extends southeastward: 25 km to Palinuro (approximately 80-minute transit), with continuation to 55 kilometers of pristine, less-crowded waters. Marina di Camerota (45 km from Marina d'Arechi) operates as an excellent provisioning stop and gourmet destination. The Cilento Marine Protected Area encompasses underwater gardens and rare seagrass meadows ideal for snorkeling and diving. White-sand beaches and dramatic rock formations (Infreschi) provide scenic anchorages and photographic opportunities.

Island routes depart southwestward: Capri (48 km, approximately 2.5–3 hours), Ischia (42 km, 2.5 hours), and Procida (45 km from Salerno). Capri's Blue Grotto boat tours, Faraglioni sea stacks, and luxury village anchorages attract affluent travelers; overnight stays allow leisurely exploration. Ischia's Mount Epomeo hiking, thermal hot springs, and natural spa facilities appeal to wellness-focused charters. Procida delivers charming harbor villages and quiet anchorages distinct from celebrity-frequented islands.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in a motor yacht charter from Marina d'Arechi?

All-inclusive charters include the vessel with professional captain and crew, all fuel and port fees, comprehensive insurance, daily provisioning, crew meals, and 24/7 support. Skippered charters typically include meal preparations (crew galley); add professional chef services (€400–800 daily) for gourmet dining. Shore excursions, restaurant reservations, and activities coordinate through your concierge but involve separate costs.

Can I charter a motor yacht for fewer than 7 days?

Yes. Minimum charter durations vary by vessel size: smaller yachts (40–50 feet) offer 3–4 day minimums; larger yachts (60+ feet) typically require 7-day minimums. Off-season (April–May, October–November) offers increased flexibility for shorter bookings. Contact Boat4You concierge to discuss your specific duration and available options.

What is fuel consumption for luxury motor yachts from Marina d'Arechi?

Fuel consumption varies by vessel size and cruising speed. A typical 60-foot motor yacht cruising at 20 knots consumes 80–120 gallons per hour. Slower cruising (10–12 knots) reduces consumption 40–50%. Weekly fuel costs (included in charter pricing) range €2,000–6,000 depending on distance traveled and vessel size. Your captain optimizes speed for fuel efficiency and schedule adherence.

Are children welcome aboard luxury motor yachts?

Absolutely. Many families charter motor yachts for multi-week Mediterranean explorations. Professional crews accommodate children with activities, age-appropriate meals, and safety protocols. Tenders, water sports (snorkeling, paddleboards), and shore excursions create memorable family experiences. Notify Boat4You during booking to arrange child-appropriate accommodations and activities.

What medical support is available aboard motor yachts from Marina d'Arechi?

Marina d'Arechi provides helipad services for emergency medical transport (15–30 minutes to Salerno Regional Hospital, Naples equivalent facilities). Onboard medical kits include advanced first-aid supplies; crew includes trained first-aid personnel. Your captain maintains 24/7 radio communication with Coast Guard and medical services. Pre-existing medical conditions should be disclosed during booking to coordinate specialized support if needed.
