Athens Riviera: Beaches and Sunset Cruises

The Athens Riviera—a string of sandy beaches and seaside villages stretching 25 km south from central Athens—is motorboat cruising heaven. Vouliagmeni, Glyfada, and Varkiza are the most famous: high-end beach clubs with bars, restaurants, and calm anchorages. A morning motorboat departure from Alimos Marina can reach Vouliagmeni's turquoise waters in 20 minutes; you anchor off a quiet beach, swim, grab lunch at a waterfront taverna, and motor back for an evening aperitif in Athens.

Sunset cruises—departing late afternoon, cruising the Riviera as the sun descends, then returning to Athens for dinner—are perennial favourites. The golden evening light, calm evening breezes, and possibility of dolphins or sea birds make these cruises memorable and romantic. Boat4You offers pre-planned sunset itineraries; many clients customize routes based on whim.

Day Charters and Island Hopping

Motorboats can reach Aegina (25 nm southeast) in 2–3 hours, making full-day island charters feasible. A typical day-charter departs Alimos 08:00, reaches Aegina by 10:30, anchors for swimming and lunch, and returns to Athens by 17:00—ideal for clients with limited vacation time. From Aegina, fast motorboats can push toward Poros or explore northern Saronic anchorages in a single dynamic day.

Speed is the motorboat's defining advantage: destinations that sailing yachts reach in 5–6 hours are motorboat destinations in 2–3 hours. This efficiency appeals to couples and small groups (2–6 people) who want maximum exploration and sightseeing with minimal time at sea.

Glyfada and Vouliagmeni: Ultimate Riviera Experience

Glyfada, just 20 km south of Athens' city centre, is the Riviera's premier beach. Home to upscale resorts, beach clubs, and the legendary Asteria restaurant, Glyfada combines Athenian accessibility with resort-style luxury. Motor from Alimos in 20–30 minutes; anchor off the main beach or nearby coves, swim, and enjoy world-class Mediterranean dining. Evening cruises along the Riviera toward Glyfada's golden-hour light are quintessential Athens motorboat experiences.

Vouliagmeni, further south, offers dramatic clifftop views and secluded coves. The Astir Palace resort, Apollo Beach Club, and dozens of smaller tavernas line the shoreline. Motorboats can anchor in multiple protected bays, each offering different swimming, snorkeling, and dining experiences. Summer evenings see the Riviera alive with beautiful people, sunset swimmers, and cosmopolitan energy—perfect for clients seeking a taste of modern Greek beach culture.

Marinas and Check-In Procedures

Motorboats are based at Alimos Marina and nearby coastal facilities. Athens International Airport is 45 minutes north; ground transport to Alimos is simple via taxi (30–40 EUR) or public bus. Most motorboat charters operate on a day-rental basis: arrive 1–2 hours before departure, complete paperwork, receive a safety briefing, and launch. No sailing certification is required for motorboat operation (though some jurisdictions require a basic license).

Boat4You handles all paperwork, insurance, and fuel logistics. You pay for fuel consumed; most motorboats burn 15–30 litres/hour depending on speed and vessel size. A full tank typically costs €50–100 to refill. Fuel costs are transparent; Boat4You provides estimates during booking.

Speed, Convenience, and Family-Friendly Operation

Motorboats operate year-round; summer crowds, winter calm, spring breezes—all are welcome. Unlike sailing yachts dependent on wind, motorboats guarantee schedules and comfort regardless of weather. Children often prefer motorboats' stability and speed over sailboat heeling. Many motorboats are equipped with cabin space (small head, compact galley), making half-day or full-day excursions comfortable for families.

Meltemi winds (July–August) are irrelevant to motorboat cruising; you simply throttle up and arrive. Water temperatures range from 16°C (spring) to 25°C (late summer). Most motorboats have sun awnings or canvas bimini tops; bring sunscreen and light clothing. Snorkeling gear is often included; enquire about underwater exploration possibilities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a boating license to rent a motorboat from Athens?

Regulations vary by boat size and jurisdiction. Many sub-25-foot motorboats can be rented without formal certification in Greece; however, basic boating knowledge and comfort with throttle/steering are essential. assesses experience during booking; cautious renters can request a skipper or hire a shorter rental with orientation assistance.

How fast do motorboats travel, and how far can they go?

Motorboats cruise at 15–25 knots (28–46 km/h) depending on model and sea state. A full tank typically provides 150–300 nautical miles of range. From Athens, you can comfortably reach Aegina, Poros, or anywhere along the Riviera in a single day—return to your hotel by evening.

Are motorboat rentals suitable for couples and small groups?

Ideal. Most motorboats accommodate 2–8 passengers comfortably. They're perfect for couples' romantic day cruises, anniversary celebrations, and small-group adventures. Day rates typically range from €300–800 depending on boat size and season.

Can I book a motorboat for a sunset cruise?

Absolutely. Evening charters departing 17:00–18:00, cruising the Riviera for 2–3 hours as the sun sets, and returning for dinner ashore are extremely popular. can arrange pre-planned sunset routes or custom itineraries based on your preferences.

What if I want a longer motorboat adventure—can I stay overnight?

Some motorboats have small cabins and basic amenities for overnight cruising. Multi-day motorboat charters are less common than day rentals but possible. Discuss With us if you're interested in 2–3 day motorboat adventures to the Saronic islands.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
