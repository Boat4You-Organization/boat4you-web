Sailing Routes from Golfo Aranci

From Mare Fun Charter & Marina, your catamaran charter opens access to over 200 kilometers of pristine Sardinian coastline. To the north, the Costa Smeralda's exclusive anchorages await—Caprera, La Maddalena, and the granite peaks of the Archipelago di La Maddalena National Park. These islands sit approximately 35 kilometers north and offer world-class hiking, snorkeling in crystalline waters, and sunset viewing from elevated vantage points.

Southward, the Tavolara Marine Protected Area provides dramatic limestone cliffs rising 565 meters from the sea, protected breeding grounds for the endangered Audouin's gull, and underwater cave systems perfect for adventurous snorkelers. The journey south to Tavolara covers roughly 25 kilometers and typically takes three to four hours of comfortable sailing with reliable northeastern winds.

East-bound routes lead toward the isolated granite formations of Molara and Molarotto, where turquoise anchorages attract fewer tourists than main charter hubs. The 40-kilometer round trip offers excellent conditions for practicing catamaran handling and discovering secluded beaches.

Weather and Sailing Conditions

The Golfo Aranci season runs April through October, with peak conditions May through September. During summer months, the Mistral wind—a cool, powerful northwest flow—provides consistent 12-18 knot breezes ideal for sailing. The thermal wind pattern brings afternoon thermals of 8-12 knots, while dawn conditions typically feature lighter 4-6 knot breezes. Winter months December through February see stronger weather and fewer charters, though skilled sailors appreciate the challenging conditions and dramatic seascape.

Spring and autumn offer the most comfortable sailing: water temperatures reach 16°C in May and 22°C by September. The typical Mediterranean high-pressure system means clear skies, excellent visibility for navigation, and minimal storm risk during the main charter season.
