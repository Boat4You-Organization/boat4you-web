Classic Sailing Routes from Wickhams Cay II

Virgin Gorda's The Baths is the BVI's premier snorkelling destination. Departing Wickhams Cay II, a southerly reach of 15 nautical miles takes 2.5–3.5 hours under normal trade-wind conditions. The Baths reward anchor arrival with massive granite boulders framing crystalline pools, excellent snorkelling, and a uniquely Caribbean beach experience. Valley anchorage to the north offers alternative overnight protection and additional snorkelling.

Cooper Island's Manchioneel Bay lies southeast, an 8-nautical-mile passage from Virgin Gorda. Protected waters, hiking trails, and world-class snorkelling at Carrot Rock and Cistern Rock make Cooper Island a multi-day destination. Norman Island, to the southwest, features famous caves, historic wreck-exploration opportunities, and the Willy T bar barge—a moored platform offering entertainment and cold drinks.

Jost Van Dyke, 20 nautical miles northwest of Wickhams Cay II, hosts legendary Foxy's on Great Harbour and Soggy Dollar Bar on White Bay. These iconic establishments are pilgrimage sites for charter sailors worldwide. Cane Garden Bay, on Tortola's north shore, offers beautiful beaches, exceptional restaurants, and easy dinghy access from offshore anchorages. Anegada, the northernmost island, challenges navigators with its barrier reef but rewards explorers with pristine anchorages, authentic culture, and fresh lobster dinners.

Seasonal Sailing Conditions and Weather

The BVI peak sailing season runs November through May. December–February offers the strongest trade winds (15–22 knots), most stable weather patterns, and ideal conditions for performance sailing. March–May brings slightly lighter winds, calmer seas, and fewer charter crowds. Easter holidays (March–April) attract families; book early for spring vacation dates.

Autumn sailing (September–November) provides variable winds but rewarding experiences and lower charter costs. October–November features occasional tropical showers but excellent sailing between weather systems. Summer (June–August) offers lighter trade winds, warmer water temperatures, and quiet anchorages for those seeking solitude.

Christmas season (December 20–January 5) brings peak prices and busiest anchorages. Hurricane season (June–November) is unusual in the BVI, with the islands lying south of typical hurricane tracks. maintains updated weather protocols and alternative routing if tropical systems approach. In practice, hurricane impacts to the BVI are rare.

Christmas Winds is a short period (typically 7–10 days around December 20–27) when trade winds strengthen slightly above normal (18–25+ knots). Sailors view this as exciting racing conditions.
