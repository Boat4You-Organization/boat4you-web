Why Choose a Motorboat from Platis Gialos?

Platis Gialos is Mykonos's premier beach destination, known for its long, sandy shoreline and lively waterfront bars and restaurants. Our motorboat charter base here gives instant access to the Aegean's clearest waters and Greece's most sought-after day-sailing routes. From Platis Gialos, you can reach adjacent Agia Anna beach and nearby Paraga in just minutes by boat, making it ideal for those wanting both convenience and variety.

Motorboats are perfect for beach-hoppers and island explorers. They're fast, fuel-efficient, and nimble enough to navigate shallow anchorages and hidden coves that larger vessels cannot reach. Boat4You motorboat charters from Platis Gialos include fuel, navigation equipment, and our support team's local knowledge of the best swimming, snorkeling, and sunset spots.

The Platis Gialos location offers both convenience and authentic Greek island culture. You'll enjoy easy access to world-class beach clubs while maintaining flexibility to explore quieter anchorages nearby. Day charters return you by evening for dinner at waterfront tavernas.

Top Day Routes and Island Destinations

One popular option is a morning departure to nearby Agia Anna, a smaller, quieter beach in the same bay just 5 minutes by boat. Agia Anna's shallow waters and gentle conditions are beloved by families and swimmers seeking a more relaxed vibe than Platis Gialos itself. Many guests spend the morning swimming and snorkeling there before returning to Platis Gialos for lunch.

Another classic route takes you to Paraga, a small anchorage and beach community also in the immediate vicinity. From there, adventurous captains can explore the rocky outcrops and sea caves of Mykonos's southern coast, ideal for snorkeling and photography. The routes are typically 3–5 hours, allowing you to maximize water time while returning for dinner.

For longer expeditions, Delos—the legendary archaeological site just 2 nautical miles west—is accessible by motorboat. Many charters include a guided walk ashore (entrance fees apply) before returning to Mykonos for sunset. The journey takes about 30 minutes each way, leaving ample time for beach exploration.

Beach Clubs and Waterfront Dining

Platis Gialos is famous for its row of upscale beach clubs, each offering sunbeds, water sports, and fresh seafood. As a Boat4You motorboat guest, you can anchor just offshore and enjoy the beach scene, or pull up to the beach club pontoon and reserve a sunbed for the afternoon. Many clubs offer chilled wine, fresh fish, and Mediterranean mezze perfect for midday feasting.

The restaurant scene is world-class: establishments cater to international visitors and upmarket clientele. Sunset dining at a beachfront taverna, with your motorboat waiting in the bay, is an experience you'll remember forever. Local favorites include grilled octopus, sea urchin pasta, and Mykonos's famous saganaki (fried cheese).

Marinas, Mooring, and Infrastructure

Platis Gialos itself is a sandy beach with good holding ground for anchor, making it a natural mooring base for our motorboat fleet. There are no formal marina berths at the beach, but the shallow waters (8–12 meters) and fine sand provide secure holding for small to medium vessels. Our team assists with anchoring and monitors weather continuously throughout your charter.

Mykonos Town's main harbor (Chora) is a 10-minute motorboat ride away and offers full marina services, though it is more crowded and touristy. Most day-charter guests find Platis Gialos's beachside setting more appealing for leisurely atmosphere and easy access to restaurants and shops.

Seasons and Best Time to Visit Mykonos

The Aegean sailing season runs from April through October, with peak conditions from June to September. Summer months (July and August) bring the warmest water temperatures (around 24–26°C) and the most stable winds. However, July and August are also the busiest and most expensive times; May, June, and September offer excellent weather with fewer crowds and better rates.

Winter sailing (November through March) is possible but less comfortable. The Aegean can be choppy and winds unpredictable. Most tourists visit Mykonos in summer, so motorboat demand peaks then. Plan ahead and book early if you want a June or July departure.

Local Provisions and Fresh Supplies

Mykonos is well-stocked with supermarkets, bakeries, and fish markets. Before or during your charter, provision the motorboat with fresh bread, local cheese, cured meats, olives, and Greek wine. The island is famous for local goat cheeses and sun-dried tomatoes. Many beach clubs prepare picnic baskets (often called a 'mezze spread') if you ask a day ahead.

Water and fuel are readily available at Platis Gialos's service pontoon. Fresh fish can be purchased from the morning fish market near Chora. Local ouzo and retsina wine pair beautifully with Aegean seafood. Boat4You team provides recommendations for the best local vendors and markets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a boating license to charter a motorboat from Platis Gialos?

Most day charters from Boat4You include a professional captain, so no license is required. For bareboat (uncrewed) charters, you'll need a valid International Certificate of Competence (ICC) or national boating license.

What is included in a typical motorboat charter?

Our motorboat charters include the vessel, fuel, safety equipment, navigation aids, and insurance. Some packages include a skipper (captain) and/or provisioning service. Snorkeling gear and water sports equipment (paddleboards, fishing rods) can be added for a fee.

Can we moor at Agia Anna or Paraga overnight?

Agia Anna and Paraga are daylight-use anchorages primarily. However, our team can arrange overnight mooring at Mykonos Town harbor if needed, or arrange a return to Platis Gialos at sunset.

Getting to Platis Gialos and Practical Information

Mykonos is easily accessible by ferry from Athens (Piraeus Port, 2 hours) or by plane (Athens International Airport, 1-hour flight). Many visitors arrive by air, rent a car, and drive to Platis Gialos in about 20 minutes. Taxis and shuttle services are readily available at the airport, and most accommodations offer transfer coordination.

Accommodation ranges from luxury resort hotels to family-run guesthouses. Many charter guests stay in Mykonos Town (Chora) and commute to Platis Gialos by taxi or public bus.
