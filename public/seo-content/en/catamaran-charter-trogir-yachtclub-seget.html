Island Routes: Drvenik, Šolta, Hvar & Beyond

Drvenik islands lie 12 nautical miles south—a leisurely two-hour motor-sail ideal for first-day confidence building. Shallow anchorages and sandy beaches characterize these quiet islands, perfect for families and new sailors.

Šolta, 15 nautical miles southwest, offers pin-backed coves and the charming harbor of Maslinica. The island's hillside vineyards and fish restaurants exemplify Adriatic island culture. Most catamaran crews anchor off Grohote village for swimming and provisioning.

Hvar, 25 nautical miles from Yachtclub Seget, remains the Adriatic's sunniest and most social island. Hvar Town's harbor accommodates large catamarans alongside charter bases and chandleries. The island's lavender, wine, and nightlife appeal to diverse crews. Catamaran stability shines when entering Hvar Town's crowded harbor during peak season.

Brač and Vis extend cruising ranges: Brač sits 18 nautical miles south (Bol village's Zlatni Rat beach is unmissable); Vis, 35 nautical miles, rewards longer passages with dramatic anchorages, fresh fish, and isolation rarely found closer to Split.

Best Seasons & Weather

Spring sailing (April–May) brings calm mornings, afternoon thermals, and water temperatures rising from 16°C to 19°C. Crowds are manageable; provisioning is straightforward. Summer (June–August) features reliable northwesterly winds (Maestral), blue skies, and temperature peaks near 28°C. July and early August see heavy charter traffic and premium pricing. Autumn (September–October) offers warm water (23–24°C), moderate winds, and fewer boats—often considered peak for experienced crews.

Local Culture & Provisioning

Trogir's old town, 3 kilometers from Yachtclub Seget, is a UNESCO World Heritage site—perfect for shore days. Split, 25 kilometers away, offers Diocletian's Palace, museums, and lively waterfront dining. Local cuisine emphasizes fresh fish, pašticada (braised beef), and wines from nearby Dalmatian vineyards. The Yachtclub restaurant serves traditional Dalmatian seafood daily; provisioning markets near the marina provide meats, cheeses, produce, and fresh fish for galley meals.
