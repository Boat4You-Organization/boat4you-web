Marina d'Arechi: Facility Overview & Services

Marina d'Arechi spans 280,000 square meters across protected basins offering 1000+ berths accommodating vessels from 10 to 200+ feet. The marina features dedicated superyacht berths with 400-amp shore power connections, integrated water/fuel delivery systems, and 24/7 security. Floating breakwaters protect inner basins from Mediterranean swell; dredged depths reach 7–9 meters, accommodating deep-draft cruising yachts and commercial vessels.

Onshore amenities include a modern terminal building (opened 2015) with Wi-Fi throughout, climate-controlled office spaces, and premium restroom facilities. Provisioning agencies operate within the marina grounds: one-stop shopping for groceries, marine supplies, fuel, and maintenance services. Crane and haul-out facilities support minor repairs and seasonal maintenance, though larger projects coordinate with specialized shipyards in Naples and Messina.

Security is paramount: 24/7 gated access, CCTV surveillance, and professional security personnel patrol the facility. Hurricane-force storm protocols include storm anchoring options and shore shelter; Marina d'Arechi's protected basins have weathered Category 1 Mediterranean storms without significant damage to moored vessels.

Catamaran Charters from Marina d'Arechi: Routes & Itineraries

Departing Marina d'Arechi places catamarans ideally positioned for three distinct cruising regions. The Amalfi Coast route heads northeastward: 15 km to Amalfi town (45-minute transit), 22 km to Positano (60 minutes), and 30 km to Ravello's surrounding coastline. Catamarans anchor 300–500 meters offshore in 20–30 meter depths; tender access to village piazzas takes 5–10 minutes. Overnight stays in Amalfi and Positano allow exploration of clifftop villages, restaurants, and shops.

The Cilento Coast route extends southeastward, beginning with 25-km transit to Palinuro (approximately 80 minutes). Palinuro's lighthouse marks the entrance to dramatic limestone cave systems accessible by dinghy. White-sand beaches, emerald coves, and rocky anchorages dominate the next 55 kilometers. Marina di Camerota, located 45 km from Marina d'Arechi, offers excellent provisioning and snorkeling. The Cilento Marine Protected Area encompasses underwater gardens ideal for diving certification courses and recreational snorkeling.

Island routes head southwestward: Capri (48 km, 2.5–3 hours cruising), Ischia (42 km from Salerno, 2.5 hours), and Procida (45 km from Salerno, 2.5–3 hours). Capri's northern anchorages offer protection and quick access to Blue Grotto tours. Ischia's diverse coastline includes Mount Epomeo views, Forio town, and natural hot springs. Procida delivers charming harbor anchorages and pastel-painted buildings distinct from more-famous islands.

Catamaran Specifications: Comfort & Performance

Marina d'Arechi regularly hosts catamarans ranging 38–55 feet, accommodating 6–12 guests across 3–5 private cabins. Twin hulls provide superior stability, minimal heel under sail, and shallow drafts (typically 1.3–1.5 meters) allowing exploration of shoal-water anchorages. Modern catamarans feature solar panels, water-makers producing 50–100 gallons daily, and advanced navigation systems including autopilot, GPS chartplotters, and weather routing.

Accommodations emphasize space and light: large cabin windows, open-air companionways, and expanded deck areas. Galleys include modern refrigeration, stove-ovens, and dishwashers. Heads feature electric toilets and hot-water showers. Flybridge sun decks provide 360-degree views and social gathering spaces; side decks offer deck chairs and loungers for daytime relaxation.

Performance from Marina d'Arechi combines reliability with efficiency. Dual diesel engines (typically 80–120 hp each) deliver 20–25-knot cruising speeds; sailing under canvas harvests Tyrrhenian winds reaching 12–20 knots during summer months. Battery banks and solar systems support overnight power needs; water-makers eliminate provisioning water storage concerns. Stabilization systems reduce motion in choppy seas.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I reach Marina d'Arechi from Salerno airport or train station?

Marina d'Arechi sits 5 km south of Salerno city center. Taxis from Salerno train station (Stazione Centrale) cost €15–20 (15-minute drive). Car rentals facilitate independent travel; parking at Marina d'Arechi costs €15–25 daily. Bus service (CSTP line 1 or 3) connects city center to marina area; journey takes 25–35 minutes.

What are Marina d'Arechi's berthing depths and vessel size limits?

Inner basins reach 7–9 meters depth, accommodating vessels up to 200+ feet with appropriate draft (usually 2–3 meters maximum for superyachts). Shallow-draft catamarans (1.3–1.5 meters) utilize outer basins without depth restrictions. Catamaran charters typically berth in dedicated sections offering easy access to provisioning and facilities.
