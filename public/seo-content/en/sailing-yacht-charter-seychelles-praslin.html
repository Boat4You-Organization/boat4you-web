Multi-Day Sailing Routes from Praslin

A classic week-long sailing yacht charter from Praslin follows the inner-island circuit: sail south to La Digue (10 nautical miles, typically a reach or beam reach depending on wind), exploring the island's eastern anchorages and famous Anse Source d'Argent. From La Digue, westbound passages reach Silhouette Island (30 nautical miles, a proper ocean passage offering thrilling sailing). The return leg visits Curieuse Island (a national park), Cousin Island (a bird sanctuary), and smaller islands depending on interests and wind. Each passage feels earned through seamanship and wind-reading—a profound difference from motorboat routines.

Wind and Sailing Seasons

The southeast trade winds (April–October) are a sailor's dream—consistent 12–18 knots, moderate seas, and stable pressure systems. These months are prime for sailing, with reliable wind enabling ambitious itineraries and exciting sailing. The northwest monsoon (November–March) brings lighter, more variable winds interspersed with occasional squalls—challenging for some, exhilarating for experienced crews. Many serious sailors actually prefer the shoulder months (March–April, September–October) when winds are moderate, the sailing is constantly engaging, and fewer tourists populate the anchorages.

Frequently Asked Questions

What certifications do I need for a bareboat sailing yacht charter?

We typically require sailing certifications (ASA, RYA, IYT), a logbook demonstrating ocean experience, or references from previous bareboat charter companies. First-time charterers should consider crewed options or hire professional skippers for their initial expedition.

How is weather forecasted while sailing from Praslin?

Skippers receive daily satellite weather briefings. Bareboat clients get detailed nightly updates via SSB radio or satellite communication systems. Itineraries adjust flexibly based on conditions; safety is prioritized above all planned routes.

What happens if winds are light or we encounter a calm?

The Seychelles rarely experiences true calms. Light-wind periods allow motor-sailing (engine + partial sails) to maintain speed comfortably. Fuel reserves are adequate for such scenarios. Experienced sailors often embrace light-wind periods as opportunities to practice seamanship and explore anchorages leisurely.

Can I learn to sail better during a crewed charter?

Absolutely. Our professional skippers welcome keen students. You can participate at any level—from standing watch and managing sails to simply learning through observation. Many clients report that week-long sailing charters accelerated their nautical education profoundly.

Praslin is positioned for extended expeditions; however, outer islands require careful passage planning, favorable weather windows, and experienced crews. assesses each itinerary for safety and feasibility. We encourage ambition but refuse to compromise safety.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics.
