Comprehensive Crew Briefings & Safety Protocols

Sailing yachts require meticulous briefing. Marina Trogir's 120-minute induction covers sail trim, keel draft in shallow anchorages, upper-wind Bura behavior, weather routing, and emergency heave-to techniques. Skippers review paper charts and digital plotters; crews practice man-overboard drills at the marina's central pontoon before departure.

Unlike catamaran turnarounds, sailing crews often extend their Thursday arrival to Friday, conducting a half-day shakedown sail near Trogir before venturing to distant islands. This additional hands-on time builds crew confidence and allows your skipper to assess sail trim and weather-helm characteristics before committing to a Hvar passage.

Sailing Routes: Trogir to Hvar, Brač, & Vis

Brač Island (18 nm south) presents a day-sail objective. Bol's Zlatni Rat beach offers protected mooring under pine-backed bluffs. The island's Plavac Mali vineyards and restaurant cluster (Konoba Riva, Konoba Ante) reward afternoon exploration.

Hvar Island (32 nm southwest) demands careful sail planning. The passage crosses open water; Mistral winds can build to 15–20 knots. Hvar Town's harbor accommodates 40+ cruising yachts and offers refueling, provisioning, and nightlife. Quieter Jelsa (north shore) suits crews seeking tranquility and sandy beaches.

Vis (42 nm distant) is a two-day adventure. The island's Vis Town, Komiža fishing village, and Green Cave anchorage appeal to sailors who relish isolation and local character. Return passages often include stops at Šolta and the Drvenik islands—shallow-draft anchorages ideal for sailing yachts.

Regatta Week Participation & Racing

The Trogir Charter Race (June) and Bol Race Week (July) attract 80–120 competing yachts. Boat4You offers race-day bookings with optional professional skippers. Handicap fleets ensure competitive balance; prize ceremonies and crew dinners continue ashore into the evening. Many crews book return charters immediately after racing.

If your sailing is less competitive, you'll still benefit from Marina Trogir's vibrant charter community. Flotilla trips leave Trogir three times weekly, offering social anchorages, group provisioning discounts, and security in convoy sailing—particularly valuable if your crew lacks Adriatic experience.

Weather & Seasonal Wind Conditions

April–May: Gentle 8–12-knot breezes, calm mornings, afternoon thermal buildups. Water 16–18°C. Ideal for novice crews and long, unhurried passages.

June–August: Reliable 12–18-knot Mistral (northwest) and Bura (northeast) winds. Water 22–25°C. Thermal thermals occur 2–4 PM. More challenging but exhilarating sailing; racing yachts thrive.

September–October: 14–20-knot winds, fewer charter vessels, and 20–22°C water. Autumn is many sailors' preferred season—consistent conditions without peak-season crowding.

Trogir Ashore & Local Culture

The UNESCO-listed old town offers Venetian architecture, cathedral bells, and narrow lanes. Market mornings (Tuesday–Saturday) display fresh fish, local Plavac Mali wines, and Dalmatian cheese. Restaurant Konoba Kaleta specializes in pašticada (slow beef stew) and fresh fish; request a waterfront table and watch your sailing yacht rock gently at Marina Trogir's pontoons.

FAQ: Sailing Yacht Charters from Marina Trogir

What sailing experience is required for a bareboat charter?

Most charters require an International Certificate of Competence (ICC) or equivalent national credential. Marina Trogir and verify certificates on booking. Crews without formal certification may charter with a professional skipper.

Can I take a sailing yacht on a regatta during my charter?

Yes. Book well in advance if your dates coincide with Trogir Charter Race or Bol Race Week. arranges race entry, and optional skippers provide race coaching if desired.

Is there a difference between a racing rig and a cruising rig on Marina Trogir yachts?

Yes. Racing yachts carry spinnaker poles, asymmetric gennakers, and stiffer masts. Cruising yachts prioritize comfort, shade, and ease of handling. Both are excellent; racing configurations suit experienced crews.

Can crews overnight in Trogir's harbor or must we anchor out?

Marina Trogir berths are reserved for turnaround days. Most crews anchor in shallow Čiovo coves (3–6 meters) or moor temporarily at neighboring anchorages. Restaurants and shops remain accessible by dinghy or shore launches.

What's the nearest sailing-supply chandlery?

Marina Trogir has an onsite chandlery stocking charts, spares, and provisioning. Larger chandleries operate in Split (20 km north). can pre-arrange equipment delivery if needed.
