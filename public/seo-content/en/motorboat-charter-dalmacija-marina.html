Motorboat Itineraries: Kornati Day Trips & Beyond

The classic Kornati day trip covers 40–50 nautical miles round-trip. Motorboats depart at 7 AM, reaching the Kornati islands by 10 AM. Four to five hours of exploration—swimming, snorkeling, waterside dining—fills the midday, with return to Sukošan by 6 PM. Boat4You recommends Sipan, Katić, and Žut anchorages for first-time Kornati visitors.

Southbound itineraries target Telašćica Bay (25 nm), Mir saltwater lake, and Dugi Otok. Motorboats make this passage in 4–5 hours, leaving ample time for afternoon swimming and evening sunset viewing from deck.

RIBs vs. Day Cruisers: Choosing Your Motorboat

RIBs (rigid-inflatable boats, 20–24 feet) suit groups of four to six seeking speed and agility. Open designs expose crews to wind and spray—thrilling for adventure-seekers, less comfortable for elderly passengers or very young children. Top speeds reach 30–35 knots; Kornati day trips take under 3 hours each way.

Day cruisers (26–32 feet) offer cabin shelter, basic galley facilities, and modest toilet/wash amenities. Speeds of 18–24 knots feel less rushed; families with young children or comfort-focused groups gravitate toward day cruisers. Both boat types feature easy-to-operate single-lever controls, modern GPS, and fishfinder electronics.

Boat4You rates both categories based on group size, itinerary distance, and passenger comfort preferences.

Seasons, Weather & Sailing Conditions

Spring (April–May) offers warm days (18–22°C), calm mornings ideal for steady-cruising, and manageable crowds. Water temperatures hover near 17°C. Summer (June–August) delivers reliable sunshine, warmer water (21–24°C), but increased boat traffic in popular anchorages like Kornati.

Autumn (September–October) combines stable weather, warm water, and fewer competing vessels. Visibility remains excellent for snorkeling and underwater photography. Winter sailing is possible for experienced crews but brings shorter daylight and occasional rough seas; most commercial charters operate April–October.

Provisioning & Waterside Dining

Sukošan's supermarkets and fish markets supply fresh provisions. Grilled fish, squid, and mussels appear daily. Local Zadar specialties include brudet (slow-cooked fisherman's stew) and pašticada (braised beef with prunes). Restaurants in small Kornati-region villages offer simple, excellent meals at waterside tables—cevapi (grilled sausages), fresh salads, and local wine.

Many Kornati anchorages feature modest taverns accessible by dinghy, serving pasta, grilled fish, and ice cream. Bring cash; payment systems are basic in remote locations.

What's the fuel consumption for a typical motorboat?
