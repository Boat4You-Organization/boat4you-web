Motorboat Classes and Capabilities

Boat4You maintains several motorboat classes in Ibiza. Open sportboats (32–38 feet) emphasize speed, shallow draft, and minimalist accommodation. They're perfect for day-trips: breakfast in Ibiza town, snorkelling off Espalmador by lunchtime, sunset drinks in Formentera. These boats run 25–40 knots and burn fuel quickly; plan 50–80 nautical miles per tank.

Cabin cruisers (38–45 feet) balance speed (20–30 knots) with overnight comfort. A cabin cruiser sleeps four to six, features a galley and head, and maintains fuel economy sufficient for longer voyages. Many cruiser-charter clients explore a different anchorage each night, dining aboard and enjoying the peace of their private floating hotel.

Luxury flybridge cruisers (45–55 feet) are floating resorts. Twin or triple engines deliver 18–24 knots, impressive range, and accommodation rival to Mediterranean boutique hotels. Flybridge cruisers are ideal for guests prioritizing comfort, space, and the prestige of commanding a substantial vessel. Boat4You often pairs these with a professional skipper and crew, though bareboat charters (with valid license) are available.

Motorboat Routes and Day-Trip Itineraries

A typical motorboat day from Marina Port might unfold like this: depart 08:30, motor to Es Vedrà's Cala d'Hort (6 nm, 15 minutes), anchor in 18 feet of clear water, spend three hours snorkelling and exploring sea caves by dinghy. Around 12:00, motor east to Ibiza's wild north coast at Portinatx (12 nm), beach lunch at a beachfront chiringuito, and back to Marina Port by 18:00—in time for sunset aperitivo and dinner at a harborside restaurant. For the adventurous, a dawn departure reaches Formentera by breakfast. Anchor off Illetas or the even more remote Cala Saona (south coast, 12 nm from Formentera town). Return to Ibiza by sunset, or stay overnight and explore Formentera's interior villages and lighthouses.

Multi-day motorboat charters often venture east toward Mallorca's quieter coves (60 nm), or west toward the Costa Blanca anchorages of Moraira and Jávea—quiet, uncrowded, and celebrated by Spanish day-boaters. A motorboat's range means you're rarely bound to the immediate Ibiza islands; the entire western Mediterranean becomes your playground.

Fuel Economy and Budget Planning

Motorboat fuel consumption varies dramatically with speed and sea state. A 40-foot cabin cruiser at cruise speed (20 knots) burns roughly 8–12 gallons per hour (fuel economy ~2–3 nautical miles per gallon). An open sportboat at full throttle (35+ knots) might burn 25–30 gallons per hour, yielding ~1.2 nm per gallon. Plan conservatively. A 150-gallon fuel tank in a cabin cruiser permits 300+ nautical miles at cruise speed; at race speeds, you'll be refuelling more frequently.

Budget €2–3 per litre for diesel fuel across the Mediterranean (Ibiza is mid-range pricing). A typical week-long motorboat charter burns 200–400 euros' worth of fuel, depending on distance and speed—far less than airline costs for an equivalent group size.

Bareboat Licensing and Insurance

Spain and the EU recognize recreational boating licenses from most countries. If you hold a valid powerboat license, Personal Watercraft certificate, or equivalent, bareboat motorboat charters are available. will verify your credentials and provide a brief familiarization with the specific vessel. First-time charterers often opt for a professional skipper (available daily) or a one-day shadowing program to build confidence.

Comprehensive insurance is mandatory and included in all charters. Hull damage, third-party liability, and crew coverage protect you. Personal travel insurance and any cancellation policy are separate and recommended.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a boat license to charter a motorboat?

Yes. requires proof of a valid recreational powerboat license or equivalent. Most countries issue these via a one-day course or exam. If you lack a license, hire a professional skipper through our partners—they'll pilot the vessel while you relax and learn.

How fast can motorboats cruise safely?

Cruising speed (optimal fuel economy) is typically 18–24 knots for cabin cruisers. Peak speed (full throttle) is 25–40 knots, but this burns fuel rapidly and increases wear. Most Mediterranean charters balance comfort at cruise speed, arriving refreshed rather than exhausted.

What's the difference between a sportboat and a cruiser?

Sportboats prioritize speed and open-air thrills; they have minimal cabin space and are ideal for day trips. Cruisers balance speed with comfort, featuring enclosed cabins, galleys, and sleeping berths. For family charters longer than a day, cruisers are superior.

Can we bring a group of friends on a motorboat charter?

Absolutely. Larger groups might charter two vessels and coordinate itineraries. Social motorboat charters are increasingly popular—the speed and comfort make them ideal for milestone celebrations (birthdays, anniversaries, reunions).

Is it hard to navigate using GPS and charts?

Modern motorboats carry GPS chartplotters (colour screens with real-time position, depth, and weather overlay). Navigation is straightforward: enter waypoints, follow the line on screen, maintain speed. If you're uncertain, a skipper removes the responsibility.
