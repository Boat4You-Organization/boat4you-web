Catamaran Charter in Pirovac: Comfortable Sailing from Dalmatia's Hidden Gem

Pirovac is Dalmatia's quiet alternative to the crowded Split bases. This charming village of around 2,000 inhabitants sits on the Morinje bay between Šibenik and Zadar, offering an intimate gateway to some of Croatia's most spectacular sailing. With the Murter island bridge just minutes away and the Kornati National Park practically on your doorstep, Pirovac-based catamaran charters combine ease of access, protected anchorages, and genuine adventure without the summer crowds. Boat4You recognises Pirovac as an underrated gem for families and couples seeking authentic Mediterranean sailing with contemporary comfort.

Why Catamarans from Pirovac Make Sense

Catamarans are perfectly suited to Pirovac's sailing environment. The Morinje bay itself is protected and shallow—typically 3–8 metres—making it an ideal starting point for twin-hulled vessels. Once you depart Marina Pirovac, you immediately benefit from the catamaran's shallow draft (1.0–1.5 metres) to navigate the narrow channels between Murter and the Tisno bridge, the gateway to the Kornati archipelago. The catamaran's wide beam and level platform provide genuine comfort during the moderate sails that characterise this region—typically 8–18 nautical miles between anchorages.

The region's winds—predominantly Maestral from the northwest in summer, with occasional Bura from the northeast—favour catamarans. The dual-engine configuration ensures reliable progress in the variable afternoon breezes, while the shallow draft opens access to the small, crystal-clear bays scattered throughout Murter and the outer Kornati islands where deeper-hulled monohulls cannot venture. This makes Pirovac-based catamarans ideal for exploring lesser-known anchorages and avoiding the main tourist routes.

The Murter Bridge and Kornati Gateway

Pirovac's strategic position lies in its proximity to Murter island via the Tisno bridge. This crossing takes just minutes by boat and opens an entirely different sailing world. The Kornati National Park—an archipelago of 89 islands and islets—lies directly north and east of Murter. For catamaran sailors, this proximity is a game-changer: you can depart Marina Pirovac in the morning and be exploring the Kornati's narrow channels and hidden bays by lunchtime.

The outer Kornati islands offer exceptional anchorages for catamarans. Places like Mala Proversa, Levrnaka, and Katina island feature protected bays with sand and seagrass holding, perfect for the catamaran's modest anchor systems. The Kornati experience from a catamaran is uniquely calm: you have the stability to navigate the sometimes-choppy waters approaching the archipelago, yet the shallow draft to penetrate bays that are often too shallow for traditional yachts.

Local Anchorages and Day-Sail Options

Beyond Kornati, the Pirovac base opens routes throughout central-northern Dalmatia. Telašćica bay on Dugi Otok—a 12-nautical-mile sail east—is a natural destination for catamarans. This vast, sheltered natural harbour can accommodate dozens of vessels, yet it rarely feels crowded. The bay's northwestern arm offers exceptional holding and a remote, wild atmosphere. On the return sail, you'll enjoy reliable Maestral winds that suit catamaran sailing perfectly.

Silba and Premuda islands, further north, offer quieter alternatives. These less-visited islands appeal to sailors seeking solitude, and a catamaran's speed and ease of handling mean you can reach them in a full day's sailing and still have time to explore before sunset. Silba's small harbour is tight but perfectly manageable for a catamaran with a competent skipper.

Within day-sail range are also the Kaštela bays to the south and Trogir's medieval waterfront (approximately 50 kilometres away). While longer day trips, they're manageable in a catamaran, especially if you depart early and use favourable winds.

Marina Pirovac: Facilities and Logistics

Marina Pirovac is a mid-sized facility operated by local brokerages. It offers fuel, fresh water, and electricity at reasonable rates (typically €40–€65 per night for a 40-foot catamaran). The marina staff are accustomed to catamaran operations and can arrange provisioning, maintenance, and local advice. The village of Pirovac itself has a supermarket, fish market, and several restaurants specialising in fresh seafood—a particular advantage for crews planning to cook aboard.

Boat4You partners with local Pirovac operators who maintain fleets ranging from 34 to 46 feet. These vessels are well-maintained and equipped with modern navigation systems, making them suitable for both bareboat and crewed charters. The laid-back atmosphere of Pirovac means fewer administrative delays than larger marinas; you can often depart within an hour of arrival.

Seasonality and Weather Windows

The ideal sailing season in Pirovac runs May through September. July and August bring reliable Maestral winds and warm, settled weather—perfect for catamarans, which benefit from steady breezes. June and September offer fewer crowds and more dynamic weather, which challenges but rewards experienced sailors. The winds during these months average 12–16 knots in the afternoon, ideal for catamaran sailing.

May can be variable, with occasional Bura squalls, but mornings are often perfectly calm—ideal for exploring shallow Kornati anchorages. October is possible but brings shorter daylight hours. April and November are challenging due to unpredictable wind and cooler water temperatures (around 15°C).

Provisioning and Local Flavours

Pirovac, while small, has excellent local provisioning. The fish market receives daily catches from local boats, offering fresh branzino, sea bass, and squid at prices lower than tourist-focused marinas. The village bakery produces fresh bread each morning. For wine, local Babić (a deep red from nearby Primošten) and Maraština (a white wine from the Šibenik region) are readily available and pair beautifully with fresh seafood.

The village's waterfront restaurants serve authentic Dalmatian cuisine: gregada (slow-cooked fish stew), pašticada (braised beef), and fresh grilled fish. Many are family-run establishments catering to locals rather than tourists, offering better value and more authentic experience than Split-area restaurants. This village character is a core appeal of Pirovac-based charters—you're eating where locals eat, not in marina-adjacent tourist traps.

Sample 7-Day Catamaran Itinerary from Pirovac

Day 1: Depart Marina Pirovac at 10:00, motor-sail through Tisno bridge to Murter. Anchor in protected bay near Murter town. Evening exploration and dinner ashore.

Day 2: Early departure into Kornati. Explore outer archipelago anchorages at Mala Proversa or Levrnaka. Full day anchored with swimming and snorkelling in clear, unpolluted waters.

Day 3: Sail to Dugi Otok, Telašćica bay. This vast natural harbour offers unmatched solitude. Afternoon swimming in sheltered coves; overnight anchored.

Day 4: Explore Telašćica's northwestern arm or attempt day sail to Silba island (weather permitting). Quieter islands, fewer vessels. Return to protected anchorage by sunset.

Day 5: Leisurely morning. Sail southward back towards Murter and Kornati. Anchor at a different spot than Day 2—perhaps Premuda or Pasman island. Evening at anchor with fresh-caught fish prepared aboard.

Day 6: Day sail to Trogir (50 km south) if energy permits, or alternative day-trip to Kaštela bays. Alternatively, remain in Kornati region exploring remaining anchorages. Return by sunset to protected bay.

Day 7: Return to Marina Pirovac via Tisno bridge. Final provisioning and exploration of the village waterfront. Handover of vessel.

Frequently Asked Questions About Pirovac Catamarans

Is Pirovac suitable for first-time charter sailors?

Yes, particularly for bareboat experience. The waters around Pirovac are generally calm, and the shorter sail distances mean less fatigue. A professional skipper is recommended if you lack Mediterranean sailing experience.

How far is the Kornati National Park from Pirovac?

Approximately 15–25 nautical miles depending on your intended anchorage. Most outer-Kornati destinations are reachable in 2–3 hours by catamaran, making it a relaxed morning departure.

What's included in a typical Pirovac catamaran charter?

Boat4You charters typically include the vessel, bedding, towels, full navigation systems, and safety equipment. Fuel, provisioning, and mooring fees are additional. Most bareboat charters run €2,500–€4,500 per week for a 40-foot catamaran.

Can I hire a professional skipper for the week?

Absolutely. Local skippers familiar with Pirovac waters and Kornati anchorages are available through Boat4You at approximately €200–€300 per day, depending on vessel size and experience level.

Is the Tisno bridge easy to navigate with a catamaran?

Yes. While the channel is narrow (approximately 10–15 metres at the bridge opening), the bridge opens regularly during summer, and catamaran beams (typically 13–15 metres) fit comfortably. Water depths are 3–5 metres, ideal for catamaran drafts.
