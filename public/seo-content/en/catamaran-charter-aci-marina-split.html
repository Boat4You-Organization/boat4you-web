Island Routes from Your Catamaran Base

Brač lies 15 nautical miles south. The village of Bol and the stunning Zlatni Rat beach appear within two hours under sail or power. Its shallow approach suits the catamaran's draft, and the island offers robust provisioning and water-sports facilities.

Hvar, the Adriatic's sunniest island, lies roughly 30 nautical miles southwest. Catamaran charters often moor in Hvar Town's spacious harbor, where restaurants and chandleries cluster near the waterfront. The island's lavender fields, fortifications, and nightlife draw sailors year-round.

Vis, the outermost island, offers a 40-nautical-mile passage. The village of Vis Town and the hidden lagoon anchorage at Green Cave provide dramatic natural contrasts. Many catamaran charters anchor in the shelter of Komiža or the southern approaches, where fish restaurants and local wines exemplify the island's character.

Šolta and the Drvenik islands complete the triangle of day-sail destinations. Shallow anchorages and pine-backed beaches characterize these less-crowded islands, ideal for crews seeking quieter Adriatic experiences.

Best Seasons for Catamaran Charters

Spring (April–May) offers warm days, calm mornings, and fewer boats. Water temperatures reach 18–19°C. Summer (June–August) brings reliable wind, blue skies, and social anchorages—but crowding and heat intensify mid-July to mid-August.

Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (22–23°C), gentle sailing breezes, and manageable crowds. Winter sailing is possible but brings shorter days and occasional rough conditions. Most commercial charters operate April through October.

Local Flavors & Provisioning

Split's food culture centers on pašticada (slow-braised beef), soparnik (chard pie), and fresh fish. The Green Market and Peškarija (fish market) supply daily catches—perfect for dinner aboard your catamaran. Local wines include Plavac Mali (bold red) and Kaštelanski crljenak, both grown nearby.

Marjan hill, accessible from the waterfront, offers sunset hikes and panoramic views of your catamaran's evening anchorage. Local restaurants cluster along the Riva; most welcome sailors and offer reduced prices for groups.

FAQ: Catamaran Charters from ACI Marina Split

What's the ideal catamaran size for groups of 6–8?

A 38–42-foot catamaran with three or four cabins suits eight passengers comfortably. ACI Marina Split can accommodate vessels up to 60 feet, though smaller catamarans offer easier handling and better value.

Can I moor directly at ACI Marina Split during my charter?

Yes. ACI Marina Split reserves berths for visiting charters. Booking in advance through guarantees a mooring. The marina charges daily mooring fees (typically €1.50–€2.50 per meter) in addition to your charter rate.

What sailing certifications do I need?

Most catamaran charters from ACI Marina Split require an International Certificate of Competence (ICC) or equivalent national license if you have prior experience. Bareboat charters often mandate a skipper certification; Boat4You's process reviews your credentials and can arrange a professional skipper if needed.

Are catamarans fuel-efficient compared to monohull yachts?

Yes, catamarans generally burn 15–25% less fuel than equivalent monohulls at the same speed, thanks to their hydrodynamic efficiency. From ACI Marina Split, this savings compounds during longer passages to distant islands.

What's the nearest refueling point beyond ACI Marina Split?

Hvar Town marina and several Brač harbors operate fuel docks. ACI Marina Split itself supplies fuel daily 8 AM–5 PM, making it rare to depart without a full tank.
