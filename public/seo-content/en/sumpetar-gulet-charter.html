Gulet Charter from Sumpetar

Sumpetar, a small coastal village nestled south of Split near the village of Jesenice, provides an intimate and authentic departure point for gulet charters. Boat4You operates three traditional wooden gulets from this quiet mainland settlement, ideal for leisurely multi-day voyages exploring Brač, Hvar, and Vis islands via slow, unhurried sailing. Gulets from Sumpetar offer genuine Croatian charm and classic Mediterranean cruising at a deliberately relaxed pace.

The Sumpetar Location Advantage

Sumpetar sits on the mainland coast between Split and Makarska, away from bustling resort areas. The village retains its fishing-community character: small family restaurants, local fishermen, and genuine Adriatic hospitality. Departure from Sumpetar feels personal and relaxed—no crowds, no formality, just preparation for your gulet voyage. Boat4You partners with local mariners who manage the fleet with personal attention and regional knowledge.

Three-Gulet Fleet & Traditional Specifications

Three wooden gulets (30–48 feet) form the Sumpetar fleet. Each vessel features authentic Turkish-style wooden construction: teak and mahogany cabins, spacious deck areas for sunbathing, and traditional sail rigs (cotton sails, auxiliary diesel engines). Modern safety systems, GPS navigation, VHF radios, and comprehensive insurance accompany the traditional aesthetic. Boat4You conducts annual maintenance and seasonal inspections.

Slow Cruising Philosophy

Gulet charters from Sumpetar emphasize a deliberate pace: departures at dawn, morning sail under gentle breezes, long lunch anchors in remote coves, afternoon swims, and sunset arrivals. This rhythm contrasts sharply with fast motorboat schedules. Boat4You's gulet crews—experienced captains and traditional sailors—navigate without rushing, allowing time for exploration, relaxation, and genuine connection with the Adriatic landscape.

Island Routes: Brač, Hvar, Vis Triangle

A typical week-long gulet charter from Sumpetar follows this pattern: Day 1, depart for Brač (15 nm, afternoon sail); Day 2, explore Bol and Dol villages, then sail to Hvar (25 nm); Days 3–4, base at Hvar Town or quieter anchorages (Galesnik, Palmižana); Day 5, sail to Vis (40 nm, overnight or split passages); Day 6, explore Vis Town and Green Cave; Day 7, return to Sumpetar. Gulet Sailing Conditions & Wind Patterns

The Split region's summer maestral winds (northwest, 8–15 knots) provide ideal sailing for gulets. Spring and autumn offer lighter breezes and fewer gales. The gulet's sail plan and heavy displacement make these traditional vessels stable and seaworthy in variable conditions; crews appreciate the security and slowness. Auxiliary diesel engines ensure arrival times even in calms.

FAQ: Sumpetar Gulet Charters

How many guests can a typical Sumpetar gulet accommodate?

Vessels range from 30 to 48 feet. Smaller gulets host 4–6 guests; larger craft accommodate 8–10 guests. All berths are private cabins with en-suite facilities, unlike traditional open-deck gulets.

Is the pace really slower than motorboat charters?

Absolutely. Gulets prioritize sailing over speed—passages average 6–8 knots under sail, compared to 15+ knots for motorboats. 's gulet philosophy centers on journey rather than destination; each day unfolds at nature's pace.

What if wind conditions are poor during our charter?

The auxiliary diesel engine ensures reliable transit. Light-wind days mean motoring or short motor-sail passages. 's flexibility allows route adjustments to maximize sailing opportunities and minimize engine use.

Can we combine sailing with cultural experiences?

Yes. Boat4You arranges island excursions: guided Old Town walks in Hvar, wine tastings on Pelješac, kayak explorations of Green Cave near Vis, and local fisherman meetings. The gulet's pace allows meaningful shore interactions.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
