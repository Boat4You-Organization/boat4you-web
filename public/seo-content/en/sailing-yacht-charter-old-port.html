The Old Port: Where History Meets Modern Sailing

The Old Port lies at the heart of Corfu Town's UNESCO World Heritage-listed Old Town, a physical and spiritual center of Mediterranean maritime culture. The harbor's eastern rim is dominated by the 16th-century Venetian Fortress, its massive stone walls and bastions testifying to centuries of strategic importance and cultural exchange. The southern waterfront features the iconic Liston, a arcaded promenade built during the French occupation (1809–1814), lined with cafés, restaurants, and pastel-colored buildings reflecting Parisian grandeur transported to a Greek island.

For sailors, mooring in the Old Port means waking to the fortress looming overhead, walking 50 meters to the Liston for morning coffee, and experiencing daily life in one of the Mediterranean's most historically layered cities. Unlike modern marinas designed purely for efficiency, the Old Port embodies the romance sailors have pursued for centuries—the integration of vessel, harbor, and human culture.

Old Port Logistics & Berth Availability

The Old Port is controlled by Corfu's municipal harbor authority and the Fortress administration. Sailing yacht berths are strictly limited and allocated based on size, draft, and maneuvering requirements. Most vessels are 35–45 feet with drafts of 1.5–2 meters. Entry and departure require careful timing and often assistance from local pilots familiar with the narrow harbor entrance and tidal currents.

Advance Booking is Mandatory: Unlike modern marinas with predictable berth turnover, the Old Port's limited slips must be reserved 6–12 months in advance. Boat4You maintains relationships with harbor authorities and secures these rare berths for clients months ahead. Late-notice inquiries (within 2–3 months) are unlikely to succeed regardless of budget or flexibility.

Seasonal Timing Matters: The Old Port's Venetian architecture is most spectacular in spring and autumn light; summer heat (32°C+) and occasional afternoon thunderstorms can diminish the experience. Many Boat4You sailors deliberately choose May, June, or September–October departures to optimize both harbor conditions and weather sailing potential.

Sailing Routes from the Old Port

The Southern Circuit (Paxos, Antipaxos, Preveza): Departing from the Old Port, pass the fortress walls and emerge into open water. The passage to Paxos (30–35nm) typically takes 5–8 hours with summer Maistro winds. Many Old Port charterers build 7–10 day itineraries south: Paxos (2 nights), Antipaxos (1 night), intermediate anchorages in southern Corfu, and potentially Preveza (55nm) for a longer multi-day cruise.

Northern Exploration (Kassiopi, Sidari): A closer alternative for sailors building confidence or seeking limited passage time. Kassiopi (45–50nm) and Sidari's Canal d'Amour (50nm) are achievable day passages from the Old Port, offering scenic anchorages and cultural exploration without extended offshore time.

Albanian Coast Adventure: For confident crews, Saranda and Ksamil (45–50nm) represent a memorable extension. The passage offshore from the Old Port is more exposed than from southern-based marinas but remains within modern racing yacht standards. Many Boat4You Old Port sailors treat the Albanian coast as their charter highlight.

Extended Passages (Multi-Week Charters): Sailors committing to 2–3 week charters can reach Lefkada (75nm), central Ionian islands, or even the Peloponnese—ambitious itineraries suitable for experienced, confident crews willing to accept weather variability.

The UNESCO Old Town: Post-Sailing Cultural Immersion

While moored at the Old Port, dedicate at least one full day to walking Corfu Town's UNESCO-listed Old Town on foot. The Fortress grounds (Fortezza) offer panoramic views across the Ionian strait to Albania and views of Paxos and Antipaxos to the south. The Museum of Asian Art, housed in an 18th-century palazzo, displays ceramics and sculptures reflecting Corfu's Venetian and British imperial legacy.

The Liston's cafés and restaurants serve Corfiot specialties (pastitsada, sofrito, lamb in tomato sauce) alongside Mediterranean seafood. Sunday passeggiata (evening promenade) along the Esplanade, the largest square in Greece, captures the essence of Mediterranean social life—families, couples, and elderly locals strolling before dinner.

Kumquat liqueur, a local specialty distilled from tiny citrus fruits grown on Corfu, makes an ideal gift or celebratory drink after an evening of sailing. Local olive oil and Corfiot wine (often overlooked in favor of larger-region wines) deserve exploration.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance must I book an Old Port sailing yacht charter?

Ideally, 6–12 months. Berths are extremely limited and allocated by harbor authorities. secures these rare slots through long-standing relationships; last-minute bookings (within 3 months) are rarely possible. Plan early to secure your dates.

Is the Old Port suitable for first-time bareboat sailors?

The harbor entrance and exit require precise maneuvering and local knowledge. While experienced bareboat sailors can manage with preparation, most first-time Old Port charterers benefit from professional skipper support (€150–250/day) for the first day, then continue bareboat if desired. strongly recommends this approach.

What are the challenges of the Old Port entrance and exit?

The harbor entrance is narrow and subject to tidal effects. Summer thermal winds can create gusty conditions near the fortress walls. Professional pilots are available for hire (€50–100 per passage) if you lack confidence or are unfamiliar with Mediterranean tidal harbor procedures. includes detailed briefing materials and pilot contact information.

Can we extend our charter if we wish to stay longer in Corfu Town?

Extensions are subject to the next charter's schedule and harbor availability. Given the Old Port's limited berths, extended stays (beyond the booked duration) are occasionally possible but must be confirmed in advance. Contact before booking if flexibility is essential to your plans.

What is the best season for an Old Port charter?

May–June and September–October offer optimal conditions: warm weather, reliable Maistro winds, fewer crowds, and lower accommodation costs if you arrive early or depart late. July–August are warm but crowded (with foot traffic, not sailing vessels). Spring (April) and autumn (October) offer pleasant sailing with occasionally cooler water (3–5mm wetsuit advised).
