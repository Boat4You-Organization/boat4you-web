Crewed vs. Bareboat Sailing Yachts

Crewed sailing yachts include professional captain and crew, managing sails, navigation, provisioning, and often cooking. Perfect for learning sailing, for those prioritizing comfort, or for groups wanting professional expertise. Bareboat sailing yachts require sailing certification (ICC/ASA recommended) and navigation competence; you manage the vessel independently. Bareboat charters cost less but demand experience and responsibility. Marina Kaštela's staff match your sailing level to the right vessel and can arrange professional skippers if you want crew support without full inclusion.

Best Seasons for Sailing Yacht Charters

May through September offers prime sailing conditions. May brings reliable wind (10–15 knots), calm seas (15–18°C), and minimal crowds—ideal for learning or casual sailing. June–August provide warmth (24–27°C) and longer daylight, though tourist density and wind intensity peak. September delivers summer sailing with lighter crowds and excellent provisioning. April and October suit experienced sailors; winter (November–March) is less ideal due to unpredictable wind patterns and colder water.

FAQ: Sailing Yacht Charters from Marina Kaštela

Do I need sailing experience to charter a sailing yacht?

Crewed sailing yachts require no experience—your crew sails. Bareboat charters require sailing certification (ICC/ASA recommended) and navigation familiarity. Many charterers hire professional skippers for support without full-crew inclusion. Check Boat4You listings for vessel-specific requirements.

How much wind is typical in the Adriatic?

May–September typically offers 10–20 knot winds, with more consistent patterns in May and September. July–August can see stronger gusts (15–25 knots). Calm days occur but aren't dominant. You'll sail most days without boredom.

What happens if there's no wind?

Most sailing yachts include small motors for calm spells. You'll motor to your destination if sails aren't viable—common practice on modern sailing yachts. This ensures schedule reliability.

Are sailing yachts comfortable for overnight cruising?

Yes. Sailing yachts feature proper cabins, full galleys, heads, and climate control. They're designed for extended voyages. Modern sailing yachts balance performance with comfort—ideal for multi-day charter weeks.

Can families and non-sailors join crewed sailing charters?

Yes. Crewed sailing yachts welcome families and non-sailors. Your crew handles sailing; you relax, learn, or participate as desired. Many families find sailing yachts educational and fun for kids.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.
