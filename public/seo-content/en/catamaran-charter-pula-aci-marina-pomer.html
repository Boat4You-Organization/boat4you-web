Sailing Routes & Destinations from ACI Marina Pomer

Brioni Islands: 20 nautical miles south—a 2.5-hour catamaran passage. Mali Brioni and Vele Brioni offer protected anchorages and national park exploration. Most charters anchor overnight and depart early-morning on return passage or onward voyage.

Rovinj Day Cruise: 32 nautical miles northeast—4 hours under sail or motor. Rovinj's picturesque harbor and hilltop old town make this the region's most photogenic day-sail destination. Early departure, lunch ashore, afternoon exploration, return by dusk.

Limski Channel: 35 nautical miles east—a 4–5-hour passage depending on wind. This dramatic fjord-like waterway, enclosed by 130-meter cliffs, rewards the overnight passage. Dinner at floating fish restaurants, overnight anchorage in calm cliff-enclosed water, morning return via sunrise navigation.

Kvarner Gulf Islands: Overnight passages to Krk, Mali Lošinj, Cres, and Rab. Multi-day itineraries (3–5 days) permit thorough exploration: provisioning at established harbors, cultural excursions, and diverse anchorages. Catamarans handle these passages reliably, with passenger comfort prioritized over speed.

Seasons, Weather & Sailing Patterns

Spring (April–May): Water 16–17°C, light winds, serene anchorages. Ideal for couples and first-time charterers. Summer (June–August): Warm days, robust maestral (12–18 knots), busy harbors. Occasional scirocco gales (southeast) 1–2 days per week. Autumn (September–October): Water 20–22°C, stable patterns, declining crowds. Most professional crews rank September–early October as premium season.

Winter (November–March): Possible but cold, rough conditions, short daylight. Most commercial operations pause. ACI Marina Pomer maintains winter availability for hardy crews at reduced rates.

Provisioning & Istrian Gastronomy

Pula's Central Market (Pijaca), supermarkets Plodine and Tommy, and ACI Marina Pomer's reception provide provisioning guidance. Fresh fish, seasonal vegetables, local cheeses, and wines are available pre-departure. Waterside restaurants in Rovinj, Novigrad, Limski Channel, and Kvarner harbors welcome catamaran crews with group rates and crew accommodations.

Istrian cuisine emphasizes truffles, asparagus, fuži pasta, prosciutto, and slow-braised beef. Local wines include Malvazija (crisp white) and Teran (bold red). Dinner ashore is standard practice; romantic sunset aperitifs on deck precede shore dining.

Bareboat vs. Crewed Catamaran Options

Bareboat Charters: Clients with sailing experience and valid certification (International Certificate of Competence or equivalent) charter autonomously. Modern navigation systems and autopilots reduce workload versus traditional sailing. Minimal engine maintenance is required; diesel engines are reliable and straightforward.

Crewed Charters: Professional skippers and stewardesses transform catamarans into floating hotels. Crew handles all navigation and mechanical operations while clients relax, explore, and socialize. Daily rates rise 40–50% with crew, but peace-of-mind and hospitality justify the premium for many parties.
