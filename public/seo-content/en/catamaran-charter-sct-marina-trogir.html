Catamaran Island Routes from SCT Marina

The southern route departs for Šolta, a 10-nautical-mile motor-sail bringing you to Maslinica bay by late morning. This protected anchorage offers excellent holding and waterfront restaurants serving fresh fish and local specialties. The catamaran's shallow draft allows exploration of Šolta's eastern and western coves, each with distinct character. Overnight here, explore the village, and depart the following morning toward Brač.

Brač island (15 nautical miles from Trogir) features Bol and the famous Zlatni Rat beach—a natural sand spit iconic in Mediterranean sailing photography. The catamaran's stability makes approaching the beach comfortable even in afternoon Maestral winds. Bol town offers provisioning, restaurants, and water-sports facilities. Many family charters base here for 2–3 days, exploring beaches and nearby anchorages.

The western route continues from Brač toward Hvar (another 12 nautical miles). Passing the Pakleni islands—a string of shallow, scenic coves—allows frequent stops, swimming breaks, and snorkeling. Hvar town rewards an overnight visit with lavender-farm day-trips, local wine tastings, and evening dining in narrow harbor-front alleys lined with restaurants and bars.

The extended route pushes to Vis island (40 nautical miles from Trogir), the outermost Adriatic island. The journey typically spans two sailing days with overnight stops at Brač or en route. Vis's traditional fishing villages, hidden coves, and remoteness appeal to crews seeking solitude and authentic Dalmatian character.

Catamarans excel in the shallow, variable conditions surrounding Trogir. The shallow draft (1.0–1.5 metres) versus monohulls' typical 1.8–2.0 metre draft opens entire bays and coves to exploration. Modern catamarans carry generous fresh-water tanks and solar panel arrays, reducing dependence on daily marina stops. For crews exploring remote islands with limited facilities, this self-sufficiency is invaluable. The dual-engine setup and responsive steering make tight anchorages manageable, even for less experienced crews.

Best Times to Charter from SCT Marina Trogir

May and June offer warm days (24–26°C), fresh morning winds, and manageable crowds. Water temperatures of 18–19°C support swimming, though wetsuits are optional. July and August bring intense heat (30–35°C), reliable afternoon Maestral winds, and busy anchorages—book early if summer sailing appeals.

September and October are widely considered ideal: water temperatures remain warm (22–24°C), day crowds diminish, and wind conditions vary enough to keep sailing engaging. April and November are possible but bring shorter daylight hours and occasional Bura squalls from the northeast.

Provisioning and Local Flavors

SCT Marina Trogir is steps from provisioning shops, supermarkets, and fresh markets. The Green Market (Zelena Tržnica) in the old town supplies daily vegetables, fruit, and local cheese. Fish markets near the harbor stock morning catches ideal for onboard preparation. Olive oils, local wines (Plavac Mali reds, Posip whites), and Croatian spirits complete the pantry.

Trogir's restaurants and waterfront cafés offer casual lunches and fine-dining dinners. Local specialties—pašticada (slow-braised beef), fresh fish prepared as gregada, soparnik (chard pie)—reflect generations of Dalmatian culinary tradition. Many catamaran charters prepare simple onboard meals and dine ashore 3–4 times per week at island tavernas.

Frequently Asked Questions About Catamaran Charters from SCT Marina Trogir

Is SCT Marina Trogir convenient for airport transfers?

Absolutely. The marina is five minutes by car from Split Airport (Kaštela SPU). Most rental-car companies have desks at the airport; a quick drive delivers you to SCT Marina Trogir ready for catamaran boarding by early afternoon on arrival day.

Are catamarans safe in the Adriatic's summer conditions?

Yes. Catamarans are inherently stable in variable winds and chop. The Maestral winds typical of summer (10–18 knots) are predictable and manageable. The Adriatic's relatively short fetch (distance across open water) means waves rarely exceed 1.5–2 metres even in strong conditions. the charter fleet maintenance ensures modern navigation systems and reliable engines.

What provisioning options exist beyond SCT Marina Trogir?

Excellent. Brač has supermarkets and fish markets in Bol. Hvar town offers gourmet provisioning and chandleries. Vis has basic supplies but limited variety—stock up before departing. The catamaran's large water and fuel tanks support 3–5 days away from major marinas, making provisioning stops natural rather than urgent.

Can a catamaran explore shallow anchorages around the Pakleni islands?

Yes. The Pakleni islands are surrounded by shallow coves (often 2–5 metres), perfect for catamarans with 1.0–1.5 metre drafts. Monohulls with deeper keels struggle to access these anchorages. A catamaran lets you explore hidden beaches and remote spots few charter yachts can reach.

What's the fuel cost for a week-long catamaran charter from SCT Marina?

Expect €350–€550, depending on engine hours and sailing-to-motoring ratio. Motor-sailing (combining engine and sail) is more economical than pure motoring. Modern catamarans with efficient diesel engines achieve approximately 1–1.5 nautical miles per liter.
