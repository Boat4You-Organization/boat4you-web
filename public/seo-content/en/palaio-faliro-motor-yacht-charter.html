Flisvos Marina and Waterfront Amenities

Palaio Faliro is home to Flisvos Marina, one of the Saronic Gulf's most prestigious mooring facilities. The Flisvos waterfront is lined with upscale restaurants, cafes, and shops. After your charter, you can enjoy fresh-caught fish at a beachside taverna or aperitivo at a rooftop bar overlooking the Saronic Gulf.

The atmosphere is sophisticated yet relaxed, embodying the essence of the Athens Riviera. The Palaio Faliro coastal area offers excellent walking and cycling paths, perfect for exploring before or after your motor yacht charter.

Day Sail Routes and Island Destinations

One classic route from Palaio Faliro takes you to Aegina, famous for its pistachio orchards and charming waterfront. The sail takes about 1.5 hours, and Aegina's protected harbor offers excellent anchorage. Explore the Temple of Aphaia, a stunning ancient ruin overlooking the sea, then dine on fresh seafood at waterfront restaurants.

Another favorite is the Poros route: sail past the green hills of the Peloponnese, arriving at this charming island town wedged between two hills. Poros is known for its narrow channels and dramatic topography. Spend the afternoon swimming in sheltered bays before returning to Palaio Faliro at sunset.

For ambitious explorers, a full-day charter to Hydra offers a glimpse of island life unchanged for decades. Hydra's strict no-cars policy and donkey-powered transportation create a unique time-warp atmosphere. Boat4You can arrange overnight charters to Hydra, returning the next day.

After a full day on the water, return to the city for fine dining and classical music performances.

International flights arrive at Athens International Airport 'Eleftherios Venizelos,' just 30 kilometers away. Many guests fly into Athens, spend a night exploring the city, then begin their motor yacht charter the next morning. Palaio Faliro is on the metro line, making transportation seamless.

Seasons and Sailing Weather

The Saronic Gulf enjoys excellent sailing conditions from April through October. Summer months (July–August) are hot and dry, with water temperatures around 24°C. The Meltemia winds blow from the north, providing steady sailing conditions ideal for motor yachts.

Spring (May–June) offers perfect sailing weather with moderate temperatures and manageable wind. Fewer tourists mean more peaceful anchorages and better restaurant availability. Fall (September–October) is equally pleasant, with warm but not excessive heat.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far is Palaio Faliro from Athens Airport?

Athens International Airport is approximately 30 kilometers away, about a 30-40 minute drive depending on traffic. Airport shuttles and taxis are readily available.

Can we visit Aegina, Poros, and Hydra in one day?

A single-day charter typically covers one island well. Aegina takes 1.5 hours to reach, or Poros takes 2 hours. Multi-day charters allow visiting multiple islands with overnight stays, which we highly recommend.

What's the difference between skippered and bareboat charters?

Skippered charters include a professional captain who navigates and operates the yacht, so you relax and enjoy. Bareboat charters require you to have a valid maritime license and handle navigation. We offer both options.

Are pets welcome aboard?

Yes, well-behaved pets are welcome. Please inform us when booking so we can prepare accordingly.

What if I'm prone to seasickness?

The Saronic Gulf is generally protected and calm. Motion sickness is rare. We provide medication upon request, and our experienced skippers choose routes that minimize boat motion.
