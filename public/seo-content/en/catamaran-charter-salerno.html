Sailing Routes from Salerno Harbor

The classic Salerno itinerary spans three days to Capri, Positano, and Amalfi town—a 58-nautical-mile loop with overnight anchoring at Furore Fjord (32nm southeast), where towering limestone cliffs plunge directly to azure water. Day-two passage to Capri takes 6–7 hours under typical northwesterly conditions, with the Blue Grotto (Grotta Azzurra) accessible only via tender due to catamaran beam constraints. Extended charters continue southeastward to the Cilento headlands, reaching Palinuro's dramatic sea arches (31nm south) by evening.

For exploration-focused parties, multi-day Salerno charters often include Salerno to Amalfi village (16nm), with overnight anchorage off Conca dei Marini's pebble beach. Return passages to Salerno typically occur via the Monti Lattari mountain ridge wind channel, where afternoon thermals create reliable 10–16 knot breezes ideal for downwind catamaran sailing.

Weather and Sailing Seasons

Salerno's sailing season peaks April–October, with summer thermal winds (northwesterly 8–14 knots) dominating afternoons. Spring conditions (March–May) feature variable morning breezes and occasional sirocco events from the south (18–25 knots, 48-hour duration), requiring reef deployment on catamaran mains. Autumn storms (September–November) bring Atlantic-origin low-pressure systems, with wind shifts to southwesterly at 15–22 knots. Winter operations (December–February) are feasible but require experienced crew: seas build to 4–6 feet, and temperature inversions create localized fog near coastal features.

Monthly rainfall concentrates in November–December (120–150mm), with minimal precipitation June–August. Summer air temperatures reach 28–32°C, cooled by offshore breezes. Sea temperature peaks in August (24–25°C) and drops to 13–14°C by February. These conditions make late April through May ideal for catamaran charter comfort, balancing wind strength with calm sea states.
