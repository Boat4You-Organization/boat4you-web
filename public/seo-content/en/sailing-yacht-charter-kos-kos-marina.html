Dodecanese Sailing Routes & Wind Patterns

The Dodecanese archipelago spans from Rhodes (south) to Patmos and Leros (north), with countless intermediate islands and anchorages. Summer Meltemi winds blow from the north at 12-18 kts, creating ideal sailing conditions. Island passages range from 20 to 60 nautical miles—perfect day or overnight sails.

Sailing yachts with their light draft can anchor in shallow, scenic bays where larger vessels struggle. Symi, Naxos, Antiparos, and lesser-known islets reward explorers with turquoise waters, fish tavernas, and authentic island life.

Exploring Islands: History, Culture & Cuisine

Rhodes, the largest Dodecanese island, boasts the medieval Old Town and legendary Colossus ruins. Symi is famous for colorful harbor houses and sponge diving heritage. Patmos, a spiritual destination since St. John's Apocalypse, offers monasteries and monastery wine. Leros has a quieter, family-friendly atmosphere with protected beaches.

Every village marina and anchorage features fresh-catch restaurants, local wines, and warm hospitality. Dock your yacht and dine ashore each evening.

Seasonal Sailing Conditions

June through September offers the most reliable sailing. July-August brings peak Meltemi (15-25 kts), requiring experienced crews but offering exhilarating sailing. June, September, and early October provide gentler conditions, fewer tourists, and lower prices.

Spring (April-May) and autumn (October-November) are shoulder seasons with variable winds, occasional calm days, and comfortable water temperatures (19-25°C).
