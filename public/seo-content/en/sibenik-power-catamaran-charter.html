Power Catamaran Charter in Šibenik, Croatia

A power catamaran (motor catamaran) combines the spacious comfort and stability of twin hulls with diesel engine efficiency and reliability. Ideal for families, mixed-experience groups, and those prioritizing stability and cabin comfort over sailing tradition. From Šibenik, power catamarans access the Kornati archipelago, Krka National Park, and protected coastal routes with weatherproof confidence and spacious living quarters.

Why Power Catamarans Excel in Šibenik Waters

Dual hulls provide unmatched stability—less heel angle means easier movement below deck and comfortable lounging in any sea state. Modern power catamarans (35–48 feet) offer open-plan saloons, 4–6 guest cabins, and spacious sun decks—more living space than equivalent monohull motor yachts. Shallow draft (0.9–1.3 m) accesses Šibenik's protected anchorages, Kornati's narrow coves, and Krka River passages that deep-draft yachts cannot reach.

Twin diesel engines deliver reliable power and fuel economy (1.5–2.5 NM per liter typical). Single-engine redundancy is a safety advantage. No sailing skills required—twin engines handle all maneuvers effortlessly. Weather independence makes power catamarans ideal for less experienced mariners or families with children.

Top Power Catamaran Routes from Šibenik

Kornati National Park Explorer (4–6 days): Depart Šibenik, motor southeast 30 NM to Murter in 2 hours, enter Kornati's 140 islands. Anchor at Levrnaka, Rava, Pirovac—shallow draft accesses tight coves monohulls cannot. Daytime island hopping, evening relaxation in spacious saloons. Snorkeling and fishing daily.

Krka River Inland Adventure: Motor upstream the Krka River to Skradin (10 NM, 1.5 hours). Anchor near Skradinski Buk waterfall and medieval town. A magical inland experience in protected waterway. Ideal for families—catamaran stability suits children better than monohulls.

Šibenik to Rogoznica (2–3 days): Motor south 25 NM (1.5 hours), stop Zlarin (coral diving), Prvić (Faust Vrančić Museum), Kaprije (peaceful village), Žirje (isolated stone houses), Rogoznica (wine region). Flexible daily scheduling, no wind dependency.

Leisurely Island Circuit (3–4 days): Slower pace emphasizing onboard comfort. Šibenik → Zlarin → Prvić → Kaprije, extended mooring in each port, explore villages and restaurants. Perfect for families with children and less experienced boaters.

Technical Advantages of Power Catamarans in Šibenik

Dual hulls reduce pitch and roll in moderate seas, creating a stable, comfortable platform. Modern power catamarans plane at cruising speeds, improving fuel efficiency versus displacement hulls. Shallow draft (under 1.3 m) accesses most Šibenik anchorages. Generator systems support air conditioning, refrigeration, and water makers—modern onboard amenities absent on sailing yachts.

Twin diesel engines provide redundancy and single-engine maneuvering capability. Charter companies in Šibenik maintain modern power catamaran fleets through Marina Mandalina (D-Marin) and Marina Frapa in Rogoznica. Regular servicing and crew support ensure reliability.

Pricing for Power Catamaran Charters

Weekly power catamaran charters (35–40 feet) in Šibenik range €1,800–€3,200, with larger models (45–48 feet) running €2,800–€4,500/week. Peak rates apply July–August. Shoulder seasons (May–June, September) offer 20–30% discounts. All-inclusive crewed charters add €250–€400/day for skipper and crew.

Fuel costs are moderate: budget €200–€350/week for 30–50 NM daily cruising. Marina mooring fees average €25–€50/night. Provisioning at Rogoznica or Šibenik supermarkets is affordable, with excellent fresh seafood at local markets.

Best Time & Bases for Power Catamaran Cruising

Ideal Seasons: June and September offer perfect weather and lower costs than peak summer. July–August are warm and busy; fuel costs are higher due to increased air conditioning demands. Water temperature is pleasant May–September (21–27 °C).

Recommended Bases: Marina Mandalina (D-Marin Šibenik) in the city center offers full services, provisioning, and seamless Šibenik Channel access. Marina Frapa in Rogoznica (25 km south) is quieter, with excellent restaurants and wine culture. Both support power catamaran check-ins and crew services.

Sample 7-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Collect power catamaran at Marina Mandalina, provisioning, motor 10 NM to Zlarin. Afternoon swimming, dinner at harborside restaurant. Overnight Zlarin.

Day 2: Morning dive/snorkel, motor 8 NM to Prvić, visit Faust Vrančić Museum, explore medieval village. Overnight Prvić.

Day 6: Motor 30 NM south to Rogoznica (2 hours), full-service harbor with wine bars, restaurants, supermarket. Provisioning and wine tasting. Overnight Rogoznica.

Day 7: Return to Šibenik (25 NM, 1.5 hours), explore old town, UNESCO cathedral. Final evening, prepare for departure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need sailing experience to charter a power catamaran?

No sailing required—twin engines handle all propulsion and maneuvering. Bareboat charters typically require proof of boating experience. First-time boaters should opt for crewed charters with a professional skipper. Boat4You connects you with experienced crew options.

How much fuel does a power catamaran consume daily?

Twin diesel engines consume 15–25 liters/hour at cruising speed (10–12 knots). A typical 6-hour day cruise costs €150–€250 in fuel. Idling at anchor is minimal-consumption. Budget €250–€400/week for moderate daily cruising patterns.

Are power catamarans more comfortable than motor yachts?

Yes, for most passengers. Shallow roll, spacious cabins, and larger saloons create superior comfort. Single-hull motor yachts feel tighter and heel slightly in turns. Families and less experienced boaters prefer catamarans' stable platform.

Can a power catamaran navigate the Šibenik Channel?

Easily. The 40 m minimum width accommodates catamaran beams (14–16 m typical). Dual engines provide precise control for the narrow turn. A pilot passage (€100–€150) is optional but helpful for first-timers.

Is the Krka River passage suitable for power catamarans?

Yes, for yachts under 45 feet with draft under 1.3 m. Twin engines deliver reliable power for narrow winding passages. Navigation is straightforward and scenic. Seasonal restrictions apply (May–September check with charter company).
