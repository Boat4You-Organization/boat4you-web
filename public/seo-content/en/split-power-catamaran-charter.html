Power Catamaran Charter in Split: Speed, Space, and Stability Combined

A power catamaran merges the best attributes of catamarans—wide beam, shallow draft, and incredible stability—with reliable diesel performance and genuine cruising speed. These dual-hulled power vessels, typically 40–60 feet, deliver steady 12–16 knot cruising, twin-engine redundancy, and the spacious deck and saloon that define the catamaran experience. Whether you're escaping crowds in the Pakleni islands, maximizing daily distances to visit more anchorages, or simply enjoying the rare combination of comfort and performance, a power catamaran in Split provides the ideal hybrid experience.

Why Power Catamarans Excel in Central Dalmatia

Power catamarans inherit catamarans' greatest advantages: shallow draft (typically 1.0–1.5 metres), minimal heel even at anchor in swell, and exceptional deck and cabin space. The added element—reliable diesel power—removes dependency on Maestral winds and calms. You cruise predictably, make daily distances effortlessly, and explore with independence from weather forecasts. The result is a vessel that combines the comfort and stability of a catamaran with the freedom and speed of motor power.

The wide beam of a power catamaran (10–12 metres on a 45-footer) means the saloon is genuinely spacious, and the deck offers comfortable lounging and sun exposure. The shallow draft opens access to Maslinica bay on Šolta, the Pakleni islands' tightest anchorages, and the remote eastern coves of Vis where deeper-drafted vessels struggle. This combination of comfort, stability, and access makes power catamarans exceptionally suited to Central Dalmatia.

Power vs. Sail: Understanding the Power Catamaran

Power catamarans prioritize speed and reliability over sailing. Most feature twin diesel engines delivering 12–16 knots cruising, allowing passage planning with confidence. Fuel consumption is predictable—typically 35–50 litres per hour at cruise speed. Many owners include solar panels and good battery systems, allowing extended periods at anchor using minimal genset (generator) runtime.

Sailing catamarans (discussed separately) prioritize sail performance while carrying auxiliary engines. The distinction matters: power catamarans are purpose-built for motoring, with hull designs optimized for fuel efficiency at cruise speed. Sailing catamarans sacrifice some speed and efficiency to excel under sail. For destinations like Split where calm weather is common and distances are moderate, the power catamaran's efficiency and reliability are genuine advantages.

Technical Advantages of Power Catamarans in Central Dalmatia

The twin-hull design provides exceptional stability and minimal motion at anchor, crucial for comfort during extended anchorage periods. The shallow draft opens access that monohulls cannot reach. Modern power catamarans feature autopilot, sophisticated GPS/chartplotting, and often integrated entertainment systems. Twin engines mean that engine failure doesn't disable the vessel—you can continue motoring, albeit more slowly.

The wide beam creates generous galley and saloon space, with often superior ventilation and comfort compared to monohulls. Large fuel and water tanks (enabled by the wide beam) allow extended cruising without frequent refuelling. Many modern power catamarans feature gensets (generators) and solar arrays, enabling comfortable living while anchored.

Pricing and What to Expect in Split

Power catamaran charters in Split typically cost €3,500–€7,000 per week, depending on size and season. A 45-foot power catamaran in high season (July–August) averages €4,500–€6,000. Low season (May, June, September, October) sees €3,000–€4,500. Fuel and mooring are additional costs; provisioning is often included or available at modest cost.

Fuel costs are moderate due to the efficient hull design—typically €400–€600 weekly at moderate cruising speeds. ACI Marina Split charges €60–€90 nightly for a 45-foot power catamaran. Provisioning for two people costs €250–€400 weekly. The total weekly cost (rental plus fuel, mooring, and provisions) typically falls between €5,200–€7,000, competitive with similarly-sized sailing yachts.

Best Season and Departure Logistics

Power catamarans operate May through October, with June through September offering warmest conditions. Power catamarans excel in shoulder seasons (May, early June, September, October) when sailing winds are variable; the engine provides reliable propulsion regardless. April and November are possible but offer cooler water and shorter daylight.

Most Split-based power catamarans depart from ACI Marina Split (largest fleet) or Marina Kaštela (quieter, 12 kilometres west). Marina Trogir (30 kilometres northeast) suits those planning northward routes towards medieval Trogir and the Kaštela bays.

Frequently Asked Questions About Power Catamarans

How does a power catamaran differ from a sailing catamaran?

Power catamarans prioritize fuel efficiency and speed at cruise speeds (12–16 knots); they carry small sails for stabilization but primary propulsion is diesel. Sailing catamarans optimize for wind performance and carry larger sails. For Split waters where calm conditions are common, power catamarans offer more reliable speed and efficiency.

Are power catamarans slow compared to motor yachts?

No. Power catamarans typically cruise at 12–16 knots, comparable to traditional motor yachts. The advantage is fuel efficiency and stability; the trade-off is less extreme speed (compared to speedboats) and less aggressive hull design.

Can I explore the Pakleni islands in a power catamaran?

Absolutely. The shallow draft and wide beam make power catamarans ideal for Pakleni exploration. You can anchor in tight spots where monohulls struggle, discovering quiet bays and excellent snorkelling.

What's the fuel cost for a week of cruising?

Typically €400–€600, depending on cruising speed and distance. Eco-cruising at 8–10 knots reduces consumption; higher-speed cruising increases fuel burn. Power catamarans are notably efficient due to their optimized hull design.

Is a power catamaran comfortable for families?

Yes. The spacious cabins, minimal heel, and broad, stable deck make power catamarans excellent for families. The comfortable motion at anchor and predictable cruising appeal to those wanting relaxation alongside exploration.
