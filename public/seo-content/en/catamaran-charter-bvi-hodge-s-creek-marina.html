Classic Routes from Hodge's Creek

Virgin Gorda lies just 8 nautical miles south, a gentle reach of 90 minutes under sail. The Baths, a UNESCO-listed natural wonder, reward anchored exploration with massive granite boulders framing crystalline pools. Beyond Virgin Gorda, Cooper Island delivers world-class snorkelling, Rum Runner's Beach, and excellent overnight protection in Manchioneel Bay.

The Indians—a small cluster of rocky formations between Virgin Gorda and Jost Van Dyke—rank among the best snorkelling sites in the archipelago. Coral heads, tropical fish, sea turtles, and occasionally eagle rays populate these waters. Passages from Hodge's Creek to The Indians take 2–3 hours under normal trade-wind conditions.

Experienced sailors often venture to Anegada, the northernmost BVI island, famous for its pristine barrier reef and lobster fishing culture. The 20-nautical-mile passage requires attention to reef navigation, but anchorages like Loblolly Bay offer unforgettable seclusion. For shorter day sails, Gorda Sound and Bitter End Yacht Club (anchored off North Sound, Virgin Gorda) provide sheltered turquoise waters and excellent provisioning.

Sailing Season and Weather Patterns

The BVI sailing season runs from November through May, with December and January offering the most stable weather and the strongest winds for performance sailing. Trade winds average 15–22 knots during peak season, providing thrilling beam reaches and powerful downwind runs. Sea state typically ranges from 1–2 meters, manageable for catamarans with their inherent stability.

Hurricane season (June–November) brings warmer, lighter winds and occasional tropical systems. Many sailors prefer the quiet beauty of the shoulder seasons—late April through early May, and September through early November—when trade winds relax but the BVI remain splendid for leisurely cruising.

Local Attractions and Provisioning

Hodge's Creek's proximity to Tortola's eastern communities means fresh groceries, restaurants, and cultural experiences are minutes away by dinghy. Cane Garden Bay, on Tortola's north shore, offers stunning sandy beaches, beachfront restaurants, and the famous painkiller cocktails at Soggy Dollar Bar—accessible by tender from offshore anchorage.

Norman Island, south of Virgin Gorda, inspired Robert Louis Stevenson's "Treasure Island." Caves line the southern coast, and the Willy T (a moored bar barge) provides themed entertainment and cold drinks. Jost Van Dyke hosts Foxy's, a legendary beach bar where the proprietor himself composes witty songs about guests, creating memories that last a lifetime.

For provisioning, Road Town (Tortola's capital) remains the primary hub with supermarkets, chandleries, and fish markets. can pre-order supplies or arrange provisioning services, ensuring your galley is fully stocked before departure from Hodge's Creek.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a sailing license to charter a catamaran from Hodge's Creek?

Bareboat charters require proof of sailing experience, typically a valid sailing certificate (RYA, ASA, or equivalent) or a demonstrated log of hours. Skippered charters require no certification—your professional crew handles sailing while you relax. assesses each skipper's qualifications during the booking process.

What's the best time to visit the BVI by catamaran?

December through March offers stable weather, strong trade winds, and ideal sailing conditions. However, autumn (September–November) and late spring (April–May) deliver fewer crowds, lower prices, and equally beautiful sailing. Hurricane season is rare; the BVI lies south of typical hurricane tracks.

Can I hire a professional crew and chef?

Extended charters (7+ days) can include a dedicated chef who plans meals, manages provisioning, and caters to dietary preferences. This option elevates the charter experience, especially for groups or special occasions.

Are pets allowed on board?

Pets are permitted on most catamarans with prior approval. Ensure pets are comfortable with motion and saltwater exposure, and carry necessary documentation if traveling internationally. Inform during booking.

What if weather forces us to change our planned itinerary?

Sailing plans are flexible—trade winds and sea conditions are monitored daily. Experienced crews adjust itineraries to maximize enjoyment and safety, often discovering unexpected anchorages and diving sites along the way.
