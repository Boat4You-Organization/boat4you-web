Alimos offers diverse motor yachts. Small coastal cruisers (12–16m, 4–6 berths, 500–800 hp) suit couples and small families seeking simplicity and coastal exploration. Bareboat motor yachts require less sailing expertise than sailing vessels. A boating license (RYA Power Boat Level 2 or equivalent) and basic seamanship suffice. Charter companies provide detailed briefings: navigation systems, engine operations, moorings, safety procedures, and emergency protocols. Many first-timers find motor yachts intuitive—steering is familiar (like driving a car), and engine control is straightforward.

Skippered & Crewed Motor Yacht Options

First-timers, large groups, or those preferring relaxation often hire a professional skipper (€130–€180/day). Power catamarans prioritize stability, deck space, and non-sailor comfort. Sailing yachts deliver traditional seacraft, lower fuel costs, and romantic sailing experiences.

Motor Yacht Charter FAQs

What licenses do I need to bareboat a motor yacht?

Most Alimos charter companies require RYA Power Boat Level 2 (British standard) or equivalent national certification. Proof of competence typically includes coastal cruising experience. No sailing experience is necessary. If you lack certification, hire a skipper or take a refresher course at the marina. coordinates licensing seamlessly.

How much does a bareboat motor yacht cost per week?

Weekly bareboat rates at Alimos range €1,200–€3,500 depending on boat size, age, and season. High season (July–August) costs more; shoulder seasons (May–June, September) offer better value. Fuel (€150–€600/week), skipper (€800–€1,200/week if hired), and provisioning are additional. shows all-in estimates during booking.

Can I hire a skipper for a few days instead of a week?

Yes. Skippers charge daily rates (€130–€180/day), weekly discounts apply. Many crews hire skippers for transition days (arrival, departure, challenging passages) while self-driving calm periods. coordinates partial skipper arrangements based on your specific needs.

Are tenders included in motor yacht charters?

Smaller yachts (12–18m) may not carry tenders; mid-range and larger yachts usually do. Check your specific listing. Tender availability enables beach access and exploration of shallow anchorages unavailable to larger vessels. clarifies tender inclusion in all listings.

What's the best season for motor yacht chartering?

May–June and September–October offer ideal conditions: steady winds, warm seas, and moderate crowds. July–August bring peak tourism and strong Meltemi winds, but motor yachts handle both well. Winter (November–March) offers solitude and dramatic seascapes for experienced crews. advises first-timers to book May–June or September for optimal balance.
