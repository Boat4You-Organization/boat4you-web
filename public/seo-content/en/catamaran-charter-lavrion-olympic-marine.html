Ancient Laurion: History Beyond the Helm

Lavrion's hinterland echoes with antiquity. The silver mines of Laurion funded Athens' naval dominance during the Classical period. Today, the Museum of Lavrion documents this heritage. Frequently Asked Questions

How far is Lavrion Olympic Marine from Athens?

Lavrion is approximately 40 kilometers (25 miles) southeast of central Athens, a 40–50 minute drive via the coastal motorway. The journey from Athens International Airport (ATH) is similarly quick, making Lavrion ideal for fly-in charters and crews arriving internationally.

What is the typical sailing distance to the Cyclades from Lavrion?

Kea is 15 nautical miles, Kythnos around 25 nautical miles, and Syros approximately 35 nautical miles. These short distances allow crews to optimize exploration, minimize open-water transits, and enjoy more time anchored in picturesque coves—a key advantage of the Lavrion position.

Are catamarans suitable for inexperienced crews?

Absolutely. Catamarans' wide beam and stability make them forgiving platforms for novice sailors. Modern charter catamarans feature intuitive controls, shallow draft for exploring protected anchorages, and dual engines for reliable power when wind is light. matches crews with appropriate vessels and offers optional skipper services.

What charter duration is optimal from Lavrion?

Seven to ten days is ideal. A week allows exploration of Kea, Kythnos, and Syros, with leisurely anchorages, swimming, and cultural visits. Longer charters enable ventures to Mykonos, Delos, or the southern Cyclades, while shorter four-day charters are feasible for focused island-hopping near Kea.

Is provisioning readily available at Lavrion?

Yes. The town of Lavrion offers supermarkets, fresh-fish vendors, bakeries, and tavernas within walking distance. Olympic Marine's staff assist with provisioning orders, allowing crews to load supplies directly onto their catamaran before departure.

What makes catamarans superior to monohulls in Greek waters?

Catamarans offer exceptional stability due to their wide dual-hull design, making them ideal for families and less experienced crews. The shallow draft permits access to shallow anchorages and protected bays unreachable by deeper-draft monohulls. Spacious interiors and separate cabins provide comfort on extended charters. The power-sailing capability ensures progress even during light-wind Saronic conditions.
