Island Sailing Routes & Destinations

Ugljan and Pašman—twin islands flanking the Zadar coastline—offer the closest, most accessible sailing experiences. Ugljan, stretching 23 kilometres long, features sheltered bays, pine-backed beaches, and the charming village of Preko. A typical sail takes 45 minutes from Borik. Pašman, across the narrow channel, mirrors Ugljan in beauty but attracts fewer crowds. Many charters spend 2–3 days exploring these twin islands, motor-sailing between anchorages and enjoying the consistent thermal winds and manageable sea states that characterize the Zadar region in summer.

Dugi Otok and Telašćica Bay lie roughly 3–4 hours south by sail. This deep, protected natural harbor ranks among the Mediterranean's finest anchorages. The bay's eastern shore rises dramatically from the water; the western side slopes gently into Mir Salt Lake (a freshwater anomaly just behind the beach) and hiking trails revealing island panoramas. Telašćica is ideal for overnight mooring, swimming in crystal waters, and experiencing the Adriatic's slower pace.

The Kornati National Park—a UNESCO-protected archipelago of 140 limestone islands—lies 30 nautical miles south. Under sail with favorable Maestral wind, you'll reach the outer islands in 5–6 hours from Borik. Many charters dedicate 4–5 days to the Kornati, sailing through narrow straits, anchoring in hidden coves, and experiencing the Adriatic's most pristine, undeveloped sailing grounds. The Kornati demands respect and careful planning, but rewards thorough sailors with unforgettable seascapes and solitude.

Shorter day-sail options include the northern islands near Nin—a historic salt-making town—and the quieter bays of the Zadar coastline itself, perfect for families or those seeking gentler wind conditions.

Best Sailing Seasons & Weather Patterns

Spring (April–May) brings warm days, calm mornings, and water temperatures climbing to 18–19°C. Winds are typically light, favoring motor-sailing, but crowds remain minimal and the sailing region retains its quiet character. Summer (June–August) introduces the reliable Maestral—a steady northwest wind creating ideal sailing conditions for monohulls. Thermal effects often generate afternoon breezes even on seemingly calm mornings, and water warms to 22–25°C. July–August experience higher activity and heat; consider early June or late August for better balance.

Autumn (September–October) is celebrated as peak sailing season: water temperatures remain warm (22–23°C), Maestral winds persist and remain consistent, crowds diminish noticeably, and extended daylight permits unhurried departures and arrivals. Winter is possible but brings variable conditions, occasional Bura squalls from the northeast, and shortened sailing days. Most sailing charters operate April–October, with September and early October offering the finest conditions.

Zadar Old Town & Local Culture

Zadar Old Town, minutes from the marina, is a UNESCO World Heritage site steeped in Roman, medieval, and Venetian history. The Roman Forum, dating to the 1st century AD, anchors the town center; nearby stand the Church of Saint Donatus (a 9th-century Byzantine-influenced structure) and the Cathedral of Saint Anastasia. The iconic Sea Organ—an art installation that produces haunting, wind-driven tones as Mediterranean waves pass through underground pipes—draws sunset visitors daily.

The town's culinary tradition centers on fresh fish, locally caught octopus, and traditional Dalmatian pašticada. The market offers daily produce, Pag cheese (from the island of Pag, north of Zadar), cured meats (prosciutto), and fresh bread. Sunset walks along the Riva promenade, drinks at waterfront cafés, and informal dinners at family-run taverns near the harbor are quintessential Zadar experiences. Local wines—particularly Babić reds and white Malvazija—enhance meals aboard your sailing yacht or ashore.

Can we reach the Kornati on a day sail from Borik?

Yes. The Kornati National Park entrance lies roughly 30 nautical miles south. With a morning departure and favorable Maestral wind, you can reach the outer islands by late afternoon, though most sailors dedicate 4–5 days to truly explore the archipelago's sheltered anchorages and dramatic coastlines.

What are mooring costs at Ugljan, Pašman, and the Kornati?

Ugljan and Pašman anchorages typically charge €20–€40 per night for a 30-foot yacht at managed harbors; natural anchorages are free but less protected. The Kornati National Park operates designated mooring buoys (€10–€20 daily) rather than traditional marina berths; vessels tie to fixed buoys to preserve the seabed environment.

Is the Borik location convenient for airport transfers?

Absolutely. Zadar Airport lies 8 kilometres away (15-minute drive). The marina can arrange taxi, rental car, or shuttle service for crew arrivals and departures. Many international sailors arrive midday, check in at the marina, and depart Borik by late afternoon or the following morning.
