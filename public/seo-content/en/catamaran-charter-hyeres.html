Catamaran Charter from Hyères Marina

Hyères Marina (Port de Hyères), the region's primary municipal marina, serves as an ideal departure point for catamaran exploration of the Côte d'Azur and the protected Îles d'Hyères national park. With modern berth facilities, reliable fuel and water services, and proximity to the city's medieval old town, Hyères Marina combines operational efficiency with authentic Mediterranean charm. Ten catamarans through Boat4You are permanently berth-ready at this facility, offering crews easy access to provisioning, restaurant amenities, and the island-studded sailing waters that define the southern Côte d'Azur. The marina's position as a working municipal port—distinct from exclusive resort marinas—preserves an authentic character while delivering the facilities and services that contemporary charter sailors expect.

Port de Hyères: Facility Overview and Operational Advantages

Port de Hyères is a modern municipal facility operated by the city authorities, accommodating both permanent resident vessels and temporary charter yachts. The marina comprises several distinct sections: the primary basin (basin principal) handling larger yachts and commercial traffic, a secondary basin dedicated to pleasure craft, and dedicated mooring zones for catamarans and other charter vessels. The facility is designed with working functionality as its primary goal—this is a harbour for sailors, not a resort—which translates to reasonable pricing, professional services, and an atmosphere of authentic maritime activity rather than luxury tourism.

Berth assignments for Boat4You catamaran charters typically occur in the dedicated pleasure-craft basin, positioned for easy access to shore facilities and minimal interference from commercial traffic. Individual berths are of standard dimensions (typically 12–14 metres for 40-foot catamarans); modern finger-pier construction and well-maintained cleats ensure secure and safe mooring. The berth layout prioritises efficiency: fuel dispensing is centralised, water connections are standardised, and shore-based facilities (reception, facilities buildings) are positioned for minimal walking distance.

Berth Layout, Mooring Configuration, and Vessel Positioning

The pleasure-craft basin occupies the western section of Port de Hyères, comprising approximately 150 berths arranged in a series of parallel finger piers (jetées). Charter catamaran berths are clustered in a dedicated section managed by the charter operators and Boat4You, allowing for convenient crew briefings, supply transfers, and coordination. The layout is designed for efficient operations: crews can board without interfering with neighbour vessels; provisioning trucks can deliver to designated zones without congestion; and fuel barges can serve multiple vessels in sequence without logistical complications.

Mooring systems are modern and standardised. Catamaran-specific berths feature appropriately spaced cleats, spring lines, and bow lines; fenders and protective equipment are typically provided or available for rental. Water depth in the dedicated berths ranges from 3.5 to 5.0 metres, sufficient for any catamaran in the Boat4You fleet. Swing-space is adequate for vessels to rotate with wind or current without touching neighbours.

Fuel, Water, Power, and Essential Services

Port de Hyères offers modern utility services meeting contemporary yacht standards. Fuel (diesel and petrol) is dispensed from a dedicated fuel pontoon (capitainerie) positioned centrally in the pleasure basin; supply is continuous and reliable, with typical availability from 08:00 to 18:00 daily in high season (adjustable in low season). Fuel quality meets all EU standards; modern dispensing equipment and metering ensure accurate charges. Fresh water is available dockside from standardised connections at each berth; water quality is reliable municipal supply.

Electrical power (220V, 16A) is provided at designated berths via pedestal outlets spaced along the finger piers. Generator mooring (for vessels requiring extended power) is available upon request. Ice production is available from the harbour authority's central facilities; rubbish disposal uses designated separated-waste bins positioned for easy access from berths.

Reception facilities (capitainerie) provide customer services: mooring administration, facility reservations, parking passes, and maritime information. English-speaking staff are typically available during peak hours. WiFi is available throughout the marina, a practical amenity for crews managing communications or work commitments during charter breaks.

Logistics, Transport, and Airport Access

Hyères benefits from exceptional transport connectivity. The city is served by Toulon–Hyères Airport (Aéroport Toulon–Hyères Provence), positioned just 4 kilometres north of the marina. This proximity creates a remarkable advantage: crews can arrive by morning flight, transfer to the marina (10-minute taxi or bus journey), and embark on an afternoon departure—seamless logistics that larger, more isolated marinas cannot match. The airport serves domestic flights from Paris, Lyon, and Marseille, as well as European connections; modern facilities include car rental, taxi services, and luggage handling.

Road access is straightforward. Hyères town centre is 2 kilometres north (10-minute walk or short drive); the motorway (A570 connecting to the A50 Toulon–Marseille highway) is 8 kilometres north, providing easy access for drivers and rental-car logistics. Public transport includes buses connecting the marina to town, airport, and surrounding regional destinations. The railway station (Hyères SNCF) is 3 kilometres away, with regional services connecting to Toulon, Marseille, and beyond.

Provisioning, Restaurants, and City Access

Hyères town is a 2-kilometre walk (or short bus ride) from Port de Hyères. The town centre features a daily market (mornings, year-round) supplying fresh produce, fish, cheese, and Provençal specialities. Supermarkets (Carrefour, Monoprix) offer standard provisioning; smaller épiceries stock wine, local products, and specialty items. Restaurants range from simple fishermen's tables to contemporary Mediterranean establishments; most cluster around the waterfront and in the medieval old town, easily accessible from the marina.

The market is exceptional: Mediterranean fish (sea bass, sea bream, grouper), local cheese, wild mushrooms (seasonal), and fresh herbs (thyme, rosemary, wild fennel). Wine from Côtes de Provence vineyards is abundant and affordable; local varieties include rosé (the region's specialty) and reds from the Var hinterland. A morning provisioning visit yields fresh ingredients for the day's sailing and evening cooking.

Catamaran-Specific Facilities and Services

Port de Hyères maintains dedicated facilities supporting catamaran operations. The slip (cale) accommodates both monohulls and catamarans for haul-out and repair; modern machinery handles yachts up to 20 tonnes. In-water maintenance (hull cleaning, inspection) is available from local dive operators. Engine servicing is provided by certified technicians available for emergency repairs or routine maintenance. Canvas and rigging repairs are available from specialists in Toulon (8 kilometres) or local providers.

Catamaran-specific knowledge is abundant: the local charter community and Boat4You crews provide peer support and practical advice on catamaran-specific handling, anchor techniques, and regional sailing knowledge. The marina's culture is collaborative; experienced sailors willingly assist first-time catamarans with docking, departing, or troubleshooting.

Sailing Routes from Hyères Marina

From Port de Hyères, the Îles d'Hyères national park islands (Porquerolles, Port-Cros, Île du Levant) lie 14–20 nautical miles distant—typically a 2–3 hour passage depending on wind and chosen route. A week-long catamaran charter from Hyères Marina might allocate four days to the islands, with two days exploring the Presqu'île de Giens (a sandy peninsula with lagoons) and its associated anchorages, and one passage day extending toward Saint-Tropez or returning to explore the mainland coast northwestward.

A three-day charter focuses on Porquerolles and Port-Cros, allowing overnight anchorages, snorkelling, and island exploration. A ten-day extension reaches Saint-Tropez and the central Côte d'Azur. Two-week charters can extend toward Cannes, incorporating the Îles de Lérins (Cannes islands) and exploring the full Côte d'Azur from the Hyères base.

Seasons and Sailing Conditions

Hyères Marina operates year-round. The sailing season runs April–October, with May–September offering optimal conditions. Summer (July–August) brings warm, stable weather, reliable Mistral winds (northwestern), and peak crowds. June and September offer warmth, fewer crowds, and more varied wind patterns. May and October deliver warm water and pleasant air, with occasional surprises from unsettled weather. Spring (April) brings warming conditions and spring wildflowers; winter (November–March) is possible but sees shorter daylight and occasional Atlantic-born storms.

Frequently Asked Questions About Hyères Marina Catamaran Charters

How far is Port de Hyères from the airport?

Toulon–Hyères Airport is 4 kilometres from the marina—approximately 10 minutes by taxi or public transport. This proximity allows same-day arrival and departure logistics unavailable at more isolated marinas.

What are berthing fees and typical costs?

Municipal berthing fees at Port de Hyères typically range €40–€70 per night for a 40-foot catamaran, including mooring, water, and electricity. High season (July–August) commands premium rates. includes mooring in most charter packages; confirm specific costs when booking.

Can I provision and fuel the catamaran easily?

Yes. Fuel and water are dispensed dockside; ice and provisions are available from the town centre (2 kilometres). The daily market offers fresh local products; supermarkets provide standard provisioning.

Is the marina suitable for first-time catamaran sailors?

Port de Hyères is well-suited to intermediate sailors. The facility is professional and well-managed; the surrounding sailing waters (Îles d'Hyères) are sheltered and forgiving. First-time sailors should consider a professional skipper; arranges skippers upon request.

How does Port de Hyères differ from other Côte d'Azur marinas?

Port de Hyères is a working municipal marina, prioritising function and reasonable pricing over luxury resort amenities. The result is authentic character, collaborative crew culture, and proximity to authentic Mediterranean city life—distinct from exclusive resort marinas like Port Grimaud or Sainte-Marguerite.
