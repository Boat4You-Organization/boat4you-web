What is a Gulet? Traditional Wooden Sailing Heritage

Gulets are elegant wooden sailing vessels, typically 60–120 feet long, with graceful lines and spacious decks. Originally built as working fishing boats, modern gulets combine traditional construction with contemporary comfort—air-conditioned cabins, hot showers, and full galleys beneath authentic wood and canvas.

Unlike fiberglass catamarans and performance yachts, gulets move slowly. Cruising speeds are 8–12 knots, encouraging immersion in seascapes, village stops, and meandering conversations. Boat4You gulets from Stobreč prioritize experience over speed—the journey is the destination.

Slow Sailing: The Gulet Philosophy

Gulet charters prioritize immersion over itinerary completion. Days begin with coffee on deck, mornings exploring small harbors, lunches served at anchor in hidden coves. Afternoons move slowly—perhaps a gentle sail toward the next village, or remaining anchored for swimming and relaxation.

Evenings mean cooking fresh catch purchased at village markets, dining under stars on the gulet's deck, and conversations that drift into night. This rhythm transforms sailing from logistical exercise into lived experience.

Dalmatian Island Life & Authentic Villages

Gulet sailing reveals villages untouched by package tourism. Food reflects fishing heritage. Fresh grilled fish, octopus salads, and slow-cooked pašticada define evening menus. Sailing Conditions & Best Seasons

Gulets excel in moderate wind and calm sea conditions. The Adriatic's northwesterly maestral (summer afternoons, 10–15 knots) propels gulets comfortably; stronger winds or rough seas require sheltered harbors and day-passage adjustments.

Spring (May–June) offers perfect gulet conditions: consistent thermals, warm days, and manageable crowds. Summer (July–August) brings more guests but also more reliable wind. Autumn (September–October) delivers warm water (22–23°C), gentle sailing, and September's intimate atmosphere. Winter is possible but sporadic; most gulet charters operate May through October.
