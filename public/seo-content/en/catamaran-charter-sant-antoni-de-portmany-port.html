Port Facilities and Infrastructure

Sant Antoni's working port has undergone significant modernization, creating dedicated spaces for catamaran operations. The port offers deep-water berths accommodating vessels up to 50+ feet, professional shore power systems (220V and 380V), freshwater connections at each mooring position, and waste management facilities meeting EU maritime standards. The fuel complex provides marine diesel and petrol, with professional fuel attendants ensuring safe, efficient refueling operations.

Modern reception facilities include 24-hour operations, customs clearance support, and multilingual staff familiar with international charter requirements. The harbor's protected anchorage provides excellent holding in sandy bottom, with modern mooring buoy systems and professional assistance teams experienced with catamaran operations.

Port Access and Transportation

Sant Antoni port is located 120 kilometers from Ibiza airport, reached via scenic 90-minute coastal road or 2-hour ferry from Ibiza town. The town's central position on the island makes it accessible from all regions. Accommodation options range from beachfront luxury resorts to family-run hostels, ensuring charter guests find comfortable pre-departure lodging.

The port's town-center location means you can spend pre-charter evenings exploring local restaurants, visiting the famous Café del Mar, or sampling Ibiza's cultural attractions. Unlike remote marinas accessible only by car, Sant Antoni offers walkable access to urban amenities alongside maritime facilities.

Provisioning and Port Services

Sant Antoni's port provisioning begins with comprehensive shopping services. Pre-charter ordering systems allow selecting groceries, wines, and specialized provisions delivered directly to your catamaran before departure. The daily market (open 7-14h) offers exceptional fresh produce, fish, and local specialties at reasonable prices.

Butchers, bakers, fishmongers, and specialty shops line streets adjacent to the port. Sofrit pagès ingredients—pork, potatoes, tomatoes—are available fresh daily. Arròs a banda fixings, including fresh fish for the stock base, can be sourced morning-of. Wine selection includes Spanish reds from nearby regions, Ibizan whites, and imported Mediterranean wines.

Marine provisioning services handle more specialized requests: ice supplies, customs clearance documentation, maritime charts for extended passages, and fishing tackle for open-water enthusiasts. Many provisions services arrange barbecue supplies, beach equipment, and entertainment options enhancing longer charters.

Mooring Procedures and Daily Operations

Check-in at Sant Antoni typically occurs 10 AM-2 PM on designated charter start dates. Professional staff conduct comprehensive vessel briefings covering safety systems, mechanical operations, navigation equipment, and local sailing protocols. This typically occupies 1-2 hours, concluding with provisioning and departure readiness testing.

Daily mooring management during charters is straightforward—most catamarans anchor in protected coves accessible from Sant Antoni (typically 20-40 minutes sailing). The harbor itself accommodates return visits for fuel, water, or urgent supplies, though most charters minimize port returns, maximizing time in remote anchorages.

Wind Patterns and Sailing Performance from Port

The embat pattern—Ibiza's famous afternoon sea breeze—develops predictably from the harbor opening perspective. Northwesterly patterns in spring and autumn create reliable 12-18 knot conditions ideal for catamaran sailing. Summer occasionally brings lighter conditions (8-12 knots), while winter Tramontana creates dynamic testing for experienced sailors.

Professional meteorological support available at the port provides detailed forecasting, wind trend analysis, and routing recommendations for extended passages. Captains and charter guests benefit from real-time weather briefings, ensuring informed decision-making about daily sailing plans.

Sailing Grounds Accessible from Sant Antoni Port

Immediate destinations include Cala Salada (5 nm, 30 minutes), Cala Bassa (6 nm, 35 minutes), and Cala Comte (8 nm, 45 minutes). Multi-day charters reach Formentera (20 nm), Mallorca's northeastern coast (60 nm), or Spanish mainland marinas. The port's position enables both quick day excursions and extended week-long Mediterranean explorations.

Es Vedrà, the mystical 408-meter rock formation, lies directly offshore and provides dramatic focal points for afternoon sailing. The formation's positioning creates sunset views unavailable from land, a unique advantage of water-based charters departing directly from Sant Antoni port.
