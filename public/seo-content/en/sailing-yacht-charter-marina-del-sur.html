Marina del Sur — The Canary Islands' Premier Sailing Harbor

Marina del Sur, nestled in Las Galletas' protected harbor on Tenerife's southern coast, represents a modern sailing-centric charter base combining excellent facilities, authentic Canarian atmosphere, and direct access to the Canary Islands' premier sailing destinations. Boat4You maintains a dedicated sailing yacht fleet at Marina del Sur, specifically selected for offshore performance, reliability, and comfortable offshore accommodation. The marina's strategic location—approximately 60 kilometers south of Tenerife North Airport and 30 kilometers southwest of Tenerife South Airport—provides convenient access without the traffic congestion dominating larger resort areas.

The Canary Islands' consistent trade winds (15-25 knots northeast), year-round warm weather, and dramatic volcanic geography combine with Marina del Sur's operational excellence to create an unparalleled sailing environment. Whether contemplating leisurely inter-island hops, challenging offshore passages, or extended Caribbean-bound Atlantic crossings, Marina del Sur provides the professional infrastructure and weather advantages enabling confident sailing ventures.

Inter-Island Passage Planning and Route Options

Marina del Sur's location offers convenient passage access toward all major Canary Islands. Westbound passages to Gran Canaria (approximately 70 nm to Las Palmas) typically require 12-16 hours under sail and auxiliary engine. Northwestbound passages toward La Gomera (approximately 60 nm to San Sebastián) span 10-14 hours. Northern coastal passages toward Tenerife's northern resorts or eastern-progression toward Lanzarote and Fuerteventura offer shorter daily sailing distances (4-8 hours) ideal for less-experienced crews.

Downwind and reaching passages—naturally favored by the northeast trade winds—reduce sailing time and crew fatigue significantly. Las Galletas-to-Gran Canaria passages naturally favor these wind angles; upwind passages toward El Hierro or La Palma require careful itinerary planning and occasional motor support. Professional Boat4You routing assistance ensures optimal passage planning matching crew capability and weather windows.

Extended itineraries encompassing two to three week charter periods permit ambitious circuits: full circumnavigation of Tenerife (70 nm), passages to multiple western islands (La Gomera, La Palma, El Hierro) providing progressively remote anchorages, or eastern-island exploration (Lanzarote, Fuerteventura) revealing desert landscapes and volcanic formations. coordinates extended logistical planning: fuel replenishment scheduling, provisioning re-stocking timing, and crew changeover arrangements supporting multi-week passages.

Anchorages and Night-Mooring Strategy

The Canary Islands' rocky, volcanic bottom composition generally provides excellent anchor holding. Sandy and muddy substrates prevalent in southern Tenerife waters (where Marina del Sur operates) permit secure anchoring with standard 35-45 pound anchors set in 6-10 meters depth. Emergency anchorages in shallower water (4-6 meters) remain possible in protected bays, though requiring careful scope management and swell awareness.

Established anchorages throughout the archipelago provide protected overnight mooring: Playa de las Américas (south Tenerife—excellent holding, beach provisioning), Arguineguin (Gran Canaria—working fishing harbor, authentic provisions), Puerto de Mogán (Gran Canaria—picturesque village, tourist amenities), Vallehermoso (La Gomera—remote western island, minimal tourism). Each location suits sailing-yacht operations perfectly with good holding ground and access to essential services.

Mediterranean mooring conventions (stern-anchor deployment with bow-dock approach) occasionally apply in crowded harbors; however, most Canary Islands anchorages utilize traditional hook-and-swing techniques. Sailing yachts' narrower beams simplify mooring procedures compared to wider catamarans. Electric windlasses standard aboard sailing yachts mechanize anchor deployment, reducing crew physical exertion during extended passages requiring frequent mooring relocations.

Provisioning Strategy and Supply-Chain Planning

Las Galletas supermarkets and local markets provide excellent provisioning access. Carrefour (large hypermarket), Mercadona, and Día (budget-conscious chains) stock international provisions, Spanish specialties, and Canarian products. Pescadería del Puerto (fish market) operates daily 07:00-13:00 offering fresh tuna, grouper, sea bream, and swordfish at reasonable prices. Local bakers provide fresh bread, pastries, and regional delicacies.

Extended cruise planning requires strategic provisioning: fresh provisions (vegetables, fruits, dairy, bread) consumed within 3-5 days; frozen proteins (fish, chicken, beef) supporting 2-3 week storage; dried goods (pasta, rice, legumes) and canned items providing emergency nutrition during extended passages.

Fuel consumption demands advance planning. Twin 25-30 horsepower auxiliary engines consume 5-8 liters hourly. Typical sailing charters emphasize wind propulsion (70-85 percent of passage time), minimizing fuel requirements. Fuel dock access at Las Galletas, Playa de las Américas, Arrecife (Lanzarote), and Puerto del Rosario (Fuerteventura) enables convenient re-fueling during inter-island transits. Budget €100-€150 weekly for standard cruising patterns.

Seasonality and Optimal Sailing Periods

November through March presents optimal sailing conditions: consistent trade winds (15-25 knots), manageable Atlantic swell (1-2 meters), and temperature ranges (22-25°C) permitting comfortable living aboard. High-season charter demand (November-March) drives elevated prices (€2,500-€4,000 weekly for 40-foot yachts) but ensures abundant fleet availability. The November ARC departure and associated pre-event excitement create vibrant maritime community atmosphere.

April-May and September-October offer excellent sailing conditions with reduced charter costs (€1,800-€2,800 weekly) and fewer competing charter crews contesting anchorage space. Lighter winds during summer (June-August) require increased motor reliance but offer calmer seas and comfortable water temperatures (21-22°C) enabling extensive swimming and snorkeling. Summer rate reductions (€1,500-€2,300 weekly) suit family charters and budget-conscious sailors.

Winter Atlantic storms occasionally disrupt November-February schedules; modern forecasting enables advance itinerary modifications minimizing disruption. Spring and summer stability make these seasons ideal for skippers first-timing charter sailing.
