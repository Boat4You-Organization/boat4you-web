Frequently Asked Questions

What's the typical cost of a motor yacht charter?

motor yachts range from €3,000–5,000/week (18–25m, entry-level) to €25,000–100,000+/week (40–50m, ultra-luxury). Costs include crew, fuel, provisioning, and mooring. Extras (helicopter transfers, private aircraft connections, jewelry insurance for onboard safe) are coordinated separately.

Can we take the yacht further afield—to Turkey, Croatia, or Egypt?

Yes. motor yachts are fully provisioned for international transit. Captain will plan multi-week itineraries spanning Greece-Turkey (Bodrum), Greece-Croatia (Dubrovnik), or extended Mediterranean legs. Italian ports, French Riviera, and North African anchorages are routine for superyachts. Confirm destination regulations and visa requirements; we handle the maritime logistics.

What if the weather turns bad?

Motor yachts are built for rough conditions and powered to seek shelter quickly. Your captain will adjust course preemptively—if storms are predicted, you'll anchor in deep harbors (Rhodes, Crete, or return north to protected marinas) well before conditions worsen. The motto: "The best condition is the one you're not in."

Are pets allowed aboard?

Most yachts accommodate pets, though crew allergies must be confirmed upfront. Dogs are easier than cats (fewer litter-box issues); exotic pets require advance notice. Pets enjoy deck time and typically bond with crew by charter's end.

Can we bring a professional photographer for events?

Absolutely. coordinates professional photographers, videographers, and drone operators (within Greek airspace regulations). They'll capture sunsets, family moments, and corporate events. Gallery uploads are arranged post-charter; images remain your exclusive property.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
