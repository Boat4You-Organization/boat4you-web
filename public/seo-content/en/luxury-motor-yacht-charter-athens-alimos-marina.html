Luxury Itineraries: Saronic Gulf & Cyclades

Crewed yachts explore waters inaccessible to larger ships. The Saronic Gulf—Aegina, Poros, Hydra, Spetses—offers established luxury anchoring, fine dining ashore, and safe harbours. Hydra's car-free appeal, Spetses' maritime heritage, and Poros' Peloponnesian proximity create a well-balanced itinerary. Extend into the Cyclades: Kythnos, Kea, and Serifos offer authentic island life, local wine regions, and pristine anchorages far from cruise crowds. The Corinthian Gulf crossing opens entirely different cruising grounds—fewer yachts, pristine anchorages, and Peloponnesian heartland exploration. Luxury Yacht Charter FAQs

What size crew does a luxury yacht typically have?

A 60–80 foot crewed yacht typically carries 4–5 crew: skipper, deckhand, chef, steward, and occasionally a dedicated dive master or spa therapist. Larger yachts (80–100+ feet) may have 6–8 crew for enhanced service and guest capacity. The crew-to-guest ratio ensures personalized attention and flawless operations.

Can I customize the itinerary during the charter?

Absolutely. Luxury charters are fully flexible. Discuss initial preferences pre-charter, but daily adjustments are welcomed. Weather, guest energy, discovered anchorages, or spontaneous interests drive route changes. The skipper and crew optimize itineraries for comfort and experience, not rigid schedules.

Are water sports and diving included in luxury charters?

Basic water sports (paddleboards, snorkelling, fishing) are typically included. Certified diving, jet skis, and premium experiences may incur surcharges. clarifies inclusions and arranges extras during booking. Many luxury charter companies waive water sports charges as standard amenities.

Premium yachts book 3–6 months ahead for peak season (July–August). Shoulder seasons (May–June, September–October) allow 6–8 weeks. Winter (November–March) permits shorter notice. Last-minute bookings (2–4 weeks) sometimes secure discounts on prime vessels. expedites bookings for any timeframe.
