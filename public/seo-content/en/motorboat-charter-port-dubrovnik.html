The Historic Old Harbor Character

Port Dubrovnik—also known as Luka Dubrovnik—existed for centuries as the city's primary maritime gateway. Medieval trading vessels departed from these waters toward Venice, Constantinople, and North Africa. While modern ferry terminals and cargo facilities relocated to larger harbors, the old port retains authentic maritime character and remains the soul of Dubrovnik's connection to the Adriatic.

The harbor's defining feature is its intimate scale. Berth space is severely limited—this is not a modern yacht base but a historic working port serving local fishermen, small ferries, and a handful of privileged charter vessels. Boat4You's three motorboats occupy this exclusive space, moored beneath the Minčeta Tower—one of Dalmatia's most photographed medieval fortifications.

Geographic Position and Logistics

Port Dubrovnik occupies the old town's southeastern shore, immediately adjacent to the Revelin Fortress and accessible through the city's main harbor gate. Unlike Gruž (northwest) or Komolac (suburban), the old harbor sits within Dubrovnik's medieval walls. The trade-off for this iconic location is limited parking and tight harbor logistics. Best Seasons for Old Harbor Departures

Spring (April–May) brings warm days (18–22°C), calm mornings ideal for motorboat exploration, and the old town alive with local activity returning after winter. Water temperatures around 17–19°C support swimming with wetsuits.

Summer (June–August) offers reliable weather and warm water (24–27°C). The old harbor becomes even more iconic photographed in summer light. However, crowds intensify dramatically—consider early June or late August for excellent conditions with fewer visitors.

Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (22–24°C), pleasant temperatures, and reduced crowds. Many consider autumn optimal—weather is stable, the old harbor's atmospheric qualities peak without summer's overwhelming tourism, and authentic maritime character dominates.
