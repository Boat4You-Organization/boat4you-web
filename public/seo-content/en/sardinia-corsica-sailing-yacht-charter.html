Maddalena Archipelago: Pristine Island Sailing

The Maddalena Archipelago represents untamed Mediterranean beauty just north of Costa Smeralda. Seven major islands—La Maddalena, Caprera, Budelli, Spargi, Santo Stefano, Santa Maria, and Razzoli—create a sailor's playground of protected anchorages and dramatic coastal scenery. The famous Spiaggia Rosa on Budelli, with its naturally pink sand, is an unforgettable destination reachable only by sailing yacht.

Sailing through the archipelago immerses you in pristine ecosystems protected as a National Park. Boat4You coordinates permitting and provides expert route guidance to ensure responsible sailing while maximizing experience. Anchor in turquoise lagoons, explore hidden grottos, and enjoy sunset anchorages surrounded by uninhabited islands. Professional crew can arrange gourmet meals onboard or guide you to secluded village restaurants accessible only by dinghy.

Bonifacio Strait: Epic Cross-Channel Sailing

The legendary Bonifacio Strait separates Sardinia from Corsica with just 12 nautical miles of challenging, exhilarating sailing. Towering limestone cliffs rise dramatically from the water, creating one of the Mediterranean's most iconic passages. Bonifacio's medieval fortified town crowns these cliffs with breathtaking architectural character. Our sailing yacht charters frequently feature Strait crossings that test sailing skills while delivering unmatched scenic rewards.

Summer conditions typically favor Maestrale winds that accelerate through the Strait, creating spirited sailing. Boat4You pairs experienced sailors with well-maintained yachts equipped for varying sea states. Once in Corsican waters, discover Porto-Vecchio's bohemian charm, Ajaccio's Napoleon heritage, and countless anchorages framed by granite peaks and pine forests. The contrast between Sardinian sophistication and Corsican wildness creates memorable multi-island narratives.

What sailing experience is required for a bareboat charter?

Bareboat charters require proof of sailing competency through recognized certifications (IYT, RYA, or ASA) and prior bareboat experience. assesses your qualifications and can recommend skippered alternatives if needed.

When is the best season for sailing in Sardinia and Corsica?

Summer (June-September) offers consistent Maestrale winds and warm weather, though crowds peak in August. Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) provide lighter winds, fewer tourists, and exceptional water clarity. Winter sailing is possible but involves shorter daylight and weather unpredictability.
