Sailing Routes and Island Destinations from Fort Burt

Virgin Gorda's The Baths—the BVI's iconic snorkelling destination—lies 15 nautical miles south. A southerly reach under normal trade winds takes 2.5–3.5 hours, delivering you to massive granite formations framing crystalline pools. Snorkelling through submerged boulder formations is unforgettable; Valley anchorage offers alternative overnight protection and additional exploring.

Cooper Island's Manchioneel Bay provides protected overnight anchorage, hiking trails, and world-class snorkelling at Carrot Rock and Cistern Rock. Norman Island's caves and the Willy T bar barge lie southwest, offering adventure and evening entertainment. These passages demonstrate the monohull's windward advantage—superior ability to approach islands from multiple directions.

Jost Van Dyke, 20 nautical miles northwest, hosts legendary Foxy's on Great Harbour and Soggy Dollar Bar on White Bay. The 2-hour passage under trade winds is exhilarating; anchoring in Great Harbour delivers social scenes and authentic Caribbean culture. Cane Garden Bay on Tortola's north shore offers beaches, restaurants, and dinghy access. Experienced sailors chart courses to remote Anegada, where barrier-reef navigation challenges reward explorers with pristine anchorages and authentic island character.

Trade Winds, Seasonal Conditions, and Racing Opportunities

The BVI lies within the Atlantic's northeast trade wind belt. From November through May, consistent 15–22-knot winds deliver ideal sailing conditions. Trade wind patterns follow predictable east-northeast directions, allowing precise passage planning and optimal sail trim. Peak season (December–February) combines strong winds with stable high-pressure weather systems.

March–May offers slightly lighter winds, calmer seas, and reduced crowds. Easter holidays (March–April) attract families; book early for spring vacation sailing. Autumn sailing (September–November) brings variable conditions but rewarding experiences and discounted rates. Summer (June–August) features lighter trade winds, warmer water, and quiet anchorages.

The BVI sailing community is vibrant. Friday night races from Road Town attract racers worldwide. The BVI Spring Regatta (late March–early April) hosts international racing teams. facilitates racing participation—racing-equipped yachts are available, and experienced, competitive crews can be arranged. Racing charters combine sporting challenges with leisure sailing—a favorite format for competitive sailors.
