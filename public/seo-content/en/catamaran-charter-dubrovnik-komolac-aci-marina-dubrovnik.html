ACI Marina Dubrovnik: Location, Facilities & Access

Situated in Komolac along the Ombla River, ACI Marina Dubrovnik occupies a protected natural setting while remaining only minutes from Dubrovnik's main attractions. The 450-berth facility includes full-service amenities: fuel dock, potable water, shore-powered berths, professional reception, and 24-hour security. Island Routes & Day-Sail Destinations

The Elafiti Islands, just 10 nautical miles northwest, include Koločep, Lopud, and Šipan—each offering sheltered anchorages, village restaurants, and pristine swimming. Most catamarans sail the Elafiti circuit in a half-day, leaving time for lunch ashore and evening exploration.

Mljet Island lies 30 nautical miles southwest. Its National Park encompasses two saltwater lakes (Veliko Jezero and Malo Jezero), pine forests, and a 12th-century monastery. Catamaran charters typically anchor in Polača Bay or Pomena Harbor, then explore the lakes via dinghy. The island's wild beauty and calm waters suit relaxed crews and photographers.

Korčula, the birthplace of Marco Polo, sits 40 nautical miles south. The island's wine region produces distinctive red wines (Crnaš, Plavac Mali); restaurants in Korčula Town and Lumbarda villages showcase local cuisine. A catamaran passage from Dubrovnik takes four hours, making it an ideal overnight destination or a two-day visit.

The Cavtat area, 20 kilometers south by road or sea, offers boutique anchorages and Austro-Hungarian architecture. Day sails to Cavtat allow swimming off small islands and dinner at waterfront restaurants—all within ninety minutes of Marina Dubrovnik.

Marina Facilities & Services

ACI Marina Dubrovnik's 450 berths accommodate vessels from 25 to 60+ meters. Water and electricity are available dockside; fuel is supplied 7 AM–7 PM daily. The modern reception building houses Wi-Fi (essential for route planning and crew communication), shower blocks, laundry facilities, and a small restaurant. Professional staff assist with route advice, weather forecasts, and local recommendations. Waste disposal, including recycling, is available. The marina's chandlery (Adriatica Supply) stocks spares, fenders, and navigational charts. Nearby supermarkets and the weekly farmers' market provide fresh provisions.

Security is professional and 24-hour. Parking is available for guest vehicles. The marina operates year-round, though most catamaran charters run April through October.

Best Seasons for Sailing

Spring (April–May) offers warm days (20–22°C), calm mornings, and manageable winds (10–14 knots). Crowds remain light; provisioning is abundant. Summer (June–August) brings reliable thermal winds, blue skies, and social anchorages—but July and August intensify heat and crowding. Water temperatures reach 24–26°C, ideal for swimming.

Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (22–24°C), gentle sailing breezes, and lighter tourist traffic. Many experienced sailors prefer autumn. Winter sailing is possible but brings shorter days and occasional rough conditions; most commercial charters close November through March.

Local Flavor & Provisioning

Dubrovnik's culinary tradition centers on fresh seafood, pašticada (slow-braised beef), and soparnik (chard-filled pastry). The Dubrovnik Fresh Market and daily vendors near the Old Town offer fish, cheese, olive oil, and seasonal vegetables. Local wines—Crnaš from Korčula and Plavac Mali from the mainland—are affordable and exceptional.

Restaurant clusters dot the waterfront and Old Town; most welcome sailors and offer group rates. Sunset walks on Lovrijenac Fortress or Stradun (the main street) provide cultural immersion. ACI Marina's own taverna serves casual lunches and dinners, popular with charter crews.

How long does it take to sail from Dubrovnik to Mljet?

Under power or light sail, the 30-nautical-mile passage takes 3–4 hours depending on wind and sea state. Most crews depart early morning to arrive by lunch, allowing afternoon exploration of the National Park.

Are there fuel docks along the Elafiti route?

Fuel is not available on the Elafiti Islands themselves; plan fuel consumption accordingly. Refueling is possible at Dubrovnik before departure or at larger marinas like Cavtat or Lopud (seasonal). ACI Marina Dubrovnik's fuel dock (daily 7 AM–7 PM) ensures you depart with a full tank.

What certifications are required to sail a bareboat catamaran from ACI Marina Dubrovnik?

Most operators require an International Certificate of Competence (ICC) or equivalent national sailing license. verifies credentials during the booking process; if you lack formal certification, we can arrange a professional skipper or recommend refresher courses.

Is the Ombla River approach suitable for large catamarans?

Yes. The Ombla estuary has been dredged and marked for charter vessels. Catamarans up to 60 meters navigate safely to the marina. The river entrance is well-buoyed; the approach takes 15–20 minutes from open water. ACI Marina staff provide detailed entry briefings.
