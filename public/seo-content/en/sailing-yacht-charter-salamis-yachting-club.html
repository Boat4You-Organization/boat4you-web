Seasonal Weather & Sailing Conditions in the Saronic

The Saronic Gulf benefits from relatively sheltered conditions compared to more exposed Aegean waters. Summer Meltemi winds blow predominantly from the north at 15–25 knots during July and August, creating ideal conditions for tactical sailing and coastal maneuvering. Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) feature lighter, more variable breezes perfect for leisurely cruising and exploration. Winter sailing is feasible but subject to stronger storms and reduced visibility. The club's experienced staff advise on seasonal patterns and recommend optimal timing based on your skill level and itinerary preferences.
