Berthing and Mooring Logistics

Marina Baotić's 400+ berths accommodate vessels from 7 to 70 meters. Your catamaran—typically 32–45 feet—moors at dedicated charter berths equipped with individual water and power connections. The marina's layout separates charter berths from permanent moorages, creating a community feel. Reception staff manage arrivals, coordinate provisioning deliveries, brief crews on local weather and routes, and provide round-the-clock support.

Catamaran-specific berth assignments prioritize the vessels' beam (width), which is wider than comparable monohulls. Marina Baotić reserves berths designed for twin-hull yachts, ensuring comfortable mooring without contact with adjacent vessels. This professionalism distinguishes Marina Baotić from smaller, less specialized facilities.

Provisioning Infrastructure and Pre-Charter Logistics

Marina Baotić functions as a logistics hub. Boat4You coordinates pre-provisioning deliveries—groceries, wine, fresh seafood, specialized ingredients—directly to your catamaran before departure. The marina's location, 3 kilometers from Trogir's markets and supermarket chains, means crews can arrange on-demand provisioning throughout their charter without traveling far from the vessel.

Trogir Access and Beyond

Trogir, 3 kilometers overland, is one of the Adriatic's most complete provisioning centers. The town's UNESCO-listed medieval old town features Renaissance palaces, cathedrals, and narrow streets steeped in history. Multiple supermarket chains, an open-air market, and a fish market supply competitive provisions. A professional chandlery chain stocks marine equipment. Most catamaran crews visit Trogir once during their charter for shopping and sightseeing.

Split Airport (Kaštela, 25 km north) is accessible by taxi or shuttle arrangements Marina Baotić can coordinate. This accessibility makes Baotić ideal for international crews flying in for their catamaran charter.

Sailing Routes and Island Access

From Marina Baotić, the Drvenik islands lie 8 nautical miles south. Morning departure reaches these pristine anchorages by late morning, leaving afternoon for swimming and beach exploration. Šolta, 6 nm west, offers sheltered harbors and provisioning stops. Brač (15 nm south) is a full-day passage with excellent landing options.

Longer passages to Hvar (28 nm) and the Pakleni islands (35 nm) are comfortable day sails for experienced crews or multi-day expeditions for leisurely exploration. The channel between Adriatic islands provides funneled wind patterns, often delivering reliable breezes when calms prevail elsewhere.

Seasonal Conditions and Best Times to Charter

Spring (April–May) offers ideal conditions: consistent mistral wind (15–20 knots), water temperatures 17–19°C, and manageable crowds. Summer (June–August) brings warmth and social anchorages but peaks crowding mid-July to mid-August; experienced crews often prefer shoulder seasons. Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (21–23°C) with reliable wind and minimal crowds—many consider it the season's apex for catamaran chartering.

Marina Baotić operates year-round, though peak season runs April through October. Winter sailing is possible but brings sporadic conditions and reduced activity levels.

Catamaran-Specific Advantages at Marina Baotić

Catamarans demand wider berths than monohulls—Marina Baotić understands this and provides appropriately-sized moorings. The facility's beam-specific layouts prevent the uncomfortable side-by-side contact that can damage catamarans at poorly-designed marinas. Additionally, the professional crew can assist with specific catamaran handling: launching tender davits, managing dual-engine operations, and coordinating electrical systems for air-conditioning and onboard comfort.
