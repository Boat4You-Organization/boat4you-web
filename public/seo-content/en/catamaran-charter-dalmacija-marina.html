Sailing Routes: Kornati, Telašćica & the Zadar Archipelago

The Kornati National Park lies just 20 nautical miles south—a 3-hour passage under sail. This park comprises 140 islands and islets in a UNESCO-protected seascape. Catamarans anchor in sheltered bays like Sipan, Katić, or Žut, where underwater visibility exceeds 20 meters and local fish restaurants serve ink pasta and spiny lobster at waterside tables.

Telašćica Bay, 25 nautical miles southwest, rewards catamaran crews with a natural saltwater lake (Mir) accessible via a narrow channel. The bay's steep limestone cliffs and protected waters suit calm-water sailing and snorkeling. Most charters dedicate two days to Telašćica's exploration.

The closer Zadar archipelago—Ugljan, Pašman, and Dugi Otok—lie 10–15 nautical miles away. These larger islands offer robust provisioning (bakeries, fish markets, wine shops), safe harbors, and village culture untouched by mass tourism. Catamarans anchor between the islands, exploring small fishing villages and swimming in turquoise shallows.

Best Seasons & Wind Patterns

Spring (April–May) offers warm days (18–22°C), calm mornings ideal for coastal cruising, and water temperatures around 17°C. Summer (June–August) brings reliable afternoon Maestral winds and water temps of 21–24°C—perfect for active sailing, though mid-July to mid-August crowds increase mooring competition.

Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (20–22°C), moderate winds, and fewer boats. Winter sailing is possible but requires experienced crews; the season closes commercially in November. recommends May, September, and October as prime charter months in the Zadar region.

Is Kornati National Park accessible year-round?

Kornati remains accessible April through October. Winter weather closes anchorages and increases risk. recommends Kornati trips between May and September for optimal conditions.
