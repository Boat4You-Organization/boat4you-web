Day-Trip Routes from Park Žudika

The Šolta route departs Trogir at 9:30 AM, reaching Maslinica bay on Šolta island (10 nautical miles, 25-minute motorboat journey) by 10:15 AM. The small basin offers protected anchorage, clear water, and exceptional snorkeling. By 10:30 AM you're in the water; lunch at a waterfront taverna follows by 1 PM. An afternoon exploring the village, browsing local crafts, and swimming concludes by 3:30 PM departure, returning to Park Žudika by 4:45 PM. This relaxed pace suits couples and small groups prioritizing cultural stops and leisurely exploration over maximizing island coverage.

The Brač route departs early (8:30 AM), reaching Bol on Brač island (15 nautical miles, 30-minute journey) by 9:30 AM. The famous Zlatni Rat beach is immediately accessible. Morning snorkeling or windsurfing spectating, lunch on the waterfront, and afternoon beach time or village exploration fill the day. Departure by 4 PM returns you to Park Žudika by 5:15 PM. This itinerary suits groups seeking a single, in-depth island stop rather than rapid island-hopping.

The Drvenik Veliki route (less frequent, requiring early starts) targets the remote Drvenik islands 20 nautical miles southwest. The motorboat journey takes 40 minutes, arriving by 10 AM at isolated anchorages featuring pristine beaches and minimal tourism. Limited facilities mean provisioning before departure is essential. This route suits adventurous groups and couples seeking solitude and dramatic seascapes.

Snorkeling and Water Activities

The shallow, clear waters surrounding Trogir are exceptional for snorkeling. From Park Žudika's motorboats, you access reefs, shallow wrecks, and underwater caves throughout the region. The Pakleni islands (between Trogir and Hvar) feature submerged rock formations and schools of fish; Šolta's eastern coast hides underwater caverns; Brač's southern coves offer dramatic underwater landscapes.

Most Park Žudika motorboats carry basic snorkeling gear (masks, fins); you're welcome to bring your own. Water visibility typically ranges 10–20 metres on clear days, making snorkeling genuinely rewarding. Best Seasons for Park Žudika Charters

May through October offers reliable conditions. Late May and early June provide warm days (24–26°C), calm mornings, and manageable crowds—ideal for first-time charterers or those seeking tranquility. July and August bring heat and thermal winds predictable by afternoon, plus busy anchorages. September and October combine warm water (22–24°C), gentler conditions, and diminished crowds.

April and November are marginal: shorter daylight hours and occasional Bura squalls challenge less experienced crews. Winter operations are minimal; most charters operate May–October.
