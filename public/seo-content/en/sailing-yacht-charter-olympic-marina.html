The Aegean Wind, Weather, and Sailing Conditions

Summer Etesian winds (northerly, typically Force 3–4) dominate June–September, creating reliable, predictable conditions skilled sailors cherish. Spring and autumn bring lighter, more variable winds—perfect for practicing sail trim and navigation. Water temperatures range from 16 degrees Celsius in April to a refreshing 24–26 degrees Celsius in August.

Gale-force winds are rare in summer but possible in spring and autumn. Winter cruising is reserved for experienced blue-water crews; the Aegean becomes unpredictable and challenging.

Best Seasons for Monohull Sailing from Olympic Marina

May and September offer the optimal balance: warm weather, stable conditions, and fewer crowds. June–August brings consistent winds and warm water, but also peak-season anchorage congestion. What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage.
