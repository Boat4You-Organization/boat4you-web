Why a Sailing Yacht from Marina Paleros

Sailing yachts reward skilled helmsmanship. Typical sailing yacht charters from Marina Paleros run 35 to 50 feet, two to four cabins, priced at roughly 1,600 to 4,800 euro per week depending on size, age and season. The berthing configuration at Marina Paleros handles sailing yachts without issue — allow enough turning room in the basin on busy Saturday turnarounds.

Technical considerations

Sailing yachts at Marina Paleros handle the region's conditions well when properly prepared. Check reefing points, engine hours, and safety gear during the check-in briefing — marinas in Greece run careful handover procedures and the briefing is the right moment to raise questions.

Anchoring and berthing

Anchor tackle on charter sailing yachts is generally sized adequately for Greece's typical bay depths (6–15 metres). Med-mooring stern-to is standard in marinas; the briefing at Marina Paleros covers the local conventions.

Provisioning and logistics

Marina Paleros sits within reasonable range of supermarkets and fresh markets. Plan a provisioning run for the first evening rather than day zero — the fleet turnaround day is not the moment to load the galley.

Sample 7-day itinerary from Marina Paleros

Day 1 — Departure and first bay

Embark after noon once the cabin preparation is complete, stock provisions, and motor to a nearby sheltered bay for the first night. Swim, rig checks, and a quiet dinner aboard set the tone.

Day 2 to Day 3 — Short hops along the coast

Cover 15–25 nautical miles per day between protected harbours and anchorages. Lunch stops at lunch-only anchorages, evenings in towns with konoba dining and a morning bakery run.

Day 4 — Longer crossing

Pick a day with favourable forecast for the longest passage of the week. Target 25–40 nautical miles of sailing or motoring depending on wind.

Day 5 to 6 — Return legs

Work back toward base with cultural stops — old town walk, a local winery, and an anchorage-only overnight if weather allows.

Day 7 — Return to base

Early morning passage back, fuel top-up, a quick galley clean, and formal disembarkation by late morning.

Local food and culture

expect fresh fish grilled on wood, Greek salad with barrel-aged feta, loukoumades (honey doughnuts), and Assyrtiko or Agiorgitiko wines.
