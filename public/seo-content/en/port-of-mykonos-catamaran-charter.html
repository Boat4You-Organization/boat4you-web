Why a Catamaran from Port of Mykonos is the Smart Choice

Choosing a catamaran for your Port of Mykonos charter inherently addresses many of the common challenges and desires associated with Cycladic sailing. The often-lively Meltemi wind, which typically blows from the north during summer, creates fantastic sailing conditions but can be less comfortable on a heeling monohull. Catamarans, with their dual hulls, offer remarkable stability, allowing passengers to move about freely, dine comfortably, and sleep peacefully even during passages. This stability is particularly valued by families or those prone to seasickness.

Furthermore, the shallow draft of most cruising catamarans becomes a significant advantage in the Greek islands. Countless pristine bays, many with soft sandy bottoms and crystal-clear turquoise waters, are simply too shallow for deeper keeled yachts. Your catamaran can nose closer to these idyllic beaches, providing easier swimming access and the unique opportunity to anchor in truly secluded spots away from the crowds. The expansive deck space, often featuring multiple lounging areas, a tramp net, and a spacious cockpit, transforms the vessel into a floating villa, perfect for entertaining, sunbathing, and enjoying panoramic views of the Aegean archipelago.

Navigating the Port of Mykonos: Old Port vs. Tourlos Marina

When you opt for a catamaran charter, your departure point in Mykonos will typically be one of two main locations, each offering a distinct experience. The Mykonos Old Port, nestled directly beneath the iconic windmills and adjacent to the charming district of Little Venice, pulses with authentic island energy. While incredibly picturesque and central to the town's vibrant dining and shopping scene, it can be a bustling and challenging spot for larger catamarans, especially during peak season. Space is often at a premium, and maneuvering requires skill and patience, often with rafted berthing.

A more practical and generally preferred option for catamaran charters is the modern Tourlos Marina, located approximately 2 kilometers north of Mykonos Town. This well-equipped base offers purpose-built facilities, including fixed berths with shore power and water, fuel docks, and generally more straightforward access for embarking on your Greek catamaran adventure. It's a quieter base, providing a more relaxed start and end to your charter, and transfers from Mykonos Airport (JMK) are typically swift. Many skippers prefer Tourlos for its ease of operation, allowing for smoother embarkation and disembarkation, particularly for larger groups or when extensive provisioning is required.

Weather and Sailing Conditions in the Cyclades

The Cycladic islands, including Mykonos, are synonymous with the Meltemi, a strong, dry northerly wind that typically dominates from July through August, sometimes extending into September. While it can bring exhilarating sailing, with sustained winds often ranging from 15 to 30 knots, it also ensures clear skies and comfortable temperatures, making for invigorating passages. Catamarans, by design, manage these conditions beautifully, offering a stable ride and making beam reaches between islands both fast and comfortable.

During the shoulder seasons of May, June, and September, the Meltemi is generally less pronounced, offering gentler breezes and calmer seas, ideal for those who prefer more leisurely cruising. October still provides pleasant sailing, though the weather becomes less predictable. Water temperatures remain inviting from late May through October, peaking in August. While the Meltemi is a defining feature, most days offer dependable breezes, and experienced skippers know how to leverage its power for efficient sailing while finding sheltered anchorages for tranquil evenings. Sudden squalls are rare, but it's always wise to monitor forecasts.

Provisioning and Dining for Your Mykonos Catamaran Charter

Starting your Port of Mykonos catamaran charter means you have access to some of the best provisioning options in the Cyclades. Large supermarkets both in Mykonos Town and near Tourlos Marina offer an extensive range of fresh produce, quality meats, international brands, and a wide selection of local Greek wines and spirits. It’s wise to provision thoroughly here, as prices on smaller, more remote islands can be higher and choices more limited. Don't forget to stock up on local delicacies like Mykonian Kopanisti cheese or aromatic thyme honey.

Dining on Mykonos ranges from world-class gourmet experiences to charming traditional tavernas. In Mykonos Town, especially around Little Venice, you’ll find upscale restaurants with breathtaking sunset views, offering exquisitely prepared seafood and modern Greek cuisine. For a more authentic taste, seek out family-run eateries away from the main tourist hubs, where you can savor local specialties such as Louza (cured pork) or amygdalota (almond sweets). Enjoying an ouzo with fresh seafood by the water is a quintessential Greek experience that you can easily combine with your sailing adventure.

Planning Your Charter: Bareboat vs. Skippered Options

When considering your Port of Mykonos catamaran charter, one of the first decisions you'll face is whether to opt for a bareboat or skippered yacht. A bareboat charter grants you full command of the vessel, offering the ultimate freedom to plot your course and explore at your own pace. This option is perfect for experienced sailors holding internationally recognized certifications, such as an RYA Yachtmaster or an ICC, along with a competent co-skipper. You manage all aspects of navigation, anchoring, and daily operations, truly immersing yourself in the sailing lifestyle.

Alternatively, a skippered catamaran provides the luxury of professional guidance, making it an excellent choice for those new to sailing, seeking a truly relaxed vacation, or simply wanting to benefit from local expertise. Your professional skipper will handle all the sailing, navigation, and mooring, freeing you to relax and enjoy the scenery. They possess invaluable local knowledge, guiding you to the best anchorages, the most authentic tavernas, and hidden gems that might otherwise be missed. This option is highly recommended if you’re less experienced or simply want to maximize your relaxation and exploration time. Beyond the Beaches: Cultural Exploration from Your Catamaran

While the beaches of Mykonos are undeniably alluring, a catamaran charter provides unparalleled access to the rich cultural tapestry woven throughout the Cyclades. The nearby island of Delos, considered one of Greece's most significant archaeological sites, is a must-visit. As the mythical birthplace of Apollo and Artemis, its ruins tell tales of ancient civilizations and religious significance, offering a profound historical experience just a short sail away. Visiting by private yacht allows for a more personal and less hurried exploration than public ferries.

Further afield, islands like Tinos, often overlooked by mainstream tourism, boast hundreds of beautifully sculpted dovecotes, charming traditional villages, and the revered Panagia Evangelistria church, a significant pilgrimage site. Syros, the capital of the Cyclades, presents a striking contrast with its neoclassical architecture and Venetian influences in Ermoupoli. Even the smaller, less populated islands like Kimolos offer glimpses into an authentic Cycladic way of life, with their whitewashed houses, friendly locals, and unspoiled natural beauty. Your catamaran is a mobile platform for cultural immersion, allowing you to connect deeply with the heart of the Aegean.

FAQ

What is the best time to charter a catamaran from Mykonos?

The ideal time for a Port of Mykonos catamaran charter is typically from late May to early July, or throughout September. During these shoulder seasons, the weather is pleasantly warm, the Meltemi winds are usually milder, and the islands are less crowded than in the peak months of July and August. You'll enjoy comfortable sailing conditions and a more relaxed atmosphere in popular spots.

Do I need a sailing license to charter a catamaran in Mykonos?

For a bareboat catamaran charter in Greece, a valid sailing license or certificate recognized by Greek port authorities is required for the skipper, along with a competent co-skipper. If you do not possess the necessary qualifications, or simply prefer to relax, you can opt for a skippered charter where a professional captain handles all the navigation. We always recommend clarifying specific licensing requirements with your charter broker.

What type of catamarans are available for charter in Mykonos?

Mykonos offers a diverse fleet of catamarans for charter, ranging from comfortable cruising models ideal for families to luxurious, fully crewed yachts. You'll typically find popular brands like Lagoon, Fountaine Pajot, and Bali available, offering various cabin configurations, amenities, and lengths to suit different group sizes and budgets. Most catamarans are well-equipped with modern navigation systems, spacious galleys, and comfortable living areas.

How far in advance should I book my Port of Mykonos catamaran charter?

Given Mykonos's popularity, especially for catamaran charters, it is highly recommended to book at least 6-12 months in advance, particularly if you plan to travel during the peak season (July-August) or have specific dates and boat models in mind. Early booking secures better availability and often more competitive pricing. Shoulder season charters might offer slightly more flexibility, but popular boats still fill up quickly.
