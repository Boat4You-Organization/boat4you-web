Day Trips & Short Excursions

Pašman island, directly across the Pašman Channel, offers the perfect half-day motorboat escape. A 15-minute run brings you to Pašman Town's harbour, a medieval village with a working fishing tradition, quiet restaurants, and mooring balls for charter vessels. Explore the waterfront, lunch on grilled fish, and return to Drage by evening. Alternatively, the island's lee side hosts several uncrowded anchorages perfect for swimming and snorkelling.

Ugljan, the longer island to the northwest, stretches 23 kilometres and combines quietness with gentle tourist services. The village of Preko offers more dining options and a livelier atmosphere than Pašman; a half-day motorboat excursion from Drage reaches Preko in 45 minutes. Spend the afternoon swimming at nearby beaches, exploring the village, and enjoy dinner ashore before the evening return journey.

Pakoštane, just 10 kilometres north along the coast, hosts a larger marina and wider provisioning options. For those wanting to stretch legs beyond Drage without major commitment, a motorboat trip up the coast offers beachfront restaurants, souvenir shops, and a busier (though still manageable) town atmosphere.

Extended Motorboat Charters: Weekly Itineraries

A week-long motorboat charter from Drage opens the entire northern Dalmatian coast. Days 1–2 explore Pašman and Ugljan thoroughly, anchoring in multiple bays and visiting fishing villages. Days 3–4 push south toward the Kornati National Park, arriving by midday and spending 24–48 hours island-hopping through the 140-island archipelago—dramatic limestone formations, anchorages of crystalline clarity, and remote coves untouched by road-based tourism.

Day 5 typically returns northward via Telašćica or Sakarun Bay, both spectacular natural harbours worth overnight mooring. Days 6–7 explore quieter northern options (Nin, the Zadar coast, or repeated favourite anchorages) before returning to Drage for departure.

Larger motorboats (35–42 feet) cruise economically at 12–15 knots, burning 10–15 litres per hour and offering capacity for groups of 4–6 and day-sailing comfort. Speedier vessels reach remote destinations quickly, trading fuel efficiency for time savings—ideal if your party values adventure variety over leisurely pacing.

Drage Marina & Local Services

Drage's marina is authentically local: roughly 50–80 berths, family-run, and deeply rooted in the fishing community. Fuel dock and water are available; the atmosphere is welcoming and informal. Unlike corporate mega-marinas, Drage's marina staff know local captains, restaurant owners, and provisioning suppliers personally—advantages for charter support and insider recommendations.

Facilities are functional rather than luxurious: basic laundry, showers, and parking. A bakery opens early, and a modest market stocks essentials. Restaurants—all within walking distance of the marina—serve fresh fish daily and welcome motorboat charterers warmly. This simplicity is Drage's charm; you're not paying for gloss, but gaining genuine hospitality and local connection.

Best Seasons for Motorboat Charters

Spring (April–May) offers calm conditions and fewer crowds—ideal for families and novice motorboat operators. Water temperatures reach 17–19°C; light winds mean reliable cruising. Summer (June–August) brings warm water (22–25°C), more boats, and busier anchorages, though Drage remains quieter than Split-based charters.

Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (22–23°C) with manageable crowds. Winter sailing is possible but demands respect for occasional storms and shorter days. Most motorboat charters operate April through October; winter availability is limited.

Food, Provisioning & Local Culture

Drage's waterfront restaurants serve daily catches—gregada (traditional fish stew), grilled sea bass, octopus salads, and local pasta. Owners fish themselves or source from neighbours; menus reflect the morning's catch, not printed standards. Wine selections favour Croatian reds and whites from nearby regions.

Provisioning at Drage's market provides basics: bread, cheese, eggs, and fresh produce. For wider selection, Pakoštane (10 km) and Biograd (15 km) offer supermarkets. Many motorboat charterers cook dinner aboard using market ingredients, creating meals that blend prepared and fresh elements—economical, flexible, and often superior to restaurant dining every night.

FAQ: Motorboat Charters from Drage

Do I need a boating licence for a motorboat from Drage?

Day-trip motorboats under 7 metres typically don't require a licence. Larger vessels may require a national boating certificate or International Certificate of Competence (ICC). What's the fuel consumption for Drage motorboat charters?

A 35-foot motorboat cruising at economical speed (12–15 knots) burns 10–15 litres per hour. Speedier cruising (18–20 knots) increases consumption to 18–25 litres per hour. Budget €600–€900 for a week of mixed-speed cruising; high-speed charters may run €1,000–€1,400 weekly.

Is Drage accessible from Split or Zadar airports?

Yes. Drage lies 35–45 kilometres from Zadar Airport (45 minutes by car) and 70 kilometres from Split Airport (two hours by car). Rental cars and shuttle services are available. supports transfer arrangements; discuss preferences during booking.

Can I do a mix of day-trips and anchoring during a weekly charter?

Absolutely. Many motorboat charterers spend some days at quiet anchorages (swimming, snorkelling, relaxing) and other days exploring new destinations. A motorboat's flexibility accommodates varied itineraries—unlike sailing yachts dependent on consistent wind.

What water-sports equipment is available?

Snorkels, masks, and flippers are typically included. Towable inflatables, jet-skis, and diving equipment may be available for rent at Drage marina or nearby Pakoštane. arranges rental coordination during booking.
