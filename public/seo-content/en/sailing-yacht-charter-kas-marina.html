Kaş Marina: Facilities and Strategic Advantage

Kaş Marina, operated under Turkish port authority standards, provides essential services including fuel, fresh water, shore power (220V and 380V), and dedicated berths for sailing yachts ranging from 30 to 60 feet. The marina features modern reception facilities, security surveillance, rubbish disposal, and repair services including rigging, electrical systems, and engine maintenance. Notably, Kaş Marina's intimate scale—accommodating roughly 150 vessels—contrasts sharply with crowded Adriatic marinas; guest-focused management prioritises personal attention and bespoke services. The harbourfont café and provisioning market lie within 50 metres of berthing; restaurants, fresh-fish suppliers, and wine merchants cluster along the waterfront promenade.

Kaş Marina's singular geographic advantage lies in its position as the southernmost Turkish mooring facility with reliable modern infrastructure. The harbour sits 95 kilometres south-east of Antalya (roughly two hours by car), 85 kilometres south-west of Mersin, and directly across from Greek territory—placing it uniquely positioned for exploring the Lycian Coast's most dramatic and historically significant sites. No other marina provides equivalent proximity to Kekova's sunken city, accessible via a straightforward 15–20 nautical mile sail (three to four hours).

The Kekova Sunken City: Sailing to Underwater History

Kekova represents the Mediterranean's most accessible and visually stunning underwater archaeological site. This Hellenistic city, partially submerged following seismic activity in the second century AD, presents an extraordinary vista of preserved foundations, residential dwellings, ceremonial quays, and temple structures visible beneath 2–8 metres of crystalline water. Sailing yachts departing Kaş Marina reach Kekova in three to four hours, arriving by late morning and typically anchoring for two to three days. The site permits both PADI-certified diving and non-certified snorkelling; visitors witness intact street grids, staircases, doorways, and carved inscriptions testifying to Kekova's prosperity as a regional trading hub.

Simena Fortress and Clifftop Exploration

Adjacent to Kekova's underwater ruins, the village of Simena rises dramatically across a steep hillside crowned by a Crusader-era fortress. Accessible only by sea or a challenging overland hiking trail, Simena preserves Lycian and Byzantine elements within its castle structure—towers, ramparts, and a small chapel overlooking three hundred metres of vertical cliff. Sailing yachts anchor in Simena's natural harbour, and tender exploration (or adventurous swimming) enables guests to explore both underwater and terrestrial archaeological features. The village's handful of restaurants and guesthouses provide authentic Turkish hospitality; the absence of road access preserves Simena's historical character remarkably well.

Cliff Diving and Water Sports from Kaş

Kaş has emerged as the Mediterranean's premier cliff-diving destination—famous worldwide among adventure enthusiasts. Natural limestone formations surrounding the harbour create vertical drops of 12–20 metres into deep, protected waters. Designated diving spots—including Aquarium Bay, Blue Grotto, and Cathedral Rock—attract experienced cliff divers and increasingly organised diving tourism. Many charter guests combine sailing itineraries with one to two cliff-diving excursions, typically arranged pre-charter through Boat4You's coordinator network. Kayaking, paddleboarding, and recreational swimming are equally accessible; Kaş's warm, calm waters (typically 25–28°C in summer) maintain comfortable swimming conditions throughout the charter season.

Bareboat Versus Crewed Sailing Yacht Charters from Kaş

Bareboat sailing yacht charters from Kaş require recognised sailing certifications (RYA Day Skipper, ASA 104, or equivalent). Qualified skippers receive pre-departure briefings on local anchoring conventions, archaeological protocols, emergency procedures, and Turkish maritime regulations. Bareboat guests enjoy complete control and typically save 35–40% compared to crewed options. However, crewed charters—wherein professional skippers and crew accompany the vessel—suit most guests exploring Kekova and surrounding archaeological sites. Crewed skippers navigate confidently in confined anchorages, manage docking and mooring, and coordinate with local dive operators and authorities governing underwater exploration. For non-sailors and families, crewed charters ensure safety, comfort, and cultural immersion without navigation responsibility.

Accommodation and Village Experience in Kaş

Kaş village comprises roughly 7,500 residents—a manageable community preserving authentic Turkish character. Whitewashed houses with blue shutters cascade down cliffsides toward the harbour; narrow pedestrian streets host family-run tavernas, spice merchants, and rug sellers. Pre-charter and post-charter accommodation options range from budget guesthouses (€30–50 per night) to upscale boutique hotels (€80–150 per night) offering rooftop terraces overlooking the Greek island of Megisti. Many Boat4You guests arrive one to two days early, exploring Kaş village, provisioning for the charter, and acclimating to local customs before departing Kaş Marina.

Provisioning and Onboard Dining

Kaş's provisioning markets exceed expectations for a village of its size. Fresh vegetable and fruit vendors open early mornings; fish merchants display daily catches (grouper, sea bream, squid, and octopus); cheese vendors offer locally aged varieties; bakeries produce fresh bread and pastries throughout the day. Crewed yachts typically provision here before departure, sourcing ingredients for multi-day voyages. Bareboat guests self-provision or engage pre-arranged provisioning services coordinating with local suppliers. Evening meals often occur ashore at waterfront tavernas—casual restaurants serving grilled fish, meze platters, Turkish salads, and regional wines at exceptional value (main courses €8–15 per person).

Weather and Sailing Conditions from Kaş

Kaş's position on the Lycian Coast ensures consistent summer winds and protection from major maritime systems. The predictable Etesian northerlies establish by mid-morning, typically 8–15 knots through mid-afternoon, declining by sunset. This wind pattern enables reliable day sailing, with morning motoring (light winds or none) followed by afternoon under-sail passages. Summer temperatures (June–August) reach 28–32°C; water temperatures climb to 27–28°C by July. Shoulder seasons (May, September) offer slightly cooler weather and reduced crowds; October remains pleasant though occasional southeasterly systems create choppier conditions. Winter months (November–March) bring cooler air, intermittent rainfall, and occasional strong southeasterlies closing anchorages; winter charters suit experienced sailors accepting weather delays.
