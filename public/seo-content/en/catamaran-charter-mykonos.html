Delos: The Sacred Island

Delos, a UNESCO World Heritage site, lies within hours of Mykonos and is accessible only by private yacht or passenger ferry. This uninhabited sanctuary—birthplace of Apollo in Greek mythology—preserves classical temples, mosaics spanning 2,500 years, and archaeological riches rivaling any Mediterranean site. Private yacht arrival avoids the ferry crowds; archaeological authorities limit daily visitors and enforce entry times. An overnight anchorage near Delos allows unhurried exploration and sunset photography. Many Mykonos-based catamarans make Delos a centerpiece of their weekly itinerary, combining cultural depth with exceptional sailing.

Mykonos Old Port and Tourlos Marina

Mykonos Old Port, the historic heart, pulses with energy: Greek tavernas, designer boutiques, windmill views, and Mediterranean nightlife. It's busy and expensive but iconic—every sailor should experience it once. The port accommodates large yachts but feels tight during peak season. Tourlos, 5 kilometers north, offers modern marina facilities: fixed berths, fuel, water, electrical hookups, and professional management. It's quieter, easier for maneuvering, and better for crews seeking calm overnights. Most Boat4You charterers base their catamaran at Tourlos and visit Old Port for dining and exploration rather than berthing.

Multi-Day Cycladic Itineraries from Mykonos

A classic week from Mykonos might read: Day 1, motor to Delos (UNESCO site, morning exploration); Day 2, sail south to Paros via Naxos (overnight in Paroikia); Day 3, day trip to Antiparos sea caves, return to Naxos anchorage; Day 4, north to Sifnos (pottery workshops, quiet fishing village); Day 5, explore Kimolos and surrounding anchorages; Days 6-7, return to Mykonos via Paros, arriving refreshed and tan. Shorter itineraries focus on the inner triangle: Mykonos-Delos-Paros, with ample time for swimming, exploring villages, and enjoying waterfront meals. Weather and Summer Sailing Conditions

Mykonos enjoys a Mediterranean climate with hot, dry summers and mild winters. The sailing season (May-October) is optimal; July-August are hottest and busiest. The Etesian wind—predictable northerly breeze typically 12-18 knots—dominates summer and drives excellent sailing conditions. Beam reaches between islands are comfortable and fast. Wind is lightest in spring (May-June) and autumn (September-October), offering different sailing rhythms. Catamarans handle the Etesian beautifully, with their broad beam providing stability and comfort. Most days offer dependable breezes; sudden squalls are rare and brief.

Provisioning and Dining in Mykonos

Mykonos offers world-class provisioning and dining. Supermarkets near both Old Port and Tourlos stock fresh vegetables, cheese, wines, and international foods. Prices are higher than smaller islands, but quality is excellent. Fish markets offer daily catches—prawns, sea urchin, octopus. Fine dining tavernas cluster in Old Port with windmill views and sunset ambiance; expect EUR 25-50+ per person. Casual beach bars and family-run tavernas offer simpler fare at EUR 12-20 per person. Many catamarans provision heavily at Mykonos at the outset, stocking quality wines and local delicacies for the week ahead.

This approach suits families, older sailors, or those combining sailing with Mykonos' cosmopolitan attractions. FAQ

Do I need sailing experience to bareboat charter a catamaran from Mykonos?

Most charter companies require proof of sailing competence (captain's license or equivalent). For less experienced crews, a professional skipper provides peace of mind and expertise. First-time charterers can take a local sailing course beforehand, earning internationally recognized certification. Many crews without formal sailing credentials successfully hire skippers; discuss your background with our team.

Are catamarans good for families with young children?

Excellent. Catamarans offer spacious cabins, stable platforms ideal for young sailors, and protected decks for safe play. The twin hulls reduce motion, minimizing seasickness. Shallow-draft designs reach protected family-friendly anchorages inaccessible to monohulls. Mykonos has calm waters nearby (Tourlos bay), perfect for swimming and snorkeling. Many families prefer catamarans over monohulls for their roominess and stability.

What's the cost of a catamaran charter from Mykonos?

Pricing varies by boat size, amenities, and season. A 38-footer costs EUR 2,500-4,000 weekly in shoulder season (May-June, September) and EUR 4,000-6,500 in peak summer. A 45-footer runs EUR 3,500-5,000 shoulder season, EUR 5,500-8,000 peak season. Skippered options add EUR 150-250 daily.

Can we include Delos in our itinerary?

Absolutely. Delos is 7 nautical miles from Mykonos—a 1-2 hour sail or motor. Most week-long itineraries include a full-day visit. Archaeological authorities limit entries; arrive early or book afternoon slots for fewer crowds. Overnight anchoring nearby allows leisurely exploration and sunset photography. Guided tours enhance the experience; arrange in advance through our partners.

Is Mykonos Old Port suitable for catamaran mooring?

Yes, but with caveats. Old Port accommodates large yachts but can feel congested during peak season (July-August). Berths are tight; maneuvering large catamarans requires skill. Most charterers prefer Tourlos for sleeping and use Old Port for day visits and dining. Tourlos marina offers modern facilities, easier access, and calmer overnight conditions.

What about seasickness on a catamaran?

Catamarans' twin hulls significantly reduce pitching and rolling compared to monohulls. Most guests find the motion comfortable and non-nauseating. The wide beam and shallow draft mean catamarans maintain stability even in moderate seas. If seasickness concerns you, book a catamaran rather than a sailing yacht; the comfort improvement is marked.

Spring and Autumn Charm

While summer fills Mykonos with international crowds, spring (May-June) and autumn (September-October) offer exceptional sailing and island experiences. Winds are lighter and more variable; temperatures are temperate. Island communities reclaim their authenticity as summer tourists depart. Prices drop 20-30%; availability is higher. Many experienced sailors prefer shoulder-season sailing for its sailing challenge and peaceful atmosphere.

Navigation and Safety from Mykonos

The waters around Mykonos and the central Cyclades enjoy excellent chart coverage and reliable navigation aids. All catamarans carry updated GPS chartplotters, paper charts, and VHF radios. Mobile phone networks provide solid coverage in harbors and islands. The straightforward geography—islands spaced clearly with good horizons—makes navigation approachable even for less experienced crews. Summer Etesian winds are remarkably consistent, allowing confident passage planning. Emergency rescue services are professional and well-coordinated; VHF reaches authorities rapidly.

Cycladic Culture and Island Experiences

Beyond sailing excellence, the Cyclades offer rich cultural experiences. Delos preserves classical civilization; Paros, marble quarries and fishing traditions; Naxos, medieval Venetian fortifications and agricultural heritage; Sifnos, traditional pottery studios where artisans work as their ancestors did. Many catamarans allocate time to explore villages—narrow winding streets, family-run tavernas, local cheeses and wines, centuries-old traditions.
