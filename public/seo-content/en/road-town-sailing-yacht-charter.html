Sailing Routes and Destinations from Road Town

A classic Road Town itinerary begins with a 15-nautical-mile southerly reach toward Virgin Gorda. Under normal northeast trade winds, this passage takes 2.5–3.5 hours. The Baths, Virgin Gorda's iconic snorkelling destination, rewards anchor arrival with pristine granite formations, crystal water, and tropical fish populations. Snorkelling extends north through Valley anchorage and south around Copper Mine Point.

From Virgin Gorda, continue southeast toward Cooper Island's Manchioneel Bay. The 8-nautical-mile passage takes 1.5 hours and includes protected anchorage, hiking trails, and premium snorkelling sites at Carrot Rock and Cistern Rock. Norman Island's caves and the Willy T bar barge lie nearby, offering adventure and evening entertainment.

Experienced sailors chart courses toward Anegada, a remote northernmost island renowned for its barrier reef, lobster fishing, and pristine anchorages. The 25-nautical-mile passages demand careful navigation and weather assessment but reward with authentic island culture and explorer's satisfaction. Conversely, Jost Van Dyke (20 nm northwest) offers Foxy's legendary beach bar and the world-famous Soggy Dollar Bar on nearby White Bay—accessible in 2 hours under sail and delivering iconic Caribbean nightlife.

Trade Winds, Seasonal Conditions, and Racing Opportunities

The BVI lies within the Atlantic's northeast trade wind belt, with winds predominantly from the east-northeast. This directional consistency allows precise passage planning and optimal sail trim. Peak sailing season (November–May) features trade winds averaging 15–22 knots—ideal for performance sailing, racing, and reliable weather patterns.

December–February is peak season: strong winds, stable high-pressure systems, and minimal tropical wave activity. March–May offers slightly lighter winds, calmer seas, and reduced crowds. Autumn sailing (September–November) brings variable conditions but rewarding experiences and lower charter costs. Summer (June–August) features lighter trades, occasional tropical showers, and exceptional serenity for those seeking quiet anchorages.

The BVI hosts competitive sailing events. The BVI Spring Regatta (late March–early April) attracts racing teams worldwide. Friday night races from Road Town provide informal competition and social gathering. facilitates participation in racing events, adding sporting challenges to your charter if desired. Many sailors view racing as the ultimate sailing education and friendships building.

Island Culture, Dining, and Provisioning
