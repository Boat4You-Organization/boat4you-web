FAQ

What experience level is required?

Bareboat charters require RYA Day Skipper or equivalent certification demonstrating navigation and seamanship competency. Beginners hire professional skippers (€150–250 daily) managing sailing operations. All-inclusive crewed charters eliminate experience requirements entirely—professional crew manages everything.

What is included in charter pricing?

Standard pricing covers vessel, crew salaries, fuel, meals, beverages, snorkeling equipment, and dinghy excursions. Premium wine, specialized activities (helicopter tours, ROV expeditions), and enhanced services incur additional fees detailed in itemized quotes provided before booking confirmation.

How flexible are itinerary changes?

Professional crews adjust itineraries based on weather, guest interests, and local conditions offering flexibility impossible on rigid cruise ship schedules. Last-minute destination changes, extended stays in beloved locations, and spontaneous cultural discoveries characterize the charter experience advantage.

What are typical weather conditions?

May–October charter season experiences minimal severe weather. Occasional autumn Sirocco winds (20–25 knots) prompt strategic anchorage repositioning but rarely prevent sailing. Professional captains prioritize guest comfort and safety maintaining protective harbor positioning when conditions warrant conservative approaches.

How far in advance should charters be booked?

Peak July–August demand requires 12–18 months advance booking due to limited premium inventory availability. June and September accommodate 8–12 week lead times. Shoulder months (May, October) permit flexible 6–8 week scheduling. Occasional last-minute cancellations surface through broker networks offering substantial discounted rates.

Motor Yacht Speed and Itinerary Flexibility

Motor yachts (40–70 ft) achieve 12–18 knots cruising speeds compared to sailing vessels' 6–8 knots. This speed advantage permits ambitious multi-island itineraries impossible via traditional sailing: 10-day Grand Tours (Palermo → Egadi → Pantelleria → Pelagie Islands → return, 250+ nm) become feasible with leisurely anchorages and extended exploration time. Early-morning departures enable afternoon arrivals, maximizing daylight exploration at each destination. Speed flexibility permits responsive itinerary modifications based on weather windows, wildlife sightings, or guest-preference adjustments—pivoting toward unexpected festivals or cultural events impossible on rigid sailing schedules.

Fuel consumption remains economical at cruising speeds (8–12 liters/hour typical); luxury motor yachts deliver cost efficiency matching sailing-yacht per-person daily expenses while providing superior comfort and itinerary flexibility. Professional captains balance fuel efficiency with itinerary optimization, positioning vessels advantageously for evening anchorages and minimizing nighttime navigation.

What is the typical crew-to-guest ratio on luxury motor yachts?

Premium luxury vessels (80+ ft) maintain 1:2 crew-to-guest ratio: 8–10 guests supported by 4–5 crew members (captain, chef, deckhand, steward, engineer). This staffing enables personalized service, dietary flexibility, rapid maintenance response, and water-sports supervision. Smaller luxury vessels (50–70 ft) typically employ captain, chef, deckhand (1:3–1:4 ratio). Higher crew density ensures responsive service, premium-quality meal preparation, seamless itinerary execution, and guest safety prioritization throughout week-long voyages.
