Motorboat Charters: Simplicity and Speed

A motorboat (also called a speedboat or day cruiser) is an open or cabin motor vessel, typically 18–32 feet, designed for day trips and short excursions. From Trogir, Boat4You's motorboat fleet accommodates 4–10 passengers and requires minimal boating experience. Motorboats offer rapid island access, casual atmosphere, and no overnight complexity. Half-day rentals cost €300–€800; full-day charters run €600–€1,500 depending on size, fuel, and season.

Trogir is perfect for motorboat day trips. Šolta (8 nm), Brač's Bol (10 nm), and Čiovo beaches (2 nm) are within 30–90 minutes. Speedier craft can reach Hvar (15 nm, 1 hour) and back within a day. Unlike bareboat charters requiring certifications, Boat4You motorboats operate under licensed captains, freeing guests to relax, swim, and explore without navigation stress. Ideal for families, groups, and couples seeking day adventures without overnight commitment.

Captain-Driven Simplicity

All motorboat charters from Trogir include a professional captain. Guests are passengers; navigation, safety, and docking are handled. This removes liability and allows full enjoyment of sea and destinations.

Flexible Scheduling

Half-day (4–5 hours) and full-day (8–10 hours) options suit various schedules. Early departures (8 am) reach Brač or Hvar by mid-morning; afternoon returns permit evening relaxation in Trogir's old town or dining on the Riva.

Motorboat Routes and Day-Trip Itineraries

Trogir's position unlocks diverse one-day motorboat excursions. Speed enables multiple stops within a single day.

Šolta Beach and Lunch Loop (Half-day, 4 hours)

Depart Trogir 9 am; motor to Šolta's Maslinica harbor (8 nm, 45 minutes). Swim, snorkel, and lunch at a waterfront konoba. Explore the village, browse local crafts. Return to Trogir by 1 pm. Relaxed, cultural, family-friendly.

Brač Zlatni Rat Beach Day (Full-day, 8 hours)

Depart 8 am; motor to Bol, Brač (10 nm, 1 hour). Spend 4–5 hours beaching, swimming, paddleboarding, and dining at Bol's restaurants. Optional: brief stop at Šolta or Drvenik en route. Return to Trogir by 5 pm. Iconic Dalmatian beach experience.

Hvar Town and Shopping (Full-day, 9 hours)

Depart 8 am; fast passage to Hvar Town (15 nm, 1 hour). Explore the marble old town, dine at harborside restaurants (Lola Bistro, Dalmatino), shop local boutiques. Optional: sunset aperitif on the Riva (harbor promenade). Return to Trogir by 6 pm. Sophisticated single-destination focus.

Island-Hopping: Šolta–Brač–Drvenik (Full-day, 10 hours)

Ambitious motorboat day: Trogir–Šolta (45 min)–Brač (1 hour)–Drvenik Veli (1.5 hours). One-hour stops at each for swimming and light provisioning. Return to Trogir by 7 pm. Maximizes island variety; suits active, experienced boaters.

Čiovo Bridge and Quiet Beaches (Half-day, 4 hours)

Ultra-local: motor across the bridge to Čiovo's eastern beaches (2 nm, 10 minutes). Snorkel, explore quieter coves, and return by early afternoon. Ideal for families with young children, minimizing travel time.

Motorboat Types and Specifications

Trogir motorboat fleets span several designs. Open speedboats (18–24 feet) lack cabins; passengers sit in the sun. Cabin motorboats (24–32 feet) offer seating, heads, and modest shelter. Performance varies: open speedboats cruise 25–40 knots (fast, thrilling); cabin cruisers cruise 15–20 knots (moderate, comfortable).

Speedboat: Performance and Thrills

Lightweight open speedboats (18–22 feet) with twin high-performance engines (2 × 150–300 hp) achieve 30–40 knots cruising speed. Acceleration is dramatic; ride is wet and wind-exposed. Ideal for thrill-seekers and active groups. Fuel consumption is high (25–35 liters per hour), but short duration limits cost.

Cabin Motorboat: Comfort and Versatility

Mid-size cabin boats (24–32 feet) with single or twin diesel engines (100–200 hp) cruise 15–20 knots. Cabins offer shelter, marine heads, and modest galleys. Fuel consumption (10–15 liters per hour) is efficient for all-day trips. Preferred for families and mixed-experience groups.

Navigation and Safety

All Boat4You motorboats include GPS, VHF radio, emergency beacons, and modern safety gear. Life jackets (all sizes) are mandatory. Captains hold Croatian boating licenses and commercial insurance; vessels pass annual EU safety inspections.

Motorboat Pricing and Rates

Trogir motorboat charters are seasonally tiered. Half-day (4–5 hours): €300–€600. Full-day (8–10 hours): €600–€1,200. High season (July–August) adds 20–30% versus shoulder (May–June, September). Winter rates drop 30–40% but offer limited availability due to weather and crew scheduling.

Rates typically include captain, basic fuel allowance, and safety equipment. Extras: lunch ashore (self-purchased), bar service, water sports equipment rental (snorkel gear €20–30, life jackets included), and gratuities (optional, 5–10% customary).

Group Discounts

Groups of 8+ may negotiate slightly reduced per-person rates. Corporate team-building charters often include themed provisioning or private chef lunch options.

Best Season and Weather for Motorboat Day Trips

May through October is viable. June and September are ideal: water 18–24°C, Maestral winds (5–12 knots providing cooling and modest waves), fewer crowds, and lower prices. July–August offer reliable sunshine and calm seas but involve tourism surges and higher rates. April and October suit experienced groups; November–March carry weather risk and limited availability.

Motorboats can handle rougher conditions than small sailboats (due to engines and professional captains), but passenger comfort declines in 2+ meter waves. Captains adjust itineraries for weather; safety overrides schedules.

Daily Provisioning

Motorboat captains often recommend bringing lunch or plan stops at island harbors where restaurants serve fresh catches and local wines. Early-morning departures mean returning for dinner on the Trogir Riva—a pleasant routine combining adventure and cultural immersion.

Water Sports and Activities Integrated

Most motorboat charters include snorkeling opportunities (gear often available for €20–30 rental if not personal). Swimming and diving off-boat are standard. Boat4You captains know secluded coves and favorable diving sites. Paddleboarding and kayaking can be added (equipment rental €20–50).

Photography and Scenic Stops

Motorboat speed permits scenic detours: slow-passes near underwater caves, photography stops at Kamerlengo Fortress backdrop, or sunset viewpoints. Captains tailor routes to guest interests.

Motorboat Charter FAQs

Do I need boating experience for a motorboat charter?

No. The captain operates the vessel; passengers enjoy the ride. Families with children 4+ are welcome. Comfort with open water is helpful; severe seasickness may impact enjoyment on rough days.

What should I bring on a motorboat day trip?

Sunscreen (crucial—reflection off water intensifies UV), sunglasses, towel, swimsuit, light jacket (cooling sea breezes), and lunch (if not dining ashore). Waterproof phone cases and cameras are useful. Avoid heavy bags; stowage is limited.

Can families with young children join motorboat charters?

Yes. Children 4+ are typically welcome. Infants and toddlers may struggle with boat motion and limited facilities. Boat4You can advise on child-friendly itineraries and vessel options. Life jackets are mandatory and available in all sizes.

Is a motorboat trip cheaper than a sailing yacht day charter?

Generally yes. Motorboat half-days (€300–€600) are more accessible than sailing yacht day charters (€600–€1,200). However, motorboats consume more fuel, so full-day costs approach sailing options. Choose based on preference: motorboat speed and simplicity vs. sailing authenticity.

What if weather turns bad during our trip?

Captains monitor forecasts and adjust routes. If conditions worsen, captains shelter in nearby harbors (free) or return early. Rescheduling or partial refunds are standard industry practice. maintains flexible cancellation policies for weather events.
