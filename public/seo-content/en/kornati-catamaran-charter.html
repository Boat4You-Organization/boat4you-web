Catamaran Charter in Kornati: Stability and Space for Island Exploration

Sailing a catamaran through Kornati's 89 limestone islands delivers the easiest, most spacious experience in Croatian waters. The dual-hull design provides unmatched stability in these shallow channels, generous deck space for sunbathing, and shallow draft—allowing access to quiet bays larger monohulls cannot reach. Whether you're a family prioritizing comfort or an experienced skipper seeking effortless navigation, a Kornati catamaran charter offers superior platform stability and living space that transforms your week into pure relaxation.

Why Catamarans Excel in Kornati Waters

Kornati's combination of open-water passages, narrow island channels, and scattered shallow anchorages demands a vessel that balances speed, stability, and shallow-water access. Catamarans check every box. The twin hulls provide exceptional stability when anchored in the lee of Vrulje or Telašćica, ensuring minimal rolling even when Maestral winds pick up. Draft ranges from 1.0 to 1.3 meters on most charter catamarans, enabling safe approach to moorings in Opat Bay, Ravni Žakan, and the protected waters around Levrnaka without anxiety.

The wide beam and spacious saloon make rough-weather sailing—such as Bura conditions between the outer islands—far more comfortable than on monohulls. Cruising speed of 8–10 knots means you reach the next anchorage quickly, maximizing time for swimming, snorkeling, and exploring Piškera's historic fishing settlement or the dramatic Stene cliffs near Telašćica.

Top Routes for Catamarans in Kornati

Seven-day circumnavigation: Depart Marina Biograd or Marina Tribunj, sail to Opat on Day 1 (the archipelago's northern anchor). Progress southward to Ravni Žakan and Smokvica, exploring quiet coves between each island. Day 4 brings you to Telašćica Nature Park—anchor in the protected southern bay and hike to the dramatic Stene cliffs or swim in the hypersaline lake Mir. Return via Levrnaka's Lojena beach and the restaurant moorings at Žut before heading back to port. This route plays to catamarans' strength: long, comfortable daily passages with calm anchorages.

Island-hopping from Murter: Betina or Jezera marinas are minutes from Kornati's northern entrance. Shorter daily legs allow families with children to explore multiple islands without fatigue. The catamaran's spacious cockpit makes sunset aperitivos unforgettable, and the shallow draft lets you anchor in places where crowded monohulls cluster in deeper water.

Technical Advantages in Kornati

Catamarans' wide beam and buoyant hulls reduce heeling, which matters when Bura funnels through narrow passages between outer islands. The dual-engine design—standard on modern charters—provides redundancy and nimbleness in tight anchorages where single-screw vessels struggle. Solar panels and generous tank capacity (500+ liters fresh water) reduce resupply anxiety; remember, no sources exist on the islands themselves.

Shallow draft is the catamaran's trump card. Approach within 50 meters of Piškera's abandoned settlement without running aground. Access the serene northern bays of Vrulje where anchoring is both scenic and protected. When summer crowds concentrate monohulls in deeper-water fields, catamaran pilots enjoy solitude.

Pricing and What to Expect

A 4-cabin catamaran in Kornati, June through September, typically rents for €3,500–€5,500 per week bareboat. Crewed options (skipper and/or chef) add €150–€250 daily. Bareboat requires proof of RYA or equivalent certification; many charterers arrange flotilla sailing or professional skippers through our partners for peace of mind. Berth-charter (day-charter or one-cabin rental) costs €200–€350 per person daily, ideal for solo travelers or couples unwilling to commit to a full boat.

Factor in the Kornati National Park entrance fee (€10–€15 per person per day, payable at park checkpoints), provisioning in Biograd or Šibenik before departure, and modest diesel costs. Crewed charters include all fuel and skipper expertise; bareboat charterers should budget €150–€200 for fuel across a week.

Best Time and Departure Points

June through September offer the steadiest winds (Maestral) and warmest seas. May and October are possible but less predictable. Departure marinas—Marina Biograd (full facilities), Marina Šibenik (convenient for southern access), Marina Tribunj, Betina, and Jezera (all on Murter, closest to Kornati's north)—provide fuel, provisioning, and crew briefings. Biograd remains the largest base; plan a morning briefing and midday departure to reach Opat or Ravni Žakan by sunset.

Avoid August peak crowds if solitude appeals; June and September offer ideal conditions with far fewer vessels in anchorages.

Sample 7-Day Catamaran Itinerary

Day 1: Biograd to Opat (15 nm, 2.5 hours). Moor in the northern bay. Visit konoba for dinner.

Day 2: Opat to Ravni Žakan (12 nm, 2 hours). Afternoon snorkel in Smokvica's crystal waters.

Day 3: Ravni Žakan to Telašćica (18 nm, 3 hours). Anchor in sheltered south bay. Hike Stene cliffs at sunset.

Day 4: Telašćica relaxation. Swim in hypersaline lake Mir. Explore nearby Levrnaka.

Day 5: Telašćica to Žut (12 nm, 2 hours). Lunch at floating restaurants. Afternoon sail to Vrulje.

Day 6: Vrulje to Smokvica (10 nm, 1.5 hours). Final sunset snorkel and evening konoba visit.

Day 7: Smokvica to Biograd (20 nm, 3 hours). Afternoon arrival, provisioning debrief.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a skipper license to charter a catamaran in Kornati?

Yes, bareboat charter requires an International Certificate of Competence (ICC) or RYA Day Skipper minimum. Charter companies verify credentials before key handover. Unfamiliar with Adriatic conditions? Hire a professional skipper through our partners or your charter agent—typically €60–€80 daily.

Is the catamaran's shallow draft really necessary in Kornati?

It's not strictly necessary but wonderfully practical. Deeper monohulls anchor safely too, but shallow draft opens hidden bays and reef passages. You'll sleep better knowing you can explore without anxiety about running aground.

What happens if Bura winds arrive during my charter?

Telašćica and Levrnaka offer protected southern anchorages. The catamaran's wide beam and low center of gravity handle rough conditions better than many monohulls. Monitor forecasts daily and plan conservative daily passages if Bura is forecast.

Can I provision the catamaran before entering Kornati?

Absolutely. Stock up in Biograd, Šibenik, or Murter. No shops, fuel, or water exist on park islands. Fresh fish and local wines are sold at island konobas (Opat, Žakan, Vrulje), but bring staples, ice, and wine from the mainland.

Is a crewed charter worth the extra cost?

Crewed allows true relaxation. The skipper navigates narrow channels, the chef serves fresh meals sourced from local konobas, and you focus on swimming and photography. For families or those unfamiliar with navigation, the investment is worthwhile.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.
