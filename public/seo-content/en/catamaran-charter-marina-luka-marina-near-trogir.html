Catamaran Charter from Marina Luka

Marina Luka (also known as Luka Marina) offers a quieter, more intimate alternative to the sprawling charter bases of Trogir and Split. Located near Trogir in the same protected waters, this boutique facility operates nine modern catamarans—a carefully selected fleet emphasizing personal service over volume. Marina Luka attracts crews seeking relaxed check-in procedures, attentive crew briefing, and the social appeal of a smaller charter community.

The Marina Luka Fleet: Modern & Reliable

Nine modern catamarans (34–42 feet) comprise Marina Luka's collection. Entry-level 34-foot vessels suit couples and small families. Mid-range 38–40-footers offer four cabins and superior stability for larger groups. All are Beneteau and Leopard models—industry-standard designs with excellent resale value and robust aftermarket support.

Modern genoas with furling systems, hydraulic outhauls, and optional spinnaker poles equip the fleet. GPS/chartplotter navigation, VHF radios, and life raft compliance are standard. Wind generators and robust battery banks support anchorage autonomy—idle your engines, drop anchor in a pristine cove, and enjoy peace without fuel consumption.

Relaxed Check-In & Departure Protocols

Unlike Saturday turnarounds at larger marinas, Marina Luka accommodates flexible arrival times. Arrive Thursday afternoon, settle aboard, and enjoy dinner in nearby Trogir (just 5 km by car). Friday morning, your 90-minute briefing covers catamaran systems, dinghy handling, and local anchorage guidance. Depart Friday afternoon or Saturday morning—your choice.

Sunday evening turnover is similarly relaxed. Return by 5 PM, tie off, and complete a brief checkout inspection. No pressure to rush; Boat4You's coordination ensures the next crew receives an equally welcoming reception. This flexibility appeals to working families and crews with irregular schedules.

Personalized Route Advice & Anchoring Guidance

Marina Luka's staff are seasoned sailors with intimate knowledge of quiet anchorages and insider restaurant recommendations. During briefing, they'll sketch preferred routes tailored to your group's experience level. First-time Adriatic sailors? You'll receive guidance on protected anchorages and calm-day passages. Experienced crews? Recommendations for challenging winds, deep-water sailing, and remote island exploration.

Paper charts, digital plotters, and handwritten notes on local harbors help crews navigate independent decision-making. Unlike flotilla support (group sailing), Marina Luka charters encourage self-reliance while providing 24/7 shore support via VHF or satellite phone if questions arise mid-voyage.

Nearby Destinations & Easy Day Sails

Brač Island (17 nautical miles) is a comfortable half-day sail. Anchor in Bol's peaceful harbor, explore the Adriatic's most famous beach (Zlatni Rat), and dine on fresh fish at waterfront restaurants.

Šolta Island (12 nm) offers quieter alternatives: Rogač, Grohote, and Maslinica villages lack the crowds of Hvar yet provide authentic Dalmatian charm, local wine, and excellent provisioning.

Drvenik Islands (6–8 nm) are perfect for first-time crews. Shallow anchorages, pine-back coves, and minimal current make these islands ideal for practicing anchor work and dinghy exploration without intimidating conditions.

Pricing & Seasonal Rates

Catamaran weekly rates at Marina Luka run €2,200–€4,600 shoulder season (April–May, September–October), rising to €3,800–€6,200 peak season (July–August). Smaller 34-footers are least expensive; 40-footers command premium pricing. Search Boat4You for real-time availability and seasonal promotions.

Fuel, mooring fees (€1.50–€2.00/meter/night), and provisioning are separate. Damage waiver insurance and 24/7 support are included. Off-season charters (November–March) are discounted 35–45%, ideal for budget-conscious crews experienced in winter Adriatic conditions.

FAQ: Catamaran Charters from Marina Luka

Why is Marina Luka less crowded than larger marinas?

Smaller operational capacity (nine catamarans vs. 70) means fewer concurrent charters. Briefing and turnaround procedures are unhurried. The trade-off: less availability during peak season. Book early to secure your preferred dates.

Can I customize my itinerary based on staff recommendations?

Absolutely. Marina Luka's staff encourage crews to adjust routes based on weather, crew mood, and real-time discovery. Unlike rigid flotillas with fixed daily destinations, your catamaran's flexibility is maximized here.

Is professional skipper support available?

Yes. Boat4You can arrange licensed skippers for additional fees. First-time sailors may opt for a skipper for the Friday shakedown sail and Saturday departure; experienced crews proceed bareboat.

What if mechanical issues arise during my charter?

Marina Luka maintains spares and has access to service technicians. Contact shore support via VHF; most issues are diagnosed and resolved within hours. Backup vessels can be arranged if a yacht must be withdrawn from service.

Can I extend my charter if I'm having a great time?

If availability permits, yes. Marina Luka's smaller size sometimes allows Sunday-to-Tuesday extensions if the following crew hasn't arrived. Contact the marina by Saturday to arrange an extension.
