Palm Cay Marina: Modern Caribbean Gateway

Palm Cay Marina is not a rustic anchorage—it's engineered efficiency masquerading as tropical leisure. The gated, 200+ berth facility features concrete floating docks, shore power on every berth (220V/110V options), fresh water, and a fuel dock operating 8 AM–5 PM daily. Reception staff coordinate with arriving crews, assist route planning, and facilitate customs/immigration pre-clearance via Nassau Customs Dock (accessible via tender or short dinghy ride).

The marina's protected basin, sheltered by outer breakwaters, creates a calm anchorage unaffected by Atlantic swell. Mooring fees typically run €1.50–€2.50 per meter nightly, though Boat4You often includes this in charter packages. The adjacent gate house provides 24-hour security, reducing theft and vandalism concerns common to open Caribbean anchorages.

Provisioning happens on-site: a small shop stocks basics (milk, bread, frozen goods), and supermarkets (Winn-Dixie, SuperValu) are accessible via shuttle or taxi within 10 minutes. The marina's restaurant/bar offers casual dining and crew social time. Many catamaran crews spend their final evening at Palm Cay's waterfront rather than rushing back to Nassau.

The Exuma Run: From Marina to Paradise

Departing Palm Cay early morning (typically 7–8 AM), catamarans undertake a 50 nautical mile passage to the Exuma Cays. Under favorable wind (northeast 15–18 knots typical), the crossing takes 6–8 hours under sail and engine-assist (many crews motor-sail to maintain schedule). The route passes through open Bahamian waters; shallow draft permits direct approach to the Exuma anchorages without tacking or strategic navigation complexity.

Typical Exuma itineraries anchor crews at Compass Cay (nurse shark and stingray swimming), Pig Beach (wild pig interactions), Thunderball Grotto (snorkeling inside tidal caves), and Staniel Cay Yacht Club (dockside dining). Three to seven days of island-hopping satisfy most crews before return to Palm Cay for resupply or onward passages.

Marina Facilities and Services

Berths & Mooring: 200+ floating berths accommodate vessels to 100+ feet. Most catamarans moor in the northwest basin, designated for charter fleet vessels. Reserved berths ensure minimal hassle upon return.

Fuel Dock: Operating 8 AM–5 PM, marine diesel and gasoline available. Pre-arrangement with reception permits evening fuel runs if necessary. Fuel cost typically €0.80–€1.20 per liter (variable with global markets).

Laundry: Self-service machines on-site. Coin-operated, modest cost.

Best Seasons for Palm Cay Charters

December–April: Prime season. Steady northeast trades (15–20 knots), warm days (75–82°F), minimal weather risk. Most expensive, books 8–12 weeks ahead. Ideal for nervous crews.

May–June: Shoulder season. Winds 12–18 knots, water warming (78–82°F). Good availability, better prices (10–15% discounts). Occasional squalls, but rarely severe. Excellent for value-conscious crews.

July–August: Summer sailing. Lighter, variable winds (8–14 knots), hot days (88–95°F). Air-conditioned catamarans maintain comfort. Discounted rates, moderate crowds. Suits crews with heat tolerance.

September–October: Autumn return. Winds ease, water very warm (82–86°F). Hurricane season risk present but statistically low. Substantial discounts attract experienced crews. Not recommended for first-time charterers.

Local Flavor and Provisioning at Palm Cay

Palm Cay Marina itself is international and efficient—not authentically Bahamian. However, the Exuma islands (your destination) offer genuine local culture: fresh conch salad at beachside shacks, grilled grouper and crawfish (Caribbean lobster) at Staniel Cay Yacht Club, local Kalik beer, and the relaxed pace of Bahamian island life.

Provisioning strategy: purchase non-perishables (pasta, rice, canned goods, spices) and longlife items at Palm Cay before departure. Fresh produce, meat, and dairy are limited in the Exumas; buy these in Nassau supermarkets or plan short visits to Great Exuma village shops for daily needs. Most crews adopt a flexible approach: cook aboard some nights, eat ashore at island restaurants others.

FAQ: Catamaran Charters from Palm Cay Marina

Can we collect a catamaran same-day after landing at LPIA?

Yes, if you arrive morning/early afternoon. Most crews clear immigration/customs by mid-day, reach Palm Cay by 2–3 PM, complete orientation by 5–6 PM, and depart next morning. Some skip the evening and depart early the same day if conditions permit and you're comfortable.

What certifications do we need for a bareboat charter?

International Certificate of Competence (ICC) or national equivalent is standard. verifies qualifications; if uncertain, crewed options remove this requirement entirely. Contact us to discuss your experience level.

Is Palm Cay Marina suitable for families with young children?

Yes. Catamarans' spacious decks, stable platforms, and shallow-water island exploration suit families. Shallow, protected anchorages (Compass Cay, Pig Beach) permit safe swimming and dinghy play. Air-conditioned cabins, modern galleys, and on-site facilities make extended cruising comfortable for children.

What if bad weather develops during our charter?

Catamarans offer excellent sea-keeping and twin engines for safe motoring. Most crews shelter at well-protected anchorages or return to Palm Cay if conditions deteriorate. Bahamian weather is generally predictable December–April; summer/autumn carries tropical weather risk.

Can we extend our charter if we're having fun?

Yes. Availability permitting, can extend your charter mid-voyage at negotiated daily rates. Notify reception ASAP if you anticipate extending; extensions during peak season may be impossible due to back-to-back bookings.
