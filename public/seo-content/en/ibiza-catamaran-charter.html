Why a Catamaran Suits Ibiza

Catamarans excel in the light to moderate winds that characterise Ibiza's sailing season (May–October). Their shallow draft allows exploration of exclusive anchorages—Cala Salada, Cala Bassa, and the crystalline waters around Es Vedrà—without the deep-keel limitations of monohulls. The twin-hull design creates a natural swimming platform and minimises heel, allowing crew to move freely below decks even in a breeze. For groups prioritising comfort and space over speed, a Boat4You catamaran delivers unmatched value.

Top Routes and Anchorages

Begin at Marina Ibiza or Marina Botafoch (central locations with full provisioning). Sail northwest toward Cala Salada, a sheltered bay with white sand and shallow turquoise water perfect for families. Continue to Cala Bassa, beloved by sunset chasers and known for beachfront chiringuitos serving fresh seafood. The crossing to Formentera is a gentle 11-nautical-mile passage; anchor off Ses Illetes for Caribbean-like clarity and fine white sand. Return via Es Pujols (Formentera's social hub) or explore the southern Ses Salines area, where protected posidonia seagrass meadows (UNESCO heritage) support marine life and justify careful anchoring.

Technical Considerations

Catamarans require slightly different sail trim compared to monohulls; wind should be more closely aligned to keep both hulls driving efficiently. Shallow-draft models (1.2–1.5 metres) suit Ibiza's protected bays but demand respect for UNESCO-protected seagrass. Most Boat4You catamarans feature electric windlasses, dual heads, and efficient engine rooms, simplifying day-to-day operations. Insurance and provisioning are comparable to monohulls; crew typically reduces by one given the reduced workload.

Bareboat, skipper-inclusive, and all-inclusive options are available through our partners; prices reflect vessel size, age, and onboard equipment. Deposits (typically 30%) are non-refundable more than 90 days pre-charter; cancellation insurance is strongly recommended given Ibiza's unpredictable summer wind patterns.

Best Time to Charter and Popular Bases

May, June, September, and October deliver ideal sailing: consistent northerly winds, warm water (22–24°C), and smaller tourist crowds than midsummer. July–August brings peak party season—Ibiza Town's nightclubs run until dawn—but also gusty afternoons and crowded anchorages. Marina Ibiza (east side) suits those wanting town access; Santa Eulalia marina (northeast) offers quieter charm; San Antonio (west) is sunset-focused and family-friendly. Formentera's Es Pujols has basic facilities and serves as an alternative overnight stop.

Sample Three-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Depart Marina Botafoch, sail to Cala Salada (2 hours). Afternoon swim and beachside dinner at a local chiringuito. Day 2: Cross to Formentera (3 hours), anchor Ses Illetes. Explore white-sand beaches and Es Pujols tavernas. Day 3: Sail Ses Salines (respecting posidonia protection), return to Ibiza via Es Vedrà, overnight at Cala Bassa. Optional: extend to visit Talamanca (scenic family beach) on return.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a catamaran stable enough for non-swimmers?

Yes. Catamarans are inherently stable; heel is minimal even in fresh wind. The wide platform and ease of boarding via the sugar-scoop transom (on most models) make them ideal for cautious swimmers and young children.

Can we visit Es Vedrà up close?

Es Vedrà (the iconic mystical rock) can be circled but not landed. Boat4You crews know the best anchorages for photographs; late afternoon light is spectacular.

How shallow can catamarans go?

Most are 1.2–1.5 metres draft. This allows entry to Cala Bassa and Cala Salada; typically request draft specs from our fleet when booking.

What's the crew-to-guest ratio for a catamaran?

A skipper handles sailing; a host/steward manages provisioning and cabin service. Experienced bareboat crews often skip the steward for cost savings.

Are there restrictions on anchoring near the posidonia meadows?

Yes. UNESCO regulations prohibit anchoring in seagrass areas. crews are trained in sustainable anchoring; buoyed moorings exist in key bays.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
