The Carenage: Historic Harbor of Caribbean Sailing

The Carenage—St. George's inner harbor—earned its name from the careening process where wooden sailing vessels were maintained. This practice required hauling vessels onto beaches for hull inspection and repairs, a process that gave the harbor its distinctive character. Today, the Carenage remains one of the Caribbean's most atmospheric harbors, surrounded by historic waterfront buildings, colorful wooden fishing vessels, and the ongoing maritime commerce that has characterized this location for three centuries.

Anchoring within the Carenage provides something increasingly rare in Caribbean tourism—authentic immersion in working maritime culture. You're not isolated at a distant mooring field watching sunsets; you're in the heart of a living harbor where fishing boats depart before dawn, local vendors sell fish and produce on the waterfront, and sailors exchange information about weather, conditions, and sailing routes. This cultural authenticity, combined with the protected lagoon conditions, makes the Carenage an exceptional homeport for catamaran charters.

The narrow harbor entrance—just wide enough to accommodate sailboats and modest catamarans—maintains the Carenage's character while keeping it accessible only to smaller vessels. This natural limitation preserves the harbor's traditional atmosphere while making catamaran maneuvering straightforward. Your Boat4You catamaran crew navigates the entrance with practiced ease, allowing you to focus on the experience of entering one of the Caribbean's most distinctive harbors.
