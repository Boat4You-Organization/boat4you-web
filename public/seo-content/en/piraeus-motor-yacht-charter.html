Best Seasons for Motor Yacht Charters

May & June: Warm days (24–28°C), calm seas, gentle winds. Ideal for families and first-time charterers. Fewer crowds than summer; excellent provisioning ashore.

July & August: Hot, predictable conditions. Most social anchorages (Hydra Town, Poros harbor). Higher prices; book 2–3 months ahead. Long daylight extends exploration time.

September & October: Still warm (22–25°C), calmer than midsummer. Excellent value. Water temperatures ideal for swimming. Fewer tourists. Highly recommended for luxury cruising.

April & November: Shoulder seasons offer best value. Weather is mild but unpredictable. Some charter operators reduce fleet availability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a captain's license to charter a motor yacht?

Bareboat motor yacht charters typically require proof of powerboat experience and often an ICC (International Certificate of Competence). Most clients opt for crewed charters, eliminating this requirement. Professional captains handle all navigation and vessel management.

Can the crew cook special dietary meals?

Absolutely. Inform in advance of dietary restrictions or preferences. Your private chef will source ingredients and prepare meals tailored to your tastes. Vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, allergies—all are accommodated.

What's included in a crewed motor yacht charter?

Crewed charters include captain, engineer, and hostess; fuel; insurance; all provisioning; gourmet meals and beverages; docking and mooring fees; and 24/7 support. Typically excluded: gratuities (15% customary), alcohol above house selections, and spa/wellness services arranged separately.

Can a motor yacht safely navigate Greek waters in rough weather?

Modern motor yachts are seaworthy and stable. Stabilization systems minimize rolling. Greek summer weather is generally mild; winter can bring rough conditions. Experienced captains monitor forecasts and adjust itineraries to avoid dangerous conditions. You're rarely forced to depart or stay at anchorage against captain's judgment.
