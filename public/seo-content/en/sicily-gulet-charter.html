FAQ

What experience level is required?

Bareboat charters require RYA Day Skipper or equivalent certification demonstrating navigation and seamanship competency. Beginners hire professional skippers (€150–250 daily) managing sailing operations. All-inclusive crewed charters eliminate experience requirements entirely—professional crew manages everything.

What is included in charter pricing?

Standard pricing covers vessel, crew salaries, fuel, meals, beverages, snorkeling equipment, and dinghy excursions. Premium wine, specialized activities (helicopter tours, ROV expeditions), and enhanced services incur additional fees detailed in itemized quotes provided before booking confirmation.

How flexible are itinerary changes?

Professional crews adjust itineraries based on weather, guest interests, and local conditions offering flexibility impossible on rigid cruise ship schedules. Last-minute destination changes, extended stays in beloved locations, and spontaneous cultural discoveries characterize the charter experience advantage.

What are typical weather conditions?

May–October charter season experiences minimal severe weather. Occasional autumn Sirocco winds (20–25 knots) prompt strategic anchorage repositioning but rarely prevent sailing. Professional captains prioritize guest comfort and safety maintaining protective harbor positioning when conditions warrant conservative approaches.

How far in advance should charters be booked?

Peak July–August demand requires 12–18 months advance booking due to limited premium inventory availability. June and September accommodate 8–12 week lead times. Shoulder months (May, October) permit flexible 6–8 week scheduling. Occasional last-minute cancellations surface through broker networks offering substantial discounted rates.

Pantelleria: Wine, Thermal Spas, and Isolation

Pantelleria—a volcanic island equidistant between Sicily and Tunisia—remains Sicily's most authentic and least touristic destination. Passito di Pantelleria sweet wine, with its concentrated raisin flavors, pairs exquisitely with evening deck dinners after challenging sailing days. Dammusi (traditional white-domed houses) dot volcanic hillsides, offering stunning photo opportunities. Laghetto delle Ondine thermal pools bubble with naturally heated mineral-rich waters, providing rejuvenating evening soaks after intense sailing. Five-day Pantelleria itineraries combine Egadi exploration with this remote island's mystique, appealing to adventurous travelers seeking authentic Mediterranean experiences unavailable through mainstream tourism channels.

Gulets based in Palermo position toward Pantelleria in 30–40 hours sailing, exploring volcanic coastlines, dramatic sea caves, and traditional fishing villages before returning via Egadi cluster. Local restaurants feature capers—Pantelleria's famous culinary treasure—prepared in endless Mediterranean variations. Professional gulet crews arrange private wine tastings at family-owned Passito producers, cultural experiences money cannot replicate through cruise-ship tourism.

Safety Briefing and Handover Process

Before departure, professional captains conduct mandatory 90-minute safety briefing covering man-overboard recovery procedures, liferaft deployment and certification verification, emergency communication protocols (radio, satellite phone), fire suppression system operation, and medical incident response procedures. All crew members demonstrate lifeboat muster participation; children participate in age-appropriate safety education without frightening implications. Life jackets, safety harnesses, and first-aid kits receive thorough inspection; crew demonstrates proper fitting for each guest.

Galley and operational system briefings follow; chefs explain dietary accommodation procedures, meal-timing flexibility, and provisioning opportunity windows at harborside markets. Deckhands orient guests to dinghy usage, snorkeling protocols, and tendering procedures minimizing crew workload while maximizing guest autonomy. Captains provide detailed passage itinerary briefings, wind forecasting updates, and cultural destination explanations creating educational framework for the week-long voyage ahead.

What should guests pack for a week-long gulet charter?

Casual nautical clothing (sun-protective layers, swimwear, lightweight wind jackets). Seasickness medication (if prone). Sunscreen (SPF 50+), sunglasses, polarized lenses for water reflection. Slip-on deck shoes (boat safety requirement). Light evening attire (sundresses, linen shirts—dinner ashore remains casual Mediterranean-style). Prescription medications in original containers. E-readers or books for calm-weather reading. Underwater camera. Toiletries, but crew supplies basic items if forgotten. Minimal luggage—cabins accommodate two duffels maximum per guest.
