Marina Botafoch: Ibiza's Premier Yachting Centre

Botafoch occupies a protected harbour basin north of Ibiza town, fed by a long breakwater and marked by the historic lighthouse that has guided mariners since the 19th century. The marina itself underwent major renovation in recent years, emerging as a world-class facility. It offers 400+ berths, ranging from 8-metre speedboats to 60-metre superyachts. The main basin features 230V/400V shore power throughout, water at every berth, comprehensive fuel distribution (diesel, petrol, and premium fuels), and a sophisticated waste management system.

The reception complex is open 07:00–23:00 daily and staffed by multilingual professionals. A full-service chandlery stocks marine provisions, electronics, fishing gear, and spare parts. Botafoch's in-house shipyard can haul vessels to 100 tonnes, offering engine overhauls, mechanical repairs, gel-coat refurbishment, and systems diagnostics. VIP services include a concierge team arranging restaurants, provisioning delivery, car rental, and even helicopter transfers—the full luxury-yacht experience.

The marina's signature landmark, the Botafoch lighthouse, rises 40 metres above the water. Adjacent to it stands the Casino d'Eivissa, Ibiza's most exclusive nightspot, where superyacht guests often book private tables and celebrate into dawn. This geographic convergence of yachting infrastructure and celebrity nightlife gives Botafoch a unique atmosphere—professional yet hedonistic, serious yet fun.

Why Charter a Motorboat from Botafoch?

For motorboat charters, Botafoch's advantages are substantial. The marina's breakwater location offers direct access to open water with minimal manoeuvring—you exit the marina gates and accelerate northwestward into clear sailing zones within 5 minutes. Contrast this to Marina Port (east side of Ibiza town), where you must navigate the crowded harbour basin and merchant shipping channels. From Botafoch, you're immediately in the islands' quietest waters.

The 20-nautical-mile passage to Formentera takes just 30 minutes in a 40-knot sportboat, or 45 minutes in a comfortable 25-knot cabin cruiser—making a full-day excursion feasible without rushed timings. More ambitious itineraries (Mallorca, the Spanish mainland coast) become genuinely accessible within a week-long charter.

Botafoch's infrastructure caters to powerboat charters. Fuel docks are efficient and modern; water is reliably available; and the full-time technical team resolves mechanical issues within hours, not days. For clients planning to use their motorboat intensively—multiple passages daily, extended range cruising, or entertaining guests—Botafoch's professional environment is indispensable.

Motorboat Classes and Vessel Selection

Boat4You's Botafoch-based motorboat fleet emphasises quality and range. Open speedboats (32–40 feet) deliver adrenaline: twin or triple high-horsepower engines pushing speeds to 40+ knots. These vessels are thrilling for day-trips, suitable for confident piloting only. Fuel consumption is high (20–30 gallons per hour at cruise speed), but range still permits 150+ nautical miles per tank.

Cabin cruisers (38–50 feet) balance performance and practicality. Twin diesel engines cruise efficiently at 20–25 knots, maintaining range of 300+ nautical miles. Spacious cabins sleep 4–6; galleys are fully appointed; and the stabiliser systems (common in this class) reduce motion in rough seas. These vessels suit families, multi-day charters, and those prioritising comfort.

Luxury flybridge cruisers (50–65 feet) are floating penthouses. Triple diesels deliver impressive range; upper flying bridges offer commanding views and ample entertaining space; and full-beam master cabins rival high-end hotel suites. Most luxury motorboat charters are skippered, with professional crew handling navigation and mechanics while you enjoy the experience.'s Botafoch fleet includes several luxury vessels available bareboat (with valid license) or fully skippered.

Motorboat Day-Trip Itineraries from Botafoch

A typical luxury motorboat day might unfold like this: breakfast at Botafoch's marina café, depart 09:00 via the breakwater gap, motor northwest toward Es Vedrà (6 nm, 15 minutes), anchor in protected Cala d'Hort (5 fathoms, sandy bottom). For a Formentera-focused day: early departure 08:00, high-speed cruise to Illetas or Espalmador (20 nm, 30 minutes in a sportboat; 45 in a cruiser), beach club lunch and champagne at Jockey Club or Experimental Beach, dinghy exploration of Formentera's pristine coves, and return to Botafoch by 18:30. Evening aperitivo at the Casino or a waterside restaurant overlooks your motorboat gently rocking at anchor—effortless sophistication.

Multi-day charters venture further. A Mallorca passage (northeast, 60 nm) reaches Puerto de Pollença in 2–3 hours, opening the dramatic sailing grounds of the Tramontana coast, the protected Cabrera Archipelago, and quieter anchorages unknown to most tourists. Alternatively, head west toward the Costa Blanca (Spain's eastern coast), anchoring at Moraira or Jávea, where coastal villages and water sports abound.

Fuel, Navigation, and Technical Support

Botafoch's fuel dock operates with industrial efficiency. Diesel and petrol are available 07:00–20:00 daily. Premium diesel (Gasoil Marina) costs €2.10–2.50 per litre (prices fluctuate seasonally and globally). A cabin cruiser's typical 250-litre fuel tank costs €500–625 to fill—enough for a week of moderate cruising. A sportboat burning fuel rapidly at high speeds will refuel more frequently but covers greater distances, offsetting per-mile fuel costs.

Boat4You provides comprehensive navigation briefings, including waypoint files pre-loaded into your motorboat's GPS chartplotter, emergency harbours, fuel stops, and local hazards. Most modern motorboats carry dual GPS units, VHF radios, and automatic identification systems (AIS)—technology that makes modern navigation almost foolproof. That said, a professional skipper is invaluable if you're unfamiliar with Mediterranean navigation or prefer not to assume that responsibility.

Technical support is embedded in Botafoch's infrastructure. Any mechanical issue—engine alarm, electrical fault, plumbing problem—can be escalated to the marina's service desk. Mechanics typically respond within 2 hours. Boat4You provides a 24/7 helpline; Spanish coast guard frequency (VHF channel 16) connects you to emergency services. In practice, modern motorboats rarely malfunction during charters, but the support framework is robust.

Weather, Seasons, and Optimal Timing

Botafoch's north-facing orientation provides excellent shelter from summer gales (which approach from the west/southwest). The Embat breeze, consistent in May–October, rarely exceeds force 5 and often settles by early evening, leaving calm conditions for evening anchoring. June, July, and September are the premium motorboat-charter months: steady weather, warm water (26–27°C), and reliable winds.

August can bring stronger thermal winds and crowded anchorages, but modern powerboats with air conditioning and stabiliser systems manage comfortably. Spring (April–May) offers longer daylight, pleasantly warm water (20–23°C), and fewer crowds. Autumn (September–October) delivers Indian-summer-like conditions: stable weather, superb visibility, and a peaceful sailing atmosphere.

Winter motorboat charters (November–March) are feasible but require more planning. Atlantic gales can reach Ibiza within 36–48 hours; weather windows are shorter; and water temperatures dip (13–17°C in January). That said, winter sailing attracts experienced crews seeking isolation and the landscapes' austere beauty. manages weather routing and safe passage planning for winter charters.

Bareboat Licensing and Skippered Charters

Bareboat motorboat charters require a valid recreational boating license. Spain recognises most national recreational powerboat certifications; EU member states' licenses are accepted; non-EU visitors may need to arrange International Boating Permits. verifies credentials and can arrange provisional licensing if needed.

Skippered charters remove the licensing requirement entirely. Your professional skipper—typically a captain with 20+ years' Mediterranean experience—pilots the vessel, handles all navigation, manages fuel and mechanical checks, and shares local knowledge. Many first-time motorboat charterers book skippered charters, returning the following year with bareboat confidence.

Can we take non-swimmers on a motorboat charter?

Yes. Modern motorboats have secure railings, spacious decks, and stable hulls. The only swimming requirement is comfort being on a boat; actual swimming ability isn't essential, though snorkelling enthusiasts should be confident in water.

What happens if we encounter rough seas underway?

Modern cabin cruisers and flybridge vessels feature active stabiliser systems that dramatically reduce motion. If conditions deteriorate, your skipper (or you, if bareboat) can slow down, motor into protected anchorages, or wait out the weather. The Balearic region rarely experiences extreme short-duration storms; larger Atlantic gales are forecast well in advance, allowing contingency planning.

Is Botafoch easily accessible from Ibiza's airport?

Yes. Ibiza International Airport is 8 kilometres south of Marina Botafoch—a 15-minute taxi ride. Many charter clients arrive mid-afternoon, transfer to Botafoch, and board their motorboat by 17:00, timing a sunset departure perfectly.
