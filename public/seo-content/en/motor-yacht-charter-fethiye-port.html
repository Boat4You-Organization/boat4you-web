Fethiye Port: Where Modern Meets Maritime Tradition

Fethiye Port has hosted Mediterranean vessels for centuries, serving as a natural anchorage protected from northerly winds. This authentic maritime atmosphere distinguishes Fethiye from purpose-built tourist marinas. Signature Motor Yacht Itineraries from Fethiye Port

A typical week-long motor yacht charter from Fethiye Port explores the full spectrum of Lycian and Aegean attractions. Your first evening anchors near Ölüdeniz Blue Lagoon, 15 kilometres south. The lagoon's famous crescent beach backed by pine-forested slopes provides immediate swimming, paddleboarding, and tender exploration. Waterfront restaurants serve grilled sea bass, mezes, and Turkish wine as sunset paints the sky orange and pink.

Days two and three venture to Butterfly Valley, where dramatic cliffs plunge into turquoise water accessible only by sea. Tender excursions up a narrow riverside gorge reach natural pools and freshwater cascades. The endemic Jersey Tiger butterflies gather in summer, their striking orange and black patterns covering flowers and vegetation. Ancient Lycian cities—Tlos and Xanthos—feature rock-hewn tombs and archaeological remains spanning two thousand years. Your motor yacht's tender carries you ashore; Boat4You can arrange experienced guides providing archaeological and historical context.

Days four through six typically explore the Gulf of Gökova and anchorages near Gemiler Island. Saint Nicholas Island (Gemiler) hosts Byzantine monastic ruins, exceptional snorkelling, and peaceful overnight moorings far from crowds. Kayaköy ghost village—the abandoned Greek settlement of the 1920s population exchange—offers haunting beauty and remarkable photography. Your motor yacht's superior stability and speed ensure comfortable transit between destinations, with ample time for exploration and leisure at each location.

Meltemi Winds, Season Planning, and Perfect Conditions

Summer sailing from Fethiye Port benefits from the meltemi—a reliable northerly wind sweeping down from the Balkans. This wind keeps daytime temperatures moderate even in July and August, though water remains warm (24–28 degrees Celsius). Motor yachts are unaffected by wind conditions, though the meltemi generally ensures calm seas perfect for swimming, snorkelling, and water sports.

The optimal season spans June through September, though shoulder seasons (May and October) offer fewer crowds and excellent conditions. Winter charters remain possible with appropriately equipped vessels; spring and autumn are preferred by many experienced travellers seeking ideal balance between weather, crowds, and activity opportunities.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a motor yacht and a gulet?

Motor yachts are modern fiberglass vessels powered entirely by diesel engines, offering speed (12–15 knots), contemporary amenities, and flexibility. Gulets are traditional wooden sailing vessels prioritising authentic experience and slower pace (4–6 knots under sail).

How much advance notice do we need for dietary requirements?

Provide dietary information during booking—ideally 4–6 weeks before departure. Your chef coordinates provisioning accordingly. Vegetarian, vegan, kosher, halal, gluten-free, and allergy-conscious menus are easily accommodated with advance notice.

Can we customise our route once onboard?

Yes. Encounter a captivating anchorage? Extend your stay. Prefer more swimming? Reduce destination visits. Your charter, your preferences.

Charter fees cover the vessel, professional crew, all meals prepared aboard, water, fuel, mooring in protected harbours, and basic snorkelling equipment. Not included: alcoholic beverages (unless pre-arranged), crew gratuities (typically 10%), diving certifications, or excursions requiring additional fees.
