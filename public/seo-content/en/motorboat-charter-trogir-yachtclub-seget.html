Operating Seasons & Weather

Spring (April–May) offers calm seas and mild temperatures (18–22°C air, 16–19°C water)—ideal for motorboat day-tripping. Summer (June–August) brings reliable weather and warm water (24–27°C), though July–August crowds intensify. Autumn (September–October) combines warm conditions (23–25°C water), moderate winds, and reduced tourism—excellent for unhurried motorboat exploration. Winter charters are possible but require caution due to unpredictable weather and shorter daylight hours.

FAQ: Motorboat Charters from Yachtclub Seget

Do I need a boating license to operate a motorboat from Yachtclub Seget?

Croatian regulations require a boating license (category B—inland/coastal) for powerboats over 6 kW. verifies credentials and can arrange professional skippers if you lack certification. Day-trippers from non-sailing backgrounds are welcome with skipper assistance.

Can I take a motorboat overnight, or are they day-charter only?

Cuddy-cabin motorboats (28–32 feet) support overnight stays for couples or small families. Open motorboats are day-charter only. coordinates overnight berth availability at Yachtclub Seget and assists with provisions.

What's the typical fuel consumption for a 28-foot motorboat?

A 28-foot cuddy-cabin motorboat consumes approximately 12–15 liters per hour at cruising speed (18–22 knots). A full-day outing (6 hours running) uses 75–90 liters—modest cost and excellent range for day-trips to Drvenik, Šolta, and Brač.

Are there fuel docks beyond Yachtclub Seget in the Split region?

Yes. Brač (Bol and Supetarska Rava), Hvar Town, and Trogir's ACI Marina all operate fuel docks.

Is fishing permitted from a chartered motorboat?

Recreational fishing is permitted with Croatian fishing licenses (day passes available). Many motorboat charters include a fishing angle—consult our team for regional hot-spots near Yachtclub Seget.
