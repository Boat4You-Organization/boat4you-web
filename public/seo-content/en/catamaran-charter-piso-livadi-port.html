Port Facilities and Moorings

Piso Livadi Port accommodates modest vessel numbers—typically five to six charter catamarans—on tender-and-anchor mooring systems. This arrangement preserves the port's authentic character whilst ensuring secure holding for your catamaran across variable sea conditions.

Water and fuel are available through local provisioning agents. Ice, provisions, and daily supplies are obtainable at village shops within walking distance of the waterfront. The port authority is cooperative and efficient, facilitating all clearance and administrative requirements.

Emergency facilities include medical services (nearby Paroikia), coast guard radio contact via VHF, and established relationships with professional riggers, engineers, and sail-repair specialists. Should issues arise during your charter, Boat4You coordinates rapid professional response.

The port's sheltered northeast exposure provides good holding in most wind conditions. Summer meltemia winds arrive from the northwest, naturally pushing vessels toward protected holding areas. Winter anchorage is less assured; most charters occur May-October when conditions are optimal.

Sailing Routes from Piso Livadi Port

Piso Livadi Port provides direct access to the central Aegean island chain. Naxos (20 nm southeast, 3–4 hours sailing) is the most popular destination—spectacular anchorages, beaches, and traditional villages reward the short passage.

Antiparos and Despotiko islands (west, 12–15 nm) provide shorter passages ideal for families or skill-building crews. The Antiparos sea caves are accessible by tender, offering unique swimming and snorkeling experiences unavailable elsewhere in the Aegean.

Week-long circuits encompassing Naxos, Iraklia, Shinousa, and Amorgos reward adventurous crews with solitude and stunning seascapes. Each island presents distinct character: Amorgos for dramatic cliffside villages, smaller islands for tranquil anchorages and traditional tavernas.

Delos (archaeological site) and Mykonos (cosmopolitan island) lie further south—achievable on longer charters by crews comfortable with open-water passages. The Aegean's island density ensures you're rarely more than 4–6 hours from a new anchorage.

Catamaran Advantages from Piso Livadi

Catamarans offer superior stability and spacious accommodation compared to monohulls. The dual-hull design provides exceptional comfort in variable conditions, making them ideal for families, less experienced sailors, or those prioritizing comfort over traditional sailing dynamics.

The wide beam accommodates comfortable decking arrangements. Generous cabin space allows private berths, reducing social friction during extended charters. Modern galley equipment and efficient systems make onboard living straightforward.

Reduced heel angle—catamarans remain nearly level whilst sailing—minimizes seasickness and allows continuous deck access. Everyone stays comfortable, engaged, and enjoying the sailing rather than retiring below deck.

Seasons and Weather Patterns

Catamaran charters from Piso Livadi run May through October. The Aegean's weather patterns are exceptionally reliable during this window: meltemia winds (northwest, 15–25 knots) predominate summer months, lighter and more variable winds prevail in shoulder seasons.

June and September are ideal months—reliable winds, warm water, manageable crowds, and long daylight hours. July and August see stronger winds and larger visitor numbers. May and October offer solitude with lighter conditions.

Water temperatures: 18°C (April) warming to 25°C by July, remaining comfortable through October. Winter sailing is possible but less appealing; facilities reduce hours and weather becomes unpredictable.
