Why a Catamaran from Marina Naviera Balear

Twin-hulled stability eliminates the heeling that unsettles passengers on monohulls, which makes catamarans the default choice for families, mixed-experience groups, and anyone prone to seasickness. Shallow draft (typically 1.2–1.5 metres) opens up anchorages closed to keeled yachts. Deck space, shade, and stable anchor holding in afternoon blows is the main reason charterers pick this platform for Spain's cruising grounds.

Typical catamaran charters from Marina Naviera Balear run 38 to 48 feet, with four to six cabins and equally numerous heads, priced at roughly 2,800 to 7,500 euro per week depending on size, age and season. Bareboat charter suits experienced skippers holding a valid licence and VHF certificate. For groups without credentials, a professional skipper runs around 180–220 euro per day plus meals. The berthing configuration at Marina Naviera Balear handles catamarans without issue — allow enough turning room in the basin on busy Saturday turnarounds.

Sample 7-day itinerary from Marina Naviera Balear

Day 1 — Departure and first bay

Embark after noon once the cabin preparation is complete, stock provisions, and motor to a nearby sheltered bay for the first night. Swim, rig checks, and a quiet dinner aboard set the tone.

Day 2 to Day 3 — Short hops along the coast

Cover 15–25 nautical miles per day between protected harbours and anchorages. Lunch stops at lunch-only anchorages, evenings in towns with konoba dining and a morning bakery run.

Day 4 — Longer crossing

Pick a day with favourable forecast for the longest passage of the week. Target 25–40 nautical miles of sailing or motoring depending on wind.

Day 5 to 6 — Return legs

Work back toward base with cultural stops — old town walk, a local winery, and an anchorage-only overnight if weather allows.

Day 7 — Return to base

Early morning passage back, fuel top-up, a quick galley clean, and formal disembarkation by late morning.
