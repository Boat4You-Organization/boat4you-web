Island Routes: Brijuni, Fažana, Kamenjak, and Beyond

Brijuni National Park, 8 nautical miles from Veruda, anchors most catamaran itineraries. The 14-island archipelago showcases pine-fringed anchorages, Byzantine ruins, and exceptional underwater visibility. A catamaran's spacious saloon and shaded sun deck encourage leisurely island hopping. Day charters to Brijuni typically depart at dawn, moor by mid-morning, and return by dusk—a perfect introduction to Istrian coastal sailing.

Fažana, 5 kilometres north, serves as a secondary provisioning hub and gateway to northern Brijuni passages. The village's working harbour retains authentic character: fishing boats, waterfront restaurants serving fresh catches, and a ferry terminal connecting to Brijuni's national park facilities. Catamaran charters often include an overnight stop at Fažana, combining exploration with local gastronomy.

Kamenjak peninsula, southernmost Istria, presents dramatic limestone scenery accessible within two hours under power. The catamaran's dual engines ensure reliable transit; the spacious saloon provides retreat during any unexpectedly choppy transit. Kamenjak's protected waters reward exploration: hidden coves, translucent anchorages, and pristine beaches unattainable by car.

Rovinj, 35 nautical miles north-west, emerges as a multi-day destination. The catamaran's generous accommodation—sleeping 8–12 passengers—enables extended family voyages. Rovinj's waterfront restaurants, narrow medieval streets, and vibrant summer culture justify overnight mooring. Veruda's professional skippers often recommend Rovinj as the pinnacle of multi-day Istrian catamaran charters.

FAQ: Catamaran Charters from Tehnomont Marina Veruda

Why choose a catamaran over a monohull sailboat from Veruda?

Catamarans offer twin-hull stability, spacious deck areas, and minimal heel—ideal for non-sailor companions, families with young children, and those prioritizing comfort over sailing performance. Shallow draft permits anchoring in protected coves inaccessible to deeper monohulls. The larger saloon supports leisurely daytime lounging and meal preparation.

Can I moor my charter catamaran at Veruda overnight?

Yes. Veruda accommodates catamaran mooring during changeover days and shorter charters. Daily mooring fees apply (typically €1.80–€2.50 per metre). coordinates mooring reservations in advance, guaranteeing berth availability. Extended mooring (beyond charter end dates) incurs additional charges.

How fuel-efficient are Veruda's catamarans?

Twin-engine catamarans burn 25–40 litres per hour at cruising speed (1000–1200 RPM). Fuel costs are transparent; Skippered charters often optimize speed for fuel efficiency, extending range and reducing refuelling stops.
