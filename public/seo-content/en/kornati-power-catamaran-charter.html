Power Catamaran Charter in Kornati: Speed and Space in a Stable Platform

Power catamarans—motorized dual-hull vessels (35–50 feet)—deliver the spacious comfort of sailing catamarans with motorized directness: fast (12–16 knots cruising), stable (minimal rolling), and practical. Unlike sailing catamarans dependent on wind, power catamarans guarantee schedule adherence while retaining the platform stability and shallow draft that make them superior to monohull powerboats in Kornati's complex waters. Ideal for families prioritizing comfort over sailing tradition, groups seeking effortless exploration, and those willing to embrace motorized convenience without sacrificing space or seaworthiness.

Why Power Catamarans Excel in Kornati's Shallow, Complex Waters

The twin-hull design offers unmatched stability and shallow draft (0.9–1.3 meters)—combining strengths no monohull achieves. In Kornati, where limestone reefs and rocky bottoms demand caution, shallow draft becomes transformative: access hidden bays larger vessels avoid. Motor reliability ensures zero wind anxiety. Twin engines (one per hull, typically 150–250 HP each) provide redundancy and powerful maneuvering in tight anchorages. The wide beam creates spacious interiors—full galleys, comfortable cabins, open-air saloons—rivaling catamarans thrice their price.

Cruising speed (12–16 knots) means flexibility: depart morning, reach distant Telašćica by early afternoon, and still anchor comfortably before sunset. No wind-dependent dawdling, no pressure to reach mediocre anchorages for fear of darkness.

Technical Strengths and Comfort Standards

Power catamarans (35–50 feet) feature 2–4 spacious cabins, full air-conditioning, generous galleys, and multiple heads—more generous than monohulls. Twin engines (150–250 HP per hull) deliver 12–16 knot cruising and efficient fuel consumption (15–25 liters/hour depending on speed and hull design). Modern builds incorporate solar panels, water makers, and large fuel/freshwater capacity, reducing resupply dependence critical in Kornati's remote setting.

The catamaran platform is incomparably stable: no heeling, minimal motion, level cabin platforms. Families with young children or elderly passengers appreciate the non-jarring seaboard experience. Swimming platform designs rival private pools; some yachts feature built-in hot tubs or water sports equipment (kayaks, snorkel gear, paddleboards).

Pricing and Bareboat/Crewed Options

Power catamaran rentals range €2,500–€4,500 weekly bareboat for 35–40 foot models; 45–50 foot catamarans reach €4,500–€6,500. Crewed versions (skipper only, or skipper + chef) add €100–€200 daily. June and September offer 20–30% savings versus July–August peak; May and October, 30–40% reductions. Most bareboat rentals include fuel; verify with the charter company.

Bareboat requires valid boating certification (RYA Day Skipper minimum); many first-timers hire professional skippers for briefing (€50–€80 for 2–3 hours), then operate independently. Crewed catamarans suit those prioritizing service and stress-free exploration.

Best Season and Convenient Departure Points

June through September, with early/late season offering calmer conditions and fewer crowds. Power catamarans suit flexible bookings (2–7 days); availability is strong even 3–4 weeks before desired dates.

Primary bases: Marina Biograd (largest hub, full provisioning and fuel services), Marina Šibenik (southern alternative), Betina and Jezera (Murter, closest to northern Kornati). All offer same-day embarkation for prepared charterers; advance booking ensures optimal vessel selection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a power catamaran more fuel-efficient than a monohull motorboat?

Comparable, possibly slightly higher. Power catamarans' twin engines and wider beam consume fuel at similar rates (15–25 liters/hour). However, the spacious platform and stability may reduce psychological stress, offsetting fuel costs with comfort value. Compare specific models' consumption specs before booking.

What's the practical difference between a power catamaran and a sailing catamaran with auxiliary motor?

Sailing catamarans prioritize canvas (full sail plans, efficient sail handling); auxiliary motors are secondary. Power catamarans prioritize efficiency and reliability (smaller sail plans if any, robust motors primary). Power catamarans suit those unconcerned with sailing tradition; sailing catamarans suit those desiring canvas option with motor backup.

Can a bareboat power catamaran be operated by first-time sailors?

Yes, with provisos. Basic boating competency (understanding throttle, trim, anchoring) is essential. Many charter companies require RYA or equivalent certification; some accept experienced powerboat operators without sailing background. Hire a professional skipper for initial briefing (€60–€80) if uncertain. You'll learn systems, then operate independently or request skipper continuation if preferred.

Are power catamarans suitable for elderly passengers or those with mobility limitations?

Excellent choice. Level cabin platforms, spacious living areas, and minimal motion accommodate mobility challenges. Discuss specific needs during booking; charter companies can identify catamarans with accessible cabins, grab rails, and minimal steps.

What's the advantage of a power catamaran over chartering a luxury motor yacht?

Cost, primarily. Power catamarans are 30–50% less expensive than crewed motor yachts. You gain bareboat independence, spacious comfort, and shallow-draft capability. The trade: no professional crew service, you manage navigation and provisioning. Ideal for confident, self-sufficient groups; less ideal for those prioritizing service and pampering.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.
