Welcome to Kavala's Marina Facility

Kavala's modern marina complex represents one of northern Greece's premier yacht facilities, serving as the operational hub for Boat4You's North Aegean charter fleet. Located directly adjacent to the historic waterfront, just steps from the Old Town's cobbled streets and bustling seafood restaurants, the marina provides a seamless transition from shore-based comfort to life at sea. The facility accommodates vessels ranging from 30 to 60 feet, with berthing capacity for 80+ yachts positioned along concrete piers featuring modern utilities and continuous supervision.

The marina's strategic location delivers unparalleled convenience: Kavala International Airport sits 30 kilometres south (45-minute transfer), the city's central market stands three blocks inland, and banking, healthcare, and provisioning services cluster throughout the surrounding district. Modern shore facilities include secure parking, individual cabin access during pre-charter preparation, showers, laundry services, and 24-hour security. Weather monitoring stations continuously track North Aegean conditions, with forecasts updated every six hours and transmitted directly to departing vessels.

Check-in Process & Documentation

Charter parties arrive at the marina office 2–3 hours before departure. Our Boat4You team collects your skipper's sailing certificate, insurance documentation, and passenger contact information. EU/EEA citizens present passports; non-EU visitors confirm visa status and provide documentation. We verify that your sailing qualifications meet the charter vessel's category requirements—bareboat charters require ASA 103, RYA Day Skipper, or equivalent certifications. The team explains emergency procedures, VHF channel assignments, weather routing, and provides detailed charts and passage notes for your intended route.

Each yacht undergoes a comprehensive pre-departure inspection with the skipper: deck equipment, rigging, safety gear, navigation systems, engine systems, and interior facilities. We demonstrate propane system operation, hot water provision, refrigeration controls, and heads systems. Fuel tanks are topped, water tanks are filled, and waste tanks are emptied. Life jackets are fitted to each crew member, and emergency beacon registration is confirmed. You receive duplicate sets of keys, engine and systems documentation, and emergency contact cards. This thorough process typically requires 60–90 minutes and ensures complete familiarity before you cast off.

Berth Allocation & Marina Facilities

Our charter yachts occupy designated berths within the marina's protected inner basin, accessible by single swing bridges that open on demand. Berth numbering follows a logical system: vessels 30–40 feet occupy north piers (shallower draft, easier manoeuvring), while larger yachts berth along southern piers with enhanced facilities. Each berth includes dedicated electrical power (220v/32a), fresh water supply with quick-disconnects, and shore-mounted waste pump-out connections. Marina security patrols continuously, with closed-circuit monitoring at all access points and emergency response systems activated 24 hours daily.

Shore facilities include climate-controlled provisioning storage where bulk orders can be pre-positioned, modern shower blocks, laundry facilities, and secure parking for crew vehicles. The harbormaster's office, staffed continuously, provides weather updates, route guidance, and emergency coordination. A fuel dock sits 50 metres from our charter berths, with diesel available at competitive rates and automatic billing charged to your charter account. Waste disposal stations accept grey water, oil, and solid refuse—essential services supporting responsible cruising. Our Boat4You team maintains priority access to the fuel dock and provisioning facilities, ensuring swift service and minimal delays.

Provisioning Strategy & Kavala's Markets

Kavala's central market district (agora), operational daily 6 am–3 pm (Sundays until noon), sits a 5-minute walk from our marina office. The market's permanent vendors offer Mediterranean staples: sun-ripened tomatoes, eggplants, fresh herbs, local cheeses, and morning-caught fish. Bakeries produce traditional Greek breads—horiatiko (village bread), feta-filled pastries, and spanakopita. Multiple fishmongers compete daily, ensuring quality and fair pricing. A dedicated olive oil merchant stocks bottles from twelve different northern Greek villages, each with distinct flavour profiles.

Our provisioning specialists can pre-arrange market shopping, delivering fresh provisions directly to the yacht during your final pre-departure hours. Alternatively, our team provides detailed market maps, vendor recommendations, and shopping lists in multiple languages. Modern supermarkets surround the waterfront, stocking European brands, dietary specialities, and international products. Wine merchants cluster near the archaeological museum, offering exceptional Greek wines at direct-from-vineyard pricing. Boat4You maintains accounts with preferred suppliers, ensuring discounted pricing and priority service. Many charterers provision heavily at Kavala, stocking 2–3 weeks of supplies before departing for extended island circuits.

Fuel, Water & Utility Services

The marina's dedicated fuel dock supplies ultra-low-sulfur diesel (ULSD) meeting European standards, with automated payment systems ensuring accurate billing. Fuel prices fluctuate seasonally but typically align with Greek national rates. Water quality meets EU drinking standards, supplied continuously at all berths through shore-based high-pressure systems. Fresh water tanks fill at 50–100 litres per minute, enabling complete yacht filling within 20 minutes. Our team demonstrates tank-monitoring systems and explains water conservation during week-long charters (approximately 2–3 litres per person daily supports comfortable cruising).

Electrical power supplies 220v/32a at each berth, sufficient for yacht refrigeration, hot water heating, and navigation system charging during shore stops. Generator fuel typically lasts 15–20 hours of continuous operation (used sparingly during anchorages). Waste management systems pump grey water directly to shore treatment facilities, and heads systems connect to sealed holding tanks emptied by the marina's pump-out service (complimentary for Boat4You charters). Oil disposal systems accommodate engine maintenance refuse. These comprehensive utilities eliminate environmental concerns and simplify extended cruising logistics.

Navigation Systems & Weather Support

Every yacht departs Kavala equipped with modern navigation systems: integrated GPS chartplotters displaying high-resolution bathymetric charts, electronic chart displays, and autopilot systems. VHF radios include digital selective calling (DSC) and emergency beacon registration. Backup paper charts cover all North Aegean routes, detailed anchorages, and emergency procedures. Our team loads your intended route into the chartplotter before departure and provides passage notes highlighting significant hazards, recommended anchorages, and local wind patterns.

Our shore-based weather monitoring service provides daily forecasts via radio transmission and email to your onboard satellite communication system (available on all charters). Meteorologists with decades of North Aegean experience interpret Greek national forecasts, adding localised insights regarding wind patterns around Thassos, current systems near Samothrace, and micro-weather phenomena affecting your specific route. Multiple weather information sources (NOAA, GRIB data, local observations) feed our decision-making process. Should conditions deteriorate unexpectedly, our coordinators advise regarding secure anchorages, alternative routes, or decisions to delay departures. This proactive weather support represents a cornerstone of 's safety commitment.

Yacht Maintenance & Emergency Support

Kavala's marina maintains a network of certified technicians specializing in marine systems: diesel engines, refrigeration, electrical systems, and propane appliances. Should mechanical issues arise during your charter, our shore team coordinates rapid technician response. Common issues—cooling system issues, fuel filter replacement, battery management—are often resolved within hours using spares maintained in the marina's well-stocked parts locker. Major repairs (engine overhauls, structural issues) may require departure to Thessaloniki's larger facilities, but our team arranges transportation and accommodation for your crew.

Emergency support operates 24/7 via satellite communication and VHF radio monitored from our Kavala operations centre. Should you experience medical emergencies, engine failure, or dangerous weather conditions, you have direct radio contact with experienced personnel who coordinate with Greek coast guard authorities, arrange rescue assistance, or provide technical guidance to resolve issues independently. Our team's deep knowledge of North Aegean hazards—particular reef systems, current patterns, seasonal wind phenomena—provides crucial support during unexpected situations. Comprehensive charter insurance covers mechanical failure assistance, medical evacuation, and emergency towing.

Check-out Process & Return Procedures

Upon returning to Kavala, you contact the marina operations centre by radio one hour before arrival, providing an estimated docking time. Our team positions shore personnel to assist with mooring, collecting fenders, and securing lines. Following secured berthing, a comprehensive post-charter inspection documents the yacht's condition, fuel remaining, water tank levels, and any damage or maintenance issues requiring attention. This inspection typically requires 30–45 minutes and includes photographs for insurance purposes when significant damage is noted.

Utility disconnections follow: electrical shore power, fresh water lines, and waste system connections are disengaged and secured. You provide final readings of engine hours, fuel consumption, water usage, and any repairs or maintenance performed. Damage deposits are reconciled—small wear-and-tear items are expected after extended cruising, while significant damage incurs charges covered by your charter deposit and insurance. Documentation is completed, remaining provisions are transferred ashore, and your personal belongings are collected. The entire process—from docking to final departure—typically requires 90 minutes. Our team ensures hassle-free conclusions and provides transparent damage assessments documented with photographs.

Pre-Charter Planning & Coordination

Eight weeks before your charter, your coordinator contacts you regarding vessel preferences, crew experience levels, and intended routes. They explain available yacht options—differences between modern performance boats and classic comfort cruisers, specification details regarding cabin layouts, galley equipment, and sailing characteristics. They provide weather statistics for your intended departure dates, suggest appropriate insurance coverage, and outline provisioning strategies. If you lack sailing experience or prefer not to skipper, they arrange professional crew options—fully qualified skippers, or full crew including cook and deckhand services.

Six weeks prior, detailed pre-charter documentation arrives: vessel specification sheets with photographs, provisioning guides tailored to your preferences, detailed route suggestions with anchorage information, emergency procedures, insurance documentation, and visa/passport requirements. Two weeks before departure, final coordination confirms your flight arrival times, airport transfers, and pre-charter accommodation. Our team arranges rental vehicles if desired, provides detailed driving directions to the marina, and confirms your pre-boarding time. One week pre-departure, final weather analysis informs any route adjustments, and detailed passage notes are prepared specifically for your itinerary. This meticulous pre-charter coordination ensures seamless transitions and eliminates unexpected surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in the charter price?

Charter fees include the yacht, all sailing equipment, safety gear, navigation systems, standard insurance, and berthing/utilities at Kavala Marina. Fuel, provisions, meals, Greek harbour fees, and optional crew services are billed separately or pre-arranged. Our team provides transparent pricing breakdowns.

Can I modify my route after departing Kavala?

Absolutely. Your charter is yours to control. Many charterers modify plans based on weather, personal preferences, or unexpected discoveries. Our shore team supports route modifications and provides updated forecasts and guidance. Alternative anchorages and emergency support remain available regardless of your specific route.

How quickly can you address mechanical issues?

Minor issues (fuel filter, cooling hose, electrical problems) are often resolved within 2–3 hours using parts and technicians maintained in Kavala. Major repairs may require returning to the marina or relocating to Thessaloniki's larger facilities. Our 24/7 support team assesses situations and coordinates appropriate responses.

What happens if I damage the yacht during my charter?

All damage is documented during final inspection with photographs. Minor wear and tear (small scratches, minor cosmetic damage) is expected and not charged. Significant damage incurs repair costs covered by your charter deposit and comprehensive charter insurance. Our team assesses fairly, avoiding disputes through transparent documentation.

Is provisioning service available before departure?

Yes. Our provisioning specialists arrange market shopping during your pre-charter hours, delivering fresh provisions directly to the yacht. Alternatively, we provide detailed market maps and vendor recommendations allowing self-provisioning. Pre-arranged bulk orders (wine, beverages, frozen items) can be pre-positioned onboard.
