Island Destinations: Salamina to Spetses

Sailing Conditions in the Saronic: Gentle and Predictable

Summer Saronic sailing brings variable northerly winds, typically 8–15 knots, with occasional stronger flows when the Meltemi spills over from the northern Aegean. The gulf's geography provides natural wind-shadow zones, allowing crews to explore lee anchorages and protected harbors. Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) feature milder, often lighter winds—perfect for teaching crews sail-handling skills and exploring at a leisurely pace. Winter sailing is possible but limited by reduced fleet availability and occasional stronger Atlantic storm systems passing through the Mediterranean.
