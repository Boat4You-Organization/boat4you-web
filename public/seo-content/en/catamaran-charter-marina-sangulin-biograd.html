Island Routes & Sailing Destinations

The Kornati National Park, a UNESCO-protected archipelago of 140 limestone islands, lies 35 nautical miles south. A typical Kornati expedition from Marina Šangulin spans 4–5 days: depart early morning, reach the outer islands by evening, and spend subsequent days exploring narrow channels, dramatic rocky coastlines, and crystal-clear anchorages. The Kornati's isolation and pristine waters make it a bucket-list destination for catamaran sailors.

Dugi Otok and its eastern anchor, Telašćica Bay, offer a 3–4-hour passage from Marina Šangulin. This protected natural harbor is celebrated for its calm waters and freshwater lake (Mir Salt Lake) sitting just behind the beach. Many catamaran charters moor in Telašćica for an overnight or two, exploring hiking trails and enjoying the island's serene character.

The Biograd Cluster islands—including Pašman and Ugljan to the north, and several small unnamed islets—provide gentler day-sail options. These islands attract fewer visitors than Hvar or Brač, making them ideal for crews seeking quieter anchorages and authentic Dalmatian hospitality. Passage times are short, fuel burn minimal, and provisioning straightforward.

For longer charters, many sailors extend south toward the Šibenik region or northeast toward the Zadar coastline, creating week-long itineraries rich in variety and cultural flavor.

Best Seasons for Catamaran Charters

Spring (April–May) offers warm days, calm mornings, water temperatures of 18–19°C, and minimal crowds. Summer (June–August) brings reliable Maestral winds, perfect sailing conditions, and vibrant social scenes at anchorages—but July and August are warm and busy. Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (22–23°C), gentle winds, manageable crowds, and the extended Adriatic sailing season. Winter sailing is possible but brings shorter days and occasional rough conditions; most charters operate April–October.

Local Food, Culture & Provisioning

Biograd na Moru's culinary tradition centers on fresh fish (particularly sea bream and sea bass), octopus preparations, and pašticada (slow-braised beef in prune sauce). The town market operates daily and supplies vegetables, local cheese, cured meats, and fresh bread. Fish markets near the waterfront offer daily catch—perfect for galley meals aboard your catamaran.

Biograd's beach-town atmosphere, combined with nearby historical sites like the Vrana Lake nature reserve and Telašćica's hiking trails, offers non-sailors entertainment options. Local wines—especially Babić reds from the Primošten region nearby—pair beautifully with Adriatic seafood.

FAQ: Catamaran Charters from Marina Šangulin

What catamaran size suits eight adults?

A 38–42-foot catamaran with three to four cabins comfortably accommodates eight people. Marina Šangulin's fleet includes several vessels in this range, all equipped with modern layouts and ample storage.

Can I berth at Marina Šangulin during my charter?

Yes. reserves mooring berths at Marina Šangulin for charter clients. Daily mooring fees (typically €1.40–€2.00 per meter) apply in addition to your charter rate, though many charters spend only the arrival/departure nights at the marina before sailing to island anchorages.

How long does it take to reach the Kornati from Marina Šangulin?

Under sail with favorable wind, 4–5 hours. Under engine or with light wind, 6–7 hours. Most charters depart early morning and reach the outer Kornati islands by evening, perfect for an overnight mooring.

Do I need a sailing certificate to bareboat a catamaran?

Yes. Most bareboat catamaran charters require an International Certificate of Competence (ICC) or equivalent national credential. reviews your credentials during booking; if you lack formal certification, a professional skipper can be arranged. See how we work for more details.

Are fuel surcharges included in the weekly rate?

Typically not. Weekly rates cover the vessel and basic mooring; fuel consumed during your charter, Kornati park permits, and guest mooring fees at other harbors are additional.
