Sailing Conditions: Meltemi and Seasonal Winds

Summer sailing in the Cyclades brings the famous Meltemi wind—typically 15–25 knots from the north in July and August. Sailing yachts thrive in these conditions, heeling efficiently and running square courses downwind toward the southern islands. Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer gentler northerlies and variable wind patterns, perfect for crews prioritizing scenic sailing over thrilling conditions. A sailing yacht's dependable auxiliary engine ensures progress even during light-wind periods, while sails remain the primary propulsion during optimal conditions.
