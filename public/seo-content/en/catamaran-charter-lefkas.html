The Ionian's geography is forgiving. Islands are close enough that a mechanical issue isn't catastrophic—you can typically motor to shelter. Anchorages are protected by natural topography; sudden squalls are rare. This makes the Ionian ideal for flotilla sailing, training charters, and guests regaining confidence after years ashore. Lefkas Access & Practicalities

Lefkas is uniquely accessible for a Greek island: a floating causeway (unique in Greece) connects it directly to the mainland via the E55/A5 motorway. Summer Sailing Conditions

June through September is prime season. Maistro, the legendary northwestern thermal breeze, builds to 15 knots by midday, then dies gracefully at sunset—perfect for morning manoeuvres and evening harbour approaches. Seas remain flat except near open straits. Water temperature peaks at 26°C in August. Rainfall is nearly nonexistent. This is Europe's most forgiving sailing climate, making Lefkas the preferred destination for flotillas, training charters, and guests regaining sea confidence. July and August see peak sunshine; May and September offer slightly softer breezes and fewer crowds.
