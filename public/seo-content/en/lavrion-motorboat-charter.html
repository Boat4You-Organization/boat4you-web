Why Choose a Motorboat from Lavrion?

Motorboats eliminate weather dependency. Calm, glassy mornings that would frustrate sailors are perfect motoring conditions for motorboat crews. No specialized sailing knowledge is required—boat handling skills are minimal if you've experience with cars or basic navigation. Family groups benefit enormously: children can nap below while parents relax topside; you dock at restaurants and beaches without anxiety about shifting wind directions; fuel consumption is predictable and economical.

Lavrion's location, 55 kilometers south of Athens and 45 minutes from the airport, makes it ideal for motorboat charters. Quick transfers mean you maximize time on water. Reliable fuel supplies and well-staffed marinas ensure your motorboat adventure is hassle-free.

Coastal Cruising and Exploration

A week-long motorboat charter from Lavrion might proceed southwest toward Hydra and Spetses (classic Saronic Gulf destinations), returning via Aegina. Fuel economy matters on extended chases. Modern motorboats consume 1–2 liters per nautical mile at cruising speed, meaning a 200-nautical-mile week costs roughly 200–400 euros in fuel—reasonable for the flexibility gained.

Best Seasons and Conditions

Motorboats work year-round. Summer (June–September) offers warm weather, calm mornings, and busy harbors. Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) bring cooler days, variable winds—irrelevant to motorboats—and fewer crowds. Winter cruising is possible but requires coastal routes and avoidance of exposed passages. Most charters cluster in April–October.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a boat license to charter a motorboat?

Regulations vary by nationality and boat size. EU citizens may charter smaller boats (under 12 meters) with basic competency proof; larger boats typically require a national powerboat license. Boat4You will clarify requirements based on your details; we can arrange professional crew if documentation is lacking.

Are motorboats expensive to operate?

Fuel is the primary cost. A typical motorboat burns 5–10 liters per hour, translating to €15–30 per hour depending on speed. A leisurely week exploring nearby islands might consume €200–400 in fuel—less than a week in a taverna-heavy yacht charter. Boat4You provides transparent fuel estimates upfront.

What's the difference between motorboats and speedboats in your fleet?

Speedboats (under 28 feet) suit day trips and adrenaline-seekers; they're quick, nimble, and exciting. Cabin cruisers (30+ feet) offer sleeping quarters, heads, and galleys for multi-day cruising comfort. will match you to the right hull based on your itinerary and group composition.

Can I charter a motorboat for just a day?

Yes! Many partners in Lavrion offer day-hire options. A Cape Sounion day trip or beach exploration is perfectly feasible. Contact our team with your dates and preferred destination.

What to Pack for a Week Aboard

Soft bags only (hard suitcases will not fit in most cabins). Layered clothing — mornings and evenings cool down, midday sun is intense. Non-marking deck shoes or quality flip-flops. Reef-safe sunscreen, polarised sunglasses, a wide-brim hat, and a waterproof phone pouch. A personal snorkel and mask if you dislike shared gear. Any prescription medication, clearly labelled, in its original packaging.

Your skipper will provide a full safety briefing before departure — life-jacket locations, EPIRB, flares, fire extinguishers, engine kill-switch, man-overboard procedure, and VHF usage. Listen carefully, ask questions, and make sure every adult aboard knows the basics. Good preparation is the quiet foundation of every relaxed charter.

What deposit is required at booking?

Most operators request 30–50% at contract signing, with the balance due 4–6 weeks before departure. Exact terms appear on your quotation before you commit.

Can I move my charter to a different week if I need to?

Usually yes, subject to fleet availability and any operator handling fee. Request the change as early as possible; mid-season swaps are harder than off-season.

Is there a standard security deposit for damages?

Yes. Most operators block a refundable deposit on your credit card at check-in, typically €2,000–€5,000 depending on vessel. A damage-waiver upgrade reduces or eliminates this hold — ask your consultant for the specific options on your chosen boat.
