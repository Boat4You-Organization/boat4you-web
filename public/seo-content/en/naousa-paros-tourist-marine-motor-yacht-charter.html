Practicalities: Licensing and Provisioning

Operating a bareboat motor yacht in Greece requires specific licensing. Typically, at least one person on board must hold a valid national or international boating license, often accompanied by a second competent crew member if the license is for inland waters. It's essential to check with the Greek port authorities and your charter provider well in advance to ensure your credentials are fully compliant. If you don't meet these requirements, a professional skipper is a straightforward and highly recommended alternative, ensuring your journey is safe and stress-free.

Provisioning for your charter from Naousa is a delightful task. The town boasts several well-stocked supermarkets where you can find all your essentials, from fresh produce to local wines. For a more authentic experience, visit the small, independent greengrocers and bakeries for local specialties. Don't forget to visit the fish market in the morning for the freshest catch, perfect for an onboard barbecue. Naousa's charm extends to its culinary offerings, so consider enjoying your evenings in one of the many superb restaurants; perhaps an ouzo and some grilled octopus by the old Venetian harbour before heading back to your yacht.

Seasonal Insights for Paros Charters

The charter season in Paros generally runs from May through October, each period offering a distinct flavor. May and June, the shoulder season, bring warm, pleasant weather, fewer crowds, and typically gentler winds. The sea temperatures are perfect for swimming, and you'll find better availability and often more attractive pricing on motor yacht charters. This period is ideal for those seeking a more tranquil and authentic Cycladic experience, with lush landscapes still vibrant from spring.

July and August mark the peak season, characterized by hot, sunny days, lively island atmospheres, and the stronger Meltemi winds. While these winds can be robust, a motor yacht handles them with greater ease than a sailing boat, allowing you to maintain your itinerary. However, booking well in advance is crucial during these months due to high demand. September and October offer another fantastic shoulder season, with the sea still warm from summer, brilliant clear days, and a noticeable calm returning to the islands. Prices typically begin to soften, making it an excellent time to enjoy the beauty of Paros without the intense summer crowds. No matter when you choose to visit, Paros provides remarkable conditions for exploring by motor yacht.

Recommended Itineraries from Naousa

Day Trip: Around Paros and Antiparos

Start your day from Naousa Tourist Marine, heading southwest towards Kolymbithres Beach, known for its moon-like rock formations and clear, shallow waters – perfect for an initial swim. Continue south along the coast to explore the stunning beaches of Santa Maria. From there, make the short hop across to Antiparos. Spend the afternoon discovering its famous sea caves and anchoring in the sparkling turquoise waters around Despotiko or the Panteronissia islets. Lunch can be enjoyed on board or at a charming taverna in Antiparos town. Return to Naousa by late afternoon, just in time for sunset drinks.

3-Day Adventure: Paros, Antiparos & Naxos

Day 1: Naousa to Antiparos. Depart Naousa, enjoying a leisurely cruise to the calmer waters between Paros and Antiparos. Explore the hidden coves and sea caves of Antiparos, stopping for multiple swims. Anchor near Soros Beach for a relaxed lunch, then motor into Antiparos town for an evening stroll and dinner ashore. Overnight at anchor or a small berth if available.

Day 2: Antiparos to Naxos. After a morning swim, set off for Naxos, roughly an 8-10 nautical mile journey. Arrive at Naxos Chora and spend the day exploring the Venetian castle, the Portara (ancient temple gate), and the town's charming alleyways. Enjoy a hearty Naxian meal, perhaps some delicious local cheeses and fresh fish. Overnight in Naxos Marina or a nearby secluded bay.

Day 3: Naxos to Naousa (via Paros East Coast). Enjoy a final Naxian breakfast before setting course back to Paros. On the return journey, explore the lesser-known east coast of Paros, finding secluded beaches like Kalogeros or Molos for a final swim. Make your way back to Naousa Tourist Marine in the late afternoon, concluding your island-hopping adventure.
