Kavala Marina: Premier North Aegean Facility

Kavala Marina represents a transformative investment in northern Greek maritime infrastructure, opening in 2020 as the region's most technologically advanced superyacht facility. Built to international five-star standards, the marina accommodates vessels up to 80 metres (260 feet), with berths engineered for the largest superyachts visiting Greek waters. The 450-metre concrete pier structure features individual vessel utilities: shore power (380v/63a for large yachts), pressurised fresh water systems (100+ litres per minute capacity), waste management connections, and fibre-optic connectivity supporting satellite communication and onboard entertainment systems.

Unlike older ports relying on traditional harbours or improvised structures, Kavala Marina features modern floating pontoons adjusting automatically to seasonal tide variations and seismic movement. The facility's breakwater system provides absolute protection from the north, precisely engineered to dissipate swell energy before it reaches berthed vessels. Individually controlled swing-gates at the marina entrance prevent unauthorised access while permitting seamless vessel movements during busy seasons. This premium infrastructure distinguishes Kavala Marina from the old port facility—a comparison that starkly illustrates recent maritime development in Greece's North Aegean region.

Berth Allocation & Superyacht Capacity

Kavala Marina operates 95 berths distributed across three main pier sections, with vessels sized strategically: small craft (under 35 feet) occupy the eastern pier, medium yachts (35–50 feet) berth along the central pier, and superyachts exceeding 50 metres occupy designated western berths featuring enhanced utilities and dedicated operational space. Boat4You controls 18 premium berths throughout the facility, selected for optimal location, security, and operational convenience. These berths feature shadowing from the northern breakwater (critical for sun protection in summer months), proximity to the harbormaster's office (essential for rapid communication), and maximum distance from fishing vessel anchorages (reducing noise and congestion).

Each berth includes dedicated infrastructure: individual electrical pedestals rated for yachts' specific power requirements (32 amp standard, 63 amp available for larger vessels), separate water supply with quick-disconnect couplings, dedicated waste discharge points, and permanently installed mooring hardware. A sophisticated marina management system tracks occupancy, utility consumption, and revenue automatically, with invoicing completed electronically upon check-out. Our Boat4You berths receive priority allocation for visiting crew members and guest vessels, ensuring convenient arrangements for extended family visits or multi-yacht flotillas.

Comparison to the Old Port

Kavala's historic port—the old waterfront facility predating the modern marina—represents a distinctly different experience. The old port features traditional concrete piers built in the 1970s, now aging and providing minimal modern utilities. Berths lack individual electrical shore power, fresh water supplies must be collected manually from scattered dock spigots, and waste management relies on informal arrangements with port authorities. The facility bustles with working fishing boats, commercial cargo operations, and ferries to Turkish destinations, creating noise, congestion, and limited privacy. Oil residue and fishing debris occasionally foul the water. However, the old port maintains authentic character—locals gather at waterfront tavernas, fishermen repair nets by hand, and maritime traditions persist unchanged for decades.

Our Boat4You fleet exclusively uses Kavala Marina's modern facility, providing the contrast: pristine berths, comprehensive utilities, professional management, and premium services. The trade-off is explicit—Kavala Marina sacrifices the old port's authentic roughness for modern convenience and superyacht standards. For charterers prioritising comfort, contemporary facilities, and hassle-free logistics, Kavala Marina represents the optimal choice. For travellers seeking deeper engagement with working maritime culture, the old port offers alternative character—though Boat4You recommends this experience via shoreside exploration rather than residential berthing.
