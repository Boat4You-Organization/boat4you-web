Marina Facilities & Arrival Logistics

Split West Coast operates a simplified mooring system ideal for charter vessels. Professional dock hands assist with arrival and departure. Island Routes & Sailing Distances

Brač island lies 12 nautical miles south. Bol's Golden Horn beach appears in under two hours. Supetar, on Brač's northern coast, offers a sheltered mooring and excellent fish taverns. The island's stone villages and vineyard-draped slopes reward afternoon exploration after mooring.

Hvar, 28 nautical miles southwest, is the Adriatic's sunniest anchorage. Your catamaran enters the spacious Hvar Town harbor, where laundry and fuel services are available. Lavender fields, medieval fortifications, and waterfront restaurants occupy landlubber days. Summer anchorages can be crowded; shoulder-season charters offer solitude.

Vis, the outermost island (38 nautical miles), brings wild beauty: Vis Town harbor, the hidden lagoon at Stiniva, and Komiža's natural shelter. Traditional fishing villages and local wines define the experience. Multi-day charters often base themselves here for full-day explorations.

Šolta and the Drvenik islands offer quieter alternatives. Shallow sandy anchorages and pine-backed coves characterize these less-touristed destinations, ideal for crews prioritizing solitude over nightlife.

Provisioning & Local Food Culture

Split's culinary soul revolves around pašticada (slow-braised beef with prunes), soparnik (chard and cheese pie), and daily fish catches. The Green Market (Zelena pijaca) and Peškarija fish market sit within walking distance of your catamaran. Stocking your galley with Dalmatian staples—local cheese, dried figs, olives, fresh vegetables—transforms meals aboard into cultural immersion.

Local wines include Plavac Mali (full-bodied red) and Vugava (crisp white), both grown within sight of the West Coast mooring. Family-run restaurants along the Riva welcome sailors; many offer group discounts and accept delivery of provisions brought aboard before evening departure.

Seasons & Weather Patterns

Spring (April–May) brings stabilizing winds and water temperatures around 18°C. June–August offers reliable sailing conditions but intense summer heat and crowded anchorages. September–October combines warm water (22°C), gentler winds, and manageable crowds—considered ideal by many charter skippers. Winter sailing is possible but seasonal charters conclude by late November.

The northern Bura wind (winter) and southern Jugo (autumn/spring) shape daily sailing decisions.'s weather briefing service and your catamaran's communication systems keep you informed of 24-hour wind forecasts.
