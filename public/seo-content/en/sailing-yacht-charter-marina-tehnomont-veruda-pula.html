Sailing Seasons & Weather Patterns

April–May (Spring): Water 16–18°C, air 18–22°C. Breezes begin light (8–12 knots); afternoon Maestral strengthens by late May. Calm mornings suit confidence-building. Modest crowds, excellent value.

June (Early Summer): Water 19–20°C, consistent Maestral 12–16 knots. Perfect sailing conditions, manageable crowds, premium pricing begins.

July–August (Peak): Water 23–25°C, Maestral 14–18 knots (occasional gusts to 22+). Hottest, most crowded, highest prices. Reliable wind compensates. Night sails remain possible due to long daylight.

September–October (Autumn): Water 21–23°C, Maestral gradually easing. Excellent balance: warm water, manageable winds, minimal crowds. Many experienced sailors prefer autumn.

November–March (Winter): Bura wind dominates (cold, northeasterly, 15–30+ knots). Water 10–14°C. Short daylight, occasional rough seas. Commercial charters pause; advanced crews only. Exceptional value.

FAQ: Sailing Yachts from Marina Tehnomont Veruda

What sailing certifications do I need for a bareboat sailing yacht?

Most operators require International Certificate of Competence (ICC) or equivalent—RYA Day Skipper, ASA 104, or national equivalent. reviews credentials and arranges skippers for crews lacking formal certification.

Is 65 yachts enough to guarantee my preferred vessel?

The fleet's scale ensures choices, but peak season (July–August) books far ahead. Early reservation (10–12 weeks) secures preferred size and layout. Off-season and shoulder months offer more flexibility and last-minute availability.

Can I do a one-way charter from Pula?

Marina Tehnomont Veruda is the charter base for the charter fleet. One-way transits are negotiated case-by-case for experienced crews undertaking passage to Zadar or other ports. Additional positioning and fuel charges apply.

Do sailing yachts come with crew?

Bareboat charters are uncrewed; you sail independently. Crewed options (skipper + hostess/chef) are available at €400–€700 daily for groups seeking hands-off experience or advanced training.

What happens in high winds?

Maestral summer winds (14–18 knots) are manageable for moderate crews. Storm conditions (>25 knots) are rare May–October but can occur. Bura winter winds can exceed 30 knots; experienced crews only.
