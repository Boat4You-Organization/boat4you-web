The Gulet Heritage & Fleet Character

Gulets represent Turkish and Croatian maritime craftsmanship—hand-built wooden sailing yachts evolved from traditional trading and fishing vessels. Boat4You's Harbour fleet ranges 45–55 feet, each gulet bearing distinct character: some feature traditional carved woodwork and classic rigging; others blend classic aesthetics with contemporary propulsion and navigation systems. The uniformity disappears with close inspection; crews often describe their gulet as having "personality"—wood patinas, cabin quirks, and engine temperaments that become endearing during week-long voyages.

Each gulet accommodates 8–14 passengers across 5–7 cabins. Below-deck interiors marry artisanal varnished-wood design with modern comfort: updated galleys, contemporary heads with hot water, and sometimes air-conditioning. Above-deck, generous lounging areas, canvas awnings, and traditional rigging define the experience. A professional crew—captain, mate, and chef—operates the vessel and cares for guests throughout the voyage.

Split Harbour: The Authentic Gulet Berth

Your crew navigates these working-port procedures with practiced ease, allowing guests to focus on cultural immersion rather than logistics.

Gulet Itineraries & Sailing Passages

From Split Harbour, a typical 7-day gulet voyage unfolds as follows: Day one, morning departure to Brač (12 nm), afternoon anchoring off a village beach and swimming; evening meal on-deck. Day two, passage to Hvar (16 nm additional), afternoon mooring in Hvar Town or a quieter village, evening exploration of fortifications or vineyard dinners. Day three advances to Vis (10 nm), offering full-day island immersion—Vis Town's character, hidden Green Cave lagoon, and Komiža's bohemian charm.

Return passages incorporate Šolta's quiet anchorages, the Drvenik islands, and final-night mooring at a protected island cove before returning to Split Harbour. Weather conditions, crew recommendations, and guest preferences adapt daily itineraries. A week-long charter covers similar geographic range, yet the pace—unhurried, social, immersive—defines the experience utterly differently from bareboat or motor-yacht speed-focused sailing.

Are Split Harbour gulets suitable for families with young children?

Absolutely. Gulets excel for families. Crew experience with children, shallow-anchoring locations for swimming, and all-inclusive services handling logistics create stress-free family voyages. Confirm cabin configurations and crew child-care experience when booking.

Your captain prioritizes safety and comfort. If rough conditions emerge, the gulet moors in a protected harbour, transforming the day into cultural exploration ashore—markets, museums, fortress hikes, or extended tavern lunches. Motor propulsion ensures safe passage even in choppy conditions.

Is Wi-Fi connectivity available aboard?

Most Split Harbour gulets carry cellular or satellite connectivity. Speed and reliability vary by location and provider. Many guests embrace the digital respite gulet sailing offers; connectivity is bonus rather than expectation.
