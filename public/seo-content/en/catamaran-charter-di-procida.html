These large passenger ferries (capacity 400–800 passengers each) occupy dedicated berths at the harbor's northeastern sector, requiring careful navigation corridor management for catamaran maneuvering. Weather Patterns and Seasonal Sailing Characteristics

Marina di Procida-based catamarans encounter consistent seasonal wind patterns supporting reliable passage planning. Spring conditions (April–May) feature northwesterly winds developing reliably from 10:00–18:00, with 8–16 knot intensities and occasional 22-knot gusts preceding Atlantic frontal passages. Summer thermal development (June–August) produces lighter afternoon breezes (8–12 knots), suitable for leisurely paced family-oriented cruising. Autumn transitions (September–November) introduce variable wind direction, with occasional southwesterly flows generating 15–25 knot passages and more significant sea-state development.

Winter operations (December–February) remain feasible between storm systems, though weather deteriorates substantially. Occasional Mediterranean gales (35+ knots, 2–4 day duration) occur monthly, necessitating advanced weather monitoring and conservative passage planning. Sea temperature variation spans 13°C (February) to 24–25°C (August), supporting extended swimming during summer months while requiring wetsuits for winter water activities. Rainfall concentrates November–December (130–150mm monthly), with virtually zero precipitation June–August, enabling extended anchorage periods and beach-based activities without weather disruption concerns.
