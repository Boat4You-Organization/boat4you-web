Summer Sailing Conditions: Meltemi Winds & Sheltered Waters

June through September delivers consistent Meltemi winds from the north (15-25 kts), providing exhilarating sailing while remaining manageable for intermediate sailors. The Sporades archipelago's geography partially shelters these waters compared to exposed southern Aegean routes. Afternoon thermal effects accelerate winds during peak hours, moderating toward evening.

Water temperatures peak at 26–28°C by August, inviting frequent swims at anchorages. Extended daylight (sunset after 8pm in midsummer) permits leisurely sailing schedules, late-afternoon arrivals, and evening exploration of each new anchorage. Spring (May) and autumn (September-October) offer lighter winds, fewer crowds, and equally rewarding sailing. Wind patterns favor starboard tacking toward Skopelos and Alonissos, creating exhilarating sailing geometry.
