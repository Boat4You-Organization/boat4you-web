Marina Operations and Deep-Water Facility Standards

Tourlos Marina operates 24/7 with professional staff fluent in English. Deep-water berths accommodate 35–60 foot yachts securely. Fuel, water, and shore power are delivered boat-side. The harbor master's office provides weather briefings, passage information, and logistical support. Security patrols and CCTV monitoring ensure overnight safety.

Chandlery and repair facilities are adjacent: sail makers, engine specialists, rigging experts, and electronics technicians are available. Same-day repairs are common. Boat4You coordinates all marina operations, allowing crews to focus on sailing.

Sailing Performance and Monohull Advantages

Monohulls offer superior sailing performance: excellent pointing ability (sailing high into the wind), light air responsiveness, and heavy air capability. Twin-keel designs allow shallow-draft exploration of protected bays; fin-keeled yachts excel in open water passages. All Boat4You sailing yachts feature modern rigging, self-tailing winches, and responsive helms.

The sailing experience—reading wind shifts, adjusting sails, and managing passage timing—remains central to monohull charters. This authenticity appeals to sailors valuing traditional seamanship over motor comfort.

Meltemi Wind Sailing and Technical Routes

The meltemi (May–September) brings Force 3–5 northerly winds—perfect for sailing enthusiasts. Close-hauled sailing to windward, tactical tacking, and crew coordination are rewarding. Passages from Tourlos involve 20–40 nautical mile legs: Tourlos to Tinos (25 nm), Tinos to Andros (20 nm), Andros to Paros (25 nm). Each passage teaches something about sailing, weather, and seamanship.

Light-wind sailing (April, October, early May) is possible but less reliable. Winter sailing (November–March) challenges experienced crews with unpredictable storms and short daylight.

Seasonal Conditions and Booking Timeline

May through September is prime sailing season. June and September offer reliable winds (Force 3–4), warm weather (25–28°C), and fewer crowds than July–August. July–August sees strongest winds (Force 4–5) and maximum boat traffic. Advanced booking (8–12 weeks) is essential for peak season.

April and October shoulder season are quieter with milder winds. Winter (November–March) is for hardy sailors embracing rough conditions and storm sailing.
