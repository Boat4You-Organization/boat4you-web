Stretching from the Bay of Naples southward to the rugged Cilento coast, Campania encompasses some 150 kilometers of dramatic coastline dotted with volcanic islands, hidden coves, and charming fishing villages. Whether you're seeking the glamour of Capri, the tranquility of the Cilento peninsulas, or the culinary excitement of the Amalfi Coast, Boat4You yacht charters deliver the perfect vessel and route for your Mediterranean dream.

This region attracts sailors and boaters from around the globe because of its unique combination of manageable sailing distances, reliable summer winds, world-class marinas, and extraordinary cultural and gastronomic experiences. The sailing season runs from late March through October, with peak conditions and warmest temperatures in June through September. The region's sheltered bays and numerous anchorages provide safe havens during wind transitions, while longer crossing opportunities satisfy adventurous crews seeking more challenging passages.

Campania's Premier Sailing Ports and Marinas

Naples (Porto di Napoli) serves as the primary gateway and home port for many of Boat4You's most popular charter fleets. Located on the northern edge of the bay, Naples offers modern docking facilities, provisioning services, and direct access to routes heading south toward Capri, Ischia, and Procida. The city itself, home to over 900,000 residents, combines historic treasures—including the National Archaeological Museum and the stunning Castel dell'Ovo—with vibrant street culture and authentic Neapolitan cuisine that charter guests absolutely must experience.

Marina di Stabia, positioned along the Sorrento Peninsula's western shore approximately 25 kilometers south of Naples, provides an excellent base for exploring the Amalfi Coast and the islands of Capri, Ischia, and Procida. This full-service marina features 400+ berths, refueling facilities, chandleries, and easy provisioning. From Marina di Stabia, the Amalfi Coast's world-famous towns—Positano, Praiano, and Amalfi itself—lie just 15-20 nautical miles to the southeast, making it ideal for charters focusing on this iconic sailing region.

Salerno's Marina d'Arechi, located on Campania's southern coast approximately 55 kilometers from Naples, serves as the gateway to the lesser-known but equally magnificent Cilento National Park and its pristine villages. This modern 520-berth facility includes sophisticated cruising infrastructure and direct access to world-class diving sites, protected coves, and authentic fishing communities that remain wonderfully untouched by mass tourism. Salerno itself is the ancient capital of the Amalfi Coast and offers rich medieval architecture and superb local seafood markets.

Agropoli and Castellammare di Stabia round out Campania's significant yacht charter bases, each offering strategic positioning for specific itineraries. Agropoli provides southern access to Cilento's protected marine reserve, while Castellammare—located on the Bay of Naples' western shore—accommodates vessels focused on Capri, Ischia, and Procida island rotations combined with cultural excursions to the archaeological sites of Pompeii and Herculaneum.

Iconic Amalfi Coast and Island Routes

The classic 7-day Amalfi circumnavigation departure from Marina di Stabia or Salerno takes guests on an unforgettable journey through Campania's most celebrated destinations. Day one typically involves short transit southeast to Positano (approximately 18 nm), where dramatic cliffside villages tumble toward turquoise waters. Days two and three navigate the Amalfi Coast proper, calling at Praiano, Amalfi town, and Ravello (accessible by tender), featuring colorful buildings, world-famous limoncello production, and excellent fresh seafood restaurants perched above the waterline. Day four usually involves a crossing south to Capri (approximately 28 nm from Amalfi), where guests explore the legendary Blue Grotto, Faraglioni rock formations, and enjoy evening aperitivos in the charming Capri town piazza.

Days five through seven typically navigate the return leg via Ischia and Procida islands, allowing charter clients to experience the thermal hot springs around Ischia's Maronti Beach, sample local wines, and discover the pastel-colored village houses of Procida before returning to the home port. This route balances famous landmarks with hidden anchorages, achievable by all skill levels, and provides numerous opportunities for swimming, snorkeling, and cultural immersion in authentic southern Italian communities.

Alternatively, the extended 10-14 day Cilento expedition from Salerno's Marina d'Arechi ventures deep into protected marine reserves, passing through dramatic limestone cliffs, exploring the medieval village of Praiano del Mare accessible only by sea, and discovering secluded beach anchorages where few tourists venture. This itinerary suits experienced sailors seeking solitude, kayaking and diving adventures, and authentic encounters with fishing communities whose ancestors settled these coves centuries ago.

Winds, Weather, and Seasonal Sailing Conditions

Campania's summer wind patterns favor sailors and motor yacht operators alike, with thermal north-westerly sea breezes averaging 10-15 knots during afternoon hours, typically strengthening in July and August as land-mass heating intensifies. These consistent northwesterlies provide ideal conditions for day sailing between islands and along the coast, with early morning calm periods perfect for anchoring and swimming. captains and crew exploit these patterns to optimize routing, often departing early morning, reaching anchorages by early afternoon, and enjoying evenings ashore in waterfront villages.

The Libeccio wind, originating from the southwest, occurs less frequently but brings moderate to strong conditions (15-25 knots) and can create larger seas, particularly on the Cilento coast's western exposures. The Sirocco, arriving from the south and southeast, typically occurs during shoulder seasons (April-May, September-October) and can bring warm, dusty conditions but generally remains manageable for charters with proper planning. Spring and fall offer more variable wind patterns and occasional rain, but fewer tourists, warmer water temperatures than northern Mediterranean venues, and spectacular light for photographers.

Water temperatures range from 17°C (63°F) in March to 26°C (79°F) in August, remaining comfortable for swimming from June through September without wetsuits. The region averages 300+ days of annual sunshine, and even winter months provide occasional warm, clear days perfect for off-season adventures. Cruisers should anticipate afternoon sea breezes even during settled weather periods and plan anchorages with adequate holding and swing room, as many Campania locations feature rocky or sparse seabed.

Gastronomic Excellence and Cultural Immersion

Campania stands as Italy's culinary crown jewel, and yacht charters provide unprecedented access to markets, restaurants, and food experiences impossible from land-based tourism. Naples' famous street food culture—including pizza, sfogliatelle pastries, and mozzarella—should be experienced directly in the city's authentic neighborhoods, not sanitized tourist zones. The Amalfi Coast showcases fresh seafood pasta, hand-rolled ravioli filled with local herbs and ricotta, and fresh fig desserts that local grocers and harborfront trattorias prepare daily using ingredients harvested hours earlier.

Ischia and Procida each maintain distinctive culinary traditions centered on locally-caught fish, heirloom vegetable varieties, and production of wines like Greco di Ischia and Lacryma Christi that have been produced for centuries. Cilento villages offer authentic agriturismo experiences—farm-based restaurants serving multi-course meals featuring wild greens, locally-raised meats, and house-made preserves—often prepared by multi-generational family operations welcoming visitors warmly. crews regularly provision directly from harbor markets, selecting peak-season produce and fresh fish directly from fishermen's catch, elevating onboard dining to memorable gastronomic experiences.
