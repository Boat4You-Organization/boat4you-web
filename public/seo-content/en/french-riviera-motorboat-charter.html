Motorboat Charter French Riviera

A motorboat is a swift, unpretentious vessel—typically 20–30 feet, open or partially enclosed cabin—designed for day cruising, quick commutes, and families seeking water access without overnight complexity. Motorboats prioritise speed (20–35 knots) and simplicity over comfort; they are ideal for exploring Îles de Lérins in an afternoon, dashing between Cannes and Saint-Tropez, or testing Mediterranean waters before committing to week-long yacht charters. They are non-residential: overnight accommodation is marina-side hotels or beachfront resorts, not onboard. Motorboat charters are affordable (€600–€1,500 daily), require minimal licensing in many jurisdictions, and suit action-oriented groups over contemplative sailors.

Why Motorboats Appeal on the French Riviera

Speed and accessibility. Whereas a sailing yacht meanders 6–8 knots, a motorboat cruises 12–18 knots or roars 25–35 knots depending on model and power. The 20nm from Cannes to Villefranche-sur-Mer takes 2.5 hours under sail; a motorboat covers it in 1.5 hours (cruise) or 45 minutes (full throttle). Families with limited vacation time and itinerary ambitions choose motorboats.

Affordability is compelling. A 25-foot motorboat rents €800–€1,200 daily (€4,000–€6,000 weekly); equivalent sailing yachts run €2,000–€4,000 daily. Licensing is relaxed in many EU jurisdictions for boats under 7 metres; some regions require only a basic boating safety course, not formal Day Skipper. Provisioning is absent (no overnight galley); fuel and refreshments are the sole expense.

Versatility wins. Motorboats anchor in shallower waters than yachts (draft 0.6–0.9m), approach rock formations and cliff faces safely, and beach-land easily for beach club lunches. Tender operations become unnecessary; you pilot directly to La Croisette in Cannes or Citadelle in Saint-Tropez.

Signature Motorboat Cruises

Îles de Lérins Day Blast: Embark Cannes morning. Motor to Sainte-Marguerite (11nm, 45 minutes), explore fortress and swimming coves (3 hours ashore). Return Cannes afternoon (11nm, 45 minutes). Cost: €900–€1,200. Simple, efficient, unforgettable.

Saint-Tropez Dash: Start Antibes or Saint-Maxime, motor 25nm southwest to Saint-Tropez (90 minutes cruise speed). Moor Baie de Saint-Tropez. Explore Citadelle, Quai de l'Amirauté seafood lunch, return evening (90 minutes). One-day adventure, zero overnight logistics.

Villefranche Romantic Escape: Nice embark. Motor east 20nm to Villefranche-sur-Mer (90 minutes). Anchor, swim, tender to village restaurants. Return Nice evening (90 minutes). Intimate without isolation; hotel accommodation awaits both nights.

Corniche Maritime (Coastal Ribbon): Multi-day motorboat cruise: Antibes→Cannes (11nm)→Îles de Lérins→Saint-Tropez (25nm from Cannes)→Îles d'Hyères/Porquerolles (40nm). Base hotels nightly; motorboat ferries morning-to-evening. No overnight yacht logistics; leisurely days, quick transits.

Italian Border Excursion: Nice base. Motor 50nm northeast to Portofino, Italy. Explore fishing village, Michelin-starred harborfront dining, return Nice evening (50nm, 3.5 hours at cruise). Feasible day-trip for experienced motorboat charterers.

Technical Specifications

Motorboats range 20–35 feet; charter fleets typically deploy 24–28 foot cabin cruisers. Single or twin outboard/inboard engines (150–300hp total) cruise at 10–18 knots, consuming 20–40 litres/hour. Fuel tanks hold 150–300 litres; range between refuelling is 60–100 nautical miles—ample for Riviera day-cruising but demands planning for longer passages.

Cabins are minimal: porta-potty (not full marine head), galley cupboard, sleeping berths for 2–4. No shower; some dockside facilities are available at marinas. Equipment: GPS/chartplotter, VHF radio, depth sounder, basic anchor tackle. Navigation is straightforward day-sailing; no night passages expected.

Acceleration from idle to full throttle is swift; helm response is immediate. For experienced boaters, motorboats are intuitive; for novices, a half-day orientation with rental staff or skipper is advised.

Pricing & Rental Terms

Motorboats rent €600–€1,200 daily (€800–€1,500 peak season July–August). Weekly discounts apply: seven days at 50–60% per-day rate (€3,500–€6,000 weekly). Fuel is typically guest responsibility; larger operators include fuel in price. Damage waivers (€500–€1,500 deposit waiver) are standard; insurance is included.

Minimum age is 18–21; valid driver's licence required (not necessarily boating licence, depending on jurisdiction and vessel size). Boat4You clarifies licensing requirements by region when booking.

Best Season & Navigation Tips

Season: April–October. Spring (April–May): 16–18°C water, 18°C air, calm seas, uncrowded. Summer (June–August): warm and busy; July–August peaks in heat and tourism. Autumn (September–October): ideal—20°C water, settling crowds, stable weather. November–March: off-season for motorboat charters; winter weather is unpredictable.

Wind Handling: Motorboats are wind-sensitive. Summer Mistral (15–25 knots from north) can render them uncomfortable; eastbound routes (Nice to Monaco) are more exposed than westbound (Antibes to Saint-Tropez and beyond). Forecast wind daily; amend itineraries accordingly. High-speed runs (25+ knots) in wind are thrilling but choppy—moderate speeds (12–16 knots) are more comfortable.

Ferry Traffic: Avoid ferry routes (Nice–Corsica, Nice–Barcelona). These ferries run north-south; staying within 2nm of the coast (parallel to shore) avoids intersections. Visual watch and radar awareness are essential in summer traffic.

Sample Three-Day Motorboat Itinerary

Day 1: Embark Cannes morning. Motor to Îles de Lérins (11nm, 45 minutes). Explore Sainte-Marguerite fortress, swim, relax. Return Cannes evening (45 minutes). Dinner ashore.

Day 2: Cannes to Villefranche. Motor northeast (40nm, 2.5 hours cruise speed). Explore roadstead, anchor, swim. Dinner ashore in waterfront restaurants. Hotel overnight (not motorboat).

Day 3: Villefranche to Monaco. Motor 5nm (30 minutes). Explore Port Hercules or offshore. Aperitif ashore, return Villefranche evening (5nm, 30 minutes), or extend to overnight Monaco hotel. Fuel and provisions refill as needed.

Do I Need a Licence?

Licensing varies by nation and boat size. French inland waterways (rivers, canals) require Permis Bateau; Mediterranean coastal motoring under 7m often requires only safety course (one day, €50–€100). EU vessels under 7m with outboard engines may be license-exempt in some countries. Non-EU citizens should carry International Driving Permit (if applicable) and passport. Boat4You verifies licensing at booking; confirm requirements before travel.

Can I Rent for a Day or Half-Day?

Yes. Most operators offer hourly (€100–€200/hour), half-day (€300–€600), and daily (€800–€1,200) rates. Discounts apply for multi-day packages. Half-day (4-hour) charters suit Îles de Lérins day-trips from Cannes or Nice-based coastal exploration.

What About Fuel & Refuelling?

Fuel is guest responsibility unless stated otherwise. Most marinas offer fuel docks (diesel and petrol). Cost is €1.50–€2.50/litre; a motorboat burning 25 litres/hour at 15 knots covers 10 nautical miles per litre, so 100nm costs €150–€250 in fuel. Budget €30–€50 daily for typical cruising.

Is There Food & Drink Onboard?

Minimal. A motorboat galley cannot support meal preparation (no stove, no refrigerator typically). Bring coolers with sandwiches, snacks, and beverages. Beach clubs and waterfront restaurants are accessible via quick mooring or anchoring; lunch ashore is standard practice. Dinner requires return to hotel base or marina-side restaurant.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I book?

For peak season (July and August), reservations 4–6 months ahead secure the best fleet selection and pricing. Shoulder months (May, June, September) typically allow booking 6–8 weeks out.

What does the charter price include?

Base pricing usually covers the vessel, insurance, and marina fees at the home base. Fuel, provisioning, skipper service, end-cleaning, and tourist tax are generally added separately. Check the specific listing on Boat4You for a full breakdown.

Do I need a sailing licence?

For bareboat charter, a valid skipper licence plus a VHF certificate are typically required, and rules vary by country. If you don't hold one, hiring a professional skipper is straightforward — pricing runs roughly 150–220 euro per day plus meals.
