Nestled in the quiet seaside town of San Vincenzo, this modern facility combines professional services with the charm of a traditional Italian port. Boat4You partners with Marina di San Vincenzo to deliver seamless charter experiences, with all logistics pre-arranged and your yacht ready to sail within minutes of arrival.

Marina di San Vincenzo Facilities Overview

The marina accommodates vessels from 8 to 45 metres, with a total of approximately 300 berths across floating pontoons and fixed quays. Modern infrastructure includes 24-hour reception, CCTV security, and well-maintained holding tanks for waste water. Most berths feature dedicated 220-volt power (16A–32A), potable water hookups, and Wi-Fi coverage across the facility. The fuel dock dispenses diesel and petrol; fuel prices are competitive with regional averages, typically €1.10–€1.30 per litre depending on season.

The marina's crane can haul vessels up to 80 tonnes for repairs or storage, and a small chandlery stocks basic spares, charts, and provisions. A dedicated chart room near reception provides updated weather forecasts, admiralty charts, and local sailing guides. For larger repairs, Piombino's shipyards (35 kilometres south) and Livorno's (80 kilometres north) offer more comprehensive facilities.

Yacht Specifications and Berth Suitability

Most Boat4You charter yachts departing from Marina di San Vincenzo are monohulls ranging from 10 to 15 metres overall length, with drafts of 1.5 to 2.0 metres. These vessels fit comfortably in standard berths (approximately 4.5 metres wide by 12–18 metres long). Catamarans are occasionally available and require slightly wider berths; the marina has dedicated dual-pontoon spaces for vessels up to 15 metres wide.

The marina guarantees berths based on vessel dimensions at the time of booking. If you're uncertain whether your preferred yacht fits your berth, email our charter team at least two weeks in advance; we can arrange alternative berths or nearby marinas (Piombino, 35 kilometres south, has excellent modern facilities as a backup).

Most Boat4You charters include 2–3 hours of dedicated on-water orientation before your solo sailing begins. Your professional skipper or broker walks you through the yacht's systems: engine start, sail controls, autopilot operation, VHF radio, navigation equipment, and safety gear. You'll motor out to a quiet anchorage near San Vincenzo, practice hoisting sails, and run through basic manoeuvres. By the time you return to Marina di San Vincenzo for the night, you'll feel confident and ready for tomorrow's sail to Elba or Piombino.

If you've booked a crewed charter, your professional skipper and crew will be aboard at check-in, already familiar with the yacht and itinerary. They'll brief you on weather forecasts, proposed routes, and any special requests (fishing, diving, romantic dinners ashore).

All Boat4You yachts departing from Marina di San Vincenzo carry comprehensive safety systems: life jackets for all crew, emergency flares, life rafts (on vessels 12+ metres), first-aid kits, fire extinguishers, and EPIRB (Emergency Position Indicating Radio Beacon) communication devices. The marina's location—within easy reach of Italian Coast Guard stations and modern rescue facilities—adds an extra layer of protection. Your vessel inspection before departure includes verification of all safety systems; you'll receive a detailed safety briefing covering emergency procedures, radio protocols, and reporting requirements.

Weather and Sailing Conditions

The Maestrale (northwest wind) dominates the Liguria–Toscana coast from May through October, typically ranging 8–15 knots in spring and autumn, occasionally reaching 20+ knots in summer. The marina's sheltered bay protects the anchorage, but once you motor out past the breakwater, you'll feel the open-sea conditions. The chart room staff provide daily briefings on weather, sea state, and forecast trends; they're experienced in reading wind patterns and can advise on the best departure times for your intended route.

Tidal range is minimal (less than 0.5 metres), simplifying navigation and anchor watch. However, the Piombino Strait (between San Vincenzo and Elba) experiences tidal currents of up to 0.8 knots during neap tides and up to 1.5 knots during spring tides; timing your passage to use the current as an ally is essential—the marina staff will plot optimal passage times for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the daily mooring cost at Marina di San Vincenzo?

Visitor berths cost approximately €1.50–€2.50 per metre per night, depending on season. A 12-metre yacht would pay roughly €18–€30 per night. includes your first night's mooring in the charter package; additional nights (if you choose to stay beyond your charter dates) are billed separately at posted marina rates.

Can I berth a catamaran at Marina di San Vincenzo?

Yes, but availability is more limited. The marina has approximately 15–20 wide berths suitable for catamarans up to 15 metres beam. Book well in advance (minimum 4 weeks) if you require a catamaran charter from Marina di San Vincenzo; can confirm berth availability before finalizing your reservation.

Is fuel included in the charter price?

No, fuel is charged separately. delivers your yacht with a full tank; you pay for any additional fuel consumed during your charter at the posted marina rate (typically €1.10–€1.30 per litre diesel). You'll settle fuel costs at the end of your charter based on meter readings.

What if I need urgent repairs during my charter?

Marina di San Vincenzo has a small on-site workshop and relationships with trusted local mechanics and riggers. For engine issues, electrical problems, or minor sail repairs, call the marina office and they'll arrange a technician (typically available within 2–3 hours). For major repairs, Piombino's yards are 35 kilometres away and well-equipped for comprehensive work.

Is there secure parking for our car at the marina?

Yes. Marina di San Vincenzo provides free secure parking for charter guests. Simply provide your vehicle registration at check-in, and staff will direct you to the car park (a 5-minute walk from your berth). The lot is lit at night and monitored by CCTV.

What happens if our yacht requires emergency repairs during the charter?

Marina di San Vincenzo has a small workshop and relationships with trusted local mechanics. Engine problems, electrical issues, or minor sail repairs are typically addressed within 2–4 hours. For major repairs, Piombino's shipyards (35 kilometres south) are well-equipped and accessible.

Can we extend our charter if we're enjoying ourselves?

Yes, subject to availability. If your yacht isn't booked for subsequent weeks, can often extend your charter on a day-by-day basis. Extensions are charged at daily rates; contact our team immediately if you wish to extend so we can confirm yacht availability and arrange additional provisioning.
