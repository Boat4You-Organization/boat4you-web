RIB Advantages: Performance & Versatility

RIBs combine inflatable tube buoyancy with rigid fiberglass hulls, delivering unsinkable stability and thrilling performance. Twin 150+ horsepower outboards push speed to 40+ knots; fuel-efficient cruising at 15–20 knots minimizes consumption. Open-cockpit designs enable guest participation in operation: steering, throttle management, and navigation inputs become crew engagement activities rather than spectator experiences.

RIBs excel at water sports: towing for wakeboarding and waterskiing, access to remote snorkeling sites, and beach landings on shallow shores. Rigid tubes provide grab-handles and integrated safety rails; families appreciate stability despite high-speed capability. Zenta's protected basin suits RIB practice; once confident, crews explore open-water island runs.

FAQ: Motorboat & RIB Charters from Marina Zenta

Do I need a license to operate a motorboat at Zenta?

International Certificates of Competence or national boating licenses are typically required. Boat4You's vetting process reviews credentials; if you lack formal certifications but demonstrate boating experience, professional captain arrangements or rental-car equivalency standards may apply. Detailed experience disclosure during booking clarifies license requirements.

What's the difference between motorboats and RIBs?

Motorboats feature traditional fiberglass hulls with enclosed cabins, higher comfort for multi-day charters, and moderate speeds (25–35 knots). RIBs offer open-cockpit versatility, higher performance (40+ knots), superior fuel efficiency at cruising speeds, and water-sports compatibility. Choice depends on priorities: comfort favors motorboats, adventure favors RIBs.

Can we include water sports (wakeboarding, waterskiing)?

Yes. RIBs support towing for both activities; motorboats can accommodate adapted systems. arranges equipment rental and safety briefings. Trained operators (for towed sports) cost €50–€100 daily. Zenta's calm basin suits practice; open waters enable confident cruising once skills develop.

Is Zenta suitable for families with young children?

Absolutely. Motorboats' stability and covered areas provide shade and weather protection. RIBs' rigid hulls and safety rails reassure parents. Shallow island anchorages (Šolta, northern Brač coasts) enable safe swimming. 's family-focused operators coordinate child-suitable itineraries, snorkeling guidance, and age-appropriate activities.

What if we run out of fuel mid-charter?

Modern motorboats and RIBs have fuel-range capabilities (typically 80–150 nautical miles at cruising speed) exceeding normal daily runs. Crews monitor fuel gauges religiously. Zenta coordinators brief captains on fuel-dock locations at Brač and Hvar.
