Why Choose a Motorboat from Borik?

Motorboats eliminate the variables of sailing. No wind dependence, no sailing certifications required, and predictable daily distances mean you spend more time enjoying destinations and less time negotiating conditions. RIBs (rigid-hulled inflatables) add thrills: they're nimble in tight harbours, stable in chop, and perfect for small-group day trips and water-sports excursions.

D-Marin Borik's location makes it ideal for motorboat-based exploration. Zadar itself—with its Roman ruins, Sea Organ, and UNESCO Old Town—is a 10-minute drive away. Ugljan and Pašman islands sit less than an hour away by speedboat. The Kornati archipelago, a sailor's paradise of 140 limestone islands, is fully accessible within a single day's journey. Boat4You's motorboat fleet ranges from compact day boats (20–25 feet) to larger family cruisers (32–45 feet), ensuring you find the right vessel for your party and itinerary.

Day-Trip & Weekend Routes from Borik

The closest escape is Ugljan island, reachable in 30 minutes by motorboat. Its northern beaches, olive groves, and the village of Preko make perfect half-day destinations. Lunch at a harborside taverna, a swim in clear bays, and return to Borik by evening—ideal for those seeking land-based accommodation with day-boat flexibility.

Pašman, across the Pašman Channel, offers equally quick access. The narrow straits between Ugljan and Pašman create visually stunning passages; in summer, these protected waters warm to 24–25°C, perfect for families and swimmers. A typical day-trip itinerary includes Pašman Town (population ~400, quiet and authentic), several anchorages with crystalline water, and lunch catches prepared fresh at local restaurants.

For ambitious day trips, the northern Kornati islands are attainable. A speedboat departure at 8 AM reaches the outer Kornati by late morning, leaving a full afternoon for exploring dramatic rocky anchorages and swimming in pristine bays. The 50-nautical-mile round trip demands respect for weather and fuel management, but the Kornati's otherworldly beauty justifies the effort. Boat4You supports these extended excursions with fuel planning and route guidance.

Multi-Day Motorboat Charters: Weekly Itineraries

A week-long motorboat charter from Borik opens the entire northern Dalmatian coast. Day 1–2 might explore Ugljan and Pašman, anchoring in quiet bays and visiting fishing villages. Days 3–5 venture into the Kornati National Park: island-hopping between Sakarun Bay (dramatic white-pebble beach), the narrow Telašćica Bay, and several mooring buoys in pristine anchorages reserved for charter vessels.

Day 6 could push northwest to the Trogir region or northeast to the Croatian coast near Nin (famous for salt production and archaeological sites). Day 7 returns to Borik, giving a full day to re-provision and enjoy Zadar's restaurants before departure.

Larger motorboats (40+ feet) carry fuel efficiently, allowing fuel-economical cruising speeds of 18–24 knots. RIBs, by contrast, prefer higher speeds (25–35 knots) but burn fuel faster; they're best reserved for day trips and weekend getaways where speed thrills are part of the appeal.

Pricing & Motorboat Charter Costs

Day-trip motorboats (20–28 feet) typically cost €400–€800 per day. Weekend (three-day) packages run €900–€1,800. Weekly motorboat charters (32–40 feet) range from €2,000 to €4,500 depending on season and vessel size. RIBs are priced similarly but may be available for premium day-trip rates (€600–€1,200/day) due to their speed and sport-boat appeal.

Best Seasons & Weather Conditions

Spring (April–May) brings mild days, calm mornings, and few crowds—ideal for families and those avoiding summer heat. Summer (June–August) offers warm water (22–25°C) and longer daylight, perfect for extended day trips, though July–August sees higher tourist activity and boat traffic.

Autumn (September–October) delivers warm water (22–23°C), manageable crowds, and consistent winds that create picturesque sea conditions. Winter sailing is possible but demands respect for occasional Bura squalls and shorter daylight hours. Most charter operators run April through October; winter charters are available by special arrangement.

Provisioning & Local Food Culture

Zadar's daily market teems with fresh fish, local cheese (especially Pag cheese), cured meats, and produce. Supermarkets near Borik stock international brands and local specialities. Many charterers provision at Borik before departure, stocking easy meals and snacks for sea days.

Dining ashore is an essential part of any motorboat charter. Ugljan and Pašman villages host family-run tavernas serving gregada (traditional fish stew), octopus salads, and local wines. Zadar's restaurant scene ranges from casual beachfront pizzerias to upscale establishments featuring contemporary Dalmatian cuisine. Sunset dinners on the Riva promenade, with the Sea Organ's haunting melodies in the background, are unforgettable.

FAQ: Motorboat Charters from D-Marin Borik

Do I need a boating licence to charter a motorboat?

It depends on the vessel size and your home country. Most day-trip motorboats under 7 metres don't require a licence. Larger vessels (over 7 metres) may require a national boating certificate or an International Certificate of Competence (ICC). Boat4You reviews your credentials during booking and can arrange a skipper if needed. Visit how we work for full details.

What's the fuel consumption for a typical motorboat?

A 35-foot motorboat cruising at economical speed (12–15 knots) burns 8–12 litres per hour. RIBs at high speed (25+ knots) consume 20–40 litres per hour depending on engines and sea state. Fuel costs vary; expect €500–€800 per week for moderate use.

Can children and non-swimmers enjoy motorboat charters?

Absolutely. Motorboats offer stable platforms with spacious decks and safety railings. Many anchorages in Ugljan and Pašman have gentle beaches perfect for families. Life jackets and water-sports equipment (snorkels, inflatable toys) are available upon request. prioritizes family-friendly charters and can recommend kid-appropriate itineraries.

Are RIBs suitable for beginners?

Yes, if operated carefully. RIBs are stable and responsive but demand respect for speed and fuel consumption. recommends a skipper for day-trip RIBs if you lack motorboat experience. The thrilling ride and access to remote anchorages make them worth considering even if you hire professional support.
