Nea Peramos: A Village Sailing Gateway

Nea Peramos sits roughly 70 kilometres west of central Athens, nestled on Megara Bay—a body of water renowned for its seafood, sheltered anchorages, and access to the Saronic Gulf and beyond. Unlike crowded commercial ports, Nea Peramos retains village character: narrow streets lined with whitewashed tavernas, working fishing boats, and locals who remember every regular visitor.

The village's reputation for fresh seafood precedes it. Grilled octopus, sea urchin, and mussels caught that morning are standards at waterfront restaurants. Charter crews frequently anchor for lunch, order straight from the boat, and eat within minutes of the catch leaving the water. This is authentic Attica—no cruise-ship tourism, just real village life and remarkable seafood.

Why Catamaran from Nea Peramos?

Catamarans are ideal for exploring Megara Bay and the waters beyond. The shallow draft lets you anchor in coves inaccessible to deeper monohulls. The stability, generous cabin space, and low noise profile make catamarans perfect for families, large friend groups, or anyone seeking comfort combined with authentic sailing. Boat4You's 19-vessel fleet at Nea Peramos ranges 38–48 feet, sleeping 6–10 guests in separate cabins with ensuite bathrooms.

Nea Peramos' position offers direct access to the Corinth Canal—the dramatic waterway separating the Peloponnese from mainland Greece. Canal transits are unforgettable: towering white cliffs, expert canal pilots, and modern infrastructure make it one of the Mediterranean's signature passages.

Routes from Nea Peramos: West to the Ionian

Many crews departing Nea Peramos head northwesterly toward the Ionian Sea, transiting the Corinth Canal and exploring the Greek islands beyond: Kefalonia, Ithaca, and the sheltered Ionian coasts. This route, impossible from Lavrion, opens a different sailing world—pine forests, dramatic limestone cliffs, and fewer tourists than the Saronic.

Alternatively, sail eastward into the Saronic Gulf proper: Aegina, Poros, Hydra, and Spetses are all within reach. The quieter western Saronic (Megara Bay's deeper reaches) is ideal for slower days and mooring-field exploration.

Nea Peramos Facilities & Village Life

The village marina facility, though small by Attica standards (60–80 berths), is intimate and well-maintained. Water, power, and fuel are available. Waste disposal and recycling services are in place. Walking distance to the village means provisioning is straightforward: shops, bakeries, and the town's fish markets are metres away from the quay.

Nea Peramos town retains a working-class character: fewer souvenir shops, more family-run tavernas. Spend an evening wandering the waterfront, chatting with fishermen, and dining on local wine—this is the Attica experience many visitors dream of finding.

Seasons & Weather Patterns

The Megara Bay and northern Saronic see gentle northerly breezes in summer (10–16 knots average), gradually increasing as you sail eastward. Spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) offer beautiful light, warm days, and fewer boats. Summer (June–August) brings the reliable Etesian winds and warm, stable weather perfect for families.

Winter sailing is possible for experienced crews, though southerly gales and colder water temperatures make spring and autumn more pleasant for most charterers.

From Village to Galley: Provisioning & Dining

Boat4You recommends a half-day pre-charter meal in one of Nea Peramos' acclaimed seafood tavernas—many are run by multi-generation fishing families. Try the local Megara Bay mussels (in vinegar, grilled, or raw) and regional white wine. The village produces excellent local cheese and preserves, perfect for stocking your catamaran's galley.

Thursday and Friday bring the open market with fresh produce, vegetables, and local specialities. Most crews provision in the morning and sail by early afternoon, anchoring for sunset and dinner.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far is Nea Peramos from Athens airport?

Approximately 60–65 kilometres west of central Athens (roughly 75–90 minutes by road, depending on traffic). arranges airport transfers and shuttle services. The direct KTEL bus route to Nea Peramos is also available.

Can we transit the Corinth Canal with a catamaran?

Absolutely. The Canal is designed for vessels up to 48 metres long and 7 metres beam (width)—all catamarans fit comfortably. Licensed canal pilots guide you through; the experience is thrilling and safe.

What catamaran charter costs apply from Nea Peramos?

A 40-foot catamaran from Nea Peramos typically costs €2,800–€4,000 per week in summer peak. Shoulder seasons offer 25–35% discounts. Check Boat4You for current rates.

Is Nea Peramos suitable for families with young children?

Yes, ideal. Catamarans offer spacious deck space, calm sailing, and nearby anchorages. The village itself is quiet and family-friendly, with shallow beach areas perfect for swimming.

Can we visit local tavernas from our catamaran?

Absolutely. Most anchorages are within dinghy reach of tavernas. Order ahead by radio, and meals are often ready when you arrive. Nea Peramos taverna owners love welcoming sailors and often offer special rates to charter crews.

What if we want to visit both the Saronic and Ionian from one charter?

A longer 10-14 day charter can achieve this. Depart Nea Peramos, sail east to the Saronic for days 2-5, return west by day 7, transit the Corinth Canal, and explore the Ionian for days 8-14. plans complex itineraries routinely.
