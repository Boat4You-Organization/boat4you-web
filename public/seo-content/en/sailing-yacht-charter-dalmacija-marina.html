Sailing Routes: Kornati, Telašćica & Beyond

The Kornati National Park (20 nm south) offers 140 islands and stunning overnight anchorages. Sailing yachts excel here: consistent Maestral winds make the passage exhilarating, while stable anchorages allow restful nights. Plan three days minimum for Kornati exploration.

Telašćica Bay (25 nm southwest) features Mir saltwater lake, accessible through a narrow drying entrance—a thrilling passage under sail. Nearby Dugi Otok offers provisioning and cultural exploration. Many charters spend four to five days between Telašćica and Dugi Otok's eastern coast.

The Zadar archipelago—Ugljan, Pašman, and Brač—lie 10–20 nm distant. These islands reward exploration: Pašman's quiet villages, Ugljan's olive groves, and Brač's Bol anchorage combine sailing engagement with cultural richness.

Provisioning, Markets & Local Flavor

Zadar's waterfront markets (Voćna Tržnica for produce, Peškarija for fish) stock daily catches and local delicacies. Monkfish, sea urchin, and spiny lobster appear on market stalls. Olive oil from Pag, local Maraschino cherries, and Herzegovina wines round out provisioning.

Sailor-friendly restaurants dot the Zadar coast. Vegetable-based brudet (stew), pašticada (braised beef), and fresh grilled fish suit post-sailing appetites. Many establishments offer group discounts and reserve tables for visiting sailors.

What's the difference between a yacht's main and jib?

The main (larger sail aft) provides power and directional control. The jib (smaller sail forward) fine-tunes pointing and balance. Monohulls like our fleet typically carry both, plus optional cruising chutes for downwind passages.

Can we hire a professional skipper for our charter?
