Sailing Yacht Routes & Exploration

From Alimos, sailing yachts access the full spectrum of Aegean cruising. The Saronic Gulf—sheltered and scenic—offers gentle first days: Aegina (14 nm), Poros (25 nm), Hydra (40 nm), and Spetses (55 nm) welcome sailors of all levels. Traditional harbours, Byzantine ruins, and hidden anchorages reward exploration.

Round Cape Sounion (20 nm southeast) and sail into the deeper Cyclades: Kythnos, Kea, and Serifos offer solitude, white sand beaches, and authentic village life far from cruise-ship crowds. The Argolic coast—Porto Cheli, Ermioni, Navplio—provides Peloponnesian history, excellent provisioning, and spectacular anchorages.

Adventurous crews can traverse the historic Corinthian Canal, entering the Corinthian Gulf and reaching Itea, Galaxidi, and even sailing near ancient Delphi. Spring and autumn winds favour long-distance passages; summer Meltemi (15–25 kts) provides thrilling downwind runs through the islands.

Sailing Yacht Classes & Specifications

Alimos offers diverse sailing monohulls. Entry-level family cruisers (32–36 feet, 6–8 berths, 1.8m draft) suit slower cruising, village exploration, and large crews. Mid-range yachts (38–42 feet, 8–10 berths, 1.9m draft) balance comfort and performance, with spacious cabins and modern galleys.

Performance cruisers (44–46 feet, 10–12 berths, 2.0m+ draft) feature advanced sail plans, nav systems, and tender hoists. These attract experienced sailors eager for longer passages and spirited sailing. All vessels include safety equipment (life jackets, harnesses, flares), navigation aids (GPS, chartplotter, autopilot), and communication (VHF, SSB on larger yachts).

Galley facilities vary by class: smaller yachts have compact galley corners; larger vessels feature full kitchens with cookers, fridges, and storage. Heads (bathrooms) range from chemical toilets on compact boats to en-suite electric systems on premium charters. Comfort increases significantly above 40 feet.

Weather, Seasons & Sailing Conditions

The Saronic Gulf and surrounding waters enjoy Mediterranean climate: warm, mostly dry summers and mild winters with occasional storms. May–June and September–October are ideal for sailing: winds 8–15 knots, sunny days, and uncrowded anchorages. Sea temperature peaks at 27°C (August) and drops to 13°C (February).

July–August brings the notorious Meltemi—a dry northerly wind averaging 15–25 knots, sometimes gusting 30+. Experienced sailors relish the strong wind; families may prefer calm anchorages. Winter sailing (November–March) challenges with occasional gales, cold air, and erratic winds, but rewards solo adventurers with solitude.

Spring and autumn typically offer the best sailing, with consistent winds, clear skies, and balanced temperatures. Boat4You advises first-time charters to book May, June, or September for optimal conditions and enjoyable passages.

Sailing Yacht Charter FAQs

What sailing experience do I need for a bareboat charter?

Most bare boat operators at Alimos require RYA Day Skipper, IYT Bareboat Skipper, or equivalent (sailing knowledge + mooring experience). If you lack certification, hire a skipper or take a refresher course at the marina before departure. coordinates certifications seamlessly.

How much does a sailing yacht charter cost?

Weekly rates at Alimos range €900–€3,000 depending on boat size, season, and inclusions. High season (July–August) costs more; shoulder seasons (May–June, September) offer better value. Fuel, skipper, and provisioning are typically extra. shows transparent, all-in pricing.

Can I hire a skipper for a week?

Yes. Professional skippers based at Alimos charge €120–€200/day (or €700–€1,200/week all-inclusive). Hiring a skipper removes navigation stress, adds expertise, and is ideal for groups wanting to learn or relax while exploring.

What's the best season for sailing yacht charters?

May–June and September–October offer ideal winds (8–15 knots), warm seas, and moderate crowds. July–August bring strong Meltemi winds and peak tourism. Winter sailing suits experienced sailors seeking solitude and challenge.

Do sailing yachts include sails and safety gear?

Yes. All sailing yachts include modern sails (main + jib, often genoa), safety harnesses, life jackets, life rafts, flares, and first-aid kits. Navigation equipment (GPS, charts, compass) is standard. Confirm exact inclusions in your listing details.
