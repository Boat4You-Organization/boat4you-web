Fort Burt Marina and Historical Significance

Fort Burt Marina occupies the site of a 17th-century military fortification, later expanded in the 18th century. Today, restored fort walls frame the marina, creating a unique sailing atmosphere blending history and modernity. The marina provides protected berths, fuel dock, fresh water, ice, and provisioning services. Reception facilities include showers, laundry, and dinghy parking. On-site restaurants and bars offer convenient dining and evening entertainment with panoramic harbor views.

The fort itself is partially accessible to visitors, offering insight into the BVI's colonial maritime heritage. Classic Island Routes from Fort Burt

Virgin Gorda's The Baths—the BVI's most iconic snorkelling destination—lies 15 nautical miles south. Cooper Island's Manchioneel Bay provides protected overnight anchorage, hiking trails, and world-class snorkelling at Carrot Rock and Cistern Rock. Jost Van Dyke, 20 nautical miles northwest, hosts legendary Foxy's and Soggy Dollar Bar on White Bay. Seasonal Sailing Conditions and Weather Patterns

The BVI peak sailing season runs November through May. December–February offers the strongest trade winds (15–22 knots), most stable weather, and ideal conditions for active sailing. March–May brings slightly lighter winds, calmer seas, and fewer crowds. Easter holidays (March–April) attract families; book early for spring vacation sailing.

Autumn sailing (September–November) provides variable winds but rewarding experiences and discounted rates. October–November features occasional tropical showers but excellent sailing between weather systems. Summer (June–August) offers lighter trade winds, warmer water, and quiet anchorages. Hurricane season (June–November) is unusual in the BVI; the islands lie south of typical hurricane tracks.

Christmas Winds (December 20–27) bring temporarily strengthened trade winds (18–25+ knots)—exciting for performance sailors but requiring weather vigilance.
