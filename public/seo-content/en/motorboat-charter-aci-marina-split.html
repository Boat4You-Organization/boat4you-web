How much fuel does a typical motorboat consume?

Motorboats (30 feet) typically burn 5–12 gallons per hour at cruising speed. A full tank (30–60 gallons) supports 50–150 nautical miles, depending on speed and conditions. Regular refueling at ACI Marina Split or island fuel docks prevents concerns.

Are motorboats comfortable for overnight stays?

Most motorboats feature basic sleeping cabins and heads (bathrooms). Compact but functional layouts suit couples and small groups. Open-air deck areas extend livable space. Crews balance onboard nights with harborside restaurant meals.

Can motorboats handle the Adriatic's occasional rough conditions?

Motorboats designed for the Adriatic handle moderate waves competently. Simple engineering and robust construction provide reliability. However, strong winds (25+ knots) warrant sheltering in protected harbors. provides weather guidance and safety support.
