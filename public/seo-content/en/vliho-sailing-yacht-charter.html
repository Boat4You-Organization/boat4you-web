Vliho Bay: Natural Harbor Excellence

Vliho Bay's geology creates the Ionian's most sheltered anchorage. The bay's deep shape, protection from all quarters, and sand-mud bottom combine to create perfect holding conditions that have made Vliho legendary among cruising sailors and wintering yacht fleets. Winter Tramontana and occasional strong southeasterlies that affect open anchorages simply don't reach Vliho's protected interior with any force. This protection—combined with professional anchorpoint systems and the Vliho Yacht Club's watchfulness—creates an anchorage where boats safely rest for months undisturbed.

Deep water (8-12 meters) means large cruising yachts anchor comfortably without dragging risk, and the sandy-mud bottom grips anchors with exceptional security that experienced sailors immediately recognize and appreciate. Decades of cruising guides and sailor reports confirm Vliho as one of the Mediterranean's most reliable anchoring locations—a fact that explains why wintering fleets choose Vliho above more commercially developed alternatives with less natural protection and holding characteristics.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I book?

For peak season (July and August), reservations 4–6 months ahead secure the best fleet selection and pricing. Shoulder months (May, June, September) typically allow booking 6–8 weeks out.

What does the charter price include?

Base pricing usually covers the vessel, insurance, and marina fees at the home base. Fuel, provisioning, skipper service, end-cleaning, and tourist tax are generally added separately. Check the specific listing on Boat4You for a full breakdown.

Do I need a sailing licence?

For bareboat charter, a valid skipper licence plus a VHF certificate are typically required, and rules vary by country. If you don't hold one, hiring a professional skipper is straightforward — pricing runs roughly 150–220 euro per day plus meals.
