Sailing Yacht Charter at Marina di Scarlino, Follonica, Tuscany: Modern Port in the Golfo di Follonica

Marina di Scarlino, located in Follonica on the Golfo di Follonica (Bay of Follonica), represents one of Tuscany's most modern and well-equipped yacht facilities. Situated 12 kilometres south of San Vincenzo and 20 kilometres north of Piombino, this strategically positioned marina offers exceptional access to Elba Island, the Giglio Islands, and the broader Tuscan Archipelago. The marina combines contemporary infrastructure with a tranquil, less-crowded atmosphere, making it an attractive alternative to busier ports. Boat4You operates sailing yachts at Marina di Scarlino, offering guests the perfect blend of modern amenities and authentic Tuscan sailing experiences.

Marina di Scarlino: Premier Modern Facility

Marina di Scarlino is a showcase of modern maritime infrastructure. The facility accommodates 700+ yachts across two main basins, with berths for vessels from small dinghies to 80-metre superyachts. Each berth is equipped with 16-amp electricity (32-amp available for larger vessels), fresh water, and WiFi connectivity. The marina's reception operates 24/7, providing comprehensive guest services: customs support, provisioning coordination, concierge assistance, and entertainment bookings.

Fuel services are state-of-the-art: diesel, petrol, and LPG are available via dedicated fuel dock with card payment and loyalty programmes. The marina includes a full-service boatyard with haul-out cranes capable of lifting vessels to 100 tonnes, enabling maintenance, repair, and refit services. Dedicated service facilities include a chandlery, electrical workshop, mechanical repair shops, and fiberglass specialists. The marina village incorporates restaurants, bars, shops, and a hotel, creating a self-contained nautical community.

Location Advantages: Golfo di Follonica Positioning

The Golfo di Follonica is a sheltered bay offering protection from strong southwesterly winds common in the Tyrrhenian Sea. This geographic advantage makes Scarlino particularly appealing during spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October) when weather transitions can bring stronger conditions. The wide, open bay provides excellent holding ground (sand and mud) for anchoring and minimal tidal swing (typically less than 1 metre), creating stable conditions for moored vessels.

The bay's geography also provides scenic sailing within the gulf itself. Day sails within the Golfo di Follonica offer calm-water exploration ideal for families, beginners, or those recovering from longer passages. The protected waters are ideal for swimming, snorkelling, and sailing training.

Access to Elba Island and the Tuscan Archipelago

Elba Island lies approximately 20 nautical miles (3–4 hours sailing) directly south of Marina di Scarlino. The route is straightforward—a beam reach in typical Mistral winds, providing ideal sailing conditions. This proximity makes Scarlino an excellent launch point for Elba-focused charters. Piombino's ferry terminal is nearby (15 nautical miles), connecting to the main Elba ferry; however, direct yacht sailing from Scarlino to Elba offers a superior sailing experience and greater flexibility.

Giglio Island lies approximately 30 nautical miles south-southwest, an easy full-day passage. The route passes the picturesque fortress village of Castiglioncello (en route—often included as a provisioning stop or overnight anchorage), making for natural stopping points and varied scenery. Capraia and the broader archipelago are similarly accessible, typically within 8–10 hours sailing.

Follonica: Coastal Town Atmosphere and Amenities

Follonica is a charming coastal town of 10,000 residents, significantly less touristy than San Vincenzo or Piombino yet offering all necessary amenities. The waterfront promenade features family-friendly beaches, seaside restaurants, ice cream shops, and casual bars. The town's historic centre (10 minutes walk from the marina) contains shops, restaurants, and cultural sites including a 19th-century iron foundry heritage museum. Local specialities include fresh seafood, focaccia, and regional Tuscan wines.

Accommodation ashore is available if you choose to extend your stay; Follonica is less crowded than nearby coastal resorts, making it an attractive base for pre- or post-charter relaxation. The town fills with Italian families during summer holidays but maintains a local character beyond peak season.

Sailing Routes from Marina di Scarlino

A typical week-long charter from Scarlino might follow this itinerary: Depart Tuesday morning for Elba's central coast near Portoferraio (3–4 hours sailing). Explore Elba's harbours, villages, and historic sites associated with Napoleon's exile (Tuesday–Thursday). Thursday afternoon, sail toward Capraia (6–8 hours, typically an overnight passage) or Giglio (5–6 hours), depending on wind and group preferences. Friday–Saturday: explore the chosen island's anchorages, villages, and marine reserves. Sunday morning: return passage toward Tuscany with possible intermediate stops at Castiglioncello or coastal villages. Sunday afternoon arrival back at Scarlino.

Alternative itineraries focus entirely on Elba with extended time for hiking, diving at shipwreck sites, and cultural exploration. Multi-week charters extend to Corsica, the Ligurian coast, or southern Tuscan coastlines (Maremma region).

Seasonality and Wind Patterns

Marina di Scarlino operates year-round, but the ideal sailing season runs May through October. Summer (June–August) brings warm temperatures (26–28°C), reliable northwesterly Mistral winds (12–18 knots), and long daylight hours—but also peak prices and crowded anchorages. May and September offer excellent balance: reliable sailing winds, warm weather (22–25°C), fewer crowds, and better value. April and October are shoulder season; weather becomes less predictable but opportunities for reduced-price charters and solitude increase.

Piombino remains the primary ferry gateway to Elba; Scarlino is preferable for pure sailing experiences.
