Island Routes from Marina Kos: Sponge-Diving & Culture

Kalymnos (8nm northeast) stands as the Eastern Mediterranean's sponge-diving capital—colorful traditional boats, active divers preparing for morning expeditions, and harbors unchanged for centuries. You can purchase authentic sea sponges directly from harvesters, supporting island livelihoods and connecting to traditions nearly lost to industrialization. Leros (12nm) offers sheltered anchorages, World War II history, and unspoiled beaches. Lipsi (18nm) features white-sand isolation and world-class snorkeling. Patmos (32nm), home of the Apocalypse monastery, attracts pilgrims and culture-seekers. Nisyros (28nm), an active volcano, provides thermal springs and geological drama. Each island is reached comfortably within 2–8 hours of sailing, leaving full daylight for swimming and exploration.

What's the Minimum Sailing Experience Required for Bareboat?

Most bareboat operators require ICC (International Certificate of Competence) or equivalent sailing credentials demonstrating navigation, safety, and seamanship competency. Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I book?

For peak season (July and August), reservations 4–6 months ahead secure the best fleet selection and pricing. Shoulder months (May, June, September) typically allow booking 6–8 weeks out.

What does the charter price include?

Base pricing usually covers the vessel, insurance, and marina fees at the home base. Fuel, provisioning, skipper service, end-cleaning, and tourist tax are generally added separately. Check the specific listing on Boat4You for a full breakdown.

Do I need a sailing licence?

For bareboat charter, a valid skipper licence plus a VHF certificate are typically required, and rules vary by country. If you don't hold one, hiring a professional skipper is straightforward — pricing runs roughly 150–220 euro per day plus meals.
