Motor Yacht Routes from Vodice

Vodice's central Dalmatian position enables access to diverse destinations, all manageable within week-long charter timeframes.

Kornati National Park (15 nm, 2 hours transit)

Kornati's 89 islands and dramatic limestone cliffs form the region's signature cruising ground. Motor yachts reach prime mooring fields (Piskera, Lavsa) within two hours, allowing full afternoon and evening leisure time. The park's protected holding ground—typically sand and mud in 10–25 feet water—accommodates motor yacht anchoring comfortably. Days spent exploring hidden coves, swimming in turquoise water, and evening anchorages define the Kornati experience.

Pakleni Islands & Hvar (40 nm, 4–5 hours transit)

Pakleni Islands cluster southwest of Hvar town, offering pristine anchorages and pine-covered scenery. Motor yachts typically reach Pakleni anchorages in mid-afternoon, allowing full leisure time. Hvar town—famous for lavender fields, cultural heritage, and lively nightlife—provides excellent restaurants, provisioning, and social opportunities. Many charters balance quiet Pakleni anchorages with visits to bustling Hvar.

Vis & Komiža (50 nm, 5–6 hours transit)

Vis, Croatia's most remote inhabited island, offers dramatic scenery, bohemian atmosphere, and pristine anchorages far from tourist crowds. Komiža, Vis's charming fishing village, retains authentic character and serves exceptional seafood. Motor yachts' speed makes this destination accessible within week-long charters, whereas sailing vessels might require extended time at sea.

Primošten & Zlarin (10–15 nm, 1–2 hours transit)

For guests preferring closer exploration, Primošten's pinewood coastline and Zlarin's car-free island character offer quieter alternatives. Motor yachts' convenience enables flexible day-tripping: morning departures, afternoon arrivals, evening anchorages elsewhere. This flexibility suits groups with varying interests or those seeking confidence-building exploration.

Krka National Park Excursions

While motor yachts cannot navigate Krka's shallow river, the park's famous Skradinski Buk waterfall complex lies 30 minutes inland from Vodice. Most crews anchor safely while arranging land-based excursions. Traditional Dalmatian peka and fresh fish await at riverside restaurants near the park entrance.

Bareboat vs. Skippered Motor Yacht Options

Boat4You offers both bareboat and skippered motor yacht charters, each suited to different crew profiles.

Bareboat Motor Yachts

Bareboat charters place full operational responsibility on your group. You navigate, handle propulsion, manage mooring and anchoring, and manage onboard systems. Boat4You requires either a recognized motor yacht qualification (RYA Power Boat Level 2 or equivalent) or documented prior motor yacht experience. Bareboat rates are typically 15–25% lower than skippered alternatives, offering cost savings for experienced operators.

Skippered Motor Yachts

A professional captain joins your crew, handling navigation and seamanship while enabling your group to enjoy cruising without full operational responsibility. Skippered rates typically add 150–250 EUR per day but provide invaluable expertise, local knowledge, and reduced operational stress. This option suits first-time charterers, mixed-experience crews, and guests prioritizing relaxation over hands-on operation.

Seasonal Motor Yacht Cruising

Motor yacht charters operate May through October, with variations in weather, pricing, and tourist activity.

Summer Season (June–August)

Warm seawater (26–27°C), reliable conditions, and full provisioning infrastructure characterize peak season. Summer thermals (12–18 knots from northwest) provide comfortable cruising conditions. Tourism peaks; popular dates book 6+ months in advance. Summer rates command premium pricing but offer most reliable weather and full-service tourist infrastructure.

Shoulder Seasons (May, September–October)

Milder conditions, fewer charter groups, and superior value distinguish these periods. May water temperature reaches 19–20°C; September maintains warmth before declining through October. Early autumn offers exceptional light, uncrowded harbors, and responsive local service. Many experienced charterers prefer September for optimal balance of comfort, value, and solitude.

Winter Considerations

November through April sees limited charter activity due to less predictable weather. Bora winds (northeasterly gusts) and occasional Jugo systems (southerly) require active weather management. manages winter bookings case-by-case for experienced crews.
