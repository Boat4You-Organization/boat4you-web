Why Choose a Power Catamaran?

Power catamarans deliver distinct advantages: the twin-hull design provides exceptional stability, even in choppy seas, while twin diesel engines offer redundancy and impressive speed—typically 18–22 knots for the vessels at Marina Kremik. The shallow draft allows access to coves and anchorages unreachable by larger monohull powerboats. Wide deck spaces and comfortable seating make the power catamaran ideal for groups prioritizing comfort and speed over traditional sailing.

Fuel efficiency is a key advantage. Power catamarans consume 20–30% less fuel than equivalent single-hull motorboats at the same speed, extending range and reducing provisioning concerns. Best Seasons for Power Catamaran Charters

Spring (April–May) offers warm days, calm mornings, and water temperatures of 17–19°C. Crowds are minimal, and the region's natural beauty is at peak condition. Summer (June–August) brings consistent winds and sunny skies, with water temperatures of 23–26°C—ideal for swimming and water sports.

Autumn (September–October) combines warm water (22–24°C) with manageable crowds and stable weather patterns. Winter sailing is possible for experienced crews but involves shorter days and occasional rough conditions. Most commercial power catamaran charters operate April through October.
