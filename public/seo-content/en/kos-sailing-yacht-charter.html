Kos: The Dodecanese's Historical & Cultural Heart

Kos Island birthed Western medicine—Hippocrates, the father of modern medical ethics, was born here around 460 BC. The ancient plane tree under which he taught still stands in the agora, shaded by gnarled branches unchanged for 2,400 years. The Neratzia Castle, built by the Knights of St John in the 14th century, overlooks the harbor with commanding medieval presence. Inland, the Asclepeion temple—sanctuary of the healing god Asclepius—attracts pilgrims and historians alike. For sailors, Kos offers immediate cultural engagement: museums within walking distance, markets bursting with local produce, and tavernas where recipes have been passed down through generations.

Kalymnos: Sponge-Diving Heritage & Island Culture

Kalymnos, just 8 nautical miles from Kos, represents a must-sail destination. The island's economy revolves around sponge-diving—a tradition spanning centuries. Harbors brim with traditional sponge boats and divers preparing for morning expeditions. You can purchase authentic sea sponges directly from harvesters, supporting local livelihoods. Tavernas serve octopus (grilled or stewed), sea urchin (uni), and lobster. If your skipper arranges it, watching or participating in sponge-diving provides unmatched cultural immersion—a connection to maritime traditions nearly lost to industrialization.

Frequently Asked Questions

How far in advance should I book?

For peak season (July and August), reservations 4–6 months ahead secure the best fleet selection and pricing. Shoulder months (May, June, September) typically allow booking 6–8 weeks out.

What does the charter price include?

Base pricing usually covers the vessel, insurance, and marina fees at the home base. Fuel, provisioning, skipper service, end-cleaning, and tourist tax are generally added separately. Check the specific listing on Boat4You for a full breakdown.

Do I need a sailing licence?

For bareboat charter, a valid skipper licence plus a VHF certificate are typically required, and rules vary by country. If you don't hold one, hiring a professional skipper is straightforward — pricing runs roughly 150–220 euro per day plus meals.
