Frequently Asked Questions About Motorboat Charter

What certification do I need to bareboat charter a motorboat?

Motorboat charters from Marina Kremik typically require RYA Level 2 Powerboat or equivalent certification demonstrating competency in powerboat operation and safety. Boat4You's team reviews qualifications and recommends skippered options if operator certification isn't held.

How much fuel does a motorboat consume daily?

Motorboats typically consume 8–15 liters fuel daily depending on engine size and operating speed. Cruising speed (8–12 knots) minimizes consumption, while faster passage (15+ knots) increases fuel costs. Typical daily motoring from Marina Kremik requires 10–20 liters, representing modest operational expense.

Are motorboats suitable for family holidays?

Yes. Motorboats suit families with children due to slower speeds reducing seasickness risk, straightforward operation minimizing crew stress, and accessible cabin accommodation. Marina Kremik's calm bay and nearby island anchorages provide gentle introduction to boating. Skippered charters allow parents to focus on family enjoyment rather than navigation.

What provisioning and supply options are available?

Primošten markets offer fresh fish, produce, bread, wine, and dry goods at reasonable prices. Motorboat operators manage provisioning independently, though some charters offer pre-provisioning services. Marina Kremik's location between Šibenik and Rogoznica provides access to larger provisioning if extended itineraries require stocking.

Can I make multiple day trips instead of overnight charters?

Yes. Motorboat charters from Marina Kremik accommodate flexible arrangements including single-day expeditions, overnight trips, and multi-day packages. Bareboat rental by the day enables return-to-marina operations for crews preferring hotel accommodation. Skippered day trips provide guided Kornati exploration with evening return to Primošten.
