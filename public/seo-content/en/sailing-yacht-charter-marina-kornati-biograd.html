Sailing Routes: Kornati, Pašman & Island Passages

Kornati National Park, 5 nautical miles south, is a 40-minute sail depending on wind. This UNESCO-protected seascape of 140 islands offers protected anchorages, restaurants accessible only by water, and underwater visibility exceeding 25 meters. Sailing yachts anchor in Sipan, Katić, Žut, and Sakarun, each offering distinct character—limestone cliffs, pine-backed shores, and traditional fish taverns.

Pašman and Ugljan islands, 8–12 nautical miles away, provide sheltered passages and village anchorages. Narrow straits between these islands suit less experienced crews and offer charming harbors with excellent provisioning. Multi-day itineraries often anchor between Pašman and Ugljan, exploring both shores.

Telašćica Bay, 18 nautical miles southwest, is achievable in a long day sail or relaxed overnight passage. The bay shelters a saltwater lake (Mir) accessible via narrow channel, dramatic 150-meter limestone cliffs, and one of the Mediterranean's most spectacular natural harbors. Sailing yachts anchor in deep, protected water while crews explore the lake by dinghy.

Dugi Otok's southern coast and Drvenik islands offer day-sail alternatives for shorter charters. Steady afternoon Maestral winds make these passages reliable and enjoyable.

Local Winds & Sailing Conditions

The Zadar region's primary wind is the Maestral, a northwesterly sea breeze that builds during afternoon hours (typically 11 AM–6 PM) during summer months. Morning offshore winds (Bura) are less common but can bring stronger conditions. Winter brings occasional Sirocco (southeasterly) winds. Water temperatures range from 11°C (January) to 24°C (August). Tidal range is minimal—typically 0.4 meters—so anchoring relies on holding rather than tidal movement.
